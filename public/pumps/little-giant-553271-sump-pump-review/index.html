Extended Analysis

Little Giant is a brand of Franklin Electric and has been manufacturing sump and sewage pumps since 1941. The 553271 (also designated 6-CIA) is the brand's standard automatic sump pump — a 1/3 HP thermoplastic submersible with an integrated float switch that activates when water reaches the switch level.

The thermoplastic construction makes this pump appropriate for clean water and lightly contaminated sump applications. For sewage, grease-contaminated water, or solids-laden fluids, the 553271 is not the correct specification — Zoeller's M53 or Wayne's CDU series handle those applications better. For clean groundwater infiltration in mechanical rooms, elevator pits, and below-grade utility spaces, the 553271's material construction is appropriate and its flow characteristics cover the majority of commercial applications.

The vertical float switch design activates at a specific water level and deactivates at a lower level, providing an on/off control band of approximately 4-6 inches of water level. This relatively wide band means the pump cycles less frequently than tethered float designs, which extends motor and switch life. For high-volume inflow applications where the pump runs nearly continuously, a wide on/off band is preferable to narrow band designs that cycle excessively.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a sump pump and a sewage pump?

A sump pump handles clean or lightly contaminated groundwater infiltration — it is not designed for solids, sewage, or grease. A sewage pump (or effluent pump) handles wastewater containing solids up to a specified diameter and is constructed with impeller geometries designed to pass solids without clogging. Using a sump pump for sewage service will result in rapid failure.

How often should a commercial sump pump be replaced?

Commercial sump pumps in continuously wet pits typically last 5-7 years with regular inspection. Pumps in dry sumps that activate infrequently can last 10+ years. Factors that shorten pump life include high cycle frequency, chemical contamination, and extended dry-run events if the float switch fails in the on position. Annual inspection and testing is recommended for commercial applications.

What discharge pipe size does the Little Giant 553271 require?

The 553271 has a 1.5-inch NPT discharge connection. A 1.5-inch PVC discharge line is standard. The discharge line should include a check valve within 12 inches of the pump discharge to prevent backflow when the pump shuts off. Without a check valve, the pump must re-prime the full discharge head each cycle, which can cause water hammer and increased wear.

Can the Little Giant 553271 run continuously?

The motor is thermally protected and can handle extended run periods, but continuous operation in a dry sump will overheat the motor. The pump is cooled by the water it sits in — a sump that drains below the motor housing will cause thermal shutdown. For high-volume applications where the pump may run dry between inflow events, a float switch that maintains minimum water level around the pump body is recommended.

What alarm options are available for the Little Giant 553271?

The 553271 itself does not include an alarm output. A separate high-water alarm float switch connected to an audible alarm or BAS dry contact input is standard practice for commercial applications where a failed pump could cause significant damage. Little Giant and other manufacturers offer companion alarm kits designed for this application.
