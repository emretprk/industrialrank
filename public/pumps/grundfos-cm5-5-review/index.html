Extended Analysis: Grundfos CM5-5 in Pressure Boosting Applications

The Grundfos CM5-5 is a horizontal, multi-stage centrifugal pump producing approximately 5 GPM at 95 feet of head (41 PSI) — the performance envelope that covers the majority of commercial pressure boosting, reverse osmosis feed, and small hydronic system applications. The CM series uses AISI 316 stainless steel wetted components throughout, which distinguishes it from the CI (cast iron) alternative for applications involving chlorinated water, treated water, or light corrosive fluids where cast iron wetted surfaces would require coating maintenance or would corrode in service.

Multi-stage design means the CM5-5 uses five impeller stages in series, each adding approximately 8-10 PSI of pressure to the flow. This is more mechanically efficient than a single-stage pump producing the same head, which would require a significantly larger impeller diameter and motor. The result is a compact, quiet pump with a smaller footprint than equivalent single-stage alternatives — relevant in mechanical rooms where space is limited and noise transmission is a concern.

The CM5-5 operates at fixed speed in its standard configuration. For variable-demand applications — domestic hot water recirculation, cooling tower makeup, or irrigation systems with varying zone demand — Grundfos offers the CME variant with a built-in frequency converter for variable-speed operation. The CME achieves 30-45% energy savings in applications with significant part-load operation versus fixed-speed operation with a bypass valve arrangement, which is the conventional but inefficient alternative.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the maximum pressure the Grundfos CM5-5 can produce?

The CM5-5 produces a maximum head of approximately 145 feet (63 PSI) at shutoff (zero flow). At its rated duty point of 5 GPM, it produces 95 feet (41 PSI). The operating range extends from zero flow at maximum head down to approximately 8 GPM at reduced head, though operating at the extremes of the curve for extended periods accelerates wear and is not recommended for continuous-duty applications.

What is the difference between the Grundfos CM and CME series?

The CM series operates at fixed motor speed (typically 3,450 RPM on 60 Hz power). The CME series integrates a built-in variable frequency drive (VFD) that modulates motor speed in response to a pressure sensor signal, maintaining constant discharge pressure regardless of flow variation. In applications with variable demand, the CME achieves 30-45% energy savings compared to a fixed-speed CM operating against a throttled bypass. The CME carries a higher upfront cost — typically 40-60% more than the equivalent CM — but the payback in variable-demand applications is typically 2-4 years.

What materials are the CM5-5 wetted components made from?

Impellers, diffusers, and the pump head are AISI 316 stainless steel. The mechanical seal is carbon/ceramic with EPDM elastomers in the standard configuration. Optional seal materials including Viton (FKM) elastomers and silicon carbide/silicon carbide seal faces are available for applications involving hydrocarbons or abrasive fluids. The motor shaft is stainless steel. No cast iron or aluminum wetted components are used in the CM series.

Can the Grundfos CM5-5 run dry?

No. Like all centrifugal pumps with mechanical seals, the CM5-5 requires liquid lubrication of the mechanical seal at all times during operation. Running dry for more than 30-60 seconds can overheat and destroy the mechanical seal, requiring replacement. For applications with intermittent water supply or risk of dry running, Grundfos offers a dry-run protection relay (DMP) accessory that monitors motor current and shuts the pump down if dry running is detected.

What is the electrical requirement for the Grundfos CM5-5?

The CM5-5 is available in 1-phase 115V/60Hz, 1-phase 230V/60Hz, and 3-phase 230/460V/60Hz configurations. The 5-stage, 0.5 HP version draws approximately 5.2 amps at 115V single-phase. A dedicated 15-amp circuit is sufficient for single-phase 115V installations. Three-phase configurations require a motor starter or VFD and are typically specified for commercial and industrial installations where 3-phase power is available.
