Extended Analysis

Rinnai holds approximately 40% of the U.S. residential tankless water heater market by volume — a dominant position built on product reliability and service network depth. The RL75iN is Rinnai's mid-tier non-condensing unit, positioned between the entry-level RU series and the condensing units that achieve 0.95+ efficiency.

The non-condensing design at 0.82 EF is less efficient than condensing alternatives (0.95+) but requires conventional flue venting rather than PVC condensate drainage. In retrofit applications where a conventional gas water heater is being replaced, the RL75iN can often use the existing flue connection. Condensing units require new venting, which adds $300-$800 to installed cost in retrofit scenarios.

At 7.5 GPM maximum, the RL75iN handles the simultaneous demand of two standard shower heads (2-2.5 GPM each) with margin for a third fixture. For applications with higher simultaneous demand — four or more simultaneous fixtures in commercial settings — Rinnai's RU199 (9.8 GPM) or multiple unit cascade configurations are required.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between condensing and non-condensing tankless?

Non-condensing units (like the RL75iN) exhaust hot combustion gases at ~350°F and achieve 0.80-0.85 efficiency. They use metal flue venting. Condensing units cool exhaust gases to ~100°F, extracting additional heat and achieving 0.95+ efficiency, but require PVC venting and condensate drainage. In retrofit applications where conventional flue venting exists, non-condensing units avoid new venting costs that often exceed the efficiency premium.

How does Rinnai cascade installation work?

Multiple Rinnai units can be linked in cascade using Rinnai's cascade controller. Up to 24 units can be linked, with the controller managing demand across units to maximize efficiency. Units activate sequentially as demand increases and deactivate as demand drops. This provides unlimited hot water at scale while maintaining efficiency through staged activation.

What gas pressure is required?

Minimum 5 inches water column (WC) static pressure and 4 WC dynamic pressure at the unit during operation. Most natural gas residential and commercial service provides 7 WC. Verify with your gas utility if service pressure is uncertain.

What maintenance does the Rinnai RL75iN require?

Annual filter cleaning and annual flushing of the heat exchanger with scale inhibitor solution if water hardness exceeds 120 mg/L (7 grains per gallon). Rinnai provides a service timer that tracks runtime and alerts at service intervals. In areas with hard water, a water softener or scale inhibitor upstream of the unit significantly extends heat exchanger life.

What warranty does Rinnai provide on the RL75iN?

12-year heat exchanger warranty, 5-year parts warranty, and 1-year labor warranty with registration. The 12-year heat exchanger warranty is the longest in the residential and light commercial tankless category. Rinnai's service network covers all U.S. markets with parts typically available within 24 hours.
