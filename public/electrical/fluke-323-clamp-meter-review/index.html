Extended Analysis

Clamp meters measure current by sensing the magnetic field around a conductor — no circuit interruption required. The Fluke 323 measures up to 400A AC with True RMS accuracy, covering every residential and light commercial branch circuit application. The jaw opening of 1.1 inches accommodates conductors up to approximately 1-inch diameter, including #2 AWG and most commercial conductors up to 350 kcmil if the insulation is not too thick.

True RMS is the critical specification for modern commercial electrical work. Non-linear loads (VFDs, switching power supplies, LED drivers, computer equipment) produce distorted current waveforms. Average-responding clamp meters read 10-40% low on these waveforms, which leads to incorrect sizing decisions and missed overload conditions. The Fluke 323's True RMS capability ensures accurate current measurement regardless of waveform shape.

The 323 does not measure DC current, power factor, or harmonics — limitations that matter for some commercial applications. For VFD input current measurement where DC bus ripple is relevant, or for power quality analysis, the Fluke 376 FC or 381 are more appropriate. The 323's value is focused, accurate AC current measurement in a compact, durable form factor at the lowest price in Fluke's professional lineup.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a clamp meter and a multimeter?

A clamp meter measures current by sensing the magnetic field around a conductor without requiring circuit interruption or probe contact. A multimeter measures voltage, resistance, and continuity using probe contact. Clamp meters typically also measure voltage as a secondary function. For current measurement above 10A, a clamp meter is safer and more practical than a series-insertion ammeter. Most field electricians carry both tools.

What does True RMS mean on a clamp meter?

True RMS calculates the actual heating value of an AC waveform, regardless of its shape. Average-responding meters assume a pure sine wave and calculate incorrectly on distorted waveforms from VFDs, switching power supplies, and non-linear loads. In modern commercial buildings, True RMS is required for accurate current measurement.

What conductor size does the Fluke 323 jaw accommodate?

The 1.1-inch jaw opening accommodates conductors up to approximately 1 inch in diameter, including #2 AWG and 350 kcmil conductors with standard insulation. For larger conductors or multiple conductors in a single measurement, the Fluke 376 with a 1.8-inch jaw is a better fit.

Can the Fluke 323 measure DC current?

No. The Fluke 323 measures AC current only. For DC current measurement, the Fluke 376 FC or 381 with DC clamp measurement capability are the appropriate tools. DC current measurement is primarily relevant for solar PV systems, battery charging circuits, and variable frequency drive DC bus measurements.

What is the Fluke 323 warranty?

Limited lifetime warranty against defects in materials and workmanship. This is Fluke's standard professional instrument warranty and covers the meter for the life of the original purchaser with normal use. Calibration services are available through Fluke's authorized calibration network for applications requiring traceable calibration records.
