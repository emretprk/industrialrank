Extended Analysis: Why Electricians Choose the Fluke 117

The Fluke 117 occupies a specific and well-defined position in the multimeter market: a True RMS, CAT III 600V / CAT IV 300V rated meter designed for electricians working in commercial and light industrial environments where non-contact voltage detection and ghost voltage filtering are daily requirements. It is not the most technically sophisticated meter Fluke makes — the 87V or 289 occupy that position — but for the core workflows of a commercial electrician, the 117 is arguably more practical.

The VolT Alert non-contact voltage detector built into the meter is the feature that sets the 117 apart from competitors at the same price point. Rather than carrying a separate voltage stick, the technician can wave the meter near a conductor and get a visual and audible indication of live voltage before making contact. In panel work and troubleshooting scenarios, this reduces probe handling time and provides a redundant safety check. Ghost voltage filtering (AutoVolt mode) distinguishes true voltage from capacitively coupled phantom voltages that plague diagnosis in environments with long parallel cable runs — a persistent problem in commercial wiring that causes misdiagnoses with meters that lack this filtering.

Input impedance is 10 MΩ for standard voltage measurements, which is appropriate for most industrial applications. For sensitive low-voltage measurements in circuits where the meter's own impedance could affect the reading, the Fluke 28 II or 87V with their high-impedance modes are better choices. The 117 also lacks a frequency measurement range above 1 kHz, which matters for VFD output measurements. For VFD work, pair the 117 with a clamp meter that handles high-frequency current measurement.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the CAT rating on the Fluke 117 and what does it mean for safe use?

The Fluke 117 is rated CAT III 600V and CAT IV 300V. CAT III covers distribution-level circuits including branch circuit panelboards, three-phase distribution, and fixed equipment. CAT IV covers utility service entrance equipment and outdoor conductors. Using a CAT II meter in a CAT III environment creates an arc flash risk if the meter's input circuit fails under a fault condition. The Fluke 117's rating is appropriate for all commercial branch circuit and distribution work up to 600V.

Does the Fluke 117 measure True RMS?

Yes. True RMS measurement accurately calculates the effective heating value of both sinusoidal and non-sinusoidal AC waveforms. In commercial environments with variable frequency drives, switching power supplies, and electronic ballasts, average-responding meters can read 10-40% low on the distorted current and voltage waveforms these devices produce. The Fluke 117's True RMS capability ensures accurate readings regardless of waveform shape.

What is ghost voltage and how does the Fluke 117 handle it?

Ghost voltage (also called phantom voltage) is a capacitively coupled voltage that appears on de-energized conductors running in parallel with energized conductors. A standard meter reads this as live voltage, potentially misleading a technician into believing a circuit is energized when it is not. The Fluke 117's AutoVolt mode applies a low-impedance load that collapses ghost voltages while allowing true live voltages to remain measurable — a critical safety and diagnostic capability in commercial wiring environments.

Can the Fluke 117 measure microamps for flame sensor testing?

Yes. The Fluke 117 measures DC microamps (μA DC) in the range required for HVAC flame sensor testing. Most gas furnace flame sensors require 1.5-4 μA DC for reliable operation; the Fluke 117 reads this range accurately. This eliminates the need for a separate flame sensor tester tool for HVAC technicians who carry the 117 as their primary meter.

How does the Fluke 117 compare to the Fluke 115?

The key difference is the VolT Alert non-contact voltage detector, which is present on the 117 but absent on the 115. Both meters share the same True RMS measurement capability and CAT ratings. The 117 also adds ghost voltage (AutoVolt) filtering. For field electricians where non-contact voltage detection is a daily safety practice, the 117 is the correct choice. The 115 is better suited to bench use or lab environments where non-contact detection is not a workflow requirement.

What is Fluke's warranty on the 117?

Fluke provides a limited lifetime warranty on the Fluke 117 against defects in materials and workmanship — one of the strongest warranty terms in the professional test equipment category. The warranty covers normal use but not damage from abuse, modification, or use outside the rated specifications. Fluke's calibration and repair service is available through authorized service centers for traceable calibration if required by facility quality programs.
