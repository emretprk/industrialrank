Industrial Electrical Equipment Reviews

Industrial electrical equipment spans from load centers and panelboards to precision measurement instruments. The common thread is safety: electrical equipment failures cause more workplace fatalities and facility fires than any other equipment category. Specification decisions here are not primarily about cost per unit but about fault tolerance, arc flash hazard reduction, and long-term serviceability.

NFPA 70 (National Electrical Code) sets the baseline requirements for equipment installation in the U.S., but equipment selection must also account for the specific application. A residential-grade panel in a light commercial setting is a code violation in most jurisdictions. Equipment rated for the correct interrupting capacity, ambient temperature range, and duty cycle is the starting point for any specification.

Our electrical equipment reviews prioritize independently verified safety ratings, field failure data from contractor networks, and total cost including installation labor requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is AIC rating on an electrical panel?

AIC (Ampere Interrupting Capacity) is the maximum fault current a breaker or panel can safely interrupt without failing. Most residential panels are rated 10kAIC. Commercial and industrial facilities often have available fault currents of 22kAIC or higher at the service entrance, requiring equipment with a matching or higher AIC rating.

What does True RMS mean on a multimeter?

True RMS (Root Mean Square) meters accurately measure both sinusoidal and non-sinusoidal waveforms. Average-responding meters can be off by 10-40% when measuring the distorted waveforms produced by variable frequency drives, switching power supplies, and other non-linear loads common in industrial settings.
