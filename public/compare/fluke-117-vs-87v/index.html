IndustrialRank Verdict: 87V Wins on Capability; 117 Wins on Value for Commercial Use

The Fluke 87V is the more capable instrument — 0.05% accuracy, CAT IV 600V, 100 kHz frequency, and Min/Max recording make it the correct tool for industrial electrical, motor control, and utility service entrance work. The Fluke 117 is the correct tool for commercial electricians who need true-RMS, NCV, and AutoVolt at a reasonable price without the industrial specification overhead. Neither is a compromise — they are correctly matched to different professional contexts.

Analysis

The Fluke 117 and 87V occupy different positions in the same brand family. The 117 is designed for the commercial electrician who needs true-RMS measurement, non-contact voltage detection, and AutoVolt in a lightweight package at a reasonable price. The 87V is designed for the industrial electrician, controls engineer, or HVAC technician who diagnoses VFDs, motor drives, and utility-level electrical systems where 0.05% accuracy, CAT IV safety rating, and 100 kHz frequency measurement are professional requirements.

The CAT IV distinction is more than marketing. IEC 61010 defines CAT IV as the measurement environment at utility service entrances, outdoor utility connections, and primary supply feeds. Using a CAT III instrument (the 117) at a CAT IV measurement point is a recognized safety violation in the US electrical trade. Industrial maintenance professionals and utility workers who regularly measure at service entrances need the 87V or equivalent CAT IV instrument.

For HVAC technicians specifically, the 117 is adequate for most work: measuring supply voltage, checking resistance on contactors, verifying thermostat wire continuity. The 87V becomes necessary for VFD output diagnostics — mini split inverter compressors, VFD-controlled motors, and commercial refrigeration with variable-speed compressors produce non-sinusoidal waveforms at switching frequencies above the 117's 100 Hz range. Accurate frequency measurement at 4–20 kHz (typical VFD carrier frequencies) requires the 87V.

If you are a commercial electrician who does not regularly work on industrial motor control or utility service entrances, the 117 is the correct purchase. If you are an industrial maintenance technician, plant electrician, or controls engineer, the 87V is the minimum specification. If you do both, own both — the 117 for daily commercial work where portability matters, the 87V for industrial diagnostics where accuracy matters.

Frequently Asked Questions

When should I choose the Fluke 117 over the 87V?
Choose the 117 for commercial electrical work — branch circuits, distribution panels, HVAC equipment maintenance, and standard voltage/resistance diagnostics. The 117's NCV and AutoVolt features, lower price, and lighter weight make it the practical daily-use tool for commercial electricians. The CAT III 600V rating covers all in-building commercial measurement environments.
When should I choose the Fluke 87V?
Choose the 87V for industrial electrical work: motor control panels, variable frequency drives (VFDs), utility service entrances, and precision calibration applications. The CAT IV 600V rating is required for service entrance work. The 100 kHz frequency range is required for VFD carrier frequency diagnostics. The 0.05% accuracy is required for calibration work.
Can the Fluke 117 measure VFD output?
Partially. The 117 can measure voltage and current on VFD output within its frequency range (up to 100 Hz). It can accurately read the fundamental 0–60 Hz motor speed signal. It cannot accurately characterize the high-frequency carrier signal (4–20 kHz) that a technician needs for VFD tuning diagnostics. For complete VFD characterization, the 87V (100 kHz range) is required.
Does the Fluke 87V have non-contact voltage detection?
No. The 87V does not have a built-in NCV function. Fluke's VolAlert 1AC-A II is a separate NCV tester ($15–$20) that pairs with the 87V. Some industrial electricians prefer this separation — using the VolAlert for initial circuit checking and the 87V for measurement — which keeps the two functions independent.
What is the warranty on Fluke multimeters?
Both the Fluke 117 and 87V carry a 3-year limited warranty covering defects in materials and workmanship. Physical damage from drops or misuse is not covered. Fluke's 3-year warranty is the longest in the professional multimeter category — Klein, Ideal, and most competitors offer 1 year.
