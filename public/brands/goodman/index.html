About Goodman

Goodman Manufacturing was founded in Houston, Texas in 1982 by Harold Goodman, who built the company on a simple premise: provide reliable HVAC at the lowest possible price. The strategy worked — Goodman grew to become the largest HVAC manufacturer in North America by units shipped before being acquired by Daikin in 2012.

The Daikin acquisition improved Goodman's manufacturing quality noticeably. HVAC contractor surveys and consumer reliability data show improvement in Goodman failure rates since 2012–2015. The brand remains below Trane and Carrier in reliability rankings but above the OEM importer tier (Pioneer, Senville, etc.).

Goodman's lifetime compressor warranty (registered) is the most unusual claim in the category at this price point. Most competitors at Goodman's price tier offer 5–7 years. The lifetime warranty applies to the original installation address and requires registration within 60 days — but it provides meaningful protection for a buyer who plans long-term ownership at a fixed address.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Goodman a good brand?
Goodman is a solid value brand for budget-constrained applications, particularly in climate zones 1–3 where advanced cold-weather performance is not required. It ranks below Trane and Carrier in independent reliability surveys but is generally better than OEM importer brands. The Daikin acquisition has improved manufacturing quality. For rental properties and replacement applications where lowest upfront cost is the priority, Goodman is a defensible choice.
Why does Goodman offer a lifetime compressor warranty?
Goodman's lifetime compressor warranty (registered within 60 days, original installation address) reflects the company's confidence in its Copeland-supplied compressor platform. The warranty is marketing differentiation against Trane and Carrier, which offer 10–12 year compressor coverage. Lifetime warranty terms limit scope to the compressor only — other parts carry 10-year coverage.
Is Goodman the same as Amana?
Goodman Manufacturing produces products under both the Goodman and Amana brands. Amana is positioned slightly above Goodman in market tier with a longer parts warranty (limited lifetime on heat exchangers). Both share manufacturing platforms and are owned by Daikin Industries.
How does Goodman compare to Trane?
Trane outperforms Goodman in independent reliability surveys by a meaningful margin and carries a higher lifetime repair cost. At Trane's $1,000–$2,000 installed premium, the choice depends on expected service life and repair frequency. For owner-occupied homes expecting 15+ year ownership, Trane typically offers better lifecycle value. For rental properties, the Goodman price advantage may not be overcome by reduced repair frequency.
Does Goodman qualify for the IRA tax credit?
Yes. The Goodman GSXH5 and most Goodman heat pump products are ENERGY STAR certified and qualify for the IRA Section 25C residential energy efficiency credit. Check the AHRI-certified combination of the specific model and coil being installed before filing for the credit.
