About Daikin

Daikin Industries was founded in Osaka, Japan in 1924 and has grown into the world's largest HVAC manufacturer by revenue, operating manufacturing facilities in Japan, the US, Europe, India, and China. The company acquired Goodman Manufacturing in 2012, giving Daikin a dominant position in both the premium (Daikin brand) and budget (Goodman brand) US residential HVAC segments.

In the mini split category, Daikin's product line spans from the entry-level 15 Series (17 SEER2, 10-year warranty) to the premium 20 Series (20+ SEER2, -13°F minimum). The brand is positioned between Mitsubishi's premium pricing and Pioneer's budget tier — offering Japanese manufacturing quality and strong warranties at mid-tier equipment prices.

Daikin holds the primary global patents on R-32 refrigerant production and is the leading advocate for R-32 adoption as the industry transition refrigerant. The MRCOOL Oasis H2i+ and other R-32 products that have entered the US market license Daikin-controlled technology.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Daikin the same as Goodman?
Daikin Industries owns Goodman Manufacturing, acquired in 2012. The two brands operate independently with different product lines, pricing, and dealer networks. Daikin products use Japanese-engineered designs and carry premium warranties. Goodman products are value-tier with lower equipment cost. Both benefit from shared manufacturing infrastructure and component supply chains.
Does Daikin have cold-climate mini splits?
Yes. The Daikin 20 Series operates to -13°F — equivalent to Mitsubishi's GL series cold-climate specification. The 17 Series operates to -4°F, which covers most zone 4 applications. For zone 5–6 primary heat, the 20 Series is the appropriate Daikin choice.
What warranty does Daikin offer?
The 15 Series carries 10 years parts and 10 years labor (registered). The 17 Series carries 12 years parts and 5 years labor (registered). The 20 Series carries 12 years parts and 5 years labor (registered). Registration within 30 days of installation is required for full coverage.
Where are Daikin mini splits made?
Daikin mini splits sold in the US market are manufactured in Daikin's Waller, Texas facility. Core compressors and refrigerant components are manufactured in Japan. The Waller facility opened in 2017 as part of Daikin's US manufacturing expansion following the Goodman acquisition.
How does Daikin compare to Mitsubishi?
Daikin and Mitsubishi both offer Japanese manufacturing quality with strong warranties. Mitsubishi leads on cold-climate performance (GL series -13°F vs Daikin 17 Series -4°F), warranty depth (12-year parts across all Hyper-Heat models), and US dealer network size. Daikin offers competitive products at generally lower equipment cost. For zone 1–4 buyers, both are excellent; for zone 5–7 primary heat, Mitsubishi's cold-weather advantage is material.
