About Trane

Trane Technologies (formerly Ingersoll Rand Climate Control) has manufactured HVAC equipment since 1913. The Trane brand's core market positioning — reliability above all — is consistently supported by independent data. J.D. Power residential HVAC satisfaction studies and Consumer Reports brand reliability surveys routinely rank Trane first or second among major residential HVAC brands.

Trane's engineering philosophy prioritizes reliability margin over leading-edge efficiency claims. The XR15, for example, uses a two-stage scroll compressor rated for extended service life rather than the variable-speed inverter technology that achieves higher SEER2 ratings. This trade-off results in measurably lower callback rates for contractors who install Trane vs most competitors.

The XV20i is Trane's answer to the Carrier Infinity 20 and Lennox XP21 in the variable-speed premium tier: 22 SEER2, -4°F minimum, communicating ComfortLink II system. At this efficiency tier, Trane's reliability reputation combines with best-in-class efficiency — making the XV20i the recommended Trane product for buyers in high-efficiency markets.

Product Lines

XV Series (Variable Speed)

XR Series (Standard)

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is Trane considered the most reliable HVAC brand?
Trane's reliability reputation is supported by consistent top rankings in J.D. Power residential HVAC satisfaction studies, Consumer Reports brand reliability data, and independent HVAC contractor surveys. The brand's engineering philosophy — prioritizing durability over leading-edge efficiency — results in lower repair frequency rates over 10-year ownership periods than most competitors at the same price tier.
What is the difference between Trane XR15 and XV20i?
The XR15 uses a two-stage scroll compressor and achieves 15 SEER2 at a lower installed cost. The XV20i uses a variable-speed inverter compressor and achieves 22 SEER2 at substantially higher installed cost. Both carry Trane's reliability heritage. The XV20i is appropriate for buyers who prioritize maximum efficiency; the XR15 is appropriate for buyers who prioritize reliability at a mid-tier price.
Does Trane make mini splits?
Yes, Trane offers ductless mini split systems under the Trane brand through its dealer network. These are positioned in the mid-tier market and do not carry Mitsubishi-equivalent cold-climate ratings. For most buyers comparing Trane mini splits vs Mitsubishi, the performance and warranty gap favors Mitsubishi in cold-climate applications.
What is Trane's warranty?
Trane XR15: 10 years parts, 12 years compressor (registered within 60 days). Trane XV20i: 12 years compressor, 10 years parts (registered). Failure to register results in a 5-year base warranty. Trane's 12-year compressor warranty is the longest in the mid-efficiency central heat pump category.
Is Trane worth the premium over Goodman?
At Trane's installed cost premium of $1,000–$2,000 over Goodman at comparable BTU sizes, the value case depends on expected service life and repair frequency. Trane's 2–3x better reliability rating vs Goodman in contractor surveys means a meaningful probability of fewer repair events over 10 years. For owner-occupied homes where reliability and longevity matter, Trane's premium typically pays back. For rental properties where lowest upfront cost is the priority, Goodman may be the correct choice.
