About Mitsubishi Electric

Mitsubishi Electric entered the North American HVAC market in the 1980s and built its dominant position through a consistent strategy: manufacturing quality, cold-climate performance, and an authorized dealer network that supports warranty claims reliably. The Diamond Contractor program — which requires technicians to complete Mitsubishi-specific training and maintain diagnostic equipment — is the operational infrastructure behind the 12-year warranty.

The Hyper-Heat platform, which Mitsubishi introduced in the US market in the early 2010s, changed the market definition for cold-climate mini splits. Flash Injection compressor technology pre-heats refrigerant vapor at low ambient temperatures, preventing the efficiency collapse that standard inverter compressors experience below 17°F. At -13°F, the Mitsubishi GL series delivers approximately 85% of rated heating capacity — a figure no competitor matched when it was introduced and few match today.

Mitsubishi's transition to R-32 refrigerant in the H2i+ series aligns the product line with EPA AIM Act phase-down requirements ahead of the regulatory schedule. The H2i+ achieves 21 SEER2 and HSPF2 11.0 — the highest certified ratings in the single-zone mini split category — while maintaining the -13°F/-22°F cold-climate operating range that defines the Hyper-Heat brand.

Product Lines

GL Series (MSZ-GL)

Standard Hyper-Heat single-zone mini splits in 9K–24K BTU. The most widely installed Mitsubishi residential line. 19 SEER2, -13°F minimum, 12-year parts warranty. The reference product for cold-climate single-zone applications.

H2i+ Series (MSZ-FS)

Enhanced Hyper-Heat with R-32 refrigerant. 21 SEER2, HSPF2 11.0, -13°F/-22°F operating range. The premium Mitsubishi offering for maximum efficiency and extreme cold-climate performance.

MXZ Multi-Zone

Outdoor units supporting 2–8 indoor zones. Allows a single outdoor unit to condition multiple rooms independently. Available in 18,000–54,000 BTU system sizes.

P-Series Commercial

Light commercial applications. Larger BTU capacities, higher duty cycle ratings, and building management system integration.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes Mitsubishi mini splits better than competitors?
Mitsubishi's advantages are concentrated in three areas: cold-climate heating performance (Hyper-Heat Flash Injection operating to -13°F), warranty length (12 years parts when registered), and service infrastructure (Diamond Contractor network covering all US metro areas). For buyers in climate zones 4–7 who want a primary heat source, no domestic competitor currently matches all three of these simultaneously.
What is a Mitsubishi Diamond Contractor?
A Diamond Contractor is an HVAC dealer who has completed Mitsubishi Electric's training program, maintains the required diagnostic equipment, and employs at least one Mitsubishi-certified technician. Diamond Contractor installation is required to receive the extended 12-year parts warranty. Standard installation by any EPA 608 contractor is valid but results in the base 7-year warranty.
What is the difference between the GL Series and the H2i+?
The GL Series uses R-410A refrigerant and achieves 19 SEER2 / HSPF2 10.0. The H2i+ uses R-32 refrigerant and achieves 21 SEER2 / HSPF2 11.0. Both operate to -13°F minimum; the H2i+ maintains useful output to -22°F. The H2i+ costs 25–35% more in equipment. For zone 4–5 applications, the GL Series performance is sufficient. For zone 6–7 or maximum efficiency, the H2i+ is the correct choice.
Where are Mitsubishi mini splits manufactured?
Mitsubishi Electric mini splits are manufactured in Thailand (indoor units) and Japan (compressors and outdoor unit assemblies). Mitsubishi's Japanese compressor manufacturing is widely cited by independent contractors as a key factor in the brand's long-term reliability track record.
Does Mitsubishi offer multi-zone systems?
Yes. The MXZ series allows 2–8 indoor zones from a single outdoor unit. Zone sizes range from 6K to 18K per zone; total system capacity ranges from 18K to 54K BTU. Multi-zone systems require Diamond Contractor installation to receive full warranty coverage.
