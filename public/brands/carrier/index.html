About Carrier

Carrier was founded by Willis Haviland Carrier, the engineer credited with inventing modern air conditioning in 1902. The company became a subsidiary of United Technologies in 1979 and was spun off as Carrier Global in 2020. Today, Carrier is one of three companies (with Trane Technologies and Lennox) that dominate the premium residential central HVAC market in the US.

Carrier's Infinity communicating system is the company's premium differentiation: a proprietary protocol linking outdoor unit, air handler, and thermostat for coordinated efficiency optimization. The Infinity 20 central heat pump achieves 20 SEER2 and -4°F minimum temperature operation, competing directly with the Lennox XP21 in the variable-speed premium tier.

In the mini split segment, Carrier offers the Performance and Comfort series at mid-tier pricing. These are not equivalent to Mitsubishi in cold-climate performance but provide competitive efficiency and Carrier's service network advantage in US markets where Mitsubishi Diamond Contractors are less available.

Product Lines

Infinity Series (Central)

Premium variable-speed central heat pumps and air conditioners. 18–24 SEER2. Communicating Infinity system for integrated efficiency optimization. The Infinity 20 heat pump is the flagship.

Performance Series (Central)

Mid-tier two-stage central systems. 16–18 SEER2. Standard installation without communicating system requirement. Good efficiency at lower installed cost than Infinity.

Comfort Series (Mini Splits)

Single and multi-zone ductless systems. 18–20 SEER2. Not cold-climate rated to Mitsubishi standards but offer Carrier's service network advantage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Carrier a reliable HVAC brand?
Carrier consistently ranks in the top tier of residential HVAC brand reliability surveys, behind Trane but ahead of most mid-tier brands. The combination of established manufacturing, a 10,000+ dealer network, and strong warranty infrastructure makes Carrier one of the most defensible brand choices in the residential market.
What is the Carrier Infinity system?
The Infinity communicating system is a proprietary protocol linking Carrier's Infinity outdoor units, Infinity air handlers, and Infinity Touch thermostats. In communicating mode, the system continuously optimizes compressor speed, fan speed, and refrigerant flow to maintain setpoint with minimum energy consumption. Non-Carrier components are not compatible with the communicating protocol.
How does Carrier compare to Trane?
Both are top-tier residential HVAC brands with large dealer networks and strong reliability records. Trane slightly edges Carrier in independent consumer reliability surveys. Carrier's Infinity communicating system is more technologically advanced than comparable Trane products at the same tier. Both carry 10-year registered warranties on premium products. The choice often depends on which brand has a stronger local dealer presence.
Does Carrier make mini splits?
Yes. Carrier's Comfort series offers single and multi-zone ductless mini splits in 9K–36K BTU ranges. These are positioned in the mid-tier market and do not carry Mitsubishi-equivalent cold-climate ratings. For cold-climate applications in zones 5–7, Carrier's central heat pump line with supplemental electric resistance backup is typically the recommended Carrier approach.
What refrigerant does the new Carrier Infinity 20 use?
The Carrier Infinity 20 uses R-454B, a next-generation HFO blend refrigerant with approximately 78% lower global warming potential than R-410A. R-454B is A2L classified (mildly flammable) and requires installer certification. Carrier transitioned to R-454B ahead of the EPA AIM Act schedule for high-GWP refrigerant phase-down.
