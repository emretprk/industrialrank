About Carrier

Carrier's origin story is the HVAC industry's origin story. In 1902, Willis Carrier designed the first modern electrical air conditioning system for a Brooklyn printing plant — a humidity control problem that required moving hot, humid air across cold coils. The technology he patented became the foundation of the global HVAC industry.

Carrier Global Corporation went public in 2020 when United Technologies spun off its HVAC and fire/security businesses. The company now operates across 170 countries with annual revenue exceeding $22 billion. In North America, the residential division sells through an exclusive dealer network of over 12,000 Carrier factory-authorized contractors.

The Infinity variable-speed platform, introduced in the mid-2000s and continuously refined through 2026, remains Carrier's flagship residential product. Greenspeed Intelligence — the proprietary variable-speed inverter technology — modulates compressor output in 1% increments, enabling the 20 SEER2 efficiency and -15°F cold-climate operation that define the premium tier of the residential heat pump market.

Carrier's transition to R-454B refrigerant (Puron Advance) is complete as of 2025, meeting the EPA Section 608 mandate for lower-GWP refrigerants in new residential equipment. All current Carrier products use R-454B.

Key Differentiators

Greenspeed Intelligence

Carrier's proprietary variable-speed inverter technology modulates in 1% increments from 25-100% capacity. This continuous modulation eliminates short-cycling, enables 20 SEER2 efficiency, and provides the -15°F cold-climate heating that makes Carrier the specification choice for all-electric residential heating in climate zones 4-6.

Diamond Contractor Network

12,000+ authorized service locations in the United States — the largest residential HVAC service network in North America. For commercial operators with service level requirements, this network density translates to same-day or next-day response in virtually every market.

Infinity System Integration

The Infinity Touch communicating thermostat enables real-time system diagnostics, efficiency optimization, and fault detection accessible via the Carrier mobile app. The system can identify most failures before they result in equipment shutdown — a meaningful advantage for commercial properties.

R-454B Transition Leadership

Carrier branded R-454B as 'Puron Advance' and completed the refrigerant transition ahead of the January 2025 EPA mandate. All current Infinity equipment is R-454B compatible with 78% lower GWP than R-410A.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Carrier Comfort, Performance, and Infinity series?

Comfort is Carrier's entry tier with single-stage compressors and 14-15 SEER2. Performance adds minor efficiency improvements reaching 15-17 SEER2. Infinity is the premium variable-speed tier at 18-20+ SEER2 with Greenspeed Intelligence, communicating controls, and cold-climate ratings. The tiers represent increasing efficiency, quietness, and system integration capability.

How do I find a Carrier Diamond Contractor?

Carrier's dealer locator at carrier.com/residential allows searching by zip code. Diamond contractors are the highest certification tier in the Carrier dealer network and are required for warranty compliance on Infinity series equipment. Standard authorized contractors can also perform warranty work on lower-tier products.

Does Carrier offer extended warranties?

The standard 10-year parts warranty with registration can be extended through Carrier's Extended Service Agreement, available through authorized dealers. Extended warranty terms and costs vary by market and system. Contact your Carrier Diamond Contractor for current extended warranty pricing.

What refrigerant does Carrier currently use?

All new Carrier residential equipment uses R-454B (Puron Advance) as of January 2025, in compliance with EPA Section 608 requirements. R-454B has a GWP of 466, compared to 2,088 for R-410A. Carrier-branded this transition under the Puron Advance name. Service on R-454B equipment requires technicians with A2L refrigerant awareness training.
