About Bosch

Bosch is a German engineering conglomerate founded in 1886. The Thermotechnology division (heat pumps, boilers, water heaters) operates globally, with the US residential HVAC business headquartered in Londonderry, New Hampshire. In Europe, Bosch HVAC equipment is widely used in commercial and residential applications. The US mini split line is a newer market entry — the IDS Premium series represents Bosch's serious push into the premium US residential segment.

The IDS Premium is the product that differentiates Bosch in the US mini split market. At 20.2 SEER2, HSPF2 11.0, and -13°F minimum temperature, it is the closest competitor to the Mitsubishi GL series on cold-climate performance at a lower equipment price. NEEP Cold Climate ASHP listing — requiring independent third-party testing — confirms the performance claims. The 10-year parts and labor warranty covers a broader scope than Mitsubishi's labor-limited warranty after year 5.

The Bosch US HVAC dealer network is smaller than Mitsubishi's Diamond Contractor network. In major US metro areas, Bosch dealers are available with competitive service response times. In smaller markets and rural areas, availability is more variable. Buyers should verify local Bosch dealer coverage before committing to the IDS Premium in markets where Mitsubishi Diamond Contractors are readily available.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does Bosch compare to Mitsubishi for cold-climate mini splits?
The Bosch IDS Premium matches the Mitsubishi GL series on cold-climate performance (-13°F minimum) and exceeds it on efficiency (20.2 SEER2 vs 19.0). The Mitsubishi leads on warranty depth (12 yr parts vs 10 yr) and US service infrastructure. For buyers in metro areas with strong Bosch dealer coverage, the IDS Premium is a compelling alternative at lower equipment cost.
Is Bosch a good HVAC brand?
Yes. Bosch is a respected engineering brand with 135 years of manufacturing history. The Thermotechnology division produces quality equipment used extensively in European commercial and residential HVAC. In the US, the IDS Premium has accumulated positive contractor and homeowner reviews since its launch. The limitation is the smaller US dealer network vs Mitsubishi and Daikin.
What warranty does Bosch offer on the IDS Premium?
The Bosch IDS Premium carries 10 years on parts and 10 years on labor when registered within 30 days of installation. This is notable: Mitsubishi's 12-year registered warranty is parts only after year 5 — Bosch's 10-year labor coverage provides broader protection for out-of-pocket service costs in years 6–10.
Is the Bosch IDS Premium NEEP listed?
Yes. The Bosch IDS Premium is listed in the NEEP Cold Climate Air Source Heat Pump Listing, which independently verifies cold-weather performance at low ambient temperatures. NEEP listing is a credible third-party confirmation of the -13°F floor and full-capacity cold-weather performance.
Does Bosch have mini splits in multiple sizes?
Yes. The Bosch IDS Premium is available in 9K, 12K, 18K, 24K, and 36K BTU single-zone configurations. Availability of all sizes in all US markets varies — the 12K and 18K are most widely stocked. Verify availability through local Bosch dealers before specifying a less common size.
