Review

The Goodman GSZ160361 positions itself as the practical choice in a crowded market of 3-ton heat pump condensers. This unit delivers 36,000 BTU of cooling capacity with Energy Star approval, making it attractive to contractors managing tight project budgets. At $2,615 before potential rebates, it undercuts premium brands while maintaining honest specifications and proven component choices.

Performance centers on a 16 SEER1 rating with a scroll compressor handling single-stage operation. The unit produces 36,000 BTU for both heating and cooling across 208/230V single-phase power at 30-amp maximum breaker size. While single-stage operation means it runs fully or off rather than modulating down during mild conditions, scroll compressors bring track records of 15-plus-year reliability in residential applications.

Pros & Cons

Pros

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the 16 SEER rating compare to newer 16 SEER2 units? +
SEER1 and SEER2 use different calculation methods, making direct comparison difficult. This 16 SEER1 unit likely performs similarly to a 13-14 SEER2 rated system. If SEER2 efficiency data matters to your decision, request comparative testing from the manufacturer or installer.
Will this condenser work with my existing furnace and ductwork? +
The 3-ton capacity requires matching 3-ton indoor coils and air handlers. Verify your existing equipment tonnage before purchasing. Line sets, electrical, and ductwork rarely require changes if replacing the same tonnage unit, but a professional inspection is essential.
Does single-stage operation significantly impact monthly energy bills? +
Single-stage cycling increases runtime during moderate weather compared to variable-speed units. Real-world impact ranges from 10-20 percent higher consumption in mild seasons. Heat-dominant climates see smaller efficiency penalties than cooling-dominant regions.
How does this Goodman compare to Carrier or Lennox equivalents at similar price? +
Goodman uses identical scroll compressors and similar component sourcing as higher-priced brands. Main differences are variable-speed stages, noise levels, and warranty terms. You're paying for performance refinements, not fundamental reliability upgrades.
What does the online registration for warranty actually require? +
Registration typically needs model number, serial number, and installation date through Goodman's website. Process takes five minutes and unlocks full ten-year coverage. Documentation saves time if future service or warranty claims arise.
