Review

The ACiQ ACIQ-36-HPC-UPG is a horizontal configuration gas furnace and heat pump combo engineered for contractors who need reliable, code-compliant equipment without overengineering. This system pairs a 100,000 BTU gas furnace with a 36,000 BTU cooling capacity in a single footprint, simplifying installation logistics. It replaces the older GSZ140371 model and incorporates Energy Star certification, making it attractive for rebate-qualified projects and homeowners conscious of operating costs.

Performance metrics reveal competent but not exceptional efficiency. The furnace achieves 96% AFUE, meaning 96 cents of every fuel dollar converts to useful heat, with plastic PVC flue compatibility reducing venting costs. The heat pump delivers 14 SEER1 cooling efficiency using R410A refrigerant and a scroll compressor, rated at 74 dBA. The variable-speed ECM blower and two-stage burner allow the system to modulate demand, reducing waste during part-load conditions where most homes operate year-round.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 96% AFUE furnace efficiency means only 4% heat loss, translating to lower monthly heating bills
  • 14 SEER cooling efficiency qualifies for Energy Star, helping homeowners recoup costs through rebates
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor adjusts to 2000 CFM maximum, improving comfort and reducing electricity consumption
  • Two-stage burner allows low-fire operation during mild weather, cutting fuel waste
  • Scroll compressor is proven reliable and quieter than reciprocating models in single-stage applications
  • 10-year warranty with online registration covers parts and labor expectations

Cons

  • Single-stage cooling means the compressor runs at full capacity or off, lacking the modulation of higher-tier systems
  • 74 dBA noise level is moderately loud for a residential condenser, noticeable during operation
  • 3.5 ton capacity stretches toward oversizing for many homes, which can cause short-cycling and reduced efficiency

Who Should Buy

Best For

Contractors installing replacement systems in homes with moderate climate swings will appreciate the straightforward single-stage design and standard refrigerant compatibility. Homeowners seeking solid mid-range efficiency without spending on variable-capacity equipment will find the balanced performance and rebate eligibility attractive.

Consider Alternatives If

Buyers in consistently cold climates who rarely need cooling should choose a furnace-only system, and those seeking whisper-quiet or ultra-high-efficiency performance should look at two-stage compressor or inverter-based alternatives.

Installation

Installation requires standard HVAC skills with no exotic complications. The unit connects via 3/8 inch liquid line and 7/8 inch suction line with 3/4 inch insulation thickness. Electrical demand ranges from 22.4 to 30 amps at 115V single-phase 60Hz, fitting standard residential panels. The A-coil downflow configuration mounts in attics or basements without awkward retrofitting, though contractors should verify ductwork clearance given the 34.5 inch height and 21 inch width.

Warranty & Reliability

ACiQ provides a 10-year parts warranty when registered online, a standard industry baseline. This covers defects in manufacturing and workmanship but excludes wear items and improper installation. Online registration is mandatory to activate full coverage, making documentation critical at handoff. Coverage aligns with typical manufacturer policies and offers reasonable peace of mind for the equipment cost tier.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 14 SEER compare to newer SEER2 ratings? +
SEER1 and SEER2 are different testing standards. This 14 SEER unit typically converts to roughly 11 SEER2 under harder conditions. The system still qualifies for Energy Star and most rebates, but direct comparisons to newer equipment require conversion math. Contractors should verify local rebate programs accept SEER1 ratings.
Is the 3.5 ton capacity appropriate for my home? +
This depends on your home's square footage and climate zone. A Manual J load calculation is essential before purchase. The 3.5 ton capacity handles approximately 3500-4200 square feet in moderate climates. Oversizing causes short-cycling and efficiency loss, so professional sizing prevents costly mistakes and warranty disputes.
What does the 96% AFUE actually mean for heating costs? +
96% AFUE means only 4% of your gas burns without producing heat, compared to 80-85% for older furnaces. In a 2000-square-foot home in a cold climate, this translates to roughly 10-15% lower heating bills annually. Actual savings vary by gas rates, thermostat settings, and insulation quality, but the gains are measurable and documented by Energy Star.
Can this system replace my old single-fuel furnace? +
Yes, the 100,000 BTU furnace handles standard residential heating loads. However, adding the heat pump cooling requires new outdoor condenser placement and refrigerant line runs. This adds labor but eliminates the need for a separate air conditioner. Total project cost is usually competitive with furnace-plus-AC replacement when both systems need updating simultaneously.
Does online registration actually matter for warranty coverage? +
Yes, it is mandatory. Without registration, ACiQ may deny warranty claims. Registration is free and typically requires serial number and purchase date. Keep receipts and documentation to prove installation date, as warranty coverage begins at installation, not purchase date from retailers.
