Frequently Asked Questions

What does 21.5 SEER2 mean for my electric bill? +
SEER2 measures seasonal efficiency under realistic conditions. A 21.5 rating typically cuts cooling energy use 30-40% versus 16 SEER baseline units, saving $300-600 annually in moderate climates. Heating savings in cold regions add another 20-30% reduction during winter months.
Can I install this myself, or do I need a contractor? +
EPA regulations require licensed technicians to handle R410A refrigerant. You cannot legally charge the system yourself. Hire a certified HVAC contractor experienced with multi-zone mini-splits. Installation typically costs $3,500-5,500 depending on labor rates and line routing complexity in your area.
How does Hyper Heat perform in freezing climates? +
Hyper Heat technology maintains heating output down to minus 13 degrees Fahrenheit, unusual for heat pumps. This means fewer emergency electric resistance heater kicks, reducing peak heating costs in northern climates. Most standard heat pumps lose efficiency below 32 degrees.
Should I choose this over a traditional split system? +
Mini-splits excel in homes without existing ductwork or where zoning matters. This quad-zone model beats traditional systems if you need independent temperature control across four rooms. Traditional systems offer lower upfront cost but consume more energy and force single-temperature operation.
What does the 5-year warranty actually cover? +
Parts only, not labor or damage from improper installation. Covers compressor, indoor/outdoor coils, and electrical components. Annual maintenance by a qualified technician is required to keep warranty valid. Proof of service documents recommended.
