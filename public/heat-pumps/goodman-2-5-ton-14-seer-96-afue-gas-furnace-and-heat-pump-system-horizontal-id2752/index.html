Review

The Goodman 2.5-ton hybrid system represents a pragmatic middle ground in HVAC technology, combining proven gas furnace reliability with heat pump cooling efficiency. Branded as ACiQ and manufactured by Goodman, this unit delivers 60,000 BTU heating capacity and 30,000 BTU cooling output in a compact A-Coil, downflow horizontal configuration. At $2466 with rebate availability, it positions itself as an accessible upgrade path for homeowners seeking efficiency gains without complete system replacement.

Performance metrics here tell a compelling story. The 96% AFUE furnace rating is exceptional, meaning only 4% of fuel energy escapes as waste during heating season. On the cooling side, 14 SEER1 efficiency places this squarely in modern standards, delivering approximately 25% better efficiency than units from 2005-2010. The variable-speed ECM blower motor at 1,200 CFM maximum adjusts output intelligently, while the two-stage burner modulates heat delivery to match actual demand rather than cycling on and off.

Installation

Installation requires standard HVAC practices with plastic or PVC exhaust flue compatibility. The unit accepts 3/8-inch liquid lines and 3/4-inch suction lines, both common in residential work. At 120 pounds with dimensions of 34.5" height, 17.5" width, and 28.75" depth, it fits typical furnace closets. Electrical requirements are straightforward, 115V single-phase 60Hz with standard breaker sizing between 18.7 and 30 amps. The suction line comes pre-insulated at 3/4-inch thickness.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec list 3 tons when the product is marketed as 2.5 tons? +
The 60,000 BTU heating capacity and 30,000 BTU cooling output technically align with 2.5-ton specifications, though the manufacturer's rating shows 3 tons. This discrepancy typically reflects how manufacturers round or certify units. Verify AHRI certificate 7368838 with your installer to confirm exact performance ratings for your application.
Can this system work with my existing ductwork from my old furnace? +
Most existing horizontal furnace installations will accommodate this unit, but ductwork evaluation is essential. The A-Coil downflow configuration and 1,200 CFM maximum airflow are standard residential specs. However, your ducts must handle 60,000 BTU heating output, requiring proper sizing and sealing. Have your contractor verify static pressure and leakage.
How much will heating and cooling actually cost to run each month? +
The 96% AFUE and 14 SEER ratings deliver real savings versus older systems. In moderate climates, homeowners typically see 20-30% lower heating costs compared to 80% AFUE units, and cooling costs drop 25-30% versus 10 SEER systems. Actual savings depend on fuel prices, climate zone, insulation, and usage patterns.
Is 14 SEER competitive in 2024, or should I wait for higher efficiency? +
14 SEER remains solid for hybrid systems but is not cutting-edge. Modern heat pumps now reach 16-19 SEER2 ratings. However, SEER1 and SEER2 are different metrics; this unit's actual performance sits between them. For your climate, compare against 15-16 SEER single-stage units to determine if the upgrade cost justifies improved efficiency.
What does online registration actually protect? +
Registration unlocks the full 10-year parts warranty. Without it, coverage drops to one year. Registration is free and takes five minutes on Goodman's website. Keep your proof of purchase and installation date handy to validate your claim if issues arise.
