Review

The ACiQ 2-ton hybrid system combines a 14 SEER heat pump with an 80% AFUE gas furnace in one compact upflow unit. This is Goodman's answer for contractors seeking a traditional split-system feel without separate indoor and outdoor equipment. At 120 pounds and 38.25 inches tall, it fits standard closet installations. The unit targets the middle market, where homeowners want efficient cooling but aren't ready to abandon gas heat entirely.

Performance specs show 24,000 BTU for both cooling and heating, adequate for small homes and apartments. The high-efficiency multi-speed blower maxes out at 1,200 CFM, matching the 2-ton capacity. Cooling runs at 14 SEER with single-stage compressor operation, meaning less modulation in moderate weather. The two-stage furnace handles peak heating demand while conserving gas on mild winter days. R454B refrigerant lines run standard 3/8-inch liquid and 3/4-inch suction with 50-foot insulated suction line included.

Installation

Installation demands 208/230V single-phase 60Hz power with 17.1 to 25-amp breaker capacity. Upflow configuration requires metal exhaust flue and proper ductwork clearance. The sweat-valve connections need HVAC certification. A-coil orientation suits vertical closet mounting. Technicians appreciate the included kit instructions and comprehensive Goodman service manual. Typical install takes one day for experienced crews.

Frequently Asked Questions

What climate zones work best for this hybrid system? +
This 2-ton unit excels in IECC zones 4 through 5B where winter temperatures regularly drop below 32 degrees Fahrenheit. In these regions, the 80% AFUE furnace supplements the heat pump efficiently. Warmer climates should consider pure heat pump solutions for better economics.
Will this fit my existing upflow closet installation? +
Very likely. At 38.25 inches tall and 29 inches square, it replaces GSZ140251 and similar Goodman units. Confirm your existing ductwork is rated for 1,200 CFM max airflow. You'll need metal flue venting for the gas furnace.
How does 14 SEER compare to newer systems? +
14 SEER is solid but no longer cutting-edge. Modern 18-24 SEER units exist, though cost significantly more. This system qualifies for Energy Star, offsetting higher cooling bills versus premium units over 10-year lifespan in moderate climates.
Should I buy this or a straight heat pump? +
Buy this if winter temperatures stay below 30 degrees regularly. The gas furnace prevents expensive electric resistance heating and heat pump defrost cycles. In warm zones, a heat pump alone costs less and operates more efficiently overall.
Is the 10-year warranty registration automatic? +
No. You must register online within 30 days of activation. Unregistered units revert to one-year coverage. Registration is free and takes five minutes through ACiQ's website.
