Review

The Goodman 2.5 ton heat pump system sits comfortably in the mid-market segment where price accessibility meets respectable efficiency. At $89, this horizontal A-coil configuration targets contractors building quotes for residential retrofits and new construction where budget constraints are real. The Energy Star certification and availability of manufacturer rebates make it attractive to cost-conscious buyers who understand that 16 SEER1 performance delivers measurable savings compared to older equipment.

This unit delivers 30,000 BTU for both cooling and heating using a scroll compressor and single-stage condenser design. The variable-speed ECM blower motor adjusts to 1,600 CFM maximum, improving humidity control and reducing the 75 dBA noise output typical of constant-speed fans. Electrical requirements are straightforward, requiring 208/230V single-phase 60Hz power with breaker sizing between 18.3 and 30 amps, making retrofit installation feasible in most residential electrical panels.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the actual tonnage of this system, 2.5 or 3.5 tons? +
The product listing shows conflicting specifications, with 2.5 ton in the title and 3.5 ton in specs. Contact Online Supply directly for clarification before purchase. This discrepancy affects sizing calculations and system matching, so verification is essential for proper application.
Can this horizontal coil replace my existing vertical downflow system? +
No, configuration must match your ductwork. Horizontal A-coil units are designed for attic or crawlspace mounting with horizontal airflow. Swapping to a different configuration requires ductwork modifications or choosing the correct style for your space to avoid performance loss.
How does 16 SEER1 compare to newer SEER2 ratings? +
SEER1 ratings run approximately 10-15 percent higher than equivalent SEER2 ratings due to different testing standards. A 16 SEER1 unit roughly equals 13-14 SEER2 performance. Always compare using the same rating standard when evaluating efficiency across products.
Should I choose the 10 kW or 15 kW electric backup heat? +
Selection depends on your climate and heat pump heating capacity. In cold climates requiring substantial auxiliary heating, 15 kW provides faster temperature recovery. Milder climates may only need 5-10 kW. Calculate peak heating load to determine which option prevents excessive runtime during winter.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Standard coverage includes manufacturing defects in components and compressor failure. Wear items like capacitors and fan motors may have shorter terms. Review the complete warranty document before purchase to understand exclusions and claim procedures.
