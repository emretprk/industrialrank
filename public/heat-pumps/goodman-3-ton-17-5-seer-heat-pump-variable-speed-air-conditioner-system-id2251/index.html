Review

The Goodman HKSC19CB represents the sweet spot in the mid-range heat pump market. This 3-ton unit combines practical engineering with accessible pricing, making it a workhorse choice for residential HVAC contractors and cost-conscious homeowners. Weighing just 9 pounds and measuring 24 inches wide by 21 inches deep, it fits standard equipment pad footprints without modification. Goodman's reputation for no-frills durability backs this model, supported by solid efficiency metrics and straightforward service parts availability.

Performance data justifies the efficiency claims. The unit delivers 35,000 BTU cooling capacity with an 18 SEER rating and 64,800 BTU heating output using R410A refrigerant. The variable-speed ECM motor adjusts airflow between part-load and full 1,375 CFM capacity, reducing runtime on mild days and noise during shoulder seasons. Two-stage compressor staging means the system rarely runs at full bore, extending component life and reducing energy spikes. Operating at 208, 230, or 240 volts single-phase, it matches most residential electrical panels without expensive upgrades.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 18 SEER efficiency rating beats the industry baseline and qualifies for Energy Star, lowering operating costs in cooling-heavy months
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor delivers 1,375 CFM maximum with modulating capacity, reducing noise and improving temperature consistency
  • Two-stage condenser design allows partial-load operation during mild weather, cutting energy waste when full capacity isn't needed
  • Two-stage scroll compressor provides reliable long-term performance and smoother operation than single-stage alternatives
  • Generous 10-year warranty with online registration protects your investment against manufacturing defects

Cons

Installation

Installation favors experienced technicians. The system requires 57 to 60-amp circuits depending on configuration, with maximum breaker size of 60 amps for circuit one and 50 amps for circuit two. Sweat-type connections use 3/8-inch liquid and 7/8-inch suction lines with 3/4-inch insulation. Line sets ship up to 50 feet, accommodating indoor-to-outdoor distances without additional component purchases. Standard tools and Goodman's detailed installation manual make setup manageable for certified installers.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs the HKSC19CB with a full 10-year parts and compressor warranty upon online registration. This coverage protects against manufacturing defects but excludes wear items, refrigerant loss from leaks, and damage from improper installation or neglect. Registration is free and straightforward, making the warranty accessible. For a unit at this price point, 10 years is competitive and reflects confidence in durability.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 18 SEER compare to newer standards? +
18 SEER exceeds the federal baseline of 16 SEER by 12.5 percent, qualifying for Energy Star certification. However, premium inverter models now reach 22+ SEER. For moderate climates, 18 SEER delivers meaningful utility savings versus older 13-14 SEER units without premium pricing.
Will this unit work with existing ductwork? +
Yes, provided your ducts match 1,375 CFM capacity at 72 dBA noise levels. The variable-speed motor adapts to moderate undersizing, but oversized or poorly sealed ducts reduce efficiency. Have a certified technician assess your current system before installation.
Does heating performance suffer in freezing temperatures? +
Yes. The 64,800 BTU heating capacity is adequate for mild winters but requires electric backup heat in harsh climates. Optional 5 to 19-kilowatt supplemental heat modules are available but add operational costs during extended cold snaps below 30 degrees.
How does the two-stage compressor improve efficiency? +
Two-stage operation runs at 60 percent capacity during mild weather, matching actual load rather than cycling on and off. This reduces compressor start-stop stress, lowers noise, and cuts energy consumption by roughly 15 to 20 percent compared to single-stage alternatives.
Is the 10-year warranty transferable if I sell my home? +
Goodman typically transfers remaining warranty coverage to new owners with proof of purchase and ownership transfer documentation. Check with Goodman directly, as policies vary by region and may require re-registration.
