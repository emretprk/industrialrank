Review

The Goodman 5-ton 16 SEER heat pump positions itself squarely in the practical middle market, neither a budget-friendly basic model nor a premium high-efficiency system. At $4,177, it undercuts many competitors offering similar SEER ratings while maintaining the brand reputation Goodman has built over decades. This unit targets homeowners and contractors who value straightforward performance and reliability without paying for exotic features or premium noise reduction.

Performance specs are solid across the board. The unit delivers 60,000 BTU of cooling and 60,000 BTU of heating capacity, making it properly sized for typical 5-ton applications. The 16 SEER1 rating is respectable for non-variable-speed equipment and should reduce seasonal energy consumption by 15-20% compared to older 13 SEER systems. The scroll compressor handles the workload with proven durability. However, the 72 dB noise level is moderately loud, and single-stage operation means it runs at full blast regardless of demand.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 16 SEER efficiency rating delivers measurable energy savings compared to 13-14 SEER units, translating to roughly 15-20% lower cooling costs annually
  • 60,000 BTU heating and cooling capacity handles most 5-ton residential applications effectively
  • Scroll compressor design is proven reliable with fewer moving parts than reciprocating alternatives
  • 10-year warranty with online registration provides solid peace of mind for homeowners
  • Single-stage operation simplifies maintenance and reduces component failure points
  • Standard 208/230V single-phase electrical compatibility fits most residential installations

Cons

  • 72 dB noise level is noticeably louder than premium models with variable-speed compressors, which often operate 8-12 dB quieter
  • Not Energy Star certified despite 16 SEER rating, missing rebate opportunities in some utility districts
  • Single-stage compressor lacks modulation for partial-load efficiency, running at full capacity even during mild weather

Who Should Buy

Installation

Installation is straightforward for experienced technicians. The unit requires 208/230V single-phase power with 37-60 amp breaker sizing, standard for residential equipment. At 331 pounds with dimensions of 35.5 by 35.5 by 38.25 inches, it fits typical outdoor pad placements. Sweat valve connections use standard 3/8-inch liquid and 7/8-inch suction lines, requiring basic refrigerant line work. No special mounting or electrical upgrades are typically needed in existing installations.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year parts warranty, activated through online registration, covers defects in materials and workmanship. This is competitive for mid-range equipment and provides genuine protection against compressor failure or component breakdown during the critical first decade of operation. Registration is simple and recommended immediately after installation to establish your coverage period.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 16 SEER compare to other common ratings? +
16 SEER sits between basic 13-14 SEER units and premium 18-20 SEER models. Compared to a 14 SEER system, you'll see roughly 12-15% lower cooling costs annually. It's above-average efficiency without paying top-tier pricing, making it attractive for value-conscious buyers.
Will this unit work with my existing ductwork and indoor handler? +
The 60,000 BTU capacity matches standard 5-ton indoor units from most manufacturers. Ensure your existing air handler is rated for R410A refrigerant and can handle 5-ton capacity. Mismatched tonnage between outdoor and indoor units reduces efficiency significantly.
Is 72 dB noise level a problem for residential use? +
72 dB is comparable to a loud window AC unit or busy traffic. It's noticeable during operation but acceptable in most yards. If noise is critical, variable-speed models operate 8-12 dB quieter but cost significantly more.
How does this Goodman compare to similar Carrier or Lennox models? +
This Goodman undercuts Carrier and Lennox pricing by 10-20% for equivalent SEER ratings. Goodman emphasizes value over premium features. Performance and reliability are comparable, though Carrier and Lennox often offer quieter operation at higher cost.
Is online registration required for the 10-year warranty? +
Yes, Goodman requires online registration to activate the 10-year coverage. Registration is free and takes minutes. Failure to register typically defaults to a shorter limited warranty, so complete this immediately after installation.
