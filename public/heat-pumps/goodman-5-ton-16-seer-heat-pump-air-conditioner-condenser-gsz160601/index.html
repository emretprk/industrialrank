Review

The Goodman GSZ160601 is a workhorse condenser positioned firmly in the contractor-favorite category. At 5 tons with 60,000 BTU capacity, it handles both cooling and heating duties for typical residential homes. The 16 SEER1 rating and Energy Star certification signal honest efficiency gains without flagship pricing. At $3,707, this unit competes in the sensible middle ground where performance meets affordability. Goodman's reputation rests on building equipment that runs dependably for 15-20 years with minimal drama.

Performance specs reveal a straightforward machine designed for reliability. The scroll compressor operates in single-stage mode, meaning it runs at full 60,000 BTU output or shuts off, with no intermediate settings. This simplicity reduces mechanical stress but also limits efficiency optimization in mild weather. At 76 dB, the unit generates meaningful noise compared to variable-capacity alternatives. The 208/230V single-phase electrical requirement matches standard residential service. Refrigerant lines connect via sweat valves, 3/8-inch liquid and 7/8-inch suction, requiring proper flaring techniques during installation.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 16 SEER1 rating meets Energy Star standards, reducing operational costs by approximately 20-30% compared to standard 13 SEER units
  • 60,000 BTU capacity handles most 5-ton applications for cooling and heating simultaneously
  • Scroll compressor design has proven longevity and fewer moving parts than rotary alternatives
  • Sweat valve connections simplify installation for experienced HVAC technicians
  • 10-year warranty with online registration provides solid coverage for parts and labor

Cons

  • Single-stage compressor runs at full capacity, creating less precise temperature control than variable-speed alternatives
  • 76 dB noise level is noticeably louder than inverter-driven or variable-capacity competitors, potentially an issue for noise-sensitive properties
  • R410A refrigerant requires specific recovery and handling during service, increasing maintenance costs over time

Who Should Buy

Best For

Contractors handling mid-range residential replacements in climates with moderate heating demands will appreciate the straightforward installation and reliable performance. Homeowners upgrading from older 10-13 SEER units on fixed budgets gain meaningful efficiency improvements without overpaying for variable-speed technology they may not fully utilize.

Consider Alternatives If

Skip this unit if you need whisper-quiet operation, require advanced modulation for minimal energy waste, or live in extreme cold climates where single-stage heating may struggle.

Installation

Installation demands professional-grade skills with R410A refrigerant handling. The 326-pound weight and 35.5 by 40-inch footprint require solid concrete pads and proper vibration isolation. Electrical needs fall within standard breaker capacity, 37-60 amps, for most homes. Sweat valve connections require careful brazing and nitrogen purging. Most experienced technicians complete hookup in 2-3 hours. Proper charging and system commissioning are critical for achieving the 16 SEER rating.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this condenser with 10 years of parts and labor coverage upon online registration. The registration requirement adds minimal friction but is mandatory to unlock full protection. This warranty ranks average for the category, matching most mainstream competitors but falling short of premium brands offering 15-25 year commitments. Real-world reliability suggests the unit easily justifies this coverage window with minimal claims.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will this unit handle heating in cold climates? +
The 60,000 BTU heating capacity works adequately in moderate climates down to approximately 35°F outdoor temperature. Below that threshold, the single-stage compressor struggles, and most systems add electric resistance heating. Extreme cold regions require variable-capacity alternatives with superior cold-weather performance curves.
Can I install this myself, or do I need a licensed technician? +
R410A systems require EPA Section 608 certification for anyone handling refrigerant. Sweat valve connections demand brazing skills and nitrogen purging knowledge. Most jurisdictions mandate licensed installation. DIY work voids warranties and violates code in nearly all areas.
How does 16 SEER compare to newer standards? +
16 SEER1 translates roughly to 13-14 SEER2 under updated testing. Current federal minimums sit at 15 SEER2, so this unit meets current standards. Variable-capacity models achieve 18-22 SEER2, but at significantly higher cost and complexity. The 16 SEER1 rating delivers genuine 20-25 percent energy savings versus older single-stage 13 SEER units.
How does this compare to Lennox or Carrier units at similar price points? +
Goodman prioritizes reliability and straightforward service over brand reputation premiums. Comparable Lennox or Carrier units often cost 15-25 percent more for equivalent efficiency. Performance differences matter less than installer familiarity and parts availability in your area. Goodman wins on value, though premium brands may offer quieter operation.
Does the 10-year warranty require anything beyond online registration? +
Online registration is the primary requirement. Original purchase proof helps, and some claims require in-home inspection. Warranty covers manufacturing defects but excludes damage from improper installation, refrigerant mishandling, or inadequate maintenance. Annual service keeps claims valid.
