Review

The Goodman 2-ton heat pump represents solid mid-market positioning in the residential HVAC space. At $1,409, it undercuts comparable 16 SEER units from premium manufacturers while maintaining the brand credibility contractors and homeowners expect. This A-coil configuration handles both upflow and downflow installations, making it adaptable to existing ductwork. The unit carries Energy Star certification and includes a 10-year warranty, signaling Goodman's confidence in their scroll compressor design and manufacturing consistency.

Performance specs tell a straightforward story. The unit delivers 24,000 BTU cooling and heating capacity with a 16 SEER1 rating, translating to roughly 20-25% better efficiency than older 13 SEER equipment most homeowners currently operate. The variable-speed ECM blower motor runs at 1,200 CFM maximum and automatically adjusts output based on load, reducing unnecessary cycling and runtime waste. R410A refrigerant and 208/230V single-phase power work with standard residential electrical, requiring only a 15-amp breaker. The 75 dB noise level sits in typical range for this tonnage class.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet list 3 ton tonnage when cooling/heating shows 24,000 BTU? +
This is a documentation inconsistency. The 24,000 BTU rating defines actual capacity at 2 tons. Order based on BTU output, not the tonnage field. Verify against your existing system and ductwork design, typically 400 CFM per ton means 2-ton sizing for this unit's 1,200 CFM maximum.
Can this unit retrofit into my existing 2-ton system without ductwork changes? +
Yes, likely. The 24,000 BTU capacity matches standard 2-ton ductwork, and the upflow/downflow A-coil design adapts to most furnace configurations. However, have a technician verify your line set sizes (3/8-inch liquid, 3/4-inch suction) and refrigerant charge compatibility with your existing outdoor condenser before installation.
How much money will the 16 SEER rating save annually compared to my old 13 SEER unit? +
Rough estimate runs $150-250 per year depending on climate and usage patterns. A unit running 1,500 cooling hours annually in a 3,000 square foot home saves approximately 18-22% on cooling costs. Your savings increase in humid climates where the compressor runs longer, so results vary significantly by region.
Is this better than a Carrier or Lennox unit at similar pricing? +
Goodman compressor reliability matches competitors, but Carrier and Lennox typically offer two-stage cooling at this price point, improving part-load efficiency. This single-stage Goodman excels at cost entry but trails premium features. Compare noise ratings and staging capability against your climate needs and budget ceiling.
What happens if I don't register for the warranty online? +
Warranty coverage becomes void without registration. Complete the online registration immediately after installation with your purchase receipt and installation date. Goodman's website provides straightforward registration, but delaying risks losing 10 years of protection if problems develop later.
