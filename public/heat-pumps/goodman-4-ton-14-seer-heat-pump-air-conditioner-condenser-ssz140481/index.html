Review

The Goodman SSZ140481 is a straightforward 4-ton air conditioning condenser positioned as an economical choice for residential HVAC applications. This unit sits in Goodman's reliable but no-frills product tier, competing directly against similarly priced units from Daikin and Lennox. At $2,661, it targets cost-conscious contractors and homeowners who prioritize function over premium features. The equipment is manufactured to standard residential specifications and carries the brand reputation that Goodman has built through decades of broad market presence.

Performance specifications show 48,000 BTU cooling and heating capacity with a 14 SEER1 efficiency rating, meeting baseline seasonal energy efficiency standards. The scroll compressor operates as a single-stage unit, meaning it runs at full capacity or off, without intermediate speed adjustments. Electrical requirements call for 208 or 230V single-phase power with breaker sizing between 26.4 and 45 amps, well within typical residential service capabilities. R410A refrigerant fills the system, which remains industry standard though phase-down timelines are shifting future focus to R454B alternatives.

Installation

Installation requires standard sweat connections via 3/8 inch liquid and 7/8 inch suction valve lines. The 35.5 by 35.5 inch footprint and 34.25 inch height fit typical concrete pad dimensions. Electricians need basic single-phase wiring knowledge, and the unit weighs 300 pounds, manageable with standard equipment handling. Professional installation is strongly recommended for proper refrigerant charging and airflow balance.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman includes a 10-year warranty on parts when registered online, a standard offering for this product category. Registration is required within 60 days of purchase to activate full coverage. The warranty covers compressor, heat exchanger, and components, but excludes labor. This protection period is competitive but not exceptional compared to some manufacturers offering 12-year coverage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this unit Energy Star certified? +
No, this model does not carry Energy Star certification. It meets baseline 14 SEER1 standards. However, it does not qualify for many state or utility rebate programs that require 15+ SEER2 ratings, potentially costing buyers hundreds in missed incentives.
Can this condenser work with my existing furnace? +
Yes, if your furnace matches 4-ton capacity and uses standard R410A refrigerant connections. Have an HVAC technician verify compatible airflow and indoor coil specifications before installation. Mismatched equipment degrades efficiency and performance.
How loud is the unit during normal operation? +
At 74 dB, it operates louder than premium models. This noise level is comparable to a vacuum cleaner and can be noticeable in quiet residential settings. Consider placement away from bedrooms if noise sensitivity is a concern.
How does this compare to a Carrier or Lennox 4-ton unit at similar price? +
Goodman prioritizes cost over brand prestige. Carrier and Lennox competitors typically offer better noise profiles and slightly higher efficiency ratings. Goodman's strength is dependable scroll compressor technology and parts availability through broad distribution networks.
What happens if something fails after three years? +
The 10-year registered warranty covers parts replacement. You pay labor costs for repairs. After 10 years, all component failures become customer responsibility, so plan accordingly for equipment lifecycle.
