This ACiQ unit is ideal for homeowners in moderate climates seeking a dependable heat pump without premium pricing. The 14 SEER efficiency and 34,100 BTU heating output make it practical for homes needing both cooling and supplemental heating.

Review

The ACiQ 2-ton heat pump (model ACIQ-24-EHPD-UPG) occupies practical middle ground in the residential HVAC market. This unit directly replaces Goodman's GSZ140251, making it attractive to contractors managing retrofit projects. The system combines modest capacity with solid efficiency credentials, positioning it as a sensible choice for homeowners seeking dependability over cutting-edge technology.

Performance specs reveal balanced engineering. The unit delivers 24,000 BTU cooling and 34,100 BTU heating, sufficient for moderate-sized homes in mixed climates. Its 14 SEER1 rating and Energy Star certification indicate competent efficiency, though not exceptional by today's standards. The scroll compressor operates single-stage, meaning it cycles on and off rather than modulating power, a design that prioritizes reliability over variable-load optimization.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 14 SEER1 efficiency meets Energy Star standards with R454B low-GWP refrigerant
  • 34,100 BTU heating output provides substantial winter performance for 2-ton systems
  • Single-stage scroll compressor design is proven reliable across residential applications
  • Multi-speed PSC blower motor adjusts airflow with 1,150 CFM maximum capacity
  • 10-year warranty with online registration gives reasonable long-term protection

Cons

  • 14 SEER is mid-range efficiency, not competitive with 16+ SEER alternatives available today
  • Single-stage compressor runs at full capacity, less efficient than variable-speed models
  • 70 dB noise level is moderately loud for residential outdoor installations

Who Should Buy

Best For

Budget-conscious homeowners in transitional climates who need balanced cooling and heating without investing in premium variable-capacity systems. Contractors replacing older Goodman units like the GSZ140251 will appreciate the direct compatibility.

Consider Alternatives If

Buyers in hot climates who prioritize cooling efficiency should explore 16+ SEER models, and those in very cold regions may want higher heating BTU ratings or dual-fuel backup.

Installation

Installation requires standard 208/230/240-volt single-phase power with 57 to 60-amp breaker capacity. The unit weighs 120 pounds and measures 45 inches tall, 17.5 inches wide, and 21 inches deep, fitting typical outdoor pad spaces. Suction and liquid lines use 3/4-inch and 3/8-inch sweat connections respectively, with 50-foot maximum line length and 3/4-inch insulation thickness specified.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman covers this unit with a full 10-year warranty upon online registration. This protection is industry-standard for mid-tier residential heat pumps. Coverage includes compressor and parts, providing meaningful security against manufacturer defects during typical equipment lifespan.

Frequently Asked Questions

What heating capacity does this 2-ton unit provide? +
The system delivers 34,100 BTU of heating output, making it suitable for supplemental or primary heat in moderate climates. Many installations include optional electric backup heat ranging from 5 to 10 kilowatts for enhanced cold-weather performance and faster recovery.
Is this compatible with my existing Goodman system? +
Yes, model ACIQ-24-EHPD-UPG directly replaces the GSZ140251 condenser unit. Your indoor air handler, line set, and electrical connections should integrate seamlessly, reducing installation complexity and labor costs for retrofit projects.
How does 14 SEER compare to newer systems? +
14 SEER is solid but middle-of-the-road. Current market leaders offer 16-18+ SEER ratings with variable-capacity compressors and enhanced controls. You'll pay more upfront for those features but recover costs through lower energy bills over 10-15 years.
Can I use this in a very cold climate? +
The 34,100 BTU heating helps, but performance drops at temperatures below 32 degrees. For regions with sustained sub-freezing winters, consider dual-fuel systems with gas furnaces or units with higher heating BTU output and integrated electric backup.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Goodman covers compressor failure, sealed-system components, and parts defects for 10 years with online registration. Labor and refrigerant are typically not included, so verify your installer's coverage terms.
