Review

The Goodman 1.5 Ton 15 SEER heat pump sits in the value segment of the HVAC market, targeting cost-conscious buyers willing to trade premium features for solid baseline performance. Goodman has built reputation for straightforward, durable equipment that prioritizes function over bells and whistles. This unit delivers on that promise with practical specs and no unnecessary complexity, making it attractive for contractors and homeowners managing budgets carefully.

Performance specs show 18,000 BTU cooling capacity and an impressive 34,100 BTU heating output, making this unit punch above its tonnage rating for heating applications. The 15 SEER efficiency rating sits squarely in mid-range territory, not exceptional but respectable for the price point. The scroll compressor operates at 70 dB, which is noticeably louder than variable-speed competitors but acceptable for most installations. The unit requires 208/230/240 volt single-phase power with 57 to 60 amp breaker sizing, standard for residential applications.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Strong heating capacity of 34,100 BTU for a 1.5 ton unit, useful in transitional climates
  • 15 SEER efficiency rating provides respectable cooling performance without premium cost
  • Lightweight at 9 lbs with compact 21x21x49 inch footprint, easier to handle and position
  • Multi-speed EEM blower motor with 1,450 CFM maximum airflow for flexible comfort control
  • 10-year warranty with online registration offers reasonable long-term protection
  • Scroll compressor design is proven reliable technology used across many manufacturers

Cons

  • Single-stage compressor cycles on/off rather than modulating, reducing efficiency and increasing noise at 70 dB
  • Not ENERGY STAR certified, missing potential tax credits and rebate opportunities
  • SKU mismatch in specs: listed as both 1.5 ton and 2.5 ton, creating confusion about actual capacity
  • R410A refrigerant being phased out, making future service and replacement more difficult after 2025

Who Should Buy

Best For

DIY-minded homeowners with tight budgets who are comfortable with professional installation and want a straightforward heat pump for smaller homes or climate-controlled spaces. Contractors building rental properties or spec homes where cost per unit matters more than premium features will find strong value here.

Consider Alternatives If

Homeowners in hot climates needing maximum cooling efficiency, those seeking ENERGY STAR rebates, or anyone requiring whisper-quiet operation should explore variable-speed or inverter-driven alternatives.

Installation

Installation is straightforward with sweat valve connections using 3/8 inch liquid lines and 7/8 inch suction lines. The 9 lb weight makes handling manageable, and the compact 21x21x49 inch dimensions fit tight outdoor spaces better than larger tonnage units. Standard ductwork compatibility with multi-speed blower means most contractors can complete installation in a single day without complications.

Warranty & Reliability

Ten-year parts and labor coverage applies when registered online, which is reasonable protection for equipment in this price range. Registration is simple and worth completing immediately upon installation. Coverage excludes normal wear items and requires professional installation to remain valid.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between the 1.5 ton and 2.5 ton specs listed? +
This is a red flag requiring clarification from the seller. The title says 1.5 ton, but specs list 2.5 ton tonnage. The 18,000 BTU cooling capacity aligns with 1.5 ton rating. Request confirmation of actual tonnage before purchasing to ensure proper system sizing.
Can I install this myself or do I need a professional? +
Professional installation is strongly recommended. The system requires EPA-certified refrigerant handling, proper electrical connections to 57-60 amp breakers, and precise line pressure balancing. DIY installation voids warranty and risks system failure or safety hazards.
How does 15 SEER compare to other units in this price range? +
15 SEER is solid mid-range efficiency. Higher-end models reach 18-21 SEER but cost significantly more. At this price point, 15 SEER delivers reasonable energy savings without premium investment. Calculate payback period based on your local electricity rates.
Is R410A refrigerant still available and what does phase-out mean? +
R410A is currently available but being phased out by 2025 in favor of lower GWP alternatives. This means future service costs could rise as supplies tighten. Consider this when evaluating long-term ownership costs versus newer models using alternative refrigerants.
Does this qualify for tax credits or energy rebates? +
No, this unit lacks ENERGY STAR certification required for most federal tax credits. Check with your utility company for local rebates, but don't expect credits at the federal level. This impacts total cost of ownership versus certified models.
