Review

This 1.5 ton Goodman hybrid system pairs a 96% AFUE gas furnace with a 14.5 SEER heat pump condenser, creating a dual-fuel solution for homeowners seeking year-round comfort at competitive pricing. At $2,666, it undercuts most brand-name alternatives while maintaining Energy Star certification. The horizontal A-coil configuration suits both upflow and downflow installations, giving contractors flexibility in retrofit scenarios where space is tight or equipment orientation varies.

The furnace delivers 80,000 BTU of heating output with two-stage burner modulation, while the heat pump condenser handles 18,000 BTU of cooling capacity. The variable-speed ECM blower motor ramps between 0 and 1,200 CFM, responding to actual load demands rather than running at full speed continuously. Real-world testing shows this approach reduces electricity consumption by 15-20% compared to single-speed units. The 70 dB noise level is acceptable for basement or garage placement, though perimeter installations may benefit from additional sound damping.

Installation

This horizontal unit requires 115V single-phase 60 Hz power with a 20-amp maximum breaker (11.8 amps minimum). Suction and liquid lines use 3/4-inch and 3/8-inch sweat connections respectively, demanding competent brazing skills or prefabricated lineset adapters. PVC exhaust venting simplifies furnace installation compared to metal pipe requirements. Total unit weight of 22 lbs makes solo handling feasible, though proper support brackets are essential.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides a 10-year parts warranty upon online registration, covering compressor, heat exchanger, and control boards. This ranks above entry-level competitors offering 5-year warranties but below premium manufacturers offering 10-year compressor-specific coverage. Registration is straightforward but mandatory to activate full coverage. No labor warranty is included, so installation service contracts matter.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet list both 1.5 ton and 3 ton? +
This appears to be a documentation error from the manufacturer or retailer. The 18,000 BTU cooling capacity and AHRI certificate number 7368834 confirm 1.5 ton rating. Verify tonnage directly with Goodman before ordering to avoid coil mismatch issues with existing ductwork or indoor handlers.
Can I install this horizontally in an attic? +
Yes, the horizontal A-coil configuration supports attic mounting, but ensure adequate clearance for the 34.5-inch height and 28.75-inch depth. Verify return air drop-down doesn't restrict airflow and confirm PVC exhaust venting can exit through roof or soffit without condensation traps.
How does 14.5 SEER compare to modern heat pumps? +
14.5 SEER rates as mid-tier efficiency today. Current ENERGY STAR minimums hit 15.5+ SEER, and high-efficiency models reach 18-20 SEER. This unit qualifies for rebates but won't match premium variable-capacity compressor systems in energy savings over 15-year lifecycles.
Should I choose this over a furnace-only system? +
The heat pump component adds cooling capability and uses free ambient heat during shoulder seasons, reducing furnace runtime 20-30% in mixed climates. If your region experiences reliable spring/fall temps above 50 degrees Fahrenheit, the dual-fuel approach pays back within 5-7 years through utility savings.
Does online registration really matter for warranty coverage? +
Yes, Goodman requires proof of registration within 30 days to activate the 10-year warranty. Without registration, coverage drops to manufacturer's limited terms. Save your receipt and complete registration immediately upon installation to protect your investment.
