Review

The Goodman 5-ton gas furnace and heat pump system represents the practical middle ground in HVAC equipment. This horizontal A-coil unit combines an 80% AFUE furnace with an 18 SEER cooling coil, making it suitable for contractors needing reliable budget equipment and homeowners upgrading aging systems. Goodman's reputation for straightforward manufacturing translates to lower material costs, passing savings to end users without sacrificing essential two-stage heating and cooling technology.

Real-world performance centers on those two stages working together. The furnace delivers 57,000 BTU with a two-stage burner that fires at reduced capacity during mild weather, cutting runtime and gas consumption. The matching condenser uses a two-stage scroll compressor and variable-speed 2,000 CFM blower to match cooling demand to actual conditions. At 75 dBA, this setup runs quieter than single-stage predecessors, and the 18 SEER rating means annual cooling costs drop noticeably in humid climates. Heating still requires 208/230V single-phase power and a 38.8-60 amp breaker, so older panels may need upgrading.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Two-stage burner and condenser provide better part-load efficiency and quieter operation at 75 dBA
  • Variable-speed blower motor maximizes comfort and reduces energy waste compared to fixed-speed alternatives
  • 18 SEER cooling rating qualifies for Energy Star, translating to real savings on summer bills
  • Horizontal configuration fits tight crawlspaces, attics, and utility closets where vertical units won't fit
  • 10-year warranty with online registration covers major components without significant restrictions
  • $2,071 price point undercuts comparable two-stage systems by $400-600

Cons

  • 80% AFUE is functional but trails modern 95%+ condensing furnaces by 15 percentage points
  • 18 SEER2 rating (converted from 16.5 SEER1) doesn't match high-efficiency mini-splits or variable-refrigerant systems
  • Two-stage compressor limits fine-tuned capacity modulation on mild days, missing potential efficiency gains
  • Metal flue requirement adds installation cost if your home lacks existing exhaust venting
  • Requires 38.8 to 60-amp circuit, which may demand panel upgrades in older homes

Who Should Buy

Contractors installing budget replacements in rental properties or fix-and-flip scenarios will appreciate the reliability and quick installation window. Installation

Horizontal installation works in attics, basements, or crawlspaces where the 38.25-inch height, 35.5-inch width and depth fit standard framings. The A-coil configuration requires metal flue venting for exhaust, which adds labor if no existing metal duct exists. Suction line insulation (1/2-inch) and proper refrigerant line sizing using the 3/8-inch liquid and 1.125-inch suction specs are critical. Most qualified HVAC techs complete installation in one day, including startup and ductwork verification.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this unit with a solid 10-year parts warranty when you register online after purchase. Coverage includes the compressor, coil, and furnace heat exchanger, though it excludes wear items like capacitors and contactors. This warranty matches mid-tier competitor offerings and provides genuine peace of mind for homeowners, though it's not the lifetime coverage premium brands sometimes advertise.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the real difference between 80% AFUE and 95% AFUE furnaces? +
An 80% AFUE furnace wastes 20% of fuel energy up the chimney, while 95% condensing models capture that heat. Over 20 years, a 95% unit saves $3,000-5,000 in natural gas depending on climate and usage. This Goodman makes sense for mild climates or short ownership periods, but northerners should consider condensing models.
Does this system need a dedicated electrical circuit? +
Yes. The unit requires 208/230V single-phase power with a breaker rated 38.8 to 60 amps. Many older homes run 100-amp main service, so you'll likely need a subpanel or upgrade existing circuits. Budget $500-1,200 for electrical work before installation begins.
How does 18 SEER cooling compare to what I'm replacing? +
If your current unit is 10 SEER (pre-2010), expect 40-50% lower summer cooling costs. Modern 20+ SEER systems save another 10-15% more, but cost $3,000+ additional. For moderate climates, 18 SEER delivers strong ROI on cooling bills while keeping total system cost under $2,500.
Can I use this furnace with propane or only natural gas? +
Goodman specifies both propane and natural gas compatibility. Propane requires an orifice conversion kit during installation, typically costing $50-100 in parts. Have your HVAC contractor confirm the model SKU LS381183850 comes set for your fuel type before delivery.
Is the 10-year warranty actually useful, or does it have hidden exclusions? +
Goodman's 10-year coverage is straightforward: major components covered after online registration. Wear items, labor, and Freon are excluded. This is honest coverage that protects against manufacturing defects, making it valuable for homeowner peace of mind.
