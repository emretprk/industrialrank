This Mitsubishi quad zone system excels for homeowners in cold climates who need efficient heating across multiple rooms without ductwork. The 11 HSPF rating and Hyper Heat technology make it capable of maintaining comfort even when outdoor temperatures drop significantly.

Review

The Mitsubishi MXZ-SM36NAMHZ replacement quad zone system represents a thoughtful balance between heating and cooling performance for challenging climates. At 36,000 BTU cooling capacity with 23 SEER2 efficiency, this wall-mounted mini split delivers respectable cooling power while maintaining exceptional energy performance. The quad zone configuration splits capacity as 9+9+9+15, enabling homeowners to prioritize comfort in specific rooms rather than conditioning entire homes uniformly.

Where this system truly distinguishes itself is heating performance. The 11 HSPF rating and Hyper Heat technology allow the unit to extract warmth from outdoor air even during severe cold snaps, delivering 33,000 BTU of heating output. The 15 EER cooling efficiency rating performs well for mini splits, while the 49 dB operating noise level keeps the system whisper-quiet during both seasons. Single phase 230V operation with 40-45 amp breaker requirements puts it within reach of most residential electrical panels.

Installation

Installation involves mounting four wall units at optimal heights, running refrigerant lines up to 15 feet between outdoor and indoor components, and connecting 5/8 inch suction and 3/8 inch liquid lines. The included wireless controller eliminates complex wiring for zone management. Most installations require a qualified HVAC technician due to refrigerant handling and electrical integration demands.

Warranty & Reliability

Coverage extends five years on parts, protecting against manufacturing defects and component failures. Labor and installation costs typically fall outside this coverage. For a system expected to operate 15-20 years, this warranty provides solid baseline protection, though some competitors offer extended terms worth comparing before purchase.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can this system heat effectively in very cold weather? +
Yes. The 11 HSPF rating and Hyper Heat technology allow operation down to subzero temperatures while extracting warmth. At 33,000 BTU heating output, it handles 3-ton equivalent heating capacity, though severe climates may benefit from supplemental backup heat on the coldest days.
What electrical upgrades might be needed? +
The system requires 230V single phase 60Hz power with 40-45 amp dedicated breaker service. Homes with older panels or limited capacity may need electrician evaluation. The 50 amp amperage requirement means most standard 60-amp circuits work, but verification is essential before installation.
How efficient is this compared to central AC? +
The 23 SEER2 rating far exceeds most central air systems at 14-16 SEER2. Zoning capability adds efficiency by conditioning only occupied spaces. Mini splits typically save 30-40% on heating and cooling energy compared to central systems in multi-story or retrofit applications.
Is this better than traditional window units? +
Absolutely. Window units deliver 8-10 EER; this system provides 15 EER cooling and 11 HSPF heating. Wall-mounted units look cleaner, operate quieter at 49 dB, provide zoned heating and cooling, and use substantially less energy than portable or window alternatives.
What does the five year warranty actually cover? +
Parts coverage protects against manufacturing defects on compressors, motors, and internal components. It does not include labor costs, refrigerant, installation issues, or damage from improper maintenance. Register promptly to maximize coverage length.
