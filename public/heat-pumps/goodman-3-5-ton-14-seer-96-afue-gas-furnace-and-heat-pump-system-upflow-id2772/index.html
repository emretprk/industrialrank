Review

The ACiQ ACIQ-4X-EHPD-UPG represents a practical middle ground between furnace-only heating and full heat pump conversion. This downflow system combines a 96% AFUE natural gas furnace with an integrated 14 SEER heat pump, allowing homeowners to leverage gas heat for deep winter months while maximizing efficiency during spring and fall. At $0 and weighing just 120 pounds, it's positioned as an economical retrofit solution for contractors upgrading aging single-stage systems.

Performance specs reveal solid real-world capability. The furnace delivers 80,000 BTU heat output with two-stage burners that reduce cycling and noise to 74 dBA. The heat pump provides 41,000 BTU cooling via a scroll compressor running at 14 SEER. A variable-speed ECM motor adjusts airflow up to 1600 CFM, matching demand rather than running at fixed capacity. Dimensions of 34.5 inches height by 21 inches wide fit standard utility closets. The system requires 115V single-phase power with 24-40 amp breaker range, minimizing electrical modifications during installation.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 96% AFUE furnace efficiency reduces natural gas consumption significantly compared to standard 80-90% units
  • Two-stage burner modulates output for quieter operation at 74 dBA and precise temperature control
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor adjusts CFM from baseline to 1600 maximum, improving comfort and reducing energy waste
  • 14 SEER heat pump provides supplemental cooling and heating during shoulder seasons at no additional outdoor unit cost
  • Dual 115V/60Hz electrical requirement simplifies installation without requiring dedicated 240V service upgrades
  • PVC/plastic exhaust compatibility eliminates expensive metal flue replacement in most homes

Cons

  • Single-stage compressor on heat pump limits efficiency gains compared to two-stage models in moderate cooling loads
  • 5-ton condenser capacity exceeds the 3.5-ton cooling rating, creating an unmatched pair that reduces system optimization
  • 74 dBA noise level runs louder than high-efficiency variable-speed compressor systems, noticeable in quiet neighborhoods
  • Downflow configuration requires basement or crawlspace installation, incompatible with attic or closet placements
  • 10-year warranty requires online registration, creating administrative friction for busy contractors or absent-minded homeowners

Installation

Installation complexity is moderate. Downflow configuration demands basement, crawlspace, or utility closet placement where supply plenum can exit downward through floor joists. Technicians must connect 3/8-inch liquid line, 1 1/8-inch suction line with 1/2-inch insulation, and 7/8-inch suction valve. PVC exhaust venting simplifies flue work. Ducting transitions from A-coil output to existing ductwork. Most experienced HVAC crews complete installation in 4-6 hours including startup and safety checks.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year parts warranty provides solid coverage when registered online within 30 days of installation. Labor claims typically fall to contractors or homeowners per installation agreements. Online registration eliminates manufacturer delays but requires proof of purchase and installation documentation. This timeframe covers compressor failures and heat exchanger defects common in first decade of operation, though it excludes wear items like capacitors and contactors.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is the condenser rated 5 tons when the system is 3.5 tons? +
The specs appear inconsistent, likely a SKU cross-reference error. The 41,000 BTU cooling output actually represents 3.4 tons, matching the system designation. Verify AHRI Certificate 7368843 for authentic matched-pair tonnage before purchase. This mismatch suggests the unit may be listed as a standalone condenser rather than complete system.
Can this system replace my old furnace without replacing outdoor equipment? +
Only if you're upgrading from an older furnace-only system without an air conditioning condenser. If existing condenser is present, you'll need to isolate it or remove it entirely. The integrated heat pump compressor cannot work alongside a separate outdoor AC unit. Consult local technician to assess your current setup.
How does 96% AFUE compare to my current furnace? +
Standard furnaces from 2005-2015 operate at 78-85% AFUE, meaning 15-22% of gas energy vents as waste heat. This 96% unit recaptures combustion heat through secondary heat exchangers, reducing annual heating costs by roughly 12-15% depending on usage. Long-term savings typically offset $400-800 equipment premium within 5-7 years.
Is this better than installing a standalone heat pump system? +
Depends on climate and priorities. Heat pumps excel in moderate climates but lose efficiency below 35 degrees Fahrenheit. Hybrid systems like this maintain full heating capacity in extreme cold while enjoying heat pump efficiency in shoulder seasons. Northern homeowners generally prefer hybrid, while southern buyers lean toward standalone heat pumps for simplicity.
What does online warranty registration require? +
Most manufacturers require proof of purchase, serial number, installation date, and installer information submitted within 30 days. Some systems now use QR code registration on packaging. Keep receipts and documentation organized. Failure to register voids warranty, so budget 15 minutes for completion immediately after installation.
