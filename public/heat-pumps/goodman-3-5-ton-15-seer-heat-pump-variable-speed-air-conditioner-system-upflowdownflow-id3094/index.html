Review

The ACiQ ACIQ-4X-EHPD-UPG is a 3.5-ton heat pump that delivers straightforward performance in a crowded market. Positioned between budget units and premium inverter-driven systems, it targets contractors and homeowners seeking Energy Star certification and rebate eligibility without paying for bleeding-edge technology. The variable-speed ECM blower is the key differentiator, enabling more efficient part-load operation and quieter idle performance than comparable single-stage competitors at similar price points.

Performance specs reveal a heating-focused design: 42,000 heating BTU against 41,000 cooling BTU is uncommon and useful for mixed climates. The 14 SEER1 rating and Energy Star badge qualify for most utility programs. The scroll compressor operates at a moderate 74 dB, acceptable but not exceptional for residential settings. Maximum CFM of 1,600 through the A-coil matches typical trunk-and-branch ductwork, though the variable-speed motor allows CPM modulation from roughly 60 to 100 percent capacity.

Installation

Installation is straightforward for experienced technicians. The unit requires 208/230V single-phase power with a 24-amp minimum breaker and 15-amp maximum breaker rating, verified during load calculation. Sweat-type valve connections simplify field fabrication. The 50-foot suction line comes pre-insulated at 1/2 inch thickness, protecting against superheat loss. Upflow and downflow configurations offer flexibility for basement, attic, or closet placements.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the 14 SEER1 rating compare to newer SEER2 standards? +
SEER1 and SEER2 use different test conditions, with SEER2 typically 15 to 20 percent lower than equivalent SEER1 ratings. This unit's 14 SEER1 translates roughly to 11 to 11.5 SEER2, competitive for non-inverter systems. Check state rebate eligibility requirements, as some programs now mandate SEER2 thresholds.
Can this unit handle 5-ton cooling demand if downsized from a larger system? +
The nameplate says 5 tonnage, but actual cooling output is 41,000 BTU, equivalent to 3.4 tons. Never oversize cooling capacity; it reduces dehumidification and cycles inefficiently. Match the unit to your load calculation, not wishful thinking. A true 5-ton system delivers 60,000 BTU.
Is the variable-speed blower worth the extra cost versus single-stage? +
Yes, for most climates. Variable-speed motors reduce short cycling, lower electricity draw during part-load operation, and improve humidity control. You'll recoup 10 to 15 percent of blower energy costs over five years, plus quieter operation and better comfort.
Does this unit pair well with an existing Goodman furnace or coil? +
The A-coil design and refrigerant lines fit most Goodman and compatible equipment, but always verify TXV valve type and subcooling settings. Cross-equipment matching requires a licensed tech to confirm pressures and superheat. Don't assume compatibility without field inspection.
What if I register late for the warranty? +
Some ACiQ and Goodman policies allow registration within 30 days of installation. Verify with your dealer before closing your system out. Late registration may void extended coverage, leaving you with limited protection.
