Review

The Goodman GPDM32406041 is a straightforward 2-ton dual-fuel package unit designed for homeowners and contractors seeking a middle ground between budget and premium equipment. It combines electric heat pump operation during mild weather with a natural gas furnace for heavy heating loads. This SKU replaces the older GPD1424060M41 model and represents Goodman's accessible entry point into modern SEER2-rated equipment. At $4,710, it positions itself below variable-capacity systems while maintaining manufacturer backing and field-proven architecture.

Performance specifications reflect a unit built for consistent output rather than peak efficiency. The cooling side delivers 23,800 BTU at 13.4 SEER2, meeting current efficiency standards for 2-ton equipment. Heating reaches 60,000 BTU total (23,800 from the compressor plus gas burner capacity) with an 8.0 HSPF rating, adequate for moderate winter demands. The 5-speed EEM blower motor tops at 850 CFM, and the scroll compressor operates at 755 RPM using R410A refrigerant. Single-stage cooling means the unit runs at full capacity or off, without intermediate modulation.

Installation

Installation is straightforward for experienced technicians. The unit arrives pre-charged with 120 oz of R410A, eliminating field charging for most residential applications. Electrical requirements are modest, 208/230V single-phase at 25-30 amp breaker range. The downflow/horizontal configuration fits most closet or basement installations. Dimensions of 47 inches wide, 51 inches deep, and 34.5 inches tall require basic clearance planning. Note that you'll purchase the thermostat separately, adding to project scope.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides 10 years of parts coverage when you register the unit online within 60 days of installation. This is standard industry practice for mid-tier equipment and provides meaningful protection against compressor or heat exchanger failure. Labor is not covered. Online registration is straightforward but mandatory for the warranty to activate, so document that serial number.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the 13.4 SEER2 rating compare to newer units? +
13.4 SEER2 is right at the 2024 Energy Star threshold for northern climates and above minimum federal requirements. It's not exceptional, but respectable for a single-stage unit. Variable-capacity models reach 16-18 SEER2 at higher prices. For most homeowners, 13.4 delivers reasonable energy bills without premium investment.
Can this unit handle very cold winters? +
The 8.0 HSPF is adequate for mild-to-moderate climates but not ideal for regions below 5,000 heating degree days. In harsh winters, the gas furnace will run frequently, offsetting heat pump gains. For Minnesota or Maine, consider higher HSPF models or auxiliary heating strategies.
Is this pre-charged unit truly ready to install? +
Yes, the 120 oz R410A charge means no field refrigerant handling on standard residential installations. Your technician still needs to check liquid and suction line pressures after startup and add small amounts if needed, but bulk charging is eliminated, reducing contamination risk.
Why is a controller sold separately? +
Goodman packages the heating and cooling unit without a thermostat to let customers choose communicating, basic, or WiFi-enabled models. This saves cost if you're upgrading an existing system with a compatible stat, but adds expense for new installations. Budget $150-400 depending on features.
What's included in the 10-year warranty? +
Parts only, not labor. Compressor, heat exchanger, coils, and scroll mechanism are covered. You must register online within 60 days. Refrigerant is typically not covered after the first year unless there's a manufacturing defect.
