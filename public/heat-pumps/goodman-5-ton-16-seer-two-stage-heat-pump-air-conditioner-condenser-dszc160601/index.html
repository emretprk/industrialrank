Review

The Goodman 5-ton two-stage heat pump sits squarely in the sweet spot for value-conscious contractors and homeowners. At 4,633 dollars, it delivers legitimate mid-tier performance without the premium pricing of Lennox or Carrier equivalents. Goodman has built credibility over two decades as a no-frills manufacturer that emphasizes durability over flashy features. This unit represents that philosophy, offering 60,000 BTU cooling and heating capacity with Energy Star certification and proven scroll compressor technology.

Performance specs matter here. The 16 SEER1 rating translates to measurable cooling efficiency, beating 13-14 SEER single-stage units by roughly 20 percent on seasonal energy consumption. Two-stage operation means the compressor runs at partial load most of the time, cycling down on mild days and only ramping to full 60,000 BTU output during peaks. The 75 dB noise level is moderate, not silent, but acceptable for outdoor installations away from living spaces. Electrical requirements are straightforward, 208/230V single-phase at 60 Hz.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 16 SEER1 efficiency beats standard single-stage units by 20-30 percent on energy consumption
  • Two-stage condenser adapts cooling output to demand, reducing runtime and wear
  • 60,000 BTU capacity handles 5-ton loads for homes up to 2,000 square feet
  • Scroll compressor is proven durable technology with fewer moving parts than reciprocating designs
  • Energy Star certified with rebate availability, offsetting purchase cost for many buyers

Cons

  • 75 dB noise level is noticeably louder than premium variable-speed competitors running at 65-70 dB
  • 10-year warranty requires online registration, which some contractors find inconvenient
  • R410A refrigerant is phase-out bound, though service availability remains strong through 2030

Who Should Buy

Best For

Homeowners in zones 4-7 who prioritize efficiency gains over silent operation, and contractors installing systems for budget-conscious clients. This unit works best in moderate climates where two-stage cooling actually cycles down regularly.

Consider Alternatives If

Anyone in a noise-sensitive environment like a bedroom window unit installation, or buyers seeking variable-speed compressors that modulate continuously for maximum efficiency.

Installation

Installation is contractor-friendly with standard sweat connections using 3/8 inch liquid and 7/8 inch suction lines. The unit weighs 331 pounds, manageable for two installers with basic equipment. Dimensions are compact at 35.5 by 35.5 by 38.25 inches, fitting most outdoor pad footprints. Breaker sizing runs 37.2 to 60 amps depending on voltage and local code, standard for 5-ton systems. No exotic refrigerant or wiring configurations.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this unit with a 10-year parts warranty, activated by free online registration within 60 days of purchase. Registration takes five minutes and protects you against compressor failure, which is the most expensive failure point. Goodman's claim processing is generally fast, though you must work through an authorized dealer. Coverage excludes labor, so factor service call costs into your ownership budget.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 16 SEER the same as SEER2? +
No. This unit is rated SEER1, the old standard. SEER2 ratings are 15-20 percent lower numerically due to stricter testing. A 16 SEER1 unit roughly equals 12-13 SEER2. Goodman has not yet released SEER2 data for this model.
Can I pair this with an air handler from another brand? +
Technically yes, but Goodman strongly recommends matching components for warranty coverage. Mixing brands like pairing this condenser with a Lennox handler voids manufacturer support. Use a Goodman air handler rated for 5-ton capacity.
How much will this save on my electric bill? +
Savings depend on your climate and current system. In zone 5, upgrading from a 10 SEER unit to this 16 SEER model typically saves 15-25 percent annually on cooling costs. Exact figures require an energy audit of your home and local electricity rates.
Is this better than the Rheem or Trane equivalent? +
Comparable units from Rheem or Trane cost 500-800 dollars more for similar specs. This Goodman offers identical core technology, scroll compressor, and two-stage operation. You're paying slightly less for equivalent performance without brand prestige.
Do I need the online registration for the warranty? +
Yes. Registration is free and required within 60 days to activate the 10-year coverage. Without registration, coverage defaults to 5 years. Ask your installer to handle it before leaving the job.
