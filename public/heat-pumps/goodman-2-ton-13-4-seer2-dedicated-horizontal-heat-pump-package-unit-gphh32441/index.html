Review

The Goodman GPHH32441 is a 2-ton packaged heat pump designed for horizontal mounting in tight spaces, replacing the older GPH1424H41 model. At 3,705 dollars, it positions itself as a value-conscious option for contractors and DIY-minded homeowners. This single-stage system delivers 24,400 BTU of cooling capacity and 22,800 BTU of heating capability, targeting applications where a mid-sized unit makes sense without stretching budgets.

The unit achieves 13.4 SEER2 cooling efficiency and 8 HSPF heating efficiency, landing squarely within current AHRI standards (certificate 208842404). The EEM blower motor reduces parasitic energy losses during operation. Cooling power reaches 24,400 BTU, while heating output hits 22,800 BTU using R410A refrigerant. Single-stage design means the compressor runs at full capacity or off, sacrificing modulation precision for mechanical simplicity and lower manufacturing cost.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 13.4 SEER2 efficient enough for today's standards? +
Yes. The 13.4 SEER2 rating meets 2023 federal minimums and qualifies for most state and utility rebates. It's not a premium 15+ SEER2 unit, but delivers solid efficiency-to-cost balance. Expect energy bills 10 to 15 percent lower than older equipment, depending on climate and usage patterns.
Can this unit fit in my attic if space is tight? +
The horizontal design is specifically built for attics and tight spaces. At 66 inches wide and 34 inches deep, measure your available space carefully. Height is 30 inches, so ensure you have adequate clearance. This configuration beats vertical units in confined installations.
How does 8 HSPF heating performance compare in cold climates? +
An 8 HSPF rating is baseline for modern heat pumps and works well in zones 4 and 5. In zone 3 or colder, you'll benefit from the optional 5 or 10 kW electric backup heat to reduce switching to resistance during extreme cold snaps and maintain comfort.
Why is this cheaper than units with similar BTU ratings? +
Single-stage compression costs less to manufacture than two-stage or variable-capacity designs. Goodman prioritizes affordability here without cutting reliability. You trade modulation precision for lower upfront cost, which makes sense for many residential applications where peak capacity suffices.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
The warranty covers parts defects and component failures when you register online. It excludes routine maintenance, refrigerant charges, labor, and wear items. Registration is required and takes minutes. This is standard industry protection for packaged equipment.
