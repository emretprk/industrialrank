Review

Goodman's 4-ton horizontal system sits in the practical middle of the residential HVAC market. At $2071, it competes directly with mid-tier Carrier and Lennox units while undercutting many premium brands. This horizontal configuration suits basements and crawlspaces where vertical space is limited. The system ships as a matched pair, meaning the furnace and heat pump are engineered together for reliability. Online Supply's SKU LS381183850 carries an AHRI certificate (204633307), confirming the coil and compressor combination meets industry standards.

Cooling performance shines with SEER 16 efficiency and 48,000 BTU capacity across 4 tons, translating to roughly 15 to 20 percent lower AC costs than 13 SEER baseline units. The scroll compressor with two-stage condenser modulates cooling in mild weather, cutting unnecessary runtime. On heat, the 80,000 BTU furnace delivers 48,000 BTU at 80% AFUE, using both natural gas and propane. Two burner stages prevent energy waste during shoulder seasons. The variable-speed blower motor ranges from 0 to 2000 CFM, matching airflow to actual demand and improving humidity control.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the real-world difference between SEER 16 and SEER 13? +
SEER 16 systems use approximately 23 percent less cooling energy annually than SEER 13. Over 10 years, this translates to roughly $1200 to $1800 in electricity savings depending on regional rates and runtime. The SEER improvement narrows in cold climates where AC usage is minimal.
Can I install this in an upflow configuration instead of horizontal? +
No. This unit is manufactured for horizontal operation only, per specification. Upflow requires a different cabinet design and blower orientation. Attempting upflow installation voids warranty and may cause operational failures due to improper refrigerant flow and oil return.
How efficient is the 80% AFUE furnace compared to high-efficiency alternatives? +
Eighty percent AFUE is industry minimum for legal sale. Ninety-five percent AFUE systems extract more heat from exhaust, saving 15 percent per heating season. In northern climates, upgrading to 95% AFUE pays back in 5 to 7 years. In moderate climates, the payback extends beyond equipment lifespan.
How does this Goodman compare to Carrier Performance series at similar price? +
Goodman edges ahead on SEER rating (16 versus 15) and price ($2071 versus typical $2400). Carrier offers longer warranty and better dealer support in some regions. Both use scroll compressors and variable-speed blowers. The choice depends on local service availability and installer preference.
Does the 10-year warranty cover labor and refrigerant? +
Parts only. Goodman covers compressor, coils, and components. You pay labor for service calls and refrigerant charges, typically $300 to $800 per visit. Extended service plans from retailers can add labor coverage for an additional cost.
