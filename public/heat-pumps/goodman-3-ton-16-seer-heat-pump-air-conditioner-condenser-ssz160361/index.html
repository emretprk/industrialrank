Review

The Goodman SSZ160361 represents the brand's practical approach to residential heat pump design. At 3 tons with 36,000 BTU capacity, this unit targets homeowners and contractors seeking dependable heating and cooling without navigating premium feature sets. Priced at $3,030, it occupies the middle ground between budget basement models and high-efficiency systems. Goodman's manufacturing reputation and straightforward engineering make this a recurring choice in the replacement market where reliability matters more than cutting-edge features.

Performance metrics show respectable efficiency credentials. The 16 SEER1 rating meaningfully outpaces older 13 SEER systems, translating to measurable monthly energy bill reductions. Both heating and cooling deliver identical 36,000 BTU output, ensuring balanced seasonal performance. The scroll compressor operates at 71 dB, moderately audible but acceptable for most residential settings. Electrical requirements stay straightforward at 208-230V single-phase with 18.6-30 amp breaker range, simplifying installation in standard residential panels.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 16 SEER1 compare to my current system's efficiency? +
If replacing equipment older than 10 years, you'll see 15-25 percent energy consumption reduction. Older 10 SEER systems waste substantial energy; 16 SEER delivers measurable savings. Monthly electric bills typically drop $30-60 depending on climate and usage patterns. Calculate your specific savings using your utility's rate structure and current consumption baseline.
Is this unit compatible with my existing ductwork and electrical panel? +
Standard 3-ton systems fit most residential ductwork designed for comparable equipment. Electrical compatibility requires 208-230V single-phase service with available breaker space for 18.6-30 amps. Have a technician verify your panel capacity before purchasing. Ductwork sizing assessment prevents efficiency loss from undersized systems.
Why isn't this Energy Star certified if it has 16 SEER efficiency? +
Energy Star standards require additional specifications beyond SEER ratings, including HSPF heating efficiency and regional performance criteria. This unit meets solid baseline efficiency but doesn't clear the premium certification threshold. Your utility may offer partial rebates even without Energy Star designation, so check local programs.
How does Goodman's single-stage compressor compare to variable-speed models? +
Single-stage runs at full capacity or off, less precise but more durable and lower-cost. Variable-speed systems modulate output for tighter comfort control and 2-3 percent better efficiency in moderate weather. Goodman's approach suits consistent climates; variable systems excel in extreme temperature regions.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Full parts and labor coverage for manufacturing defects including compressor failure, refrigerant leaks, and component breakdowns. Online registration required for activation. Warranty excludes normal wear, installation errors, and improper maintenance. Review the complete documentation for specific limits.
