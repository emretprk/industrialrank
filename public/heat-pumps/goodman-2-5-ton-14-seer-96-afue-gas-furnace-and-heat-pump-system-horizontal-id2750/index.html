Review

The Goodman ACIQ-30-EHPD-UPG represents a straightforward approach to dual-fuel heating and cooling. This gas furnace and heat pump combination targets homeowners who need serious heating capacity without sacrificing cooling performance. With a focus on efficiency rather than premium features, it competes in the middle tier of residential HVAC systems, offering reliable operation at a reasonable price point for contractors stocking inventory.

Performance metrics are solid across the board. The furnace delivers 80,000 BTU of heating output with an impressive 96% AFUE rating, meaning nearly all fuel converts to usable heat. Cooling provides 30,000 BTU at 14 SEER1 efficiency and carries Energy Star certification. The scroll compressor operates as a single stage, so it runs at full capacity rather than modulating with demand. The multi-speed ECM blower motor reaches 1200 CFM maximum and intelligently adjusts to match seasonal needs, avoiding the energy waste of constant-speed models.

Installation

This unit requires standard 115V, single-phase power with a breaker sized between 18.7 and 30 amps, making it compatible with most existing home electrical service. Suction and liquid lines connect via sweat fittings (3/4-inch and 3/8-inch respectively), and the system uses R454B refrigerant. The plastic PVC flue exhausts to the right side. Weight of 120 pounds requires two technicians for safe handling in tight attic or basement spaces.

Warranty & Reliability

Coverage includes a lifetime heat exchanger warranty, 10-year unit replacement guarantee, and 10-year parts coverage when you register online. This three-tier structure protects against major component failures during the critical first decade of ownership. Online registration is required to activate parts coverage, so don't overlook this step at installation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the product say 2.5 tons but the specs show 3 tons? +
This appears to be a data entry error in the specifications. The tonnage field shows 3 tons while the product title claims 2.5 tons. Verify with the manufacturer using SKU ACIQ-30-EHPD-UPG, as tonnage determines system sizing. A 30,000 BTU cooling output equates to approximately 2.5 tons, suggesting the title is correct and the tonnage spec may be mislabeled.
Will this system fit in my attic with limited headspace? +
The horizontal configuration measures 34.5 inches tall, 17.5 inches wide, and 28.75 inches deep, making it suitable for tight spaces. However, you'll need clearance for the 50-foot suction line insulation and PVC flue venting to the right side. Have your installer verify attic framing and ductwork routing before ordering to avoid installation problems.
How much will this save me on heating bills compared to a standard 80% AFUE furnace? +
A 96% AFUE unit recovers 16 percentage points more fuel energy than an 80% model. On a $1,200 annual heating bill, you'd save roughly $192 per year. Over a 15-year lifespan, that totals nearly $2,900 in fuel savings, plus the lifetime heat exchanger warranty justifies the higher initial investment.
Is single-stage cooling adequate, or should I upgrade to a two-stage compressor? +
Single-stage works fine in steady-state heating and cooling but wastes energy during spring and fall when partial loads occur. Two-stage compressors modulate capacity and deliver 15-20% better efficiency in shoulder seasons. If you live in a true four-season climate with mild springs and falls, a two-stage system provides better annual efficiency.
What does online registration for warranty coverage involve? +
You'll register your system with Goodman using the model and serial number within a specified window (typically 30-60 days of installation). Registration unlocks the full 10-year parts coverage. Without it, you're limited to the 10-year unit replacement only, losing parts protection. Keep your documentation and registration confirmation safe.
