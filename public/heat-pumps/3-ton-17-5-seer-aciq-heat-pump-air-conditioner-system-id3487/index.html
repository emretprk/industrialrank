Review

The ACiQ 3-ton heat pump represents a practical middle ground in the split system market. Priced at $660, this Online Supply unit targets contractors and budget-conscious homeowners seeking reliable performance without premium pricing. It delivers 36,000 BTU cooling and 34,000 BTU heating capacity, making it suitable for most residential applications in moderate climates. The variable-speed compressor and communicating capabilities elevate it beyond stripped-down budget options, while the 10-year warranty provides genuine consumer protection.

Performance metrics show solid efficiency credentials. The 17.5 SEER rating qualifies it as Energy Star certified, and the variable-speed scroll compressor achieves an EER range of 9.5 to 13, meaning the unit adjusts its output based on demand rather than cycling on and off. Maximum CFM reaches 2,500, powered by a 0.333 HP variable-speed motor running at 1,050 RPM. Single-phase 208/230/240V electrical compatibility and a 40-amp maximum breaker requirement simplify installation in most homes.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does 17.5 SEER actually mean for my monthly cooling costs? +
SEER 17.5 means the unit removes 17.5 BTU of heat per watt-hour of electricity consumed. Compared to older 10 SEER units, expect 40 to 50 percent lower cooling costs. Actual savings depend on your climate, usage patterns, and local electricity rates. Your contractor can estimate savings based on your specific location.
Can I install this myself, or do I need a licensed technician? +
EPA regulations require EPA-certified technicians to handle refrigerant work, including charging this R410A system. You cannot legally self-install. Hiring a licensed HVAC contractor ensures proper charging, ductwork balancing, and warranty validity. Installation costs typically range from $1,500 to $2,500 depending on your region and ductwork condition.
How does variable-speed operation actually save energy compared to single-stage units? +
Variable-speed compressors modulate capacity to match demand rather than cycling on and off repeatedly. This continuous operation at partial load reduces energy waste and improves efficiency. The scroll compressor achieves higher EER ratings (9.5 to 13) at varying speeds, especially during partial-load conditions typical of spring and fall.
How does this ACiQ unit compare to equivalent Carrier or Tempstar models? +
ACiQ is Carrier's value brand. This unit replaces FCM4X4800AL and matches Carrier KFCEH2601C10 and Tempstar EHK10AKB specifications. Expect identical performance and reliability. The main difference is price, with ACiQ typically costing 15 to 25 percent less than equivalent Carrier-branded units sold through traditional channels.
What backup heating options are available if my heat pump can't keep up in winter? +
Optional electric resistance heat strips range from 5 to 30 kilowatts. A 10 kW strip suits most 3-ton systems in moderate climates. Your installer will size based on your winter heating demands and desired temperature recovery speed. Larger strips cost more but heat faster during extreme cold.
