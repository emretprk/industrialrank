Review

The ACiQ 5-ton heat pump is a straightforward, competent air conditioning and heating solution built on Goodman's established platform. Positioned in the mid-efficiency tier, this horizontal A-coil unit delivers reliable performance without cutting-edge technology costs. It's designed for contractors who value predictability and homeowners seeking practical efficiency gains over their existing systems. The 57,000 BTU cooling capacity and 58,000 BTU heating output suit most single-family residential applications across moderate climate zones.

The 14.5 SEER1 efficiency rating represents solid modern performance, though it reflects legacy SEER1 measurement rather than the newer SEER2 standard used on premium units. The variable-speed ECM blower motor is the efficiency workhorse here, automatically adjusting CFM from partial to 2,000 maximum based on demand. This modulation reduces wasted fan energy during low-load periods. Backup heat options range from 3 kW to 20 kW, providing flexibility for retrofit installations. The scroll compressor handles single-stage cooling with adequate capacity and proven durability in field service.

Installation

Installation requires standard refrigerant line sizing, 3/8 inch liquid line and 1 1/8 inch suction line with 1/2 inch insulation thickness. The unit ships with 50 feet of pre-insulated lineset, covering most residential runs. Electrical demands range 208 to 230V single-phase 60Hz, with minimum 34.6 amp breaker and maximum 60 amp capacity. Horizontal placement suits attic mounting, requiring adequate clearance for airflow and access for future service.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this unit with a 10-year parts warranty activated through online registration. This coverage is solid for the price point, though not exceptional compared to some competitors offering lifetime compressor warranties. Registration is straightforward and strongly recommended to activate full protection. Coverage applies to factory defects, not installation errors or refrigerant loss from field work.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the 14.5 SEER1 rating actually mean for my electric bill? +
SEER1 measures cooling efficiency; 14.5 means 14.5 BTU of cooling per watt-hour consumed. For this 57,000 BTU unit, expect approximately 3,900 watts input at peak load. Real savings depend on runtime hours, thermostat settings, and local electricity rates. Variable-speed operation improves part-load efficiency beyond the SEER rating.
Is this unit compatible with my existing ductwork and indoor coil? +
This replaces model GSZ140611 and works with matching Goodman indoor A-coils rated for 5-ton systems. The 3/8 inch and 1 1/8 inch line sizes are industry standard. Verify your indoor coil is compatible before purchase, and ensure ductwork design supports 2,000 CFM maximum airflow without excessive static pressure.
How does single-stage cooling affect comfort and efficiency? +
Single-stage operation runs at full capacity or off, without intermediate levels. This suits steady-state cooling but causes more frequent short cycles in mild weather. Two-stage units provide smoother control and lower run percentages. For moderate climates with variable temperatures, the variable-speed blower helps compensate by modulating airflow rather than cycling the compressor.
How does this compare to newer SEER2-rated heat pumps? +
SEER2 uses stricter testing conditions and typically rates 15-20% lower numerically than SEER1. A comparable SEER2 unit might show 11.5 to 12.0, though real-world efficiency is similar. Newer units often include two-stage compressors and variable-speed components for better modulation. SEER2 units often qualify for larger utility rebates.
Does online registration really matter for the warranty? +
Yes, registration is required to activate the full 10-year coverage. Without it, coverage may default to shorter periods. Registration is free and takes minutes through Goodman's website. Keep proof of registration with your documentation in case claims arise.
