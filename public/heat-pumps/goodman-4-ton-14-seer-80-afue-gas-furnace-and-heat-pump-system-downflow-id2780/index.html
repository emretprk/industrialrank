Review

The ACiQ 4-ton downflow system bridges heat pump and furnace technology for contractors seeking affordable dual-fuel solutions. This unit pairs a 14 SEER1 cooling coil with a 100,000 BTU two-stage gas furnace, delivering year-round conditioning without requiring separate equipment. At 120 pounds and 34.5 inches tall, it fits tight equipment closets common in downflow installations. The system directly replaces the older Goodman GSZ140491, simplifying retrofit work and keeping compatibility within the Goodman ecosystem.

Performance specs reveal a capable but conventional design. The 46,000 BTU cooling capacity pairs with a single-stage scroll compressor running at 14 SEER1, meeting Energy Star requirements without cutting-edge efficiency. The two-stage gas burner operates at 80% AFUE, a baseline efficiency that keeps costs low but sacrifices the 10-15% savings offered by condensing furnaces. Electrical demands stay modest, requiring 26.4 to 40 amp breakers at 120V single-phase. The multi-speed blower motor handles up to 2,000 CFM, supporting flexible ductwork configurations.

Pros & Cons

  • Dual-fuel configuration delivers 100,000 BTU heating via two-stage gas burner plus 46,000 BTU heat pump cooling
  • R454B refrigerant aligns with EPA phase-down requirements for future compliance
  • Multi-speed blower motor optimizes comfort and reduces runtime noise at 74 dBA

Cons

Installation

Downflow orientation suits attic or closet placement where return air flows from above. Requires metal exhaust flue, 3/8 inch liquid line, and 1 1/8 inch suction line with 1/2 inch insulation up to 50 feet. Sweat valve connections demand basic soldering skills or professional labor. Electrical integration involves standard 120V controls, manageable for experienced installers but requiring licensed work in most jurisdictions.

Warranty & Reliability

ACiQ provides 10-year coverage when registered online, protecting against compressor and furnace failure during the critical ownership window. This registration-based structure means buyers must document purchase within specified timeframes. Coverage typically excludes wear items, refrigerant, and service labor, so maintenance records matter for claim validation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does this 4-ton unit list 5-ton tonnage in specifications? +
The 46,000 BTU cooling output rounds to 5-ton capacity, while the 100,000 BTU heating and system application define it as 4-ton equipment. Manufacturers sometimes reference tonnage by cooling BTU alone. Always verify capacity matches your home's calculated load rather than relying solely on tonnage labels.
Can I install this myself, or do I need a licensed technician? +
Gas furnace work requires state-licensed HVAC and gas fitters in most jurisdictions, plus EPA certification for refrigerant handling. While handy homeowners might manage ductwork connections, hiring professionals ensures code compliance, safety, and warranty validity. Installation complexity justifies professional labor costs.
How does 14 SEER1 compare to modern heat pumps available today? +
14 SEER1 meets current minimum Energy Star standards but trails emerging 16-18 SEER2 units by 15-25% in cooling efficiency. For climates requiring extended heat pump operation, higher SEER ratings yield faster payback. The 80% AFUE furnace limits overall system efficiency in heating-dominant regions, favoring newer units above 95% AFUE.
What are realistic operating costs versus a newer 18 SEER system? +
Cooling energy consumption runs roughly 28% higher on this 14 SEER unit versus 18 SEER equivalent, translating to $200-400 additional annual costs in moderate climates. Gas heating at 80% AFUE also trails 95% condensing units by $100-200 yearly in heating seasons. Lower upfront cost offsets 3-5 years of efficiency losses.
Does online warranty registration actually protect my investment? +
Yes, but only if completed within the specified timeframe. Registration validates purchase date and serial number, essential for claim processing. Missing registration voids coverage, so complete it immediately upon installation. Keep documentation for reference during any future service needs.
