Review

The Goodman 4 Ton 16 SEER heat pump system occupies the practical middle ground of the residential HVAC market. With a 16 SEER rating and two-stage compressor design, it targets homeowners and contractors who value steady performance over cutting-edge technology. The horizontal A-coil configuration suits attic or crawlspace installation where space is limited. At this price point, you're getting a variable-speed system that competes directly with units from Lennox and Carrier that cost significantly more.

Performance specs show 48,000 BTU cooling and heating capacity with a scroll compressor that ramps between stages based on demand. The variable-speed ECM blower delivers up to 2,000 CFM while using 25% less electrical energy than fixed-speed motors. At 16 SEER1, this unit should reduce summer cooling costs by 15-20% compared to 13 SEER baseline models. The two-stage design means the compressor runs at partial capacity 70-80% of the time, reducing wear and noise during mild weather.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 16 SEER rating qualifies for Energy Star, reducing annual cooling costs by 15-20% versus baseline units
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor adjusts output from 0 to 2,000 CFM, improving comfort and reducing runtime noise
  • Two-stage condenser prevents short-cycling and extends compressor lifespan in moderate outdoor temperatures
  • Flexible electric backup heat from 3 kW to 20 kW lets you match your existing furnace or add supplemental heating
  • 10-year warranty with online registration covers parts and labor on most components

Cons

  • Spec sheet lists both 4 Ton and 5 Ton tonnage, creating confusion about actual capacity and system matching

  • R410A or R22 refrigerant compatibility suggests older design that may become costlier as R22 phases out

Installation

Installation requires standard 208/230V single-phase power with a 27.7 amp minimum breaker, common in most residential homes. Refrigerant lines include 3/8 inch liquid and 1 1/8 inch suction connections with 1/2 inch insulation thickness and 50 feet maximum line length. Sweat-style valve fittings demand soldering skill or professional installation. Most contractors complete installation in 4-6 hours after ductwork verification.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides 10 years parts and labor coverage on this unit when you register online, which takes five minutes. The warranty covers the compressor, condenser coil, and blower assembly. Labor coverage at your home makes this stronger than mail-in warranty programs. Extended warranties are available but rarely necessary with proper maintenance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this a true 4 Ton or 5 Ton unit, since specs list both? +
The spec sheet shows conflicting information. Contact the seller or Goodman directly for clarification before purchasing. This discrepancy affects system matching and capacity calculations. Ensure your evaporator coil tonnage matches whatever you confirm here.
Can I install this myself if I have HVAC experience? +
Soldering the refrigerant lines and balancing the system requires EPA certification and specialized gauges. Most jurisdictions require licensed technician installation for warranty validity. DIY installation risks voiding coverage and creating safety hazards.
How does 16 SEER compare to newer SEER2 ratings? +
16 SEER1 roughly equals 12-13 SEER2 under new testing standards. Don't compare this directly to new units rated in SEER2. For accurate efficiency comparison, convert both to the same standard or ask your contractor for real-world cooling costs.
Should I choose this over a single-stage Goodman unit at lower cost? +
The two-stage design and variable-speed blower justify the premium through lower utility bills and quieter operation. Over 15 years, you'll recover the price difference in energy savings while enjoying better comfort. Single-stage units cost less upfront but run louder.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover after I register? +
Parts and labor on the compressor, condenser, and blower motor. Refrigerant lines and electrical components have shorter coverage. Read the full warranty document before purchase to confirm what's excluded.
