Review

The Mitsubishi 36,000 BTU quad zone mini split system represents a premium solution for multi-zone HVAC applications. With four independent indoor units controlling 9,000, 9,000, 12,000, and 15,000 BTU capacities respectively, this system addresses complex residential layouts where standard single or dual zone solutions fall short. The wall-mounted configuration and included wireless controller make it a practical choice for both new construction and retrofit applications.

Performance metrics confirm this system's efficiency credentials. The 23 SEER1 cooling rating places it in the top tier of residential mini splits, translating to measurable energy savings over a system's 15-year lifespan. Heating efficiency reaches 12.5 HSPF in moderate conditions, with capability down to 5 degrees outdoor temperature. The 15 EER rating demonstrates strong peak load performance. Operating on 208/230V single phase with a 30-40 amp breaker requirement, installation fits standard residential electrical infrastructure.

Installation

Installation demands professional expertise. The system requires four separate wall-mounted indoor units with individual refrigerant lines, electrical connections, and condensate drainage at 5/8 inches. Line sets extend 30 feet maximum. Single-phase power simplifies electrical requirements compared to three-phase systems. The wireless controller eliminates complex thermostat wiring. Proper evacuation and refrigerant charging with R410A demands EPA certification.

Coverage includes five years of parts warranty, providing reasonable protection against component failure. The terms apply across all four indoor units and the outdoor condenser. Five-year coverage is industry standard but relatively conservative, considering mini split systems often operate reliably for 15-20 years with proper maintenance. Frequently Asked Questions

Can I control each zone independently with this system? +
Yes, the quad zone design provides completely independent control across all four spaces. Each zone operates on its own 9,000-15,000 BTU capacity with individual wireless thermostat control. This allows different rooms to maintain different temperatures simultaneously without compromise or cross-communication between zones.
What electrical infrastructure do I need for installation? +
The system requires 208/230V single-phase 60 Hz service with a 30-40 amp breaker. Unlike three-phase commercial units, this fits standard residential electrical panels. You'll need dedicated circuits for stable operation, and a licensed electrician should handle connections to ensure code compliance and safety.
How does the 23 SEER1 efficiency rating translate to actual savings? +
23 SEER1 means the system delivers 23 BTU of cooling per watt of electricity consumed. Running 2,000 cooling hours annually, a mid-efficiency unit costs roughly 30-40% more to operate. Over 15 years, this efficiency advantage saves homeowners $2,500-4,000 in electricity costs depending on local rates.
How does this compare to running four separate single-zone mini splits? +
Four individual mini splits offer identical zoning but require four separate outdoor condenser units consuming more space and creating more noise. A quad zone system uses one outdoor unit, reducing noise from 19-43 dB to a single source and simplifying maintenance while delivering comparable efficiency.
What's covered under the five-year warranty? +
Parts coverage includes all four indoor units, the outdoor condenser, and electrical components for five years from installation. Verify compressor-specific protection details with your supplier, as coverage specifics sometimes vary between manufacturers and distributors.
