Review

The Goodman 1.5-ton system with 80,000 BTU furnace occupies a practical middle ground in the HVAC market. At $2,666, it undercuts premium manufacturers while delivering respectable efficiency ratings: 96% AFUE and 14 SEER. This upflow A-coil configuration fits standard closet installations, and the dual-fuel design accepts both natural gas and propane, differentiating it from competitors locked into single fuel sources.

Performance metrics reveal solid engineering. The 96% AFUE rating means only 4 BTU per 100 are lost as exhaust, versus 8 BTU at 92% AFUE. The 14 SEER rating (18,000 cooling BTU output) qualifies for Energy Star status and regional utility rebates. Variable-speed ECM blower runs at reduced speed during partial-load conditions, lowering electricity draw compared to fixed-speed alternatives. Two-stage burner operation maintains modulation during mild weather, though the single-stage compressor cannot reciprocate this efficiency advantage on cooling cycles.

Installation

Installation requires 115V single-phase 60Hz power with 11.8 to 20-amp breaker compatibility, standard in most homes. Sweat connections use 3/8-inch liquid lines and 3/4-inch suction lines with 3/4-inch insulation thickness. PVC plastic exhaust flue is required. The 34.5-inch height, 17.5-inch width, and 28.75-inch depth fit tight closet spaces. Suction line extends 50 feet maximum, accommodating split systems in larger homes.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides 10-year parts warranty with online registration. This is industry standard, neither exceptional nor weak. Registration typically takes 15 minutes online and activates coverage immediately. Ten years covers most component failures and provides peace of mind for homeowners planning 15-plus-year ownership. Some competitors offer lifetime heat exchanger coverage, which Goodman does not highlight here.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does 96% AFUE actually mean in dollar savings? +
AFUE measures heating efficiency. At 96%, $100 spent on fuel produces $96 of heat. At 92%, only $92 converts to usable heat. Over a five-year heating cycle in cold climates, the 4% difference saves roughly $300-500 on gas bills. Savings scale with local gas prices and heating degree days.
Can I install this system myself or do I need a licensed contractor? +
Most jurisdictions require EPA Section 608 certification for refrigerant handling, which prohibits DIY installation. Licensed contractors understand sweat connections, pressure testing, and evacuation procedures. Professional installation also preserves warranty coverage. Budget $1,200-2,000 for labor in addition to the $2,666 equipment cost.
Why is tonnage listed as 3 tons when the system is 1.5 tons? +
This appears to be a spec discrepancy. The 18,000 BTU cooling output equals 1.5 tons (12,000 BTU per ton). The 3-ton listing may reference condenser size or refer to a different product variant. Verify capacity with the AHRI certificate number 7368834 before purchase to confirm actual specifications.
How does this compare to a Carrier or Lennox 14 SEER system? +
Goodman uses identical refrigerant (R410A) and achieves equal efficiency ratings. Carrier and Lennox charge 20-30% premiums for brand recognition and local support networks. Goodman parts are equally available through HVAC supply houses. Real-world performance differences are negligible, making Goodman better value for budget-conscious buyers.
Is the 10-year warranty enough protection? +
Ten years covers most component failures within normal lifespan. Heat exchangers typically last 15-20 years, so extended warranties aren't critical unless financing beyond year 10. Register online immediately after installation to activate full coverage and keep proof of purchase.
