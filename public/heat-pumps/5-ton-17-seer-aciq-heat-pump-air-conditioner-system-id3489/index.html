Review

The ACiQ 5 ton 17 SEER2 heat pump represents strong value in the mid-market residential split system category. Manufactured to Carrier and Bryant equivalent specifications, this unit delivers modern variable-speed efficiency without the brand premium. At $3,166 fully installed cost, it positions well against OEM-branded competitors while meeting Energy Star certification requirements and maintaining communicating system compatibility with contemporary thermostats.

Performance specs show a unit built for moderate climates. The 60,000 BTU cooling capacity handles typical 5-ton residential loads, while 17 SEER2 efficiency translates to measurable utility bill reductions compared to 13-14 SEER equipment. Variable-speed scroll compressor and matching condenser provide EER ratings between 9.5-13 depending on conditions. The 0.75 HP variable-speed blower modulates 300-1300 RPM, preventing overshooting and reducing cycling losses that plague fixed-speed equipment.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 17 SEER2 compare to older SEER1 ratings on this unit? +
This unit carries an 18 SEER1 rating, which roughly converts to 17 SEER2 using EPA's newer testing standard. SEER2 uses more rigorous conditions including lower outdoor temperatures, making the 17 rating genuinely strong for heat pump performance. Real-world efficiency typically tracks within 5-10 percent of rated values.
What electric backup heat options exist for cold climates? +
Auxiliary heat strips available in 5, 8, 10, 15, 20, 25, or 30 kW configurations. The 10 kW option balances cost and performance for most installations. Larger capacities increase first cost but reduce auxiliary runtime in heating-dominated climates, improving overall system efficiency.
Is this unit compatible with existing Carrier or Bryant installations? +
Yes. The ACiQ replaces FCM4X6000AL and matches Carrier KFCEH2601C10, Tempstar EHK10AKB, and Bryant KFCEH2601C10 specifications. Existing lineset connections, electrical sizing, and mounting requirements align with these equivalents for straightforward replacement scenarios.
How does the variable-speed operation affect homeowner comfort? +
Variable-speed compressors and blowers operate more often at lower capacity, maintaining temperature setpoints with fewer temperature swings compared to fixed-speed units. This produces steadier comfort and reduced thermostat hunting. Most homeowners notice quieter operation and more consistent conditioning.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
No-hassle replacement warranty covers defects in materials and workmanship. Registration requirement is simple online process. Coverage typically excludes installation labor and damage from improper service or environmental factors, but parts replacement under warranty is straightforward.
