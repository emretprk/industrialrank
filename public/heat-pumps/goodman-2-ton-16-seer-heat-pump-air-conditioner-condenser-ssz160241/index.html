Review

The Goodman 2-ton heat pump is a workhorse unit that balances performance, cost, and installation simplicity. At $2,436 with 16 SEER1 efficiency, it occupies a practical middle ground between entry-level equipment and premium systems. The 2-ton capacity serves smaller homes, apartments, and retrofit projects where oversizing isn't necessary. Goodman's reputation for straightforward design over innovative features makes this unit popular with regional HVAC contractors who value serviceability and parts availability.

The specifications reveal a traditionally engineered heat pump built for reliability rather than sophistication. Both heating and cooling output sit at 24,000 BTU, delivering adequate capacity for moderate temperature swings. The 72 dB noise level is acceptable but not exceptional, particularly during compressor-on cycles. Scroll compressor architecture, 208/230V single-phase electrical compatibility, and 17.9 to 30-amp breaker tolerance integrate smoothly into existing residential infrastructure without costly panel upgrades.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 16 SEER1 compare to newer SEER2 ratings I see advertised? +
SEER1 and SEER2 use different test conditions. This unit's 16 SEER1 roughly translates to approximately 13-13.5 SEER2 under newer, more realistic testing. Comparisons between units require matching rating standards, not treating them interchangeably. Always ask manufacturers for equivalent SEER2 values when shopping.
Can my existing air handler work with this condenser unit? +
Compatibility depends on your current system's tonnage, refrigerant type, and electrical configuration. This unit uses R410A refrigerant, so older R22 air handlers need replacing. Your HVAC technician should verify tonnage matching and perform load calculations. Mixing equipment generations sometimes works but reduces efficiency and warranty coverage.
Why isn't this Energy Star certified if it's 16 SEER? +
Energy Star requires higher efficiency thresholds than raw SEER ratings. This unit meets baseline 16 SEER specifications but falls below Energy Star minimums, likely due to seasonal heating efficiency or sound ratings. Missing certification typically costs $300-500 in foregone rebates in program regions.
How does the single-stage compressor affect monthly heating and cooling bills? +
Single-stage units run at full output or off, lacking intermediate efficiency stages. In moderate weather, a multi-stage unit modulates capacity downward, saving energy. You'll notice higher electric costs during mild spring and fall months. Cold winters and hot summers see less efficiency difference compared to variable-capacity competitors.
What maintenance does this unit require annually? +
Standard heat pump maintenance includes checking refrigerant charge, cleaning condenser coils, inspecting electrical connections, and replacing air filter every 90 days during heavy use. Annual professional service runs $150-250. The scroll compressor design is relatively low-maintenance compared to rotary types.
