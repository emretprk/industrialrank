How to Choose

Flow rate sizing: the calculation most buyers skip

The critical sizing number is simultaneous flow demand, not total household size. Two showers at 2.0 GPM each plus a dishwasher at 1.5 GPM requires a unit rated for at least 5.5 GPM. Groundwater temperature also affects available output — a unit rated 7.5 GPM at 70°F inlet will deliver less at 45°F. Use the coldest groundwater temperature for your location in your sizing calculation.

Condensing vs non-condensing: the efficiency trade-off

Condensing units (0.90–0.97 EF) recover heat from exhaust gases, requiring PVC venting but achieving 15–20% lower gas bills than non-condensing units. Non-condensing units (0.80–0.87 EF) use stainless steel or aluminum venting but are simpler to install. The efficiency premium on condensing units pays back in approximately 4–7 years for average US households.

Installation requirements that affect total cost

Gas tankless water heaters require a gas line sized for the BTU input — most units need 3/4 inch gas line for the last 10 feet. Venting for non-condensing units uses existing Category III stainless steel flue; condensing units vent through PVC. Some installations require gas line upgrading, which adds $300–$800 to installation cost.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a tankless water heater cost to install?
Whole-home gas tankless water heater installation typically runs $800–$1,500 in labor, plus any gas line or venting modifications. Total installed cost ranges from $1,800–$3,200 for most residential projects. Electric tankless heaters cost less for the unit but often require panel upgrades, which can add $1,000–$2,500.
Do tankless water heaters work in cold climates?
Yes, but flow rate drops as inlet water temperature falls. A unit rated for 7.5 GPM at 70°F inlet delivers approximately 5.0 GPM when the inlet is 40°F. For climate zones with very cold groundwater in winter, size up by at least one GPM tier. Freeze protection is built into all major brands for temperature down to about 20°F; below that, supplemental pipe insulation is needed.
What maintenance does a tankless water heater need?
Annual descaling with a citric acid flush is recommended in areas with hard water (above 11 grains per gallon). The inlet filter screen should be cleaned every 6–12 months. In-line water softener cartridges extend service intervals in very hard water areas. Beyond descaling and filter maintenance, no regular service is needed.
Is a tankless water heater worth the price?
For households using more than 40 gallons per day, tankless units typically provide a positive NPV over a 15-year period compared to tank units. The break-even point is 6–10 years. The non-financial benefit — never running out of hot water — has independent value for large households or homes with high-demand fixtures.
