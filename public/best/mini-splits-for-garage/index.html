How to Choose

Garage sizing is different from house sizing

A garage has high infiltration, large door openings, and often minimal insulation. Add 25–40% to the standard 20-25 BTU/sq ft rule for an attached, uninsulated garage. An insulated and air-sealed garage approaches standard calculation. If the garage has a south-facing wall with no shade, add another 10%. When in doubt, oversize — mini splits are most efficient at moderate load.

Ceiling height and wall mount position

Mini split indoor heads are designed for wall mounting at 7–8 feet height. In a garage with a 10-foot ceiling, a wall-mount unit will condition the air efficiently. For taller ceilings (12+ feet), consider a ceiling-cassette mount to distribute conditioned air more evenly. Most mini splits have a vertical throw range of 15–20 feet.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size mini split for a 2-car garage?
A standard attached 2-car garage of 500–600 sq ft needs 24,000 BTU (2 tons) if insulated, or 30,000 BTU if uninsulated. Single-car garages of 200–300 sq ft typically need 9,000–12,000 BTU. Always size for the heat load — a garage's cooling load is usually lower than its heating load in cold climates.
Can I install a mini split in a garage myself?
With MRCOOL DIY, yes — the pre-charged quick-connect lineset eliminates refrigerant handling. You will need to run a dedicated 240V electrical circuit, which requires basic wiring skills. Other brands require EPA 608 certification for the refrigerant connection. Most jurisdictions require a building permit regardless of which brand you use.
Does a garage mini split need any insulation to work?
A mini split will function in an uninsulated garage, but it will cycle frequently, run at full capacity most of the time, and consume significantly more energy. Insulating the garage door (R-8 kits available for $50–$150), the walls, and the ceiling will cut the unit's run time by 40–60% and dramatically reduce operating costs.
