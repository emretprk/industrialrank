How to Choose

Why consumer pint ratings are misleading for basement use

The DOE switched to a new energy test standard in 2020, but all consumer dehumidifiers are still rated at 80°F/60% RH. Most basements operate at 60–70°F — a temperature where compressor-based dehumidifiers lose 40–60% of rated capacity. A unit labeled 70-pint delivers 30–42 pints in a typical basement. Santa Fe and Aprilaire test at 65°F/60% RH, which reflects real basement conditions.

Drain options: gravity, pump, and hose connection

Consumer dehumidifiers drain by gravity into a bucket or through a hose to a floor drain at or below the unit level. Santa Fe Ultra120 includes an internal condensate pump capable of 16 feet of vertical lift — critical if your floor drain is above the unit mounting point. Pump-equipped units eliminate bucket emptying and allow automatic continuous operation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size dehumidifier do I need for a 1,500 sq ft basement?
At 65°F with moderate moisture, a 1,500 sq ft basement needs approximately 70–90 PPD at real operating conditions. Using Santa Fe's 65°F rating system, that means a Santa Fe Advance2 (70 PPD) or Aprilaire E100 Pro (100 PPD). Using consumer 80°F ratings, you would need a unit labeled 120–150 PPD to get the same real-world performance.
Should I run my basement dehumidifier continuously?
Continuous operation at a set humidity level (45–50% RH for most basements) is the recommended approach. Running the unit only when humidity spikes creates moisture fluctuations that promote mold. Modern whole-home units like Santa Fe and Aprilaire use humidity-sensing controls to modulate operation automatically.
Can a dehumidifier prevent basement mold?
Maintaining relative humidity below 50% eliminates the conditions mold requires. However, dehumidification is one layer of moisture control — not a substitute for fixing water intrusion sources, sealing foundation cracks, or improving drainage. If water is entering as bulk water rather than vapor diffusion, a dehumidifier alone will not solve the problem.
