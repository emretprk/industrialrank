Buying Guide

BTU and Sizing

Mini split sizing is the most common error in the purchase process. The rule of thumb is 600-700 BTU per square foot for a well-insulated space at 8-9 foot ceilings. For poorly insulated spaces or areas with high solar gain, use 900-1,000 BTU per square foot. Oversizing — installing a larger unit than the space requires — causes short-cycling, which reduces efficiency and dehumidification performance significantly. When in doubt, size correctly rather than oversizing for 'extra capacity.'

DIY vs Professional Installation

The primary consideration is refrigerant handling. Standard mini splits require vacuum pump evacuation and refrigerant charging by an EPA 608 certified technician. MRCOOL DIY systems use pre-charged line sets that require no refrigerant handling, making legal homeowner installation possible. For professional installation, budget $300-$800 for single-zone systems and $600-$1,200 for multi-zone systems in addition to unit cost.

SEER2 and Efficiency

SEER2 is the 2023 DOE efficiency standard for air conditioners and heat pumps. The federal minimum is 15 SEER2 in the South and 14.3 SEER2 in the North. Most mini splits in this list achieve 17-27 SEER2. The efficiency difference between 17 SEER2 and 22 SEER2 costs approximately $60-$90/year in electricity for a 12K unit running 1,500 annual hours. The efficiency premium is meaningful for high-use commercial applications; it is secondary to reliability and service access for moderate-use residential applications.

Cold Climate Ratings

Standard mini splits lose heating efficiency below 25°F and typically reach a practical minimum around 0°F. Hyper-Heat technology (Mitsubishi and LG) maintains near-rated capacity to -13°F. For northern climates — particularly climate zones 5 and 6 — Hyper-Heat capability is the correct specification if the system is the primary heat source. For supplemental heating applications where a backup heat source exists, standard inverter systems are adequate.

Warranty and Service

Warranty terms range from 5 years (budget brands) to 7 years (Mitsubishi, LG). Equally important is service network accessibility. Mitsubishi's Diamond Contractor network can service equipment in virtually any U.S. market within 24-48 hours. Budget brands have parts available nationally but fewer trained service technicians. For commercial applications with uptime requirements, Mitsubishi's service infrastructure justifies the price premium.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best mini split for cold climates?

The Mitsubishi MXZ series with Hyper-Heat technology is the definitive cold-climate mini split specification. Rated to -13°F with 85% capacity maintained at 5°F, it outperforms every competing system in cold-weather heating. The LG LS120HEV2 Art Cool also carries a -13°F Hyper Heating rating. For DIY cold-climate applications, the MRCOOL Oasis Hyper Heat is the only pre-charged option with meaningful cold-climate performance.

What size mini split do I need for a 500 sq ft space?

For a 500 sq ft space with standard insulation and 8-foot ceilings in a moderate U.S. climate, a 9,000 BTU (9K) mini split is typically correctly sized. For poorly insulated spaces, sunrooms, or spaces with large window areas, a 12K unit may be appropriate. The Pioneer WB009 9K and Senville LETO 12K are the recommended specifications for this size range.

Can one mini split cool multiple rooms?

A single-zone mini split cools one room directly. For multiple rooms, a multi-zone outdoor unit with separate indoor air handlers in each room provides the correct configuration. The MRCOOL DIY 2-Zone system is the only DIY multi-zone option. Mitsubishi MXZ-3C and MXZ-4C systems are the professional specification for 3-4 room applications.

What is the installed cost of a mini split?

For a single-zone professionally installed mini split: $1,800-$4,500 depending on brand and BTU size. For a MRCOOL DIY system: $1,100-$2,200 including the unit and electrical circuit. For a Mitsubishi 3-zone system installed by a Diamond Contractor: $5,500-$9,000. For a Mitsubishi 4-zone commercial system: $8,000-$16,000.
