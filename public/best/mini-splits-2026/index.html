How to Choose

BTU sizing: the only spec that actually matters first

Undersizing is the most common mini split mistake. The standard rule is 20–25 BTU per square foot for a well-insulated space; add 10% for high ceilings or south-facing glass. Oversizing causes short-cycling, which increases humidity and compressor wear. If you are between sizes, choose the larger unit with a variable-speed compressor — it will modulate down.

SEER2 and what it costs you over ten years

SEER2 replaced SEER as the US efficiency standard in 2023. A unit at 15 SEER2 costs roughly 25% more to run than one at 20 SEER2 at current electricity prices of $0.16/kWh. On a 24K unit running 1,200 hours per year, that difference is approximately $180 annually — meaning the efficiency premium on a 20 SEER2 unit pays back in four to six years.

DIY vs professional installation: real cost breakdown

Professional installation adds $1,500–$3,500 depending on location, lineset length, and whether electrical work is required. MRCOOL DIY eliminates this entirely for straightforward applications. However, professional installation is required by most jurisdictions for whole-home systems and is strongly recommended if the lineset exceeds 25 feet or requires vertical drops.

Cold climate performance: minimum operating temperature

Standard mini splits lose efficiency and capacity below 17°F. Units rated for cold climates — Mitsubishi Hyper-Heat, Daikin Aurora, and MRCOOL's Oasis series — maintain rated capacity to -13°F or below. If your location sees more than 10 nights per year below 20°F, a cold-climate-rated unit is not optional.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best mini split for a 500 sq ft room?
For 500 square feet with standard 8-foot ceilings and adequate insulation, a 12,000 BTU (1-ton) unit is correct. The Mitsubishi MSZ-GL12NA or MRCOOL DIY 12K are both appropriate choices. If the space has high ceilings, large south-facing windows, or is in a hot climate, step up to 18,000 BTU.
How long do mini splits last?
A professionally installed mini split from a major manufacturer typically lasts 15–20 years with annual filter cleaning and a professional coil cleaning every 3–5 years. DIY-installed units have the same mechanical lifespan if the lineset connections are correctly made. The compressor is the most vulnerable component — units with variable-speed (inverter) compressors tend to outlast single-speed models.
Do mini splits work in cold weather?
Standard mini splits operate down to approximately 5°F but lose significant capacity below 17°F. Cold-climate units like the Mitsubishi Hyper-Heat and Daikin Aurora series maintain full heating capacity to -13°F and can provide supplemental heat down to -22°F. For climates that regularly drop below 0°F, a cold-climate-rated unit is essential.
What SEER2 rating should I look for?
The federal minimum as of 2023 is 13 SEER2 in northern states and 14 SEER2 in the south. For most buyers, a 17–20 SEER2 unit offers the best payback period — higher efficiency units cost more upfront but reduce operating costs enough to recover the premium within 5–8 years. Units above 22 SEER2 are primarily for buyers with very high run hours or premium electricity rates.
Can I install a mini split myself?
MRCOOL's DIY series is specifically engineered for homeowner installation using pre-charged quick-connect linesets. Other brands require EPA 608 certification to handle refrigerant. Even with MRCOOL DIY, you will need basic electrical skills to run a dedicated 240V circuit, and most local building codes require a permit. Check your jurisdiction's requirements before beginning.
