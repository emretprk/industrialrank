How to Choose

CAT ratings: what they mean and why they matter

CAT (Category) ratings define the measurement environment. CAT II covers household receptacles. CAT III covers building services — panels, 3-phase distribution, commercial wiring. CAT IV covers the service entrance and outdoor conductors. For commercial electrical work, CAT III 600V is the minimum. Never use a CAT II instrument on a commercial panel — transient overvoltages can cause arc flash in instruments not designed for that environment.

True RMS vs average-responding: when it matters

True RMS multimeters accurately measure the RMS value of any waveform — sinusoidal or not. Average-responding meters give correct readings only on pure sine waves. Variable frequency drives, switching power supplies, and electronic ballasts produce non-sinusoidal waveforms. If you measure any of these loads, True RMS is not optional. All Fluke and Klein professional meters include True RMS.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best multimeter for electricians?
The Fluke 117 is the best all-around choice for commercial electrical work. The Fluke 87V is the better choice for HVAC and industrial motor diagnostics. For value-tier buyers, the Klein MM400 covers standard residential and commercial electrical diagnostics at half the price of entry-level Fluke.
Do I need a CAT IV multimeter?
CAT IV is required for work at the service entrance — the point where utility power connects to the building. For all wiring inside the building, CAT III 600V or 1000V is sufficient. Most electricians work in CAT III environments and the Fluke 117 or 87V covers those applications. CAT IV instruments are appropriate for utility and metering technicians.
Is Klein as good as Fluke?
Klein produces reliable, accurate instruments for standard electrical diagnostic work. For most commercial electrical tasks, a Klein MM400 performs to specification. Fluke's advantage is in specialized measurements — VFD diagnostics, high-current clamp capability with iFlex, and Fluke Connect wireless logging — that Klein does not offer at equivalent price points.
How long do Fluke multimeters last?
Fluke instruments routinely last 15–25 years in professional use. The 87V design has been in continuous production since the 1980s with incremental updates. Fluke's 3-year warranty covers manufacturing defects; beyond that, Fluke's repair program services instruments well outside warranty. The durability is part of why Fluke commands a price premium over consumer brands.
