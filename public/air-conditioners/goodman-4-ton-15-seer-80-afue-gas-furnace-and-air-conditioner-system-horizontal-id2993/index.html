Review

Goodman positions this 4-ton combo system as a straightforward replacement for contractors and homeowners who want reliable climate control without premium pricing. The horizontal A-coil configuration suits tight attic spaces common in residential retrofit work. At 2,071 dollars, it undercuts many competitors while maintaining respectable efficiency credentials and recognized brand backing. This is practical equipment for the middle market, not a showpiece but a workhorse.

Cooling performance delivers 48,000 BTU across two compressor stages, achieving 15.5 SEER with variable-speed blower optimization. The furnace generates 80,000 BTU heating with two-stage burner control, matching the 4-ton cooling capacity effectively. Maximum CFM of 2,000 ensures adequate airflow for residential spaces up to roughly 2,000 square feet. Energy Star certification confirms above-baseline efficiency, though 80% AFUE represents older technology compared to 90-plus percent alternatives available today.

This horizontal unit requires 120V single-phase power with 27.7 to 45-amp breaker sizing, standard for residential applications. Most contractors complete installation in 4-6 hours once electrical and ductwork rough-in is finished.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this system work with R22 refrigerant if I have older ductwork? +
Yes, this model accepts both R410A and R22, offering flexibility for existing installations. R410A is standard and recommended for new systems. If your home currently uses R22, technicians can retrofit this unit, though compatibility with existing components requires verification before installation proceeds.
What electrical upgrades are needed before installation? +
You need a dedicated 120V, 1-phase, 60 Hz circuit with a breaker sized between 27.7 and 45 amps. Most homes already have adequate service. An electrician can confirm in minutes. Budget 300 to 600 dollars if new panel circuits or upgrades are required.
How does the 15.5 SEER rating compare to modern systems? +
Fifteen-point-five SEER is solid mid-range performance, about 20 percent better than older 13 SEER units but trailing newer 18-plus SEER models. For moderate climates, the energy savings versus older equipment justify the purchase quickly. High-efficiency upgrades cost significantly more upfront for modest additional operational gains.
Is 80% AFUE good enough for heating, or should I choose 95% AFUE? +
Eighty percent AFUE is acceptable and lower-cost but wastes 20 percent of fuel as exhaust heat. Ninety-five percent systems recoup the 1,500 to 2,500 dollar premium through utility savings over 12-15 years in cold climates. Moderate regions see slower payback, making 80% reasonable for budget-conscious buyers.
Does the 10-year warranty cover compressor failure from normal wear? +
Yes, manufacturing defects in the scroll compressor are covered for 10 years with registration. Normal wear from proper use is included. Misuse, incorrect installation, or using unapproved refrigerants voids coverage. Keep service records to protect your claim.
