Review

The 3 Ton Goodman gas furnace and air conditioner system is a well-engineered combo unit that delivers high seasonal efficiency without premium pricing. This downflow configuration solves a real problem for homeowners and contractors who need compact equipment that fits horizontal or downflow duct arrangements. Goodman's reputation for reliability in the commercial and residential sectors carries significant weight here, and the ENERGY STAR certification signals genuine efficiency credentials.

Performance numbers tell the story. The furnace achieves 97% AFUE with a modulating burner that fires at 80,000 BTU and adjusts output based on heating demand, which is significantly more efficient than fixed-stage alternatives. On the cooling side, the 16 SEER1 rating exceeds the labeled 15 SEER, moving 34,800 BTU with a scroll compressor and R410A refrigerant. The variable-speed ECM blower reaches 1,600 CFM maximum and adapts fan speed across both heating and cooling cycles, optimizing comfort and reducing unnecessary runtime.

Installation

This system uses standard 115V single-phase power with a 30-amp maximum breaker, making it compatible with most residential electrical service. The plastic PVC exhaust flue requirement is straightforward and reduces installation complexity compared to stainless steel alternatives. Downflow configuration requires proper ductwork placement below the unit, and the A-coil style pairs with most evaporator coils. Suction and liquid lines are 7/8" and 3/8" respectively, with 50-foot insulation included for typical residential runs.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the 97% AFUE compare to older furnaces on energy bills? +
A 97% AFUE unit converts nearly all fuel to usable heat, versus 80-85% AFUE units that waste 15-20% up the flue. On a $1,200 annual heating bill, this system could save $180-240 yearly compared to older equipment, recovering the efficiency premium in 3-5 years.
What size home is this 3-ton system right for? +
Three tons handles approximately 1,200-1,500 square feet depending on insulation, climate, and sun exposure. Have a load calculation performed before installation to confirm tonnage matches your home, as oversizing or undersizing reduces efficiency and comfort.
Why is the cooling efficiency 16 SEER1 when the product is labeled 15 SEER? +
SEER1 is the newer rating standard that uses updated test conditions, resulting in slightly lower numbers than legacy SEER ratings. The 16 SEER1 actually reflects more accurate real-world performance than traditional SEER measurements.
How does single-stage cooling compare to variable-capacity alternatives? +
Single-stage runs at full power or off, which cools quickly but uses more energy on mild days. Variable systems modulate capacity for efficiency on part-load conditions. Single-stage is typically $2,000-3,000 cheaper and sufficient for moderate climates.
Is the 10-year warranty sufficient protection? +
Yes, the 10-year term covers the most failure-prone period. Most furnaces and condensers last 15-20 years with maintenance, so this warranty protects your largest financial risk window effectively.
