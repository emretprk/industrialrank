Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet list both 15 SEER and 16 SEER2 ratings? +
SEER2 is the newer testing standard using realistic weather conditions, replacing the older SEER metric. This unit's 16 SEER2 rating is superior and more accurate for today's climate. The 15 SEER reference may reflect previous marketing materials. When comparing systems, always use SEER2 numbers for apples-to-apples efficiency comparison.
What causes the tonnage discrepancy between 3.5 ton and 5 ton? +
The spec sheet lists 5 ton tonnage while cooling capacity measures 42,000 BTU, which calculates to 3.5 tons. This appears to be a data entry error on the supplier's part. Contact Online Supply to clarify actual system tonnage before purchase, as this affects proper load matching and warranty validity.
How much will this system save compared to older equipment? +
Compared to 1990s furnaces running 60-65% AFUE, you'll save 20-25% on heating costs. The 16 SEER2 cooling saves 30-40% versus pre-2006 window units. Actual savings depend on local fuel costs, usage patterns, and climate zone. Most homeowners recover equipment costs in 7-9 years through utility reductions.
Is the variable speed blower worth the price difference? +
Variable speed motors reduce short-cycling energy waste and provide quieter operation than single-speed alternatives. They typically cost $150-250 more upfront but deliver 10-15% blower efficiency gains. For most installations in moderate climates, the payback occurs within 4-5 years of operation.
Does online registration actually matter for the warranty? +
Yes, absolutely. Goodman requires online registration to activate the 10-year coverage period. Without registration, warranty defaults to five years. Register immediately after installation using your AHRI certificate number and purchase date.
