Review

The ACiQ ACIQ-48-EHPD-UPG represents a practical entry-point condenser for contractors managing equipment replacements and budget-focused homeowners. As a direct replacement for three Goodman GSX models, this 4-ton unit addresses the growing market need for R454B-compatible equipment without premium costs. Weighing 120 pounds with sweat valve connections, it maintains straightforward installation protocols familiar to HVAC professionals.

Performance metrics are straightforward. The unit delivers 48,000 BTU cooling capacity rated at 13 SEER1 efficiency, positioning it within acceptable ranges for baseline residential systems. The R454B refrigerant eliminates compatibility issues with older R-22 and R-410A systems. Operating at 76 dB on a single-stage compressor, acoustic output ranks higher than inverter-driven models but acceptable for outdoor placement. Electrical requirements are modest at 208/230V single-phase, 60Hz with a 26.3-45 amp breaker range.

Installation

Installation demands standard HVAC practices. The vertical 24.5" uncased coil pairs with 3/8" liquid and 1 1/8" suction lines using sweat connections. Frequently Asked Questions

Can this ACiQ unit replace my older Goodman GSX condenser? +
Yes, this unit directly replaces GSX130483, GSX130484, and GSX130485 models. Verify your current model number before ordering. The R454B refrigerant requires inspection of existing coils and lineset for compatibility, though most Goodman systems transition smoothly.
What electrical service do I need for installation? +
The unit requires 208/230V single-phase, 60Hz power with a 26.3 to 45-amp breaker. Confirm your panel can accommodate these requirements before purchasing. Most residential installations fall comfortably within standard 200-amp service capacity.
How does 13 SEER1 compare to newer high-efficiency units? +
SEER1 ratings of 13 meet baseline efficiency standards but fall below premium units rated 16-18 SEER1. This unit prioritizes cost over maximum energy savings, suitable for moderate climates and budget-conscious buyers willing to accept slightly higher utility costs.
Should I choose this over higher-SEER alternatives? +
Select this unit if replacement cost is primary concern or your climate doesn't justify premium efficiency investments. Choose higher-SEER models if you plan 20+ year ownership or operate in extreme heat climates where efficiency compounds savings.
Does the 10-year warranty cover everything? +
The warranty covers manufacturing defects and component failures under normal operation when registered online. Labor costs and refrigerant are typically excluded. Review the full warranty document during purchase to understand specific coverage limits.
