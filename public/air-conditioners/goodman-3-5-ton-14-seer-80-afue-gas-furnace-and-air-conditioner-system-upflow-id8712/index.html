Review

The Goodman 3.5-ton furnace and air conditioning combo represents solid middle-ground equipment for residential HVAC systems. This upflow configuration uses R410A refrigerant and delivers 80,000 BTU heating capacity paired with 42,000 BTU cooling output. It's positioned as a reliable, cost-effective option for homeowners and contractors seeking proven performance without premium pricing. Online Supply backs it with a 10-year warranty upon registration, adding confidence to the purchase decision.

Real-world performance centers on the 80% AFUE furnace efficiency and 16 SEER1 cooling rating, both solid accomplishments in this price segment. The scroll compressor handles the load efficiently at 42,000 BTU, while the multi-speed PSC blower motor enables better part-load efficiency than single-speed designs. Operating at 73 dBA during peak cycling represents typical noise for single-stage systems. The 2000 CFM maximum airflow and A-coil configuration support proper air distribution in standard residential ductwork without requiring oversizing.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 16 SEER1 rating with Energy Star certification beats the 14 SEER marketing claim
  • 80% AFUE furnace efficiency is respectable for gas heating and appropriate for moderate climates
  • 42,000 BTU cooling capacity suits 3.5-ton loads well for most residential applications
  • Scroll compressor design is proven reliable with minimal field failures
  • Multi-speed PSC blower motor provides flexibility and quieter operation at lower speeds

Cons

  • 73 dBA noise level is moderately loud, noticeable during compressor cycling
  • Single-stage compressor means it runs full capacity or off, missing intermediate efficiency opportunities
  • Spec sheet contains discrepancies (listed as 3.5-ton and 5-ton tonnage ratings)
  • 80% AFUE is good but not exceptional compared to 95%+ high-efficiency models available today

Who Should Buy

Best For

Homeowners in moderate climates who need a straightforward, dependable split system without spending on premium efficiency features. Contractors installing standard upflow systems in budget-conscious residential replacements will appreciate the straightforward installation and proven Goodman reliability.

Consider Alternatives If

Anyone in very cold climates needing maximum heating efficiency, or homeowners prioritizing quietest operation should explore variable-capacity or higher AFUE alternatives.

Installation

This upflow system requires standard residential installation with 120V single-phase 60Hz power and a 22.4 to 35 amp breaker. The metal flue exits from the top, common in upflow configurations. Valve connections are sweat-type with 3/8 inch liquid line and 1 1/8 inch suction line, both standard sizes. The 30 by 21 by 21 inch dimensions fit typical utility closets. Professional HVAC installation is essential for proper refrigerant charging and ductwork integration.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year parts warranty requires online registration after purchase, which is standard across Goodman's consumer lineup. This coverage applies to the compressor, coils, and control components, protecting your investment against manufacturing defects. Activation is straightforward and typically takes minutes online through the manufacturer's portal.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet list both 3.5-ton and 5-ton ratings? +
The product appears to reference conflicting tonnage designations in source documentation. The 42,000 BTU cooling capacity and 3.5-ton title are accurate for this model. The 5-ton reference may indicate a related product line or documentation error. Confirm tonnage with your dealer before purchasing to ensure proper sizing for your home.
What electrical requirements does this system need? +
The system operates on standard 120V single-phase 60Hz residential power with a breaker sized between 22.4 and 35 amps. Most homes have adequate electrical service for this configuration. Your electrician should verify panel capacity and install the proper breaker size per local code during installation.
How does 16 SEER1 compare to the advertised 14 SEER rating? +
The actual 16 SEER1 specification exceeds the advertised 14 SEER claim, which is a pleasant surprise. SEER1 uses updated testing standards. This efficiency level is respectable for entry-level systems but falls short of high-efficiency models rated 18 SEER or above available from competitors.
Is this system suitable for very cold climates? +
The 80% AFUE furnace is adequate for moderate climates but not ideal for extreme cold. Regions with sustained subzero temperatures benefit from 90%+ AFUE condensing furnaces that recover exhaust heat. Consult your local HVAC contractor about climate-appropriate sizing before deciding.
Does the 10-year warranty cover everything? +
The warranty covers compressor and coil failures but requires online registration. Refrigerant, labor, and shipping costs are typically excluded. Review the full warranty terms at purchase to understand exact coverage limits and claim procedures.
