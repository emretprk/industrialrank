Review

The Goodman 3-ton dual-stage system represents a practical middle ground in the combined furnace-condenser market. This upflow configuration combines an 80,000 BTU furnace with 35,000 BTU cooling capacity, positioned for residential applications where both heating and cooling require replacement. The two-stage design on both sides allows the system to ramp down during mild weather, avoiding the energy waste of constant full-capacity operation.

Performance numbers tell the story here. The furnace achieves 96% AFUE, meaning 96 cents of every heating dollar actually heats your home, while the condenser reaches 18 SEER2 with its scroll compressor and two-stage operation. The variable-speed ECM blower motor adjusts to exactly 1,600 CFM maximum when needed, but ramps down quietly during partial-load conditions. Energy Star certification confirms these specs meet federal efficiency thresholds. At 71 decibels, the condenser runs at moderate volume, acceptable for most residential locations.

Installation

This upflow A-coil design fits vertical installations in attics or closets with its 34.5-inch height and 21-inch width footprint. You'll need 115V single-phase power with a 21.3 to 35-amp breaker, standard for most homes. Liquid line uses 3/8-inch connections while suction line runs 7/8-inch with 3/4-inch insulation. PVC flue venting simplifies installation compared to metal alternatives. Sweat valve connections require basic soldering skills.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this unit with a lifetime heat exchanger warranty, covering the furnace's most expensive component. Parts carry 10-year coverage when you register online, typical industry standard protection. This combination of lifetime primary component coverage and decade-long parts support provides solid peace of mind, though labor costs fall on the homeowner.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does a 3-ton system list 5-ton cooling capacity in the specs? +
This appears to be a specification discrepancy. The cooling BTU of 35,000 actually equals approximately 2.9 tons (35,000 divided by 12,000 BTU per ton), aligning with the 3-ton rating. Cross-reference AHRI certificate 7368835 to confirm actual capacity for your application.
Can I install this myself without HVAC certification? +
You can handle ductwork and electrical connections, but EPA regulations require certified technicians to handle refrigerant work. Flue venting, thermostat wiring, and gas line connections also require licensed professionals in most jurisdictions. Improper installation voids warranties.
How much will this actually save on utility bills compared to older systems? +
A 96% AFUE furnace versus 80% AFUE saves roughly 16% on heating costs. The 18 SEER2 condenser versus 13 SEER saves approximately 28% on cooling. Combined savings depend on your climate and usage patterns, but annual savings typically range 15-22% for dual fuel applications.
Does this system integrate with smart thermostats? +
Yes. The ECM variable-speed blower and two-stage heating and cooling work well with modern smart thermostats. However, verify compatibility with your specific thermostat model before purchasing. Some older Wi-Fi models have limited two-stage support.
What does the lifetime heat exchanger warranty actually cover? +
It covers manufacturing defects in the furnace heat exchanger if it cracks or leaks. Normal wear and corrosion from improper maintenance are excluded. You must register online within 60 days of installation to activate coverage.
