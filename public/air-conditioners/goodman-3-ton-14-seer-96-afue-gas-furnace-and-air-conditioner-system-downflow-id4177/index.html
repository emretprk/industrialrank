Review

The Goodman ACiQ 3 Ton combo system positions itself squarely in the practical efficiency segment, delivering legitimate upgrades over baseline equipment at a price point that won't require financing discussions. At $647, this downflow configuration combines a high-efficiency furnace with a mid-tier air conditioner, making it a logical choice for replacement jobs. The ENERGY STAR certification and 10-year warranty signal reasonable longevity expectations, while the variable-speed blower motor separates it from stripped-down competitors.

The furnace hits 96% AFUE, meaning nearly every BTU of fuel converts to usable heat compared to older 80% AFUE units that waste 20% up the chimney. The air conditioner delivers 36,000 BTU cooling capacity at 14 SEER1 efficiency, which translates to lower summer energy costs. The two-stage burner and variable-speed ECM blower are the key differentiators here, preventing the on-off cycling that creates temperature swings and wastes energy. For homeowners upgrading from 1990s equipment, the efficiency jump is substantial and shows up immediately in utility bills.

Installation

Downflow configuration fits retrofit installations where ductwork runs below the furnace. Requires 115V single-phase power with 18.7 to 30-amp breaker, standard for most homes. Plastic PVC exhaust flue compatibility is typical of modern furnaces. The A-coil style integrates cleanly with the air conditioning side. Installation complexity is moderate, requiring proper refrigerant charging and ductwork matching to the 2000 CFM blower capacity.

Warranty & Reliability

Ten-year parts warranty requires online registration, a simple step most owners complete during installation. This covers compressor, furnace heat exchanger, and major components, providing meaningful protection during the equipment's prime years. Labor warranty details should be confirmed with your installer, as they vary by contractor and region.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much can I expect to save on heating costs compared to my old furnace? +
If replacing 80% AFUE equipment with this 96% AFUE unit, you recover 16 percentage points of efficiency. On a typical 100,000 BTU furnace running 1,500 hours annually, that's roughly $200 to $300 in annual savings depending on gas prices. Savings scale with heating demand and fuel costs in your region.
Will this fit my existing ductwork and air handler location? +
The downflow design works when ducts are below the furnace, common in basements and utility closets. Check your existing furnace orientation first. The 30 inch height and 21 inch width fit standard closet spaces. Your installer should verify static pressure compatibility with your duct system at 2000 CFM capacity.
How does 14 SEER compare to newer systems I've heard about? +
14 SEER1 is solid mid-tier cooling. Newer systems reach 16 to 18 SEER2, but those cost significantly more. For moderate climates, 14 SEER delivers measurable savings over 10-year-old units without the premium price tag of cutting-edge equipment.
What's the difference between this system and a higher-priced Goodman model? +
Premium Goodman systems add variable-capacity compressors for smoother cooling, quieter scroll compressors, and higher SEER ratings up to 18. This model uses single-stage operation, which is less efficient at part-load but costs considerably less and still beats most baseline units.
Do I need to register online for the warranty to apply? +
Yes, the full 10-year warranty requires online registration. Without registration, coverage may default to a shorter period. Registration is free and takes minutes during or after installation.
