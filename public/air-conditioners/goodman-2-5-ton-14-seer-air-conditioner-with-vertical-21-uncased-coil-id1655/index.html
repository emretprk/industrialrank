Review

The ACiQ 2.5-ton air conditioner with 21-inch vertical uncased coil occupies a practical middle ground in the residential HVAC market. Rated at 30,000 BTU cooling capacity with 14 SEER efficiency, it targets contractors and homeowners seeking dependable performance without premium pricing. The unit uses modern R454B refrigerant, aligning with environmental regulations that are phasing out older refrigerants. At this price point, it competes directly with basic Goodman and Carrier models, making it relevant for budget-conscious system replacements.

Performance specifications are straightforward and honest. The single-stage rotary compressor delivers full-load cooling at 30,000 BTU, while the 14 SEER rating indicates moderate efficiency. The unit operates on 208/230V single-phase power with a 17.1-25 amp breaker window, making it compatible with most residential electrical panels. At 72 dB, noise output is acceptable for outdoor condenser installations but notably higher than variable-speed alternatives. The 3/8-inch liquid and 3/4-inch suction line sizing is standard for this tonnage class.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 14 SEER compare to other budget air conditioners? +
14 SEER is solid mid-range efficiency. Entry-level units run 13 SEER, while premium models hit 16-18 SEER. This ACiQ unit offers better efficiency than absolute budget options while keeping costs lower than high-end variable-speed units. Real-world savings depend on your climate and run hours.
Will this uncased coil work with my existing air handler? +
The 21-inch vertical uncased A-coil requires an air handler designed for this configuration. Verify your current handler accepts uncased coils before purchasing. Mismatched coil and handler combinations cause performance problems and warranty voidance. Contact your HVAC supplier to confirm compatibility.
Is R454B refrigerant better than R410A? +
R454B is EPA-approved and has lower global warming potential than R410A, making it more environmentally friendly. Performance and efficiency are comparable. R454B is now standard in new systems, so choosing this unit future-proofs your investment against refrigerant restrictions coming in 2025.
How does the 72 dB noise level affect comfort? +
72 dB is similar to normal conversation at six feet away. It's acceptable for most installations but noticeably louder than variable-speed units at 65-68 dB. If your condenser sits near a bedroom or living space, consider whether higher operating costs for quieter equipment make sense.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Parts coverage includes compressor, condenser coil, and control components. It requires online registration within 30 days of installation. Labor and refrigerant are not covered. Check regional terms, as some areas have different coverage limits.
