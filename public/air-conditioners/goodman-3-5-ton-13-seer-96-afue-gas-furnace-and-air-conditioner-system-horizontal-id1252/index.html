Review

The Goodman 3.5-ton horizontal combo system balances efficiency and affordability in a market crowded with budget units on one end and premium variable-capacity equipment on the other. This SKU combines a 120,000 BTU gas furnace with a 42,000 BTU air conditioning condenser, making it suitable for homes ranging from 1,200 to 1,600 square feet depending on insulation and climate. The horizontal orientation targets contractors and homeowners dealing with space constraints where traditional vertical casings create installation headaches.

Performance metrics are solid across both heating and cooling. The furnace achieves 96% AFUE, meaning 96 cents of every energy dollar converts to usable heat, while the air conditioner delivers 13 SEER cooling efficiency. The multi-speed blower motor pulls up to 2,000 CFM, adapting fan speed to match heating and cooling loads. Single-stage operation keeps the compressor and burner straightforward, trading variable precision for proven longevity and lower service costs.

Installation

This system requires 115V single-phase 60 Hz power with breaker sizing between 23.9 and 40 amps, making it compatible with standard residential electrical service. PVC or plastic exhaust flue vents heat safely outdoors with right-side flue location. Refrigerant lines use 3/8 inch liquid and 7/8 inch suction piping with sweat valve connections, and installation manuals are available through Online Supply. Horizontal mounting requires solid structural support and proper condensate drainage.

Warranty & Reliability

Coverage includes a 10-year parts warranty when you complete online registration with Online Supply. This protects compressor, heat exchanger, and major components against manufacturing defects. Labor coverage varies by installer and location. This warranty is industry-standard for Goodman mid-range equipment but falls short of extended protection offered by premium brands.

Frequently Asked Questions

What square footage does this 3.5-ton system handle? +
A 3.5-ton system (42,000 BTU cooling capacity) typically covers 1,200 to 1,600 square feet depending on insulation quality, climate zone, and ductwork efficiency. For hot climates or poor insulation, size down to 1,200 square feet; for mild climates with good sealing, extend toward 1,600 square feet. Always have a load calculation performed before installation.
Will this fit in a tight attic space? +
Yes. The horizontal configuration measures 24.5 inches high, 21.125 inches wide, and 26 inches deep, plus you need clearance for the 30-foot suction line insulation. Most attics can accommodate it, but verify ductwork routing and return air paths don't restrict airflow. Measure carefully before ordering.
How does 13 SEER compare to modern air conditioners? +
13 SEER is above-average for single-stage equipment but trails variable-capacity units rated 16 to 24 SEER. However, it beats budget models at 10 to 11 SEER. For moderate climates with mild summers, 13 SEER delivers solid savings without the premium cost and complexity of inverter-based systems.
Is this better than a Lennox or Carrier equivalent? +
Goodman matches Lennox and Carrier performance at lower cost but sacrifices some sound insulation and variable staging. This unit excels for budget-conscious contractors. If noise reduction or precision zoning matters more, premium brands justify higher pricing.
Does online registration actually matter for the warranty? +
Yes. The 10-year coverage requires completing online registration with Online Supply. Without registration, warranty may be limited. Register promptly after installation to avoid disputes if problems arise later.
