Review

Goodman's horizontal furnace-AC system occupies a practical sweet spot in the residential market. At 2.5 tons cooling and 40,000 BTU heating, it handles moderate climate zones without oversizing costs. The unit comes pre-charged and ready for straightforward installation in attics, basements, or crawlspaces. Online Supply backs the product with a 10-year warranty and positions it as an accessible upgrade path for homeowners replacing 15-20 year old equipment.

The 96% AFUE furnace efficiency means substantially lower winter heating bills compared to standard 80-85% models. Cooling performance at 16 SEER2 and 29,000 BTU meets Energy Star criteria, qualifying many buyers for utility rebates. The variable-speed ECM blower motor runs at 1200 CFM maximum, automatically adjusting airflow to match heating or cooling demand. Two-stage burner operation adds comfort consistency while reducing energy waste during partial-load conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet list 2.5 tons but the tonnage shows 3 tons? +
The 2.5-ton rating refers to cooling capacity (29,000 BTU divided by 12,000 per ton), while the 3-ton designation is a manufacturer classification. Actual cooling output is 29,000 BTU. This discrepancy appears in some Goodman specifications and shouldn't affect performance, though it creates labeling confusion.
Can I install this in a downflow configuration instead of horizontal? +
No, this unit is certified only for downflow/horizontal airflow direction. Installing it vertically would void the AHRI certificate and warranty. Verify your ductwork orientation before purchase, as retrofitting from horizontal to downflow typically requires equipment changes.
How much will the 96% AFUE save compared to my old 80% furnace? +
A 16-percentage-point efficiency gain saves roughly 20% on annual heating costs. Exact savings depend on climate, fuel prices, and usage patterns. In heating-heavy regions like Minnesota or New York, annual savings can exceed $300-500, often recovering upgrade costs within 5-7 years.
Is the 16 SEER2 rating the same as 15 SEER mentioned in the product title? +
No, SEER2 is the newer EPA testing standard adopted in 2023, typically 15-20% lower than legacy SEER ratings. This unit's 16 SEER2 roughly equals approximately 19-20 older SEER points, making it a solid mid-range cooler rather than premium efficiency.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover if something breaks? +
The warranty covers parts and compressor failures from manufacturing defects. Labor, refrigerant recharge, and service calls cost extra. Online registration must be completed within 30 days of installation. Damage from improper installation or maintenance voids coverage.
