This Goodman system excels for contractors and homeowners seeking reliable, efficient heating and cooling without premium pricing. The 96% AFUE furnace paired with a 16 SEER air conditioner makes it ideal for moderate climates where both heating and cooling demand is substantial.

Review

The Goodman GMEC960803BN represents a practical middle ground in the residential HVAC market. This two-stage combination system integrates an 80,000 BTU gas furnace with a 24,000 BTU (2-ton equivalent) air conditioner in one compact upflow A-coil design. Goodman has built its reputation on reliable equipment that balances performance with affordability, and this SKU delivers on both fronts for contractors and homeowners seeking efficient climate control without flagship pricing.

Performance metrics here are genuinely impressive. The furnace achieves 96% AFUE, meaning 96 cents of every fuel dollar converts to usable heat, while the air conditioner delivers 16 SEER cooling efficiency. Two-stage operation on both heating and cooling lets the system modulate output to match actual demand, reducing short-cycling and wear. The ECM multi-speed blower motor maxes out at 1,200 CFM and runs quietly at 71 dB, providing better dehumidification than single-stage units during shoulder seasons.

Installation

Professional installation is mandatory for this gas appliance. Technicians must connect refrigerant lines using the 3/8-inch liquid and 3/4-inch suction valve sizes, establish proper gas supply connections, vent the flue to PVC/plastic ducting on the right side, and wire 115V single-phase power with a 15.7 to 20-amp breaker. The 118-pound condenser unit fits standard outdoor pad locations, and the 34.5-inch tall, 28.75-inch deep indoor furnace integrates easily into attics or basements with upflow ductwork.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this system with a lifetime heat exchanger warranty covering the most expensive internal component, plus 10-year unit replacement and 10-year parts coverage upon online registration. This is solid middle-market protection that protects against manufacturing defects without the extended coverage of premium brands. Registration unlocks full benefits, so buyers must complete this step after purchase.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the product title say 2 Ton but specs show 3 Ton? +
The cooling capacity is genuinely 24,000 BTU (2 tons), but the specification sheet lists 3 Ton tonnage, likely referencing furnace classification. Contact the seller for clarification before purchasing to confirm you're getting the capacity your home requires.
Can I install this myself or do I need a contractor? +
Licensed technician installation is legally required. Gas line connections, refrigerant charging, electrical work, and venting all demand expertise. Many jurisdictions mandate HVAC licensing. DIY attempts risk safety hazards, voided warranty, and code violations.
How much will my heating bills drop with 96% AFUE? +
Savings depend on your current system. Upgrading from an 80% AFUE furnace saves roughly 16 percent on heating costs. At typical residential gas bills, this translates to $200 to $400 annually in colder climates, recovering installation costs in 5 to 8 years.
Is 16 SEER adequate or should I buy a higher-rated AC? +
16 SEER is legitimate mid-tier efficiency. High-efficiency models reach 20+ SEER but cost significantly more. For moderate cooling climates, 16 SEER delivers solid payback in 7 to 10 years without premium pricing. Cold climates or minimal cooling needs make higher SEER less cost-effective.
What does online warranty registration actually cover? +
Registration unlocks the full 10-year parts and unit replacement protection plus lifetime heat exchanger coverage. Without registration, coverage may be reduced or limited to basic manufacturer defects. Complete registration immediately after installation.
