Review

The Goodman 2 Ton 14 SEER system occupies the practical middle ground of the residential HVAC market. Priced at $643, it delivers respectable efficiency without the complexity or cost of premium brands. ACiQ manufactured this unit with straightforward engineering, targeting contractors and homeowners who prioritize reliability and affordability over cutting-edge features. The downflow configuration suits basement or closet installation common in many homes.

Performance metrics tell a straightforward story. The furnace achieves 92% AFUE efficiency, meaning 92 cents of every heating dollar directly heats your home. Cooling delivers 24,000 BTU at 14 SEER rating with a scroll compressor and single condenser stage. The multi-speed blower reaches 1,200 CFM maximum airflow, adjusting based on demand. At 70 dBA, operation stays reasonably quiet. R454B refrigerant aligns with environmental regulations anticipated through 2030.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will this 2-ton unit adequately heat and cool a 3,000 square foot home? +
The 24,000 BTU capacity (2 tons) suits homes up to 2,000-2,500 square feet in moderate climates. Larger homes require oversizing to 3 tons or higher. Have a professional load calculation performed to ensure proper sizing for your specific location.
Can I install this in an upflow position if my basement is tight on space? +
This unit supports both upflow and downflow configurations per the spec sheet. Verify your ductwork layout accommodates the chosen orientation before purchase. The 30-inch height works in standard 8-foot basements when properly positioned.
How does 14 SEER compare to newer SEER2 standards? +
SEER2 represents a stricter efficiency test protocol introduced in 2023. A 14 SEER unit typically converts to approximately 10-11 SEER2 equivalent. This remains respectable for budget systems, though premium units now advertise 16-18 SEER2 ratings.
Does this Goodman compete with Lennox or Carrier units at this price point? +
At $643, this undercuts premium brands significantly. Lennox and Carrier equivalents run $900-1,200. Goodman sacrifices some modulation features but maintains reliability. Compare warranty terms and local contractor support when evaluating brands.
What happens if my system fails within the warranty period? +
ACiQ covers parts replacement through the 10-year registration warranty. Labor coverage depends on your contractor's enrollment. Contact ACiQ's warranty department with your registration documentation and serial number within the coverage period.
