Review

The Goodman 3-ton upflow gas furnace and air conditioner system represents strong value in the budget-to-mid-range HVAC category. At $504, this complete package positions itself as an alternative to contractor-exclusive premium brands, making it attractive to homeowners shopping online. The system carries AHRI certification (7424267) and Energy Star approval, indicating it meets established efficiency standards. Online Supply, the distributor, bundles rebate opportunities with the purchase, potentially lowering the actual cost further.

Heating performance comes from a 100,000 BTU modulating furnace rated at 97% AFUE, meaning 97 cents of every dollar spent on gas converts to usable heat. Cooling capacity reaches 35,000 BTU through a two-stage scroll compressor delivering 18 SEER1 efficiency. The variable-speed ECM blower motor runs between 0 and 2000 CFM, allowing better humidity control than fixed-speed alternatives. At 71 dB, the system operates quieter than many competitors, reducing the noise complaints often associated with older equipment.

Installation

Installation requires standard upflow configuration with plastic or PVC exhaust flue on the right side. The A-coil design accommodates most existing ductwork and air handlers. Electrical needs are modest: 115V, single phase, 60Hz, with breaker range between 21.3 and 35 amps. Refrigerant lines require 3/8 inch liquid and 7/8 inch suction with 3/4 inch insulation thickness. Professional installation is strongly recommended to ensure proper charging, ductwork sealing, and system commissioning.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides a 10-year parts warranty with online registration at no additional cost. This covers compressor, heat exchanger, and most major components. The coverage is transferable if you sell the home, adding slight resale value. Standard exclusions apply for improper installation, maintenance neglect, and refrigerant-related issues. Consider purchasing extended service contracts from your installer for labor coverage beyond the first year.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the listing show 17 SEER but specs list 18 SEER1? +
The promotional title references older SEER methodology. Actual specification is 18 SEER1, the current industry standard introduced in 2023. SEER1 ratings are typically 10-15% lower than legacy SEER ratings, so verify this matches your climate zone requirements before purchasing.
Can I install this system myself or do I need a contractor? +
Professional installation is essential. Refrigerant charging, ductwork sealing, and gas line connections require EPA certification and specialized tools. Improper installation voids the warranty and creates safety hazards. Budget $1,500 to $2,500 for professional installation labor.
How much will this system reduce my heating and cooling bills? +
Compared to a 13 SEER AC and 80% AFUE furnace, expect 20-30% annual savings depending on climate, usage patterns, and home insulation. In heating-dominant climates, the 97% AFUE furnace delivers the largest benefit. Your actual results depend on proper sizing and installation quality.
Is this system better than Lennox or Carrier equivalents? +
At equal efficiency ratings, no. Goodman and Lennox share similar technology and reliability. Lennox carries premium pricing for brand reputation and marketing. This Goodman system delivers nearly identical performance at roughly 40-50% lower cost, making value proposition stronger than prestige.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Parts replacement for manufacturing defects in compressor, heat exchanger, coils, and blower motor. Does not cover labor, refrigerant, or damage from improper installation. Requires online registration within 30-60 days of purchase to activate.
