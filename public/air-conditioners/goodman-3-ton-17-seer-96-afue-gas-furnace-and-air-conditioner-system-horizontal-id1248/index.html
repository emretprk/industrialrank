Review

The Goodman GMVC960804CN is a workhorse horizontal combo unit positioned for budget-conscious homeowners seeking measurable efficiency upgrades. This system pairs a gas furnace with an air conditioner in a single horizontal chassis, eliminating the need for outdoor condenser placement. The 3.5-ton capacity handles mid-sized homes effectively, and Energy Star certification means it meets EPA efficiency thresholds that unlock utility rebates in many regions. Goodman's reputation for straightforward, no-frills engineering translates to fewer proprietary components and easier parts availability.

Performance specs tell the real story here. The cooling side delivers 35,000 BTU at 18 SEER2 efficiency using R410A refrigerant with a two-stage scroll compressor. The heating side fires up to 80,000 BTU with 96% AFUE across two burner stages, meaning 96 cents of every dollar spent on gas converts to usable heat. The variable-speed ECM blower automatically adjusts airflow from 0 to 1,600 CFM based on demand, reducing short-cycling and improving temperature consistency. Operation at 71 dB is moderate, roughly equivalent to background office noise.

Installation

Installation requires a licensed HVAC technician due to gas line connections, venting configuration, and refrigerant handling. The horizontal A-coil design fits standard attic or crawlspace mounting locations. Electrical connection uses single-phase 115V 60Hz power with breaker requirements between 21.9 and 35 amps. PVC plastic flue venting (mounted right-side) simplifies routing compared to metal ductwork. Total installation time typically runs 6 to 8 hours for experienced teams.

Warranty & Reliability

Coverage includes lifetime protection on the heat exchanger plus 10 years of parts coverage with online registration. The heat exchanger warranty is the standout, since that component runs $800 to $1,200 to replace. Parts coverage includes compressor, motors, and controls. Note that labor is not covered, and registration is required to activate the full term.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this system work with my existing ductwork? +
Yes, if your ductwork currently supports a horizontal furnace configuration. The maximum airflow of 1,600 CFM requires ducts sized for at least 4-inch trunk lines. Have a technician verify CFM capacity before purchase to avoid undersized duct problems that reduce efficiency.
What breaker size do I need for electrical installation? +
Minimum 21.9 amps, maximum 35 amps. Most installations use a 30-amp dedicated breaker. Your electrician will determine final sizing based on voltage drop and local code. Do not attempt to install a breaker yourself, this requires licensed electrician work.
How much will my heating bill drop compared to an older furnace? +
The 96% AFUE rating means roughly 4 to 6 percent annual savings versus a 90% AFUE furnace, assuming similar usage. In cold climates with $1,500 annual heating costs, expect $60 to $90 in annual savings. Rebates often offset 20 to 30 percent of equipment cost.
Is 18 SEER2 better than SEER1 ratings I see on older units? +
Yes. SEER2 testing uses more realistic summer conditions and higher outdoor temperatures than legacy SEER1 ratings. An 18 SEER2 unit outperforms a 17 SEER1 unit by roughly 3 to 5 percent in typical operation, translating to measurable cooling bill reductions.
Can I transfer this warranty if I sell my home? +
Most manufacturers allow non-transferable warranty unless specified otherwise. Check Goodman's current policy, as some regional dealers offer extended transferable coverage for additional fees. Registration is required regardless to activate the full 10-year parts term.
