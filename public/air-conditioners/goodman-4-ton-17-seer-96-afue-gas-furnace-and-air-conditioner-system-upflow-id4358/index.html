// Bottom Line
This Goodman system works best for homeowners in mixed climates who want reliable heating without premium pricing. The 96% AFUE furnace and 18 SEER2 cooling coil deliver solid efficiency gains, though the 4-ton cooling capacity may undersize homes needing more than 15,000-17,000 sq ft of cooling.

Review

The Goodman CAPT4961D4 represents the practical middle ground in HVAC equipment. This 4-ton system combines a furnace and air conditioning coil, offering both heating and cooling in one package. With a 96% AFUE rating and Energy Star certification, it appeals to homeowners balancing efficiency upgrades with budget constraints. Goodman's reputation for straightforward reliability makes this system popular with contractors handling residential replacements in moderate climate zones.

Performance numbers tell a compelling efficiency story. The furnace delivers 120,000 BTU heating output at 96% AFUE, meaning nearly all fuel converts to usable heat. Cooling reaches 47,000 BTU through an 18 SEER2 rated coil with two-stage compressor and scroll technology. The multi-speed ECM blower runs up to 2,000 CFM, automatically adjusting to match heating and cooling demands. This modulation reduces energy consumption during partial-load conditions, common in spring and fall.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 96% AFUE furnace efficiency ranks among the best available, reducing winter heating costs significantly
  • 18 SEER2 cooling rating meets modern efficiency standards without high-end pricing
  • Multi-speed ECM blower motor adapts airflow to actual demand, improving comfort and reducing waste
  • Two-stage burner and condenser provide modulated operation for better temperature control
  • PVC/plastic exhaust flue option simplifies venting compared to metal flue systems
  • 10-year warranty with online registration offers solid coverage for parts and labor

Cons

  • 4-ton cooling capacity with 47,000 BTU output limits this to smaller homes, typically under 17,000 sq ft
  • SKU mismatch shows 5-ton tonnage in specs but 4-ton in product title, creating confusion about actual capacity

  • Requires 208/230V single-phase power, limiting installation options in some older homes

Who Should Buy

Best For

Homeowners in moderate climates replacing older furnaces want efficient heating with balanced cooling performance. Contractors installing systems in smaller homes appreciate Goodman's straightforward design and PVC venting flexibility.

Consider Alternatives If

Buyers in hot climates needing robust cooling should explore higher-tonnage units. Those prioritizing ultra-quiet operation should consider competitors with lower decibel ratings.

Installation

Installation favors contractors familiar with Goodman equipment. The upflow/horizontal configuration suits basements and utility closets. Plastic PVC exhaust venting simplifies ductwork compared to metal flue requirements. At 76 pounds and 30 inches tall, the unit fits standard equipment spaces. Electrical connections require 208/230V single-phase 60Hz service with breaker sizing between 29.3 and 50 amps.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman includes a 10-year warranty on parts and labor when you register online. This extended coverage protects against compressor failure, heat exchanger cracks, and blower motor issues. Registration takes minutes and provides peace of mind. However, labor coverage typically applies only during the first five years, so confirm terms before purchase.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this system include both furnace and air conditioning? +
Yes, this is a complete split system with the gas furnace and evaporator coil included. You'll need a separate outdoor condenser unit rated for 4-ton capacity. The 47,000 BTU cooling capacity and 120,000 BTU heating work together for year-round comfort.
Can I install this in my basement with upflow orientation? +
Yes, the upflow/horizontal configuration supports basement installation. PVC plastic exhaust venting simplifies routing through basement walls or ceilings. Ensure adequate return air access and install the drain pan properly to prevent water damage.
What's the difference between SEER2 18 and older SEER ratings? +
SEER2 uses updated testing standards reflecting real-world conditions better than legacy SEER ratings. An 18 SEER2 rating indicates high efficiency. This system qualifies for Energy Star certification and many utility rebates in efficiency-focused regions.
Is a 4-ton system large enough for my 2,000 sq ft home? +
Generally yes, but depends on insulation, climate, and sun exposure. Rule of thumb suggests 400-500 sq ft per ton, making 4 tons adequate for 2,000 sq ft in moderate climates. Have a professional load calculation done before purchasing.
What happens after my 10-year warranty expires? +
Coverage ends entirely after 10 years. Service calls become out-of-pocket expenses. Extended warranty plans from retailers or Goodman may be available at purchase, so ask about options upfront.
