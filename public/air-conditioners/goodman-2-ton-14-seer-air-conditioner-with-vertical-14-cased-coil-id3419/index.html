Review

The ACiQ 2-ton vertical A-coil air conditioner positions itself as a practical middle-ground solution for residential cooling upgrades. At 24,000 BTU capacity with 14 SEER efficiency, it competes against mid-tier units from major manufacturers while maintaining aggressive pricing. The vertical cased coil design (14 inches wide, 21 inches deep) fits standard ductwork configurations, and R454B refrigerant ensures forward compatibility with evolving EPA standards. This unit targets contractors managing volume replacements and budget-conscious homeowners unwilling to stretch for premium variable-capacity systems.

Performance-wise, the 24,000 BTU output adequately handles 2-ton cooling loads in moderate climates. The 14 SEER rating translates to measurable savings compared to 10-12 SEER legacy equipment, though it trails high-efficiency competitors at 16-18 SEER. A rotary compressor drives the single-stage operation, offering straightforward reliability without variable-capacity modulation. The 71 dB noise level ranks at the louder end of acceptable, and electrical requirements (208/230V, 1-phase, 14.3-25A breaker) remain standard for most residential installations, requiring no special panel upgrades.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 14 SEER rating delivers respectable efficiency gains over older 10-13 SEER units, reducing cooling costs by approximately 10-15% annually
  • R454B refrigerant is EPA-compliant and future-proof, avoiding obsolescence as regulations tighten
  • Rotary compressor is proven technology, generally more reliable than reciprocating designs in this tonnage class
  • Compact footprint at 14 inches wide and 22 inches tall simplifies installation in tight attic or closet spaces
  • 10-year warranty with online registration provides solid long-term protection for residential applications

Cons

  • Single-stage compressor lacks variable capacity, meaning it runs at full output or off with no modulation for part-load efficiency
  • 71 dB noise level is noticeably louder than variable-stage competitors, potentially problematic in noise-sensitive locations
  • Not Energy Star certified, missing qualification for some rebate programs and utility incentives
  • Specs list conflicting tonnage (2-ton vs 3-ton designation), creating confusion during specification and ordering

Installation

Installation involves standard refrigerant line connections using 3/8-inch liquid lines and 3/4-inch suction lines, both available in 50-foot lengths with 3/4-inch insulation thickness. The compact dimensions (22 inches tall, 14 inches wide) fit tight attic spaces. Mounting requires standard equipment pad installation. Electrical work demands straightforward single-phase 208/230V connection with appropriate breaker sizing between 14.3 and 25 amps. No special tools or refrigerant recovery equipment beyond industry standards is necessary.

Warranty & Reliability

ACiQ provides 10 years of parts and compressor coverage when registered online, a solid offering for residential use. Registration is straightforward and typically completed during installation. This warranty covers manufacturing defects but excludes damage from improper installation, refrigerant leaks from external sources, or lack of maintenance. Online registration is mandatory to activate coverage, so contractors should ensure homeowners complete this step immediately.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this really a 2-ton or 3-ton unit, given conflicting specs? +
The 24,000 BTU capacity confirms 2-ton operation (12,000 BTU per ton). The 3-ton tonnage listing appears to be a specification sheet error. Always reference the 24,000 BTU figure for load calculations and ductwork sizing. Contact the manufacturer if spec sheets vary across sources.
What electrical upgrades might I need for this unit? +
Most homes have adequate service. The unit requires 208/230V single-phase 60 Hz power with breaker capacity between 14.3 and 25 amps. Standard 20-amp breakers work fine. If your panel is already maxed or uses older 120V-only circuits, you may need a simple disconnect installation near the outdoor unit.
How much cooler is this than my 10-year-old AC? +
A 14 SEER unit consumes roughly 12-15% less electricity than a 10-12 SEER system for equivalent cooling. On a $120 monthly cooling bill, you'd save approximately $15-18 monthly. Over a 10-year lifespan, that totals $1,800-$2,160 in energy savings before utility rate increases.
How does this compare to a Carrier or Lennox 14 SEER model? +
ACiQ units offer comparable efficiency ratings but emphasize cost leadership over variable-stage compressors or smart controls found in premium tiers. Carrier and Lennox 14 SEER options often cost 15-20% more. Performance is functionally equivalent, though brand reputation and local service networks favor larger manufacturers.
Will this qualify for utility rebates? +
Without Energy Star certification, this unit likely misses federal rebate programs. Check your local utility and state incentive databases, as some programs accept 14 SEER equipment regardless of Energy Star status. Rebates vary significantly by region and program year.
