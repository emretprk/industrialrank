Frequently Asked Questions

What's the real difference between the 15 SEER rating and 16 SEER2 specs listed? +
SEER2 is the newer EPA testing standard using realistic conditions. The 16 SEER2 rating indicates superior efficiency compared to older SEER measurements. This system exceeds its advertised baseline, delivering better cooling efficiency than the 15 SEER claim suggests for actual real-world operation.
Can this system handle propane instead of natural gas? +
Yes, the furnace accepts both propane and natural gas with proper burner setup during installation. Confirm your fuel type with the installer, as burner orifices differ between fuels. The system ships ready for natural gas but conversion is straightforward for propane applications.
How much will this system reduce my heating and cooling costs? +
A 97% AFUE furnace typically saves 20-30% on heating versus 80% AFUE units. The 16 SEER2 cooling saves similar percentages compared to 13-14 SEER systems. Actual savings depend on local utility rates, climate, and insulation quality. Most homeowners recover the investment in 5-8 years through energy savings.
Is single-stage cooling a drawback compared to two-stage units? +
Single-stage means the compressor runs at full capacity when active, cycling on-off as needed. Two-stage systems modulate output, improving comfort and efficiency at part-load. For most climates, single-stage works well and costs less. Two-stage benefits most in mild spring and fall weather where full capacity isn't needed constantly.
What does the 10-year warranty cover and what's excluded? +
Parts coverage includes the compressor, heat exchanger, and refrigerant circuit components when properly registered. Labor, refrigerant, and maintenance items are not covered. Damage from improper installation, electrical surges, or lack of maintenance voids coverage.
