Review

The Goodman 2.5 ton horizontal furnace-AC combination sits squarely in the contractor-preferred middle market, balancing proven reliability with genuine efficiency upgrades. This SKU pairs an 80,000 BTU furnace rated at 96% AFUE with a 30,000 BTU air conditioner delivering 14 SEER2 performance. At $2755, it positions itself as accessible equipment for homeowners tired of utility bills while contractors appreciate straightforward installation and familiar support channels.

The furnace's 96% AFUE efficiency represents a meaningful step above baseline 80-90% units, potentially saving $150-250 annually depending on heating degree days and gas rates. Cooling delivers respectable performance through a single-stage rotary compressor operating at 72 dBA, adequate for moderate load management. The two-stage burner and variable-speed ECM blower motor add operational flexibility, reducing short-cycling on mild days and minimizing runtime hours. This combination achieves Energy Star certification with R454B refrigerant, ensuring current compliance without future phaseout concerns.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much will this system reduce my heating costs compared to an older 85% AFUE unit? +
The 96% AFUE rating burns 11 percentage points more efficiently. In a 6000 heating degree day climate, this typically saves $150-250 annually on natural gas. Results vary by local gas rates, insulation, and thermostat habits, but the efficiency gain is real and measurable over time.
Can this horizontal unit fit in my attic, and what clearance do I need? +
Yes, the horizontal configuration is designed for attic mounting. Dimensions are 34.5 inches tall by 17.5 inches wide by 28.75 inches deep. Plan for 18-24 inches of clearance on sides and top for service access, and ensure adequate return ductwork to prevent airflow restriction.
Is 14 SEER2 sufficient cooling efficiency, or should I pay more for 16+ SEER? +
14 SEER2 is solid mid-range cooling that handles most residential loads efficiently without premium pricing. Higher SEER ratings require variable-stage compressors and add $800-1200 to total cost. Choose 16+ SEER only if you live in hot climates, run AC constantly, or prioritize maximum efficiency above cost.
How does this single-stage compressor compare to two-stage cooling systems? +
Single-stage means the compressor runs full-output or off. Two-stage systems modulate capacity, reducing short-cycling and humidity swings. Single-stage is simpler, more reliable, and $600-900 cheaper, but two-stage provides better comfort in variable-load conditions. This unit suits stable climates and moderate usage patterns.
Do I need to register the warranty immediately, or does coverage start automatically? +
Registration is required to activate the full 10-year coverage. Without it, you typically receive 1-2 years by default. Register online immediately after installation to avoid coverage gaps. Keep your AHRI certificate number 7368834 and installation documentation for warranty claims.
