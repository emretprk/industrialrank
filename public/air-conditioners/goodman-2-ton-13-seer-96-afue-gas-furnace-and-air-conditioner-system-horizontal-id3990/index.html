// Bottom Line
This 2-ton Goodman system excels for contractors and homeowners in moderate climates needing efficient dual-fuel heating and cooling. The 96% AFUE furnace with variable-speed ECM blower and 13 SEER condenser make it ideal for budget-conscious buyers prioritizing operational cost savings over premium features.

Review

The Goodman 2-ton system (model ACIQ-24-EHPD-UPG) positions itself in the value-conscious segment where efficiency meets affordability. This combination unit packages a 60,000 BTU furnace with a 24,000 BTU air conditioner into one horizontal A-coil configuration. Energy Star certification and the option to register online for 10-year parts coverage suggest Goodman believes in this product's market staying power. Contractors appreciate Goodman's broad parts availability and straightforward design.

The furnace delivers exceptional 96% AFUE efficiency through two-stage burner technology and a variable-speed ECM blower reaching 1200 CFM maximum. The condenser operates at 13 SEER with R454B refrigerant, putting it in the efficient but not ultra-premium cooling category. At 75 dB during operation, acoustic performance is acceptable for utility closets or mechanical rooms but not ideal for noise-sensitive areas. The system requires 115V single-phase 60 Hz power with 10.3 to 15 amp breaker sizing, making it compatible with standard residential electrical service.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 96% AFUE furnace efficiency reduces heating costs substantially, especially valuable during winter months in cold climates
  • 13 SEER condenser with single-stage rotary compressor delivers steady cooling at 24,000 BTU for 2-ton applications
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor adjusts to 1200 CFM maximum, improving comfort and reducing energy waste during partial-load operation
  • Two-stage burner provides modulating heat output for more consistent temperature control and faster warm-up cycles
  • Dual fuel capability supports natural gas or propane, offering flexibility for properties with either supply type
  • Lifetime heat exchanger warranty demonstrates manufacturer confidence in furnace longevity and durability

Cons

  • 13 SEER rating is solid but not premium, lagging behind newer 14-16 SEER systems in summer efficiency
  • Single-stage compressor cannot modulate cooling capacity, cycling on and off rather than adjusting output smoothly
  • 75 dB noise level at operation is noticeably louder than variable-capacity systems, which may concern noise-sensitive installations

Who Should Buy

Best For

Contractors installing complete systems in moderate climates who value heating efficiency and reliability over advanced features. Homeowners with natural gas or propane access who plan to stay in their homes long enough to recoup efficiency investment through lower utility bills.

Consider Alternatives If

Installation

Installation follows standard horizontal placement with 3/8 inch liquid and 3/4 inch suction lines using sweat valve connections. The unit measures 34.5 inches high, 17.5 inches wide, and 28.75 inches deep, fitting tight spaces. Plastic or PVC exhaust flue requirement simplifies venting compared to metal ductwork. Suction line insulation at 3/4 inch thickness comes pre-applied. This system pairs well with standard furnace staging and thermostat controls, reducing commissioning time.

Warranty & Reliability

The heat exchanger warranty reflects Goodman's confidence in furnace durability and protects buyers against expensive mid-life failures. Frequently Asked Questions

What does 96% AFUE actually mean for my heating bills? +
AFUE (Annual Fuel Utilization Efficiency) of 96% means 96 cents of every dollar spent on fuel converts to usable heat. A 90% AFUE system wastes 10 cents per dollar. Over 20 years with typical usage, this 6-point difference saves hundreds to thousands depending on climate and energy prices.
Can I use this system with my existing thermostat and ductwork? +
Yes, as long as your existing ductwork connects to the horizontal A-coil configuration and your thermostat supports two-stage heating and single-stage cooling. Verify ductwork airflow capacity matches the 1200 CFM blower maximum. Professional installation assessment is essential for compatibility.
How does 13 SEER compare to newer systems I'm seeing advertised? +
13 SEER meets federal minimum efficiency standards and performs competently in moderate climates. Newer 14-16 SEER systems run 10-20% more efficiently in cooling but cost 30-50% more. For moderate climates, 13 SEER justifies the lower price over a 10-15 year lifespan.
Should I choose this or a variable-capacity compressor system? +
This single-stage system costs significantly less upfront and operates reliably for 15-20 years. Variable-capacity units run 20-40% higher but provide superior comfort through continuous modulation and whisper-quiet operation. Choose based on budget constraints and noise sensitivity.
What does the lifetime heat exchanger warranty really cover? +
It covers heat exchanger failure from manufacturing defects and material breakdown. This does not cover damage from poor maintenance, improper installation, or refrigerant contamination. Labor is your responsibility, but the part replacement cost is eliminated.
