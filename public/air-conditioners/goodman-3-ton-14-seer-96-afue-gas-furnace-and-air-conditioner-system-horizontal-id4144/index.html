Review

The Goodman 3-ton gas furnace and air conditioner system occupies a practical middle ground in the residential HVAC market. This horizontal configuration addresses a real installation constraint, filling space where traditional vertical units won't fit. Goodman's reputation for contractor-friendly pricing and straightforward service parts availability makes this an attractive option for replacement projects in established homes where ductwork runs through tight attics or low crawlspaces.

Efficiency numbers tell a solid story here. The furnace delivers 96% AFUE with dual-stage burners that modulate between high and low fire to match heating demand, translating to approximately 80,000 BTU of usable heat output per hour. The air conditioner side rates 16 SEER2 with 34,800 cooling BTU capacity using a scroll compressor and R410A or R22 refrigerant. The variable-speed ECM blower motor running up to 1,600 CFM ensures air distribution improves comfort while reducing short-cycling losses that waste energy in single-speed designs.

Installation

This unit requires 208/230V single-phase 60 Hz power with a 30-amp maximum breaker (18.7 amps minimum). The plastic PVC exhaust flue simplifies routing compared to traditional metal vents, a major advantage in attics. Horizontal orientation demands proper refrigerant line sizing using the included 3/8-inch liquid and 7/8-inch suction lines with 3/4-inch insulation thickness. Standard installation takes 4-6 hours for experienced HVAC technicians familiar with Goodman systems.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman includes a 10-year parts warranty upon online registration, covering compressor, heat exchanger, and most internal components. This is competitive for mid-tier equipment but note that evaporator coil coverage is limited and requires separate documentation. Registration is mandatory to activate the full warranty period, so documentation at installation matters.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 96% AFUE compare to my current furnace in real dollars? +
If replacing a 78% AFUE unit heating a 2,000 sq ft home, expect approximately 15-20% lower heating bills, typically $300-500 annually depending on natural gas rates and climate zone. The 80,000 BTU output matches most residential three-ton air conditioning systems, ensuring balanced heating and cooling.
Can this horizontal unit work in a tight attic where I have only 26 inches of vertical space? +
Yes, the 21-inch height plus ductwork clearance requires approximately 28-30 inches total. Confirm your actual attic opening and measure twice before ordering. The compact footprint is specifically designed for this constraint, making retrofits feasible where traditional upright cabinets fail.
Is 16 SEER2 cooling adequate for a three-ton system in hot climates? +
16 SEER2 is respectable for a single-stage condenser in moderate to hot climates, delivering solid efficiency during peak summer, though two-stage competitors reach 18-20 SEER2. The 34,800 cooling BTU output handles typical three-ton loads; your climate zone and home insulation determine adequacy more than equipment rating.
Should I choose R410A or R22 refrigerant for this system? +
R410A is standard in all new systems and required by EPA regulations since 2020. R22 availability is declining rapidly and phased out by 2030. Specify R410A unless you're retrofitting an existing R22 system and want compatibility, which reduces efficiency and increases costs unnecessarily.
What happens if I don't register the warranty online? +
Without registration, your warranty reverts to the manufacturer's limited coverage, typically 5 years instead of 10. Registration takes 10 minutes and requires your AHRI certificate number 7368843 and proof of installation. Do this before your technician leaves the job site.
