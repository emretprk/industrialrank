Review

The Goodman 3.5-ton split system sits squarely in the mainstream HVAC market, offering a practical upgrade path for homeowners retiring older single-stage equipment. This upflow configuration with 80,000 BTU heating capacity and 42,000 BTU cooling output addresses typical residential loads across modest-sized homes. Goodman has earned respect as a reliable workhorse brand, and this model reflects that positioning without premium pricing or cutting-edge efficiency claims.

Performance specs reveal balanced design choices. The 80% AFUE furnace with two-stage burners reduces energy waste compared to 60-65% older units, though it trails modern 95% systems. The 13 SEER air conditioner delivers respectable cooling efficiency but operates as single-stage only, meaning it cannot modulate capacity on cooler days. The high-efficiency multi-speed blower at 2000 CFM maximum airflow promotes even temperature distribution while the 75 dB noise level remains acceptable for residential spaces.

Installation

Installation complexity is moderate. The metal exhaust flue requirement is standard for gas furnaces. Electrical needs are straightforward, requiring 208/230V single-phase 60 Hz service with breaker sizing between 23.9 and 40 amps. Physical dimensions of 26 inches height, 21 inches width, and 21 inches depth fit standard closets and furnace rooms. A-coil configuration with 3/8-inch liquid valve and 7/8-inch suction line requires proper refrigerant line insulation.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year parts and compressor warranty covers major failures when registered online, which is becoming industry standard. This timeframe provides reasonable protection during the critical early years while falling short of some competitors offering 12-year coverage. Labor costs, refrigerant, and diagnostics typically fall outside coverage, so review the full warranty document before purchase.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much will this system cost to operate compared to my old 1990s furnace? +
An 80% AFUE unit saves approximately 25-30% on heating fuel versus a 60% AFUE furnace from that era. The 13 SEER air conditioner consumes roughly 20-25% less cooling electricity than 10-11 SEER units. Actual savings depend on your climate, usage patterns, and local utility rates, but expect noticeable reductions on winter and summer bills.
Can I install this in my attic, or does it need to be in the basement? +
Yes, this unit is rated for both upflow and downflow configurations. Basement installation uses upflow orientation, while attic placement uses downflow mode. Your HVAC technician will position the furnace appropriately during installation and ensure proper ductwork connections for efficient airflow distribution throughout your home.
What's the difference between two-stage and single-stage heating? +
Two-stage burners operate at lower capacity during mild winter weather, reducing cycling and improving temperature consistency. Single-stage systems run full-blast whenever heat is needed. This furnace's two-stage design enhances comfort and slightly reduces energy consumption during shoulder seasons, though heating season savings vary by climate.
Why is this 13 SEER system less efficient than advertised 16 SEER units at similar prices? +
SEER ratings reflect laboratory conditions and paired component efficiency. Higher SEER units typically use variable-capacity compressors, enhanced coil designs, and better insulation. This system's single-stage compressor and traditional reciprocating design deliver reliable performance at lower cost, prioritizing value over maximum efficiency optimization.
Does the 10-year warranty cover labor if my compressor fails? +
No, the 10-year warranty covers the compressor and parts only. Labor costs for replacement service calls are the customer's responsibility. Review your complete warranty document from Online Supply to understand exactly what expenses you'll owe if repairs become necessary.
