Review

The ACiQ furnace and air conditioning system represents a practical mid-tier option for contractors and homeowners needing straightforward equipment replacement. Built on Goodman's proven platform, this 3.5-ton unit delivers respectable efficiency ratings without the complexity or cost of premium variable-capacity systems. The horizontal configuration with right-side flue makes it particularly valuable for installations where vertical furnaces are impossible, such as cramped attics or tight crawlspaces common in older homes.

Performance numbers are solid across the board. The furnace achieves 96% AFUE efficiency on heating, converting nearly all fuel to usable warmth, while the air conditioning side delivers 14 SEER with 41,000 BTU cooling capacity. The multi-speed blower motor allows technicians to fine-tune airflow between 0 and 2,000 CFM, matching ductwork to the home's specific layout. Single-stage compressor operation means the unit cycles fully on and off rather than modulating output, which works well during cold snaps or peak cooling but sacrifices efficiency during shoulder seasons.

Installation

Horizontal installation requires proper clearance and support for the 120-pound unit. The plastic PVC flue exhausts to the right side, limiting mounting positions. Right-side flue placement simplifies venting but demands careful ductwork design to avoid condensation backup. Multi-speed blower accommodates various duct configurations. Technicians should verify 208-230V single-phase 60Hz electrical service and confirm 24-40 amp breaker sizing matches local codes.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year warranty requires online registration to activate full coverage. This protects against compressor failure, heat exchanger defects, and component breakdowns, though labor coverage varies by installer network. Registration takes five minutes and must happen within 30 days of installation. This warranty period is competitive for mid-tier equipment but trails some premium manufacturers offering 12-15 year terms.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does this furnace show both 3.5 ton and 5 ton tonnage in the specs? +
Tonnage confusion arises from the coil matching the condenser capacity rating. The actual system operates at 3.5 tons cooling capacity with 41,000 BTU output. The 5-ton reference likely indicates the matched evaporator coil's rated capacity. Verify coil tonnage with your installer to confirm proper refrigerant charge.
Can I install this in a vertical attic space instead of horizontal? +
No, this unit is specifically configured for horizontal operation with the flue exiting to the right side. Vertical installations require an upflow furnace model. Attempting vertical mounting risks condensation issues, improper draft, and warranty voiding. Contact ACiQ for upflow alternatives if your space permits.
How does 14 SEER compare to newer 15-16 SEER systems? +
Each SEER point roughly equals 3-5% efficiency gain. A 16 SEER system costs 15-25% more but recovers that premium over 8-12 years in mild climates. For moderate regions, 14 SEER balances upfront cost against utility savings effectively. Northern climates prioritize heating efficiency (96% AFUE), where this unit excels.
Is a single-stage compressor problematic compared to two-stage or variable units? +
Single-stage compressors run at full capacity or off, cycling on mild days when partial cooling would suffice. This increases wear and reduces efficiency 5-8% on shoulder season days. However, they're reliable, simpler to repair, and far less expensive than variable-capacity systems. Better for steady summer/winter use than fluctuating spring/fall demand.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Covers defects in compressor, heat exchanger, coils, and components under normal operation. Labor coverage depends on your installer's service plan. Does not cover maintenance items like filters, refrigerant leaks from poor installation, or damage from improper electrical sizing. Online registration is mandatory within 30 days.
