Review

Goodman has dominated the value HVAC market for years, and this 3-ton downflow system shows why. At $643 for a matched furnace and air conditioner combo, it undercuts most competitors by hundreds of dollars while maintaining solid efficiency credentials. The 97% AFUE furnace and 16 SEER2 air conditioner both carry Energy Star labels, meaning buyers get certified efficiency without premium pricing. The downflow configuration fits tight spaces, making this a practical choice for attic installations in existing homes.

Performance specs are honest without pretense. The furnace delivers 80,000 BTU of heating using a modulating burner that adjusts output, and the air conditioner produces 34,800 BTU of cooling with a scroll compressor. The variable-speed ECM blower motor caps out at 1,200 CFM and runs efficiently at lower speeds between cycles. Refrigerant compatibility covers both R410A and R22, useful if you are replacing older systems. At 73.5 dB, noise levels run slightly higher than premium models, but typical for this price tier and cooling capacity.

Installation

Downflow installation suits attic or basement spaces where ductwork runs downward through the home. The A-coil design requires proper condensate drainage, and PVC or plastic flue venting is mandatory. Connection points include 3/8 inch liquid lines and 7/8 inch suction lines with 3/4 inch insulation. Electrical demand runs 115V single-phase 60 Hz with breaker sizing between 18.7 and 30 amps. Standard residential installation typically takes one to two days.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does 97% AFUE actually mean for my heating costs? +
AFUE measures how efficiently fuel converts to heat. At 97%, this furnace wastes only 3% of gas as exhaust, compared to 80-90% on older units. A typical home spending $1,200 yearly on heating might save $200 or more annually. Savings depend on your local gas rates, climate, and home insulation quality.
Can this system handle R22 refrigerant if I have an old R22 system? +
Yes, it accepts both R410A and R22, which is helpful during transition periods. However, R22 is being phased out and becomes increasingly expensive. Many contractors recommend installing R410A and retiring the old R22 unit completely rather than mixing refrigerant types across equipment.
Is a 16 SEER2 rating good for cooling efficiency? +
Absolutely. SEER2 replaced the older SEER rating in 2023 and uses tougher testing standards. A 16 SEER2 unit exceeds minimum requirements and qualifies for Energy Star status. Expect cooling electricity costs 20 to 30 percent lower than 14 SEER models in comparable homes.
How does the modulating burner improve comfort compared to single-stage heating? +
Modulating burners vary flame size based on heating demand rather than cycling full-on and full-off. Your home reaches target temperature faster, maintains steadier warmth, and uses less gas during mild days. Single-stage burners work fine, but modulating technology prevents temperature swings and improves efficiency.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Parts and compressor labor are covered when you register online. It protects major components like the heat exchanger and compressor. Installation defects and maintenance issues may fall outside coverage, so ask your installer about extended service plans if desired.
