Review

The Goodman 3-ton (or 3.5-ton, per spec sheet) horizontal gas furnace and air conditioner system represents a straightforward, middle-market approach to home climate control. Manufactured by Goodman, a brand with decades of contractor presence, this unit combines an 80,000 BTU furnace with 36,000 BTU cooling capacity in a single package. It's designed for horizontal installation in attics, crawlspaces, or basements, making it flexible for various home configurations.

Performance metrics look solid across both seasons. The furnace delivers 96% AFUE (Annual Fuel Utilization Efficiency), meaning 96 cents of every heating dollar translates to warm air for your home. Cooling-side SEER 14 efficiency is respectable, particularly for a single-stage scroll compressor. The unit uses R410A or R22 refrigerant and pairs with a multi-speed blower motor capable of 1,600 CFM maximum airflow. At 73 dBA, it operates at moderate noise levels typical of this class.

Installation

Installation requires standard HVAC practices: refrigerant lines (3/8 inch liquid, 3/4 inch suction), electrical connection at 115V single-phase 60Hz, and compliance with local gas and electrical codes. The horizontal A-coil configuration fits tight spaces. Plastic or PVC flue venting is required. Most licensed contractors complete installations in one to two days. Ductwork sizing should match the 1,600 CFM blower capacity.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this system with 10 years coverage when registered online, which is solid protection for parts and labor. Online registration is straightforward. Coverage typically includes compressor, heat exchanger, and major components. However, verify exact terms and regional limitations, as some labor coverage varies by location and installer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the product title say 3 tons but specs show 3.5 tons? +
The spec sheet lists 3.5 tons tonnage while the title references 3 tons. This appears to be an SKU or inventory labeling inconsistency. Confirm actual capacity (36,000 BTU cooling) with your supplier before purchase to ensure it matches your ductwork and home requirements.
Can I use this in my attic if space is tight? +
Yes, the horizontal A-coil configuration fits compact spaces. Dimensions are 34.5 inches tall, 21 inches wide, and 28.75 inches deep. Weight is 35 pounds. Ensure adequate clearance for airflow around return and supply plenums, and verify ductwork compatibility before ordering.
How does SEER 14 compare to newer systems? +
SEER 14 is respectable but not premium. Modern units reach SEER 16 to 18, offering 10 to 30 percent better cooling efficiency. However, at 96% AFUE furnace efficiency, heating performance remains competitive. Single-stage cooling limits part-load efficiency in shoulder seasons.
Is this system better than a heat pump alternative? +
No. Heat pumps deliver superior year-round efficiency in mild climates but struggle in severe cold. This gas furnace excels in harsh winters. Choose based on your climate, heating costs, and whether you need dedicated cooling capacity for hot summers.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Typically parts and labor on compressor, heat exchanger, and sealed refrigerant components. Verify specific coverage and regional labor limits with your installer. Online registration is required to activate coverage.
