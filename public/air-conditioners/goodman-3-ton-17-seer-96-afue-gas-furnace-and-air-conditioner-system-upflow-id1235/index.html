Review

Goodman's 3-ton upflow system represents intelligent budget positioning in the value HVAC segment. At $504, this unit pairs a 96% AFUE gas furnace with an 18 SEER1 air conditioner, delivering two-stage heating and cooling without premium pricing. The scroll compressor and variable-speed ECM blower indicate Goodman invested in operational efficiency rather than flashy features. This system targets homeowners seeking measurable energy savings and contractors needing dependable equipment for volume installations.

Real-world performance numbers tell the story. The 120,000 BTU furnace heats efficiently via two-stage burners that modulate between 40-100% capacity, while the 35,000 BTU condenser cools at 18 SEER1 efficiency. The variable-speed ECM blower reaches 2,000 CFM maximum, automatically adjusting between heating and cooling cycles. At 71 dB, noise levels remain acceptable for most installations, though not silent. Energy Star qualification unlocks rebate eligibility in most utility programs, offsetting initial purchase costs within 2-3 years.

Frequently Asked Questions

The specs show both 3 ton and 3.5 ton tonnage. Which is correct? +
The condenser is rated 3.5 ton (35,000 BTU cooling capacity), while the furnace delivers 3 ton equivalent heating output. This mismatch is common in budget systems. Size your installation based on the condenser's 3.5-ton capacity to avoid undersizing. Clarify with your installer before ordering components.
Can I install this myself as a homeowner? +
Not recommended. Refrigerant handling requires EPA certification, and improper charging reduces efficiency by 15-25%. Electrical work demands local code compliance. Hire licensed contractors for installation, but you can handle thermostat wiring and basic maintenance like filter changes yourself.
How much will this save me compared to a 13-15 SEER old unit? +
The 18 SEER1 cooling efficiency versus 13 SEER older units delivers roughly 25-30% cooling cost reduction. The 96% AFUE furnace versus 78-80% older furnaces cuts heating costs 15-20% annually. Exact savings depend on climate and usage patterns, but most owners recoup the investment within 5-7 years.
How does this compare to higher-end Goodman or Lennox models? +
Premium models offer 20+ SEER2, variable-stage compressors, and quieter operation above 80 dB. This unit sacrifices those features for price. If efficiency is your priority and budget is limited, this system delivers solid returns. If comfort and quiet matter more, allocate an extra $300-500 for upgraded models.
What if something breaks within warranty? +
Contact Goodman with your registration number and AHRI certificate (7368842). They cover parts replacement for most failures. You pay labor costs and any service fees. Response time typically runs 5-7 business days for authorization, so budget for potential downtime during peak seasons.
