Review

The Goodman 3.5 ton gas furnace and air conditioner system represents solid middle-market value in residential HVAC. At $736, it positions itself as an accessible upgrade path for homeowners moving beyond 80-90 AFUE furnaces and basic SEER equipment. This downflow configuration suits many standard installations, and the two-stage cooling with modulating heat stages suggest this is engineered for efficiency rather than bare-minimum operation. Goodman maintains strong contractor relationships and parts availability across most regions.

The efficiency metrics impress for the price point. The furnace delivers 97% AFUE with an 80,000 BTU heating capacity and modulating burner stages that adjust output between high and low, avoiding energy-wasting on-off cycling. Cooling performance reaches 18 SEER2 rating (marketed as 17 SEER) with a two-stage scroll compressor and 35,000 BTU capacity, running at 71 dB. The variable-speed ECM blower motor continuously adjusts airflow to actual load rather than fixed-speed operation, further improving efficiency. These features deliver tangible annual energy savings versus standard equipment.

Installation

This furnace requires a trained, licensed technician for safe gas line connection, vent installation, and electrical setup. The unit draws 115V single-phase 60 Hz power with breaker requirements between 21.9 and 35 amps. PVC/plastic venting is acceptable, simplifying exhaust routing. The A-coil style and sweat connections demand proper HVAC knowledge. Most installations take 4-6 hours including ductwork integration. Never attempt DIY installation due to gas safety hazards and code violations.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides a 10-year parts and labor warranty when you complete online registration at purchase. This exceeds industry minimum coverage and demonstrates manufacturer confidence in reliability. Registration is straightforward and typically takes five minutes. The extended period protects against compressor failure, heat exchanger issues, and major component defects. Read the warranty document carefully to understand exclusions and registration deadlines.

What's the difference between SEER and SEER2, and why does the listing show 17 versus 18? +
SEER2 is the newer efficiency rating using stricter testing standards. This unit scores 18 SEER2, equivalent to roughly 17 SEER under older metrics. The specs show both numbers due to transition period in industry labeling. SEER2 is the authoritative number for comparing to current competitors and confirming Energy Star eligibility.
