Review

The Goodman 3-ton gas furnace and air conditioner system targets the competitive residential replacement market with solid efficiency credentials. Badged as a 97% AFUE furnace paired with an SEER1 18 condenser, this unit checks boxes for homeowners seeking Energy Star certification and lower operating costs. Online Supply prices it aggressively, and the rebate eligibility adds real incentive value for budget-conscious buyers looking to reduce upfront expense.

On paper, the numbers look strong. The furnace delivers 100,000 BTU of modulating heat output with a 97% AFUE rating, meaning 97 cents of every dollar spent on gas becomes usable heat. The air conditioner side produces 35,000 BTU cooling capacity with an 18 SEER1 rating and two-stage scroll compressor. The variable-speed ECM blower motor maxes at 2000 CFM, adjusting speed to match demand rather than cycling constantly, which keeps noise at a reasonable 71 dBA.

Installation

This downflow configuration suits basements or attics where ductwork feeds downward or horizontally. The unit requires 115V single-phase 60Hz power with a 21.3 to 35 amp breaker, making it compatible with standard residential electrical. Critical for installers, the PVC plastic exhaust flue requirement differs from traditional metal venting, so verify local code acceptance before committing.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this system with a 10-year parts and labor warranty upon online registration. This is competitive for the residential market and covers the compressor, furnace heat exchanger, and coil equally. Registration is simple but mandatory, so contractors should advise homeowners not to skip this step.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet say both 3 ton and 5 ton tonnage? +
This is a data inconsistency in the product listing. The title claims 3 ton, but specs list 5 ton. The 35,000 BTU cooling capacity actually aligns closer to 3-ton output. Contact Online Supply for clarification before purchasing to confirm your actual system size.
Can this furnace use propane instead of natural gas? +
Yes. The fuel type spec lists both propane and natural gas compatibility. However, propane conversion typically requires field adjustment and a different orifice kit. Your HVAC technician must configure it correctly for your fuel source.
Is SEER1 18 significantly better than SEER2? +
The spec lists SEER1 18, which is the older rating standard. SEER2 is the current DOE metric and rates 15-20% lower than SEER1 equivalents. A SEER1 18 likely translates to roughly SEER2 14-15, still respectable but not quite as impressive under modern measurement.
How does the 97% AFUE compare to heat pump systems? +
At 97% AFUE, this furnace is excellent for gas heating. Heat pumps typically deliver 200-300% efficiency in moderate climates but fail below 25 degrees Fahrenheit. In cold winters, this gas furnace outperforms heat pumps and costs less upfront.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
It covers defects in compressors, heat exchangers, and coils with online registration. Labor and parts are included. Normal wear, improper installation, and lack of maintenance void coverage. Always register immediately after installation.
