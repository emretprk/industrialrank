Review

The Goodman 3-ton two-stage system sits in a competitive middle market where efficiency meets affordability. At $89 for the condenser unit alone, this represents the equipment cost only before installation, ductwork, and the matching furnace component. Online Supply positions it as an Energy Star qualified option targeting contractors and homeowners tired of single-stage efficiency penalties but unwilling to spend $15,000 on premium brands.

Performance numbers tell a practical story. The 18 SEER rating and 35,000 BTU cooling capacity suit 3-ton applications in moderate climates, while the matching furnace delivers 100,000 BTU heating output at 96% AFUE efficiency. The two-stage scroll compressor runs at 71 dBA, quieter than single-stage alternatives during partial-load operation. Variable-speed ECM blower technology supports up to 2000 CFM, enabling better dehumidification during shoulder seasons when cooling demand drops.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 18 SEER rating qualifies for Energy Star certification, reducing cooling costs compared to older single-stage units
  • 96% AFUE furnace efficiency means minimal heating fuel waste, especially valuable in cold climates with long winter seasons
  • Two-stage compressor and burner provide part-load operation, running quieter at 71 dBA during mild weather conditions
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor adjusts output from 0 to 2000 CFM, improving humidity control and comfort consistency
  • R410A or R22 refrigerant compatibility offers flexibility if you have existing line sets from older systems

Cons

  • Product listing shows conflicting tonnage specs: title claims 3 ton but specs list 5 ton, creating uncertainty about actual capacity
  • Downflow-only configuration limits installation flexibility in homes with basement or crawlspace furnace placement

Homeowners in moderate-to-cold climates replacing 15-year-old systems who can absorb installation costs upfront. Contractors managing mid-range residential projects where efficiency rebates matter and customer budgets sit around $4,000 to $6,000 for complete system installation.

Installation

Downflow configuration requires installation in an attic, closet, or utility space above living areas with adequate clearance for the 34.5-inch height and 28.75-inch depth. Sweat connections demand a competent technician for refrigerant line brazing. Plastic PVC exhaust flue meets current codes and costs less than metal alternatives. Electrical requires 115V single-phase 60Hz service with 21.3 to 35-amp breaker capacity.

Warranty & Reliability

Ten-year parts warranty applies with online registration, covering compressor, coils, and components. Registration is mandatory to activate coverage, so document your purchase and serial number immediately. Labor coverage is not included, meaning repairs after year one require service technician fees. This warranty depth matches mid-range competitors but trails premium brands offering 10-year labor protection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the 18 SEER rating actually save money compared to older 13 SEER systems? +
Yes, approximately 28% annual cooling energy reduction in moderate climates. A 13 SEER system cooling a 2,000 square foot home costs roughly $1,200 annually in cooling alone. This 18 SEER unit reduces that to about $860, saving $340 yearly depending on local rates and usage patterns. Payback typically occurs within 8-12 years through energy savings alone.
Can this replace my existing 3-ton unit with minimal ductwork changes? +
Possibly, but verify your current ductwork handles 2000 CFM maximum airflow without excessive static pressure. Older homes sometimes have undersized ducts causing efficiency loss. Have an HVAC contractor perform static pressure testing before purchase. Downflow configuration requires attic space, which some homes lack, creating installation complications.
How does 96% AFUE furnace efficiency compare to high-efficiency condensing units? +
96% AFUE is excellent, approaching top-tier efficiency without the complexity and cost of 98% condensing models. You'll recover 96 cents of every dollar spent on natural gas or propane as heat. The remaining 4% escapes through the flue, which is negligible compared to older 80-85% AFUE systems still operating in many homes.
Is Goodman equipment as reliable as Carrier or Lennox? +
Goodman owns the same parent company as Carrier and uses similar engineering standards. Real-world reliability depends more on installation quality and maintenance than brand prestige. This model's 71 dBA noise level and scroll compressor design are proven technologies. Budget models sometimes cut corners on components, so verify your contractor uses OEM parts during service.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover if something breaks? +
Parts coverage only. Compressor, coils, refrigerant circuits, and blower components are covered for manufacturing defects. Labor, service calls, and refrigerant are your responsibility. Registration must be completed within 30 days of installation to activate coverage.
