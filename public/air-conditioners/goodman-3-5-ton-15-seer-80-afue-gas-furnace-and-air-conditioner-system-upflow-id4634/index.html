Review

The Goodman 3.5 ton split system represents a solid middle-market choice for residential HVAC replacement. Manufactured by Online Supply under Goodman branding, this upflow configuration pairs an air-cooled condenser with an A-coil furnace setup. Energy Star certification signals compliance with baseline efficiency standards, appealing to homeowners who want certified performance without paying for premium-tier technology. At this price point, the system competes directly with other mainstream brands in the contractor-grade segment.

Cooling output reaches 42,000 BTU with a 16 SEER2 rating, translating to meaningful electricity savings versus older 10-12 SEER equipment. The furnace delivers 80,000 BTU heating through a two-stage gas burner operating at 80% AFUE efficiency. A scroll compressor handles the cooling cycle while the high-efficiency multi-speed blower motor adjusts CFM up to 2,000, optimizing airflow across varying demand. The 73 dB operating noise level keeps runtime reasonably quiet for a single-stage unit.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 16 SEER2 efficiency rating qualifies for Energy Star certification and delivers measurable cooling cost reductions
  • 80% AFUE furnace meets baseline efficiency standards with two-stage burner for better temperature control
  • High-efficiency multi-speed blower motor adjusts to varying load conditions, improving comfort and reducing energy waste
  • Scroll compressor design is proven, reliable, and widely supported by HVAC technicians nationwide
  • 10-year warranty with online registration provides decent protection for homeowners investing in this system
  • 42,000 BTU cooling capacity pairs well with 80,000 BTU heating for balanced seasonal performance
  • Single-stage scroll compressor runs quieter than rotary designs at 73 dB

Cons

  • 80% AFUE furnace lags behind high-efficiency units (90% AFUE) by $800-1200 over system lifetime
  • Single-stage compressor cannot modulate cooling, creating minor temperature swings in mild weather
  • Specification discrepancy lists both 3.5 ton and 5 ton tonnage, creating confusion for proper sizing
  • No variable-speed compressor means less flexible part-load efficiency during shoulder seasons

Who Should Buy

Best For

Homeowners in moderate climates seeking dependable performance without premium costs qualify for this system. Contractors installing budget-tier equipment for rental properties or first-time buyers benefit from strong reliability and established parts availability.

Consider Alternatives If

Skip this unit if you need maximum efficiency (pay more for 18+ SEER2), live in extreme heat/cold zones requiring oversized capacity, or want modulating compressor technology for superior part-load comfort.

Installation

Upflow/horizontal configuration fits most standard attic or closet installations. Requires 120V single-phase 60 Hz power with breaker sizing between 23.5 and 40 amps, matching standard residential electrical service. Suction line insulation (1/2 inch thickness) and 50-foot maximum line length accommodate typical residential distances between outdoor and indoor units. Metal exhaust flue requirement means proper ductwork planning is essential.

Warranty & Reliability

Ten-year parts and labor warranty activates upon online registration, protecting against manufacturer defects during the critical first decade. Coverage includes compressor, heat exchanger, and functional components but excludes wear items and maintenance-related failures. This matches industry standard for equipment at this price tier, though premium brands sometimes offer 12-year protection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this system qualify for federal tax credits or rebates? +
Energy Star certification makes it eligible for some utility rebates, but 16 SEER2 falls below the 18 SEER2 threshold for many federal tax incentives. Check your local utility and state programs, as rebate amounts vary significantly by region and change annually.
What are the actual electrical requirements for installation? +
The system needs 120V single-phase 60 Hz service with a breaker rated between 23.5 and 40 amps. Most standard homes already have adequate service. Have a licensed electrician confirm your panel capacity and breaker availability before ordering.
How much will operating costs differ from my old air conditioner? +
Compared to a 10 SEER unit, the 16 SEER2 rating delivers roughly 37% better cooling efficiency. On typical summer usage, homeowners report 30-50 dollar monthly savings. Actual savings depend on local electricity rates, climate, and thermostat usage patterns.
Is this better than the more expensive Lennox or Carrier equivalents? +
Goodman offers comparable reliability and parts availability at lower cost, though premium brands include better warranties and quieter components. The real-world performance gap is modest. Budget the price difference toward maintenance and service quality instead of chasing brand names.
What happens if something breaks after the warranty expires? +
Component replacement costs typically run 400-800 dollars for compressors and 200-500 dollars for circuit boards. Scroll compressors have strong aftermarket support, keeping long-term repair costs reasonable compared to exotic designs.
