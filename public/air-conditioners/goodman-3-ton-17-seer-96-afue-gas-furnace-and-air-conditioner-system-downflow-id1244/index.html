Review

The Goodman 3 Ton combo system positions itself as the practical choice for budget-conscious homeowners and contractors. At $3,136, it undercuts premium manufacturers while maintaining respectable efficiency credentials. This downflow configuration works well in basements and utility closets common in many homes. Goodman's manufacturing scale keeps costs competitive without sacrificing essential two-stage technology, making it attractive for straightforward replacement installations.

Performance numbers tell the real story here. The furnace achieves 96% AFUE, meaning nearly every BTU of fuel converts to usable heat, translating to measurable savings on gas bills. The condenser hits 18 SEER2, which qualifies for Energy Star status and significantly reduces summer cooling expenses. The scroll compressor with two-stage operation reduces unnecessary short-cycling during mild weather. Variable-speed ECM blower motor adapts output from 0 to 2000 CFM, optimizing comfort while avoiding energy waste typical of fixed-speed designs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this unit really provide 120,000 BTU of heating? +
Yes. The two-stage burner produces 120,000 BTU on high fire and modulates down on mild days. This capacity suits homes ranging from 3,500 to 5,000 square feet depending on insulation and climate zone. Proper sizing should always be confirmed by load calculation before installation.
Can I install this myself, or do I need a licensed technician? +
Local codes typically require licensed HVAC technicians for gas furnace and refrigerant work. The installation manual (available as PDF) provides guidance, but refrigerant handling and gas connections demand EPA certification. Improper installation voids warranty and creates safety hazards.
How much will this system reduce my energy bills compared to a 10-year-old unit? +
Older furnaces often operate at 78-80% AFUE, while this hits 96%, roughly a 15-18% heating bill reduction. Cooling improvements depend on the old unit's SEER rating, but upgrading from 10 SEER to 18 SEER2 typically saves 25-30% on AC costs during summer months.
Is there a tonnage discrepancy between the product title and specs? +
The specs list 5-ton tonnage and 35,000 cooling BTU, but the product title states 3 ton. This inconsistency is confusing. Verify the actual capacity with the seller or manufacturer before purchasing to ensure it matches your home's cooling load requirements.
What happens if my system fails after 5 years? +
The 10-year warranty covers parts replacement at no cost for manufacturing defects. Labor costs and service calls are typically not covered. Proper annual maintenance extends reliability and can help avoid failures outside the warranty window.
