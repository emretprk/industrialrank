Review

Goodman's 3.5-ton gas furnace and air conditioner combination occupies a well-established middle tier of the residential HVAC market. This upflow configuration targets replacement installations where existing ductwork and clearances suit standard cabinet dimensions. The 100,000 BTU heating capacity pairs with 42,000 BTU cooling output, delivering moderate capacity for typical three to four-bedroom homes. Online Supply positions this as a sensible upgrade path for contractors and homeowners seeking proven performance without premium pricing.

The furnace delivers 80% AFUE efficiency with two-stage burners and a variable-speed blower motor, features that balance heating output with modulation capability. Cooling efficiency reaches 14 SEER with a scroll compressor and single-stage condenser operating on R410A or R22 refrigerant. The variable-speed blower pulls maximum 2000 CFM and requires 208/230V single-phase 60Hz electrical service with 23.5 to 40 amp breaker sizing. Energy Star certification validates its efficiency credentials, though 80% AFUE ranks below modern 90-95% alternatives now standard in efficient replacement markets.

Pros

  • Variable-speed blower motor improves comfort and reduces runtime energy waste compared to fixed-speed alternatives
  • Two-stage burner on furnace allows for modulating heat output, better matching heating demand throughout the season
  • Energy Star certified with 14 SEER cooling and 16 SEER2 rating for the AC portion
  • Compact footprint at 30 inches tall fits many existing installations designed for standard upflow furnaces
  • 10-year warranty with online registration covers manufacturing defects and provides reasonable peace of mind
  • Dual fuel capability accepts natural gas or propane without modification

Cons

  • 80% AFUE is dated for 2024 standards, high-efficiency alternatives at 95%+ AFUE would cut heating costs significantly in cold climates
  • 14 SEER cooling efficiency lags behind modern 16-18 SEER systems that compete at similar price points
  • Single-stage condenser limits cooling modulation on mild days, forcing full-capacity operation when partial cooling would suffice

Installation

Installation demands metal flue venting for the furnace exhaust and standard R410A lineset connections using 3/8-inch liquid and 1 1/8-inch suction lines with 1/2-inch insulation thickness rated for 50 feet maximum run. The 30-inch height and 21-inch width and depth fit most upflow closet installations. Dual-fuel setup requires only gas line selection, no hardware changes. Two-stage burner and variable-speed blower integration adds modest complexity but remains within standard contractor skill levels.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides a 10-year parts warranty when buyers register online, covering manufacturing defects across the furnace and air conditioning components. Registration is essential, as coverage drops significantly without it. This timeframe aligns with industry mid-range offerings and provides adequate protection for systems in normal residential use, though premium brands extend to 12-15 years on select units.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the actual cooling capacity, 3.5 or 5 tons? +
Specs list conflicting tonnage data. The 42,000 BTU cooling output equals 3.5 tons, making that the accurate tonnage. The 5-ton listing appears to be a specification error. Verify with your installer before purchase to ensure sizing matches your home's requirements.
Will this furnace work with my existing ductwork and clearances? +
The 30-inch height, 21-inch width, and 21-inch depth fit standard upflow furnace spaces in most homes. If your original unit fits a similar footprint, this should slot in cleanly. Have your installer measure beforehand to confirm vertical clearance and horizontal spacing meet installation manual requirements.
How much will this system cost to operate monthly compared to a 95% AFUE furnace? +
The 80% AFUE rating means 20% of heating fuel becomes wasted exhaust. A 95% AFUE system uses approximately 94% less fuel for the same heat output. In northern climates heating six months annually, this difference translates to $300-600 more per heating season on this Goodman unit.
Is 14 SEER cooling good enough, or should I upgrade to 16 SEER? +
14 SEER meets baseline efficiency standards but costs roughly 15-20% more to operate than 16 SEER models. In warm climates with four to five months cooling season, upgrading to 16-18 SEER often recovers costs within seven to ten years through reduced utility bills.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
The warranty covers manufacturer defects in materials and workmanship across the furnace and AC components. Online registration is required. It does not cover installation errors, maintenance neglect, refrigerant top-offs, or wear items like filters and thermostats.
