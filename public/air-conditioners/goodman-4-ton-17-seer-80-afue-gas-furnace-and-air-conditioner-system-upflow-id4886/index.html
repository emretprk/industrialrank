Review

Goodman's 4-ton gas furnace and air conditioner system at $2,071 positions itself as a practical midmarket option for homeowners and contractors seeking dependable HVAC without premium pricing. The combo unit packages a variable-speed furnace with an air conditioning condenser in a compact 30 by 24.5 by 21 inch footprint, suitable for upflow installations in basements and utility closets. With Energy Star certification on the cooling side and basic but proven Goodman engineering, this system appeals primarily to replacement scenarios and secondary properties.

The cooling side impresses more than heating. The AC delivers 47,000 BTU capacity with a two-stage scroll compressor and 17 SEER efficiency rating, utilizing R410A refrigerant and achieving 72 dB output. The furnace contributes 80,000 BTU via two-stage burners and 80% AFUE, paired with a variable-speed motor reaching 2,000 CFM maximum. For cooling-dominant climates, this setup manages temperature swings efficiently. For heating-dependent regions, the 80% efficiency means noticeably higher natural gas bills compared to 95%+ AFUE systems offering 15 to 20 percent better fuel economy.

Installation

Installation requires 208 to 230V single-phase 60Hz power and breaker sizing between 26.3 and 45 amps depending on demand. The system needs metal exhaust flue for the furnace and standard refrigerant lines with 1/2 inch insulation thickness. Valve ports are 3/8 inch liquid and 1 1/8 inch suction with sweat connections. Most technicians complete residential installs in one day, though ductwork modifications may extend timelines.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides a 10-year warranty on parts and labor when registered online, protecting against defects in manufacturing and materials. This middle-tier coverage beats budget brands offering five years but trails premium manufacturers offering lifetime compressor guarantees. Registration is simple and recommended immediately after purchase to activate full protection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec list 5-ton tonnage when the product title says 4-ton? +
This appears to be a data entry error in the specification sheet. The title, cooling BTU of 47,000, and pricing all indicate a 4-ton system. Verify with the retailer before purchase to ensure correct unit size for your home's cooling load calculations.
Can I use this furnace with propane instead of natural gas? +
Yes, this Goodman unit supports both fuels. The dual-fuel capability makes it ideal for areas without natural gas service. Conversion typically requires a minor orifice adjustment during installation, handled by most HVAC technicians.
How does 80% AFUE compare to modern high-efficiency furnaces? +
80% AFUE means you lose 20 percent of fuel energy as waste heat up the chimney. Modern 95% AFUE systems recover that heat, cutting heating costs by roughly 15 to 18 percent. Over 15 years, upgrading to 95% AFUE saves most homeowners $1,500 to $3,000 in heating fuel.
How does this 17 SEER system compare to competing 4-ton units? +
At 17 SEER, this Goodman sits at entry-premium level. Competitors like Lennox and Carrier often offer 18 to 20 SEER models at similar prices, offering 6 to 15 percent better cooling efficiency. Budget improves but long-term savings justify the upgrade in hot climates.
Is the 10-year warranty good enough? +
The warranty covers major defects adequately for a budget system. Registration is mandatory. Consider paying for extended parts coverage if you value protection beyond 10 years.
