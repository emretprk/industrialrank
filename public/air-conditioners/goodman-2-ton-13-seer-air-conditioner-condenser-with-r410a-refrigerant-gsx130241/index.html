Review

The Goodman GSX130241 is a straightforward, no-frills air conditioning condenser positioned firmly in the economy tier of residential HVAC equipment. At $1,070, this 2-ton unit targets contractors and homeowners replacing failed systems without budget for premium upgrades. Goodman builds reputation on simplicity and durability rather than cutting-edge features, and this condenser embodies that philosophy. It's the kind of equipment you install, it runs reliably for years, and you don't think about it much after that.

This unit delivers 24,000 BTU of cooling capacity with a 13 SEER efficiency rating, which meets current federal minimums established in 2015. The rotary compressor is a proven single-stage design that cycles fully on or off with no intermediate speeds. Operating at 208/230 volts single-phase 60 Hz, it requires minimal electrical upgrades in most homes. While 13 SEER isn't exceptional today, it provides measurable savings over older 10 SEER equipment while keeping equipment cost accessible for price-sensitive buyers.

Installation

Installation is straightforward for experienced HVAC technicians familiar with sweat-valve connections. The 3/8-inch liquid line and 3/4-inch suction line are standard sizes widely available for field repairs. The compact 26-inch cube dimensions (27.5 inches tall) fit most outdoor pad spaces without requiring relocation. Electrical integration is simple, requiring only 10.3 to 15-amp breaker sizing. Typical installation takes 4-6 hours for a straightforward replacement job.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this condenser with a full 10-year parts warranty when you register online within 30 days of installation. This coverage protects against compressor failure, refrigerant leaks, and component defects during the decade when most failures occur. Online registration is quick and eliminates paperwork hassles. This warranty is competitive for economy-tier equipment and provides meaningful peace of mind during the equipment's most reliable years.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 13 SEER still a good choice in 2024? +
Yes, for budget applications. 13 SEER meets minimum federal standards and delivers approximately 20-25 percent better efficiency than pre-2015 equipment. However, 16-SEER two-stage units offer noticeably better part-load performance. If operating costs concern you, compare long-term monthly savings against the higher upfront price of premium units.
What systems does this condenser pair with? +
This unit requires a matching Goodman air handler or furnace of equivalent 2-ton capacity using R410A refrigerant. Mixing brands is possible but requires careful matching of tonnage and refrigerant type. Your HVAC contractor should verify compatibility with existing indoor equipment before ordering this condenser.
How loud is 75 dBA during normal operation? +
At 75 dBA, this unit runs noticeably louder than modern inverter-driven compressors, roughly equivalent to a vacuum cleaner. In quiet neighborhoods or adjacent to living spaces, you'll hear it cycle on. If noise is a concern, dual-stage or variable-speed units operate significantly quieter at partial load, though they cost more upfront.
How does this compare to higher-efficiency Goodman models? +
Goodman's GSX160361 offers 16 SEER two-stage capability for roughly $300-400 more. The trade-off: better part-load efficiency and quieter operation. For homes running AC 12-16 hours daily in hot climates, that premium may return via lower electric bills. For moderate climates, this 13 SEER model provides sufficient value.
Does the warranty cover refrigerant? +
The 10-year parts warranty covers refrigerant loss only if caused by covered defects like sealed-system leaks. Refrigerant loss from installer damage or accidents isn't covered. Always verify your technician uses proper evacuation procedures before charging.
