Review

The ACiQ 3.5-ton system represents solid value in the budget HVAC segment. This upflow A-coil configuration combines an 80,000 BTU Goodman gas furnace rated at 97% AFUE with a matching 42,000 BTU air conditioning condenser. At $647, it undercuts many competitors while maintaining Energy Star certification and respectable efficiency across both heating and cooling modes. The unit targets contractors and homeowners seeking straightforward equipment replacement without premium pricing.

Performance metrics show the furnace's strength in heating applications. The modulating burner stages adjust output for better part-load efficiency, while the variable-speed ECM blower motor delivers up to 1,600 CFM. Cooling performance is competent, though 13 SEER certification places it in the mainstream category rather than the efficiency leadership tier. The 75 dB noise level during operation exceeds quieter models, which matters for bedroom installations or noise-sensitive locations.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does this system compare to higher-SEER units in cooling performance? +
The 13 SEER rating delivers adequate cooling for moderate climates but lags 15-16 SEER competitors in hot regions. You'll notice 15-20% higher cooling electricity costs versus premium units, though purchase price advantage is substantial. Best for mixed heating/cooling climates where furnace efficiency matters more.
Will this upflow configuration work in my basement installation? +
Yes, the upflow A-coil design works well for basement placement with ductwork running upward. The 34.5-inch height and 21-inch width fit standard mechanical room spaces. Verify ceiling clearance for the suction line insulation before installation and plan adequate return air supply.
What does 97% AFUE actually mean for my heating bills? +
97% AFUE means 97 cents of every heating dollar becomes warmth, with 3 cents lost through the exhaust. Compared to 80% AFUE systems, you'll see 15-20% annual heating cost reductions on a $1,500 winter bill. This adds up quickly in northern climates.
Is the modulating burner better than a two-stage furnace? +
Modulating burners offer smoother operation than two-stage units, adjusting continuously to meet demand rather than switching between high and low. They typically deliver 2-3% better part-load efficiency. However, two-stage units cost less upfront while still providing comfort improvements over single-stage models.
Does the 10-year warranty cover labor or just parts? +
The warranty covers parts only upon online registration. Labor is your responsibility. Many contractors offer separate labor warranties, so confirm coverage with your installer before signing the contract.
