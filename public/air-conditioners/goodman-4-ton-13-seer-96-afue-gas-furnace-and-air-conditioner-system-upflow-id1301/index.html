Review

Goodman has built its reputation on delivering honest value to the HVAC market, and this 4 ton combination system reflects that approach. Pairing an 80,000 BTU gas furnace with a 48,000 BTU cooling condenser, the SKU LS381183850 targets homeowners ready to upgrade from older equipment without premium pricing. The upflow A-coil configuration fits standard residential installations where space matters, and the 10-year warranty provides reasonable peace of mind for contractors and DIY buyers alike.

The efficiency numbers tell the real story here. The furnace delivers 96% AFUE, which is genuinely competitive for non-condensing models and will noticeably reduce heating costs compared to 80-percent equipment. On the cooling side, 13 SEER represents solid mid-range performance that qualifies for utility rebates in many regions. The multi-speed blower motor provides flexibility for balancing comfort and noise, while the reciprocating compressor has decades of field-proven reliability backing it.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 96% AFUE gas furnace means heating efficiency near the top of non-condensing models, translating to lower winter utility bills
  • 13 SEER cooling efficiency is respectable mid-range performance that qualifies for many utility rebates
  • Single-stage reciprocating compressor offers proven reliability with straightforward maintenance and repair history
  • A-coil design in upflow configuration works for homes where furnace goes in basements or attics with limited space

Who Should Buy

Best For

Budget-conscious homeowners replacing 10-15 year old systems who want efficiency improvements without premium pricing. This system makes sense for those in climate zones where both heating and cooling demand exists and who value proven Goodman reliability.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 13 SEER compare to modern standards? +
13 SEER is solid mid-range efficiency that beats older 10 SEER systems by roughly 30 percent in cooling costs. Modern premium units reach 15-18 SEER, but those command significant price premiums. For replacement applications, 13 SEER delivers tangible savings without excessive upfront cost.
Will this system qualify for tax credits or rebates? +
The lack of Energy Star certification limits federal rebate eligibility. However, local utility companies frequently offer rebates for systems meeting 13 SEER or higher, and the 96% AFUE furnace may qualify separately. Contact your utility before purchase to confirm current incentives.
Can I use this for cooling only or heating only? +
Technically yes, though it makes little economic sense. The 96% AFUE furnace wastes potential value in cooling-only climates, while the 13 SEER condenser is unnecessary in heating-only applications. Match the system to your climate for best payback.
How does 76 dB compare to other condensers? +
76 dB is moderate, roughly equivalent to heavy traffic noise. Newer variable-capacity systems run quieter at 72-74 dB. If your unit sits near a bedroom or patio, consider placement carefully or look at premium low-noise models.
What happens after the 10-year warranty expires? +
Coverage ends, and repair costs become your responsibility. Reciprocating compressors typically run 15-20 years if maintained properly. Budget for eventual replacement, but this system should deliver solid lifespan before major failures occur.
