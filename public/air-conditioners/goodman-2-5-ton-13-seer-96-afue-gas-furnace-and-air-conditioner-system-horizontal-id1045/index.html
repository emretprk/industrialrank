Review

The Goodman 2.5-ton gas furnace and air conditioner system sits comfortably in the contractor-grade middle market. Rated at 80,000 BTU heating capacity with 96% AFUE efficiency and 30,000 BTU cooling at 13 SEER, this horizontal A-coil unit targets replacement jobs in established homes rather than high-performance new construction. Online Supply bundles the complete matched pair, simplifying purchasing and ensuring proper system compatibility.

Performance specs tell a practical story. The 96% AFUE furnace converts nearly all fuel to usable heat, translating to lower winter energy bills in gas-heated homes. The 13 SEER air conditioner provides competent summer cooling without cutting-edge efficiency, making it suitable for moderate climates and moderate usage patterns. Single-stage operation means the system cycles on and off rather than ramping capacity, a trade-off that keeps price down but misses efficiency gains during partial-load conditions.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 96% AFUE furnace efficiency reduces heating fuel consumption by roughly 15-20% compared to 80% AFUE units
  • 13 SEER cooling delivers solid performance, hitting EPA mid-range standards for air conditioning efficiency
  • Multi-speed blower motor allows customized airflow between 0-1200 CFM for balanced comfort and efficiency
  • Horizontal A-coil configuration fits tight attic or closet installations where vertical space is limited

Cons

Who Should Buy

Best For

Contractors replacing matched 2.5-ton systems in homes built between 2000-2010, and budget-conscious homeowners in zones 4-7 who prioritize upfront savings and proven Goodman durability. This system fits tight horizontal attic spaces where standard vertical units don't work.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this system work with both R410A and R22 refrigerant? +
The specs indicate compatibility with both R410A and R22, though the condenser ships pre-charged with one type. Verify your installation requirements and existing system type before ordering, as mixing refrigerants causes equipment failure and voids warranty protection.
Can this horizontal unit fit in a standard attic installation? +
Yes. At 34.5 inches tall and 28.75 inches deep with right-side flue venting, it fits typical attic framing. Confirm your joist spacing and plumbing routing beforehand. You'll need 50 feet of insulated suction line, included per the spec sheet.
How does 13 SEER compare to newer high-efficiency systems? +
13 SEER meets 2006 federal minimums but trails modern units rated 16-21 SEER. Expect 15-25% higher summer cooling costs than premium systems. For moderate climates with moderate usage, the efficiency gap rarely justifies the equipment premium, making this suitable for budget-conscious replacements.
Why single-stage instead of variable-capacity operation? +
Single-stage keeps cost down by eliminating variable valve and control complexity. It cycles on and off fully rather than ramping capacity, slightly reducing part-load efficiency. Trade-off favors upfront affordability over operational optimization.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Goodman's 10-year parts warranty covers manufacturer defects in compressors, heat exchangers, and components when registered. Labor, refrigerant, and field service calls are not covered. Verify coverage terms with your installer before purchase.
