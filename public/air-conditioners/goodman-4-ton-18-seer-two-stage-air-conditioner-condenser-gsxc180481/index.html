Review

The Goodman GSXC180481 is a solid mid-range condenser unit designed for homeowners and contractors seeking reliable cooling with strong efficiency credentials. At 4 tons and 47,000 BTU capacity, it handles typical single-family home cooling demands without oversizing. Priced at $3,557, it positions itself as a reasonable alternative to premium brands while delivering respectable specifications that make it a fixture on contractor spec sheets across warmer regions.

Performance data shows the 18 SEER1 rating and two-stage compressor design working together effectively. The scroll compressor operates at 72 dB, which is acceptable for outdoor placement but worth considering if your condensing unit sits near a bedroom window. The two-stage capability means the unit runs at reduced capacity during mild cooling days, lowering energy consumption and extending equipment life. R410A refrigerant is standard, and the unit meets ENERGY STAR certification requirements.

Installation

Installation requires 208/230V single-phase power with breaker sizing between 26.3 and 45 amps, placing it within typical residential electrical service. The 290-pound weight is manageable for two technicians. Sweat-type valve connections with 3/8-inch liquid and 1 1/8-inch suction lines integrate with standard copper tubing. Pairing with a compatible two-stage indoor coil maximizes efficiency benefits, though it operates with single-stage equipment at reduced performance.

Warranty & Reliability

Warranty coverage is genuinely strong here: lifetime compressor warranty, 10-year unit replacement, and 10-year parts limited coverage. This stacks favorably against competitors and provides meaningful protection for owners planning 10 to 15-year system lifecycles. The compressor warranty especially appeals to contractors concerned about callback liability during the critical early years.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can this condenser be paired with my existing single-stage furnace and coil? +
Yes, it will operate but loses efficiency advantages. Two-stage compressors deliver full benefits only with matching indoor equipment. Pairing with single-stage components means the unit runs at full capacity constantly, negating the efficiency and wear-reduction benefits of two-stage technology. Consider upgrading the indoor coil if budget allows.
What electrical upgrades might I need for installation? +
Check if your home has 208/230V single-phase service with adequate breaker capacity of 26.3 to 45 amps. Most homes have this, but older installations may require panel upgrades. A licensed electrician should verify your electrical service before ordering to avoid costly on-site surprises during installation.
How much will this condenser reduce my cooling costs compared to a 13 SEER unit? +
The 18 SEER rating uses about 28 percent less energy than 13 SEER equipment over a cooling season. Actual savings depend on your climate, usage patterns, and local electricity rates. In warm regions running cooling six months yearly, expect $400 to $800 in annual savings, payback within 4 to 5 years.
How does the Goodman two-stage design compare to variable-speed inverter units? +
Two-stage offers good flexibility at lower cost than inverter technology, but inverter units provide finer capacity modulation and quieter operation. For most homeowners, two-stage delivers sufficient efficiency and reliability. Inverter models cost significantly more but excel in mild climates with frequent partial-load operation.
Does the lifetime compressor warranty apply if I sell my home? +
Goodman's lifetime compressor warranty is non-transferable and applies only to the original homeowner. If you sell the property, the warranty reverts to 10-year limited coverage for the new owner.
