Review

The Goodman 2.5 Ton horizontal furnace and air conditioner system occupies a sweet spot in the mid-range HVAC market. At $115 for the condenser unit alone (furnace pricing typically runs $800-1,200 separately), this combination delivers solid efficiency ratings without enterprise-level costs. Online Supply brands like Goodman have earned contractor trust through proven durability and straightforward installation, making this system a practical choice for residential retrofits and new construction alike.

Performance metrics tell the real story here. The furnace achieves 96% AFUE efficiency with dual-stage burners and a variable-speed ECM blower motor that adjusts output between 5-20 amps, reducing electricity waste. The air conditioning side delivers 29,000 BTU cooling capacity at 16 SEER with a scroll compressor and R410A refrigerant compatibility. Operating at 71.5 dBA keeps noise within acceptable residential ranges, though the single-stage condenser means efficiency dips during part-load operation on mild days.

Installation

This horizontal A-coil design requires careful ductwork planning since airflow direction is fixed downflow/horizontal. Installers need PVC or plastic exhaust flue capability, standard 115V single-phase 60Hz electrical service, and proper breaker sizing between 17.1 and 25 amps. Suction line insulation must reach 3/4 inch thickness, and refrigerant lines extend 50 feet maximum. Most contractors complete installation in 4-6 hours once staging location is confirmed.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides 10 years of parts coverage when you register online after purchase, a significant protection tier for residential systems. This covers compressor, heat exchanger, and controls but typically excludes labor costs. Registration is straightforward through their website and extends manufacturer support beyond the standard 1-year coverage, making it worthwhile for homeowners planning long-term ownership.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the cooling capacity (29,000 BTU) not match the heating output (80,000 BTU)? +
This reflects regional load differences. Heating demands peak in winter cold snaps, while cooling peaks in summer humidity. The 2.5-ton cooling (30,000 BTU approximate) handles most residential cooling loads adequately. Always confirm sizing via Manual J calculation to prevent oversizing, which wastes energy.
Can I install this myself, or do I need a licensed technician? +
EPA Section 608 certification is required to handle R410A refrigerant in most states. PVC flue and electrical work typically need licensed contractors. Many homeowners handle ductwork prep and staging, then hire professionals for refrigerant charging and safety inspections.
How much will this reduce my heating and cooling bills compared to a 13 SEER, 78% AFUE system? +
The 96% AFUE furnace cuts heating costs roughly 18% versus 78% AFUE units. The 16 SEER air conditioner saves approximately 20% cooling costs over 13 SEER equipment. Combined annual savings typically reach $400-600 in moderate climates, with payback in 8-12 years.
Does this system qualify for rebates or tax credits? +
Yes, Energy Star certification makes it eligible for federal tax credits (currently up to $600-2,000 depending on AFUE and SEER ratings). Many utility companies offer additional rebates. Check with your provider and IRS guidance, as incentives change annually.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Parts only. It covers compressor, heat exchanger, blower motor, and controls. You pay labor costs for repairs. Registration is required and free. Excludes damage from installation errors, refrigerant abuse, or improper maintenance.
