Review

The Goodman 5-ton horizontal system represents smart mid-market positioning in the HVAC replacement space. This combo unit pairs an 80,000 BTU furnace with a 60,000 BTU air conditioner, using proven scroll compressor technology and R410A refrigerant. At $2,071, it undercuts premium brands while maintaining Goodman's reputation for reliable, uncomplicated operation. The horizontal A-coil design specifically targets tight installations where vertical clearance or footprint matters, making it practical for attics and crawlspaces.

Performance-wise, the 80% AFUE rating meets federal efficiency baselines without excessive complexity, while the SEER16 cooling efficiency qualifies for ENERGY STAR recognition. Two-stage heating and cooling operation reduce short-cycling and deliver quieter, steadier climate control compared to single-stage units. The variable-speed blower motor adjusts between zero and 2,000 CFM depending on load, lowering electricity draw during partial-load operation. Requiring 208/230V single-phase power and between 37.2 to 60 amps keeps electrical upgrades minimal for most residential installs.

Installation

Installation fits standard horizontal applications with upflow capability. The unit weighs 301 pounds, requiring at least two technicians for safe handling. Metal exhaust flue is mandatory, so homes without existing metal venting infrastructure need ducting additions. Suction line measures 7/8 inches and liquid line 3/8 inches, standard copper sizes any licensed HVAC contractor stocks. Electrical connection is straightforward 208/230V single-phase, though breaker sizing between 37.2 and 60 amps requires panel assessment.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides 10 years of parts and labor coverage when you register online, one of the longer terms at this price point. This protects against compressor failure, heat exchanger cracks, and component defects, though it does not cover refrigerant or routine maintenance. Registration is essential since coverage defaults to one year without it. Ten years typically covers most homeowner scenarios, outlasting average equipment tenure in moderate-use residential settings.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the real-world heating output on natural gas versus propane? +
The 80,000 BTU rating holds steady for both fuels, though propane delivers slightly higher energy density per cubic foot. Most contractors recommend confirming your existing fuel line pressure and meter capacity during installation. Pressure regulators differ between fuel types, so specify your fuel source when ordering to ensure correct burner configuration.
Will this fit my attic space if I have limited height? +
At 38.25 inches tall and 35.5 inches wide and deep, the horizontal configuration works in tight attics where vertical units won't squeeze in. Measure your available space carefully and confirm airflow paths above and below won't obstruct. You'll need clearance for servicing the blower and heat exchanger annually.
How much will I save monthly compared to an older 60% AFUE furnace? +
At 80% AFUE, you gain 20 percentage points efficiency. On a 100,000 BTU heating load, expect 15 to 20 percent lower natural gas consumption, saving roughly $200 to 300 yearly in moderate climates. Actual savings depend on local gas rates and winter severity, but payback typically occurs within seven to ten years.
Is SEER16 enough or should I jump to SEER18? +
SEER16 handles moderate climates well with two-stage cooling efficiency. SEER18+ units save maybe $150 yearly in hot climates but cost $800 to 1,200 more upfront. If you're in the South or Southwest and running AC constantly, SEER18 pays off faster. For mild climates, SEER16 is sensible.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Parts and labor on compressor, heat exchanger, coils, and electrical components when registered online. Excludes refrigerant, maintenance, and wear items. Most failures covered fall under compressor seals and heat exchanger defects, protecting your largest investments in the system.
