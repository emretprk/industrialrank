Review

The Goodman 2.5/3 Ton Gas Furnace and Air Conditioner System represents a significant value inflection in the mid-range HVAC market. Online Supply prices this complete downflow unit at $643, undercutting most Carrier, Trane, and Lennox equivalents by 40 to 60 percent. Built on Goodman's proven platform, it combines a modulating gas furnace with a fixed-capacity scroll compressor, addressing both heating and cooling in a single SKU. The downflow A-coil design offers installation flexibility across attics, basements, and horizontal applications.

Performance specs are genuinely competitive. The furnace delivers 80,000 BTU of heating output at 97% AFUE, meaning 97 cents of every dollar spent on gas becomes usable heat. The AC condenser achieves 16 SEER2 (the metric replacing SEER1), cooling 29,000 BTU per hour using R410A refrigerant. The variable-speed ECM blower motor runs up to 1,200 CFM and adjusts automatically, cutting parasitic electrical draw versus constant-speed motors. At 71.5 dB, compressor noise sits at typical levels for scroll-type equipment.

Installation

Downflow installation requires right-side flue exit through plastic PVC piping, standard in most homes. Suction line (7/8 inch) and liquid line (3/8 inch) connect to outdoor coil; suction line includes 3/4 inch foam insulation to prevent condensation. Electrical demand ranges from 17.1 to 25 amps at 115V single-phase 60 Hz, fitting standard residential panels. AHRI certificate 7424270 confirms matched-system ratings. Most licensed installers complete setups in 6 to 8 hours.

Warranty & Reliability

Coverage spans 10 years on all parts when registered online, a solid mid-tier commitment. This covers compressor failure, heat exchanger cracks, and motor defects, but excludes misinstallation, refrigerant leaks from improper service, and wear items like capacitors. Registration is simple and typically takes five minutes through Goodman's portal.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the title say 2.5 ton but specs show 3 ton? +
The product listing contains conflicting information. Cooling capacity is rated at 29,000 BTU (approximately 2.4 tons), but tonnage field lists 3 ton. Contact Online Supply to clarify actual condenser capacity before purchase, as this affects heat load calculations for your home.
Can I install this myself if I'm experienced with HVAC? +
EPA regulations require certified technicians to handle refrigerant recovery and charging. However, experienced installers can handle ductwork connections, flue piping, and electrical wiring. Most homeowners hire licensed contractors to manage the R410A side, typically reducing labor costs by 20 to 30 percent.
How much will this save on energy bills compared to my 15-year-old system? +
Older furnaces operate at 78 to 82% AFUE, while this unit hits 97%, saving roughly 18 to 20 percent on heating costs. AC efficiency gains are harder to quantify without baseline SEER data, but 16 SEER2 cuts cooling costs 30 to 40 percent versus 10 SEER equipment from 2005.
How does this compare to Carrier or Lennox at higher price points? +
This Goodman unit matches or exceeds their mid-tier efficiency ratings (97% AFUE, 16 SEER2) but uses fixed-stage compressors instead of variable-capacity models. You trade some weather-responsive efficiency and slightly longer warranties for 50 percent lower total system cost. Ideal if budget drives purchasing decisions.
What happens if something fails in year 3? +
The 10-year warranty covers parts replacement at no cost. Goodman ships compressors, motors, and coils directly to your technician. Labor costs and refrigerant charges are your responsibility, typically running $200 to $600 depending on fault type and technician rates.
