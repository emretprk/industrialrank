Review

The Goodman 2.5-ton horizontal system represents a practical middle ground in the HVAC market, targeting contractors and homeowners prioritizing installation simplicity and heating performance. At the 89 dollar price point (equipment only), this unit competes directly against basic builder-grade systems while delivering legitimate efficiency certifications. The ACiQ branding indicates OEM manufacturing, ensuring quality control comparable to Goodman's residential lines. With 60,000 BTU heating capacity and 30,000 BTU cooling, the specifications align with modest-to-medium residential applications.

Performance metrics tell an encouraging story on the heating side. The 96% AFUE rating means nearly all fuel converts to usable heat, with minimal stack loss from plastic PVC venting. The two-stage burner operates at reduced fire during mild weather, extending equipment life and reducing short cycling. Cooling efficiency registers at 14 SEER1, which meets current federal minimums but trails premium units reaching 16-18 SEER2. The single-stage rotary compressor runs continuously at full capacity, driving efficiency down during partial-load conditions typical of spring and fall months.

Installation

Installation requires standard horizontal plenum mounting with proper ductwork connections. The 115-volt, 1-phase electrical supply demands a 17.1 to 25-amp breaker, fitting typical residential panels without upgrade costs. Suction and liquid line sizes measure 3/4 inch and 3/8 inch respectively, matching common refrigerant line gauges. Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this system with a 10-year warranty upon online registration, covering parts and compressor defects. This warranty length exceeds the 5-year industry baseline but trails 10-year extended coverage available on premium models. Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet list 3-ton cooling when marketed as 2.5-ton? +
The 30,000 BTU cooling capacity technically rates this as a 2.5-ton unit (30,000/12,000 equals 2.5). Older AHRI classifications sometimes round differently. Verify compatibility with your existing furnace tonnage before purchase to ensure proper refrigerant charge and system balance.
Can I install this horizontally in my attic space? +
Yes, the horizontal configuration with 21.125 inch height fits tight attic installations. Verify 26 inches of depth clearance and ensure PVC exhaust venting routes safely to exterior. Return air plenum access requires minimum ductwork modifications for this form factor.
How does 14 SEER compare to newer air conditioners? +
14 SEER meets 2023 federal minimum requirements but underperforms premium units achieving 16-18 SEER2. For cooling-dominated climates, expect higher summer bills. The single-stage compressor cannot modulate capacity, so it cycles on-off rather than ramping down during mild weather.
Is this Goodman system the same quality as higher-priced alternatives? +
The 96% AFUE furnace portion is genuinely competitive. The cooling side uses budget components with single-stage compression. Think of this as a heating-first solution where furnace performance justifies selection, while cooling represents acceptable baseline rather than premium efficiency.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Parts and compressor defects are included after online registration. Labor costs, refrigerant, and routine maintenance fall outside coverage. This warranty protects against manufacturing defects but requires proof of proper installation and maintenance records.
