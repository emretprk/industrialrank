Frequently Asked Questions

How much can I save annually compared to older air conditioners? +
An 18 SEER unit running 1,200 annual cooling hours consumes roughly 2,700 kWh versus 3,450 kWh for a 14 SEER model. At $0.13 per kWh, that's approximately $98 yearly savings. Actual savings vary by climate, thermostat settings, and system matching.
Does this require a smart thermostat to work properly? +
No smart thermostat is mandatory, but two-stage equipment performs best with a thermostat supporting stage control. Standard single-stage thermostats will run the unit on high-stage continuously, eliminating efficiency benefits. A basic two-stage capable thermostat costs $80 to $150.
What makes the two-stage design more efficient? +
Two-stage compressors modulate capacity based on demand. On mild days below 75 degrees, low-stage operation runs at reduced output, minimizing runtime. High-stage engages only during peak heat. This reduces energy waste from oversizing and extends compressor lifespan.
How does this Goodman compare to Lennox or Carrier 18 SEER units? +
Goodman undercuts premium brands by $400 to $800 while matching 18 SEER ratings. Lennox and Carrier often include enhanced compressor technology and quieter operation at 68-69 dB. Goodman wins on budget; competitors win on longevity and noise.
What's the difference between SEER and SEER2 ratings? +
SEER2 uses updated testing standards reflecting real-world temperatures. This unit shows 18 SEER under older metrics. Equivalent SEER2 rating would be approximately 15, making direct comparisons important when shopping newer models.
