Review

The Goodman GSX140361 3-ton condenser sits in the practical middle of the residential cooling market. This single-stage unit delivers 36,000 BTU of cooling capacity using R410A refrigerant and a scroll compressor, specifications that align it squarely with replacement and new-build applications where equipment costs matter as much as performance. Goodman's reputation for reliability without exotic features makes this condenser a go-to for contractors managing multiple jobsites.

The 14 SEER rating represents solid efficiency that meaningfully outperforms 10-13 SEER legacy equipment while remaining accessible at $1,406. The scroll compressor handles single-stage cycling reliably, though it won't match the seasonal efficiency of two-stage or variable-capacity competitors. Standard R410A compatibility keeps service costs reasonable, and electrical requirements of 208/230V at 18.7-30 amps fit standard residential panels without upgrades.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 14 SEER adequate for a 3-ton system in 2024? +
Yes, 14 SEER provides 30-40% efficiency improvement over legacy 10-SEER units and justifies replacement economics in most climates. Newer SEER2 ratings would score approximately 11-12, making this unit still competitive for applications where $2,000+ efficiency premiums don't align with actual utility savings.
What indoor unit pairs best with this condenser? +
Any Goodman 3-ton coil or air handler with matching R410A refrigerant and compatible linesets works effectively. Cross-brand matching is possible if cooling capacity aligns at 36,000 BTU and refrigerant type matches. Your installing technician can verify compatibility using Goodman's specification guides.
How does single-stage compare to two-stage cooling? +
Single-stage compressors run at full 36,000 BTU capacity whenever cooling is needed, cycling on and off more frequently than two-stage units. Two-stage operates at reduced capacity during mild weather, reducing energy waste and enhancing seasonal efficiency, but costs significantly more upfront.
How does this Goodman compare to a Lennox XC13? +
The Lennox XC13 offers higher SEER ratings around 16-17 but costs $2,200-2,800 more. This Goodman prioritizes budget accessibility with respectable 14 SEER performance. Choose Lennox for maximum efficiency, Goodman for best value in moderate climates.
Does the 10-year warranty cover labor costs? +
No, the 10-year warranty covers parts only, including compressor, condenser coil, and fan motor. Labor and refrigerant charges require separate service contracts or homeowner cost-sharing depending on your coverage plan.
