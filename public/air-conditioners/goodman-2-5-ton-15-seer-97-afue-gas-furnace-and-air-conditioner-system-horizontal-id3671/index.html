Review

The Goodman 2.5 ton (or 3 ton, per specs) horizontal gas furnace and air conditioner system sits in a solid middle ground for residential HVAC upgrades. At $115, this Online Supply unit bundles two major components into one compact horizontal package measuring 21.125 inches tall and 26 inches deep. It's designed for homes where attic or crawlspace installation makes a downflow/horizontal configuration practical. The system qualifies for Energy Star certification and includes a 10-year warranty with online registration, making it attractive to both DIY-savvy homeowners and professional installers.

Performance specs reveal a well-balanced system. The furnace delivers 60,000 BTU of heating output at 97% AFUE, meaning minimal fuel waste during winter. On the cooling side, the unit produces 29,000 BTU with a 16 SEER2 rating, comfortably outpacing minimum efficiency standards. The modulating burner automatically adjusts flame intensity to match heating demand, reducing short-cycling and energy waste. The variable-speed ECM blower runs at 1200 CFM maximum, allowing it to slow down when partial conditioning is sufficient. Both features reduce operational noise and electricity consumption compared to traditional single-speed furnaces.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 97% AFUE furnace efficiency recovers nearly all fuel energy, reducing heating costs substantially
  • 16 SEER2 rating on the air conditioner significantly exceeds the 13 SEER2 minimum standard
  • Modulating burner and variable-speed ECM blower optimize comfort and reduce runtime waste
  • Scroll compressor is proven technology with solid reliability records across residential applications
  • Plastic PVC exhaust compatibility simplifies installation in most homes, no masonry venting required

Cons

  • Single-stage compressor limits part-load efficiency compared to dual-stage alternatives
  • 71.5 dB noise level is moderate and may be noticeable during cooling cycles in quiet spaces
  • Specs show a tonnage discrepancy (product title claims 2.5 ton but specs list 3 ton, creating confusion)

Who Should Buy

Installation

Installation requires basic HVAC competency or professional service. The unit connects to either natural gas or propane with standard fittings. Refrigerant lines use 3/8-inch liquid and 7/8-inch suction connections with 3/4-inch insulation wrapping up to 50 feet. PVC exhaust piping simplifies flue venting, avoiding expensive masonry work. Electrical requirements are modest: 115V single-phase 60 Hz, with breaker sizing between 17.1 and 25 amps. Horizontal orientation makes it ideal for tight attic or crawlspace scenarios.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year parts warranty requires online registration to activate and applies to the entire system. This coverage protects against manufacturer defects in the compressor, heat exchanger, and major components. However, coverage excludes normal wear, improper installation, and refrigerant loss from external damage. Registration is straightforward and essential for claim eligibility.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between the product title claiming 2.5 tons and the specs listing 3 tons? +
This discrepancy appears in the manufacturer specs provided. The cooling capacity of 29,000 BTU aligns closer to a 2.5-ton unit (30,000 BTU standard), while the heating output of 60,000 BTU is typical for 3-ton systems. Confirm tonnage with the seller before purchase to match your home's load requirements accurately.
Can I install this myself if I'm handy but not HVAC-certified? +
Gas furnace and refrigerant work legally require EPA Section 608 certification in most U.S. states. Professional installation is strongly recommended to ensure safety, proper calibration, and warranty validity. Improper installation voids coverage and creates dangerous conditions.
How much will this system reduce my heating and cooling bills? +
Savings depend on your old system's efficiency and local climate. If upgrading from an 80% AFUE furnace and 10 SEER air conditioner, expect 15-25% total energy savings. The 97% AFUE furnace and 16 SEER2 condenser maximize efficiency, but actual results vary by usage patterns and home insulation quality.
How does this compare to a Bryant or Carrier equivalent system? +
Goodman is owned by the same parent company as Bryant and Amana, sharing core components but typically pricing lower. This unit's modulating burner and variable-speed blower match mid-tier Bryant performance at better pricing. Higher-end Carrier systems offer more advanced controls but cost significantly more.
What does the AHRI certificate number 7424269 tell me? +
The AHRI number confirms this specific combination of furnace and condenser has been tested and certified for safety and efficiency claims. It verifies the 16 SEER2 and 97% AFUE ratings are legitimate and helps contractors cross-reference compatible components.
