Review

The Goodman 3 Ton 13.4 SEER2 Package Air Conditioner occupies a practical sweet spot in the residential cooling market. At $3,355, it positions itself between budget builder-grade equipment and premium high-efficiency systems. This horizontal configuration package unit is engineered for straightforward rooftop or ground-level installation, making it a popular choice for replacement projects where contractors want reliable performance without oversized costs.

Performance specs tell a solid efficiency story. The 13.4 SEER2 rating represents a meaningful jump from legacy 10-13 SEER1 equipment, translating to lower summer utility bills for homeowners. The 35,600 BTU cooling capacity pairs well with the two-stage scroll compressor, which ramps output based on load rather than cycling on and off constantly. At 1,200 CFM maximum airflow and operating at 78 decibels, this unit balances output with reasonable noise levels for residential settings.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 13.4 SEER2 rating delivers solid efficiency gains over older units, typically 20-30% better than 10 SEER equipment
  • Two-stage scroll compressor provides variable cooling output, reducing short-cycling and improving humidity control
  • Horizontal package design simplifies installation on rooftops or tight crawl spaces where vertical units won't fit
  • 35,600 BTU cooling capacity with 1,200 CFM airflow properly handles 3-ton loads across diverse home sizes

Installation

Installation requires a dedicated 208/230V single-phase 60 Hz circuit with 24.4-35 amp breaker capacity. Horizontal mounting accommodates rooftops and compact mechanical spaces where vertical units don't fit. Total weight of 387 pounds demands proper structural support and lifting equipment. The horizontal design simplifies ductwork connections compared to vertical packages, reducing installation labor complexity.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this unit with a 10-year parts warranty upon online registration, a standard industry term. Parts coverage protects against compressor, condenser coil, and fan motor failures but typically excludes labor costs. Registration is straightforward and recommended immediately after installation to activate the full coverage period.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much can I expect to save on cooling costs with 13.4 SEER2 versus my old 10 SEER unit? +
SEER2 is roughly 25-30% more efficient than legacy SEER1 ratings. Real-world savings depend on your climate, runtime hours, and electricity rates, but homeowners typically see 20-30% reductions in cooling costs. In hot climates running 6-8 months annually, that could mean $200-400 yearly savings.
Can I install this horizontally on my rooftop, and will it need reinforcement? +
Yes, the horizontal configuration is designed for rooftop mounting. At 387 pounds, your roof structure must support distributed weight safely. Most modern residential roofs handle this easily, but older or lightweight roof trusses may need inspection. Consult a structural engineer if uncertain about roof loading capacity.
What does two-stage cooling actually do versus single-stage compressors? +
Two-stage operation runs at 60-70% capacity during mild cooling demand, then kicks to full 100% power on hot days. This reduces energy waste, improves humidity control, and minimizes annoying on-off cycling. You'll notice more consistent comfort, especially in moderate weather, plus lower energy consumption.
How does this compare to higher-end Goodman 16+ SEER2 units? +
Premium Goodman models reach 16-18 SEER2 with variable-speed compressors and quieter operation (72-75 dB), but cost $4,500-6,000. This 13.4 model covers most residential cooling needs effectively. Upgrade only if you're in extreme climates or want maximum future resale value and lowest operating costs.
Will R410A refrigerant be a problem when my unit needs service after 2025? +
R410A is being phased out, though service technicians will still work with existing systems. Future repairs may cost more as supply tightens and inventory decreases. If long-term serviceability is a concern, consider R32 or A2L units instead, though this model won't face immediate obsolescence.
