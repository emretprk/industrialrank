Review

The Goodman 3-ton horizontal system occupies a practical middle ground in the HVAC market. With its 120,000 BTU furnace output and 36,000 BTU cooling capacity, it handles heating and cooling for homes in the 1,500 to 2,000 square foot range. This unit ships as a complete paired system from Online Supply, eliminating compatibility guesswork. The horizontal A-coil design makes it particularly valuable for installations where traditional vertical equipment won't fit, such as tight attics or cramped crawlspaces.

Performance metrics tell a solid story. The 96% AFUE gas furnace ranks among the best non-condensing options available, meaning 96 cents of every heating dollar converts to home warmth. On the cooling side, 13 SEER efficiency hits the current minimum federal standard but doesn't break new ground. The single-stage reciprocating compressor operates at 74 dB, which is perceptible but not unusual for this class. The multi-speed blower motor at 2,000 CFM maximum airflow allows better humidity and comfort management than fixed-speed designs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is this unit 3.5 ton but marketed as 3 ton? +
The 3-ton designation refers to cooling capacity (36,000 BTU divided by 12,000), while the actual system size is 3.5 ton. Heating capacity reaches 120,000 BTU (10 tons), reflecting the furnace output. This is normal product naming in HVAC, where cooling capacity drives system classification.
Will this fit in my attic with horizontal ductwork? +
Yes, the horizontal A-coil configuration is specifically designed for this application. At 30 inches long and 21.125 inches wide, verify your space dimensions beforehand. The downflow/horizontal airflow direction means you need adequate space below for duct connections. Contact your installer to confirm measurements.
How does 13 SEER compare to newer high-efficiency units? +
13 SEER meets current federal minimums but lags behind 16-18 SEER two-stage systems. In moderate climates, the efficiency difference may only justify upgrade costs every 10 to 15 years. Hot climates favor higher SEER ratings due to longer cooling seasons.
Can this replace my old GSX130362 model? +
Yes, it's a direct replacement. The specifications list shows it replaces GSX130362, GSX130363, and GSX130365 models. Compatibility with your existing furnace and ductwork should be verified by your HVAC contractor before purchase.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
The warranty covers defects in parts and materials for ten years with online registration. Labor coverage varies by installer. Review specific terms with your contractor, as some components may have different coverage periods.
