Review

The ACiQ 1.5 ton air conditioner (model ACIQ-18-EHPD-UPG) occupies a practical middle ground in the residential cooling market. At 18,000 BTU capacity with 14 SEER1 efficiency, this unit targets contractors and homeowners who need straightforward, cost-effective cooling without premium pricing. The unit's use of R454B refrigerant reflects current environmental standards, while its rotary compressor design offers proven durability in light-to-moderate cooling applications.

Performance metrics show this condenser delivers solid baseline cooling performance. The 14 SEER1 rating meets minimum efficiency standards and aligns with units priced competitively in the market. Operating at 71 decibels during single-stage compression, noise levels are moderate but present. The unit accepts standard 3/8 inch liquid lines and 3/4 inch suction lines, making field connections straightforward for experienced technicians. Electrical requirements of 208/230V single-phase 60 Hz with a 30-amp maximum breaker fit most residential panels without upgrades.

Installation

Installation requires standard HVAC practices. The 21 inch width and 22 inch height fit typical concrete pads. The included 50-foot suction line with 3/4 inch insulation thickness prevents superheat loss during longer runs. Connect the 3/8 inch liquid line and 3/4 inch suction line per the CAPF installation manual. Upflow or downflow configuration adapts to most coil orientations. Vacuum, charge with R454B, and verify electrical connections per code.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides a 10-year parts and compressor warranty when you register online. This coverage is solid for the price point and protects against manufacturing defects. Online registration is required to activate full coverage, so save documentation. The evaporator coil carries separate Goodman warranty terms, so verify coil warranty details with your specific matched system.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes R454B better than older refrigerants in this unit? +
R454B is a low-GWP (global warming potential) replacement for R410A that meets current EPA regulations while maintaining similar cooling capacity. It's environmentally friendlier and already approved for new equipment sales, so you won't face phase-out concerns with this unit over the next decade.
Can I use this condenser with non-Goodman evaporator coils? +
Technically yes, if tonnage and refrigerant match. However, Goodman factory-matched systems ensure optimal performance and warranty coverage. Mixing brands may void warranties or reduce efficiency. Consult the CAPF specifications sheet before pairing with third-party coils.
Is 14 SEER1 good enough, or should I spend more on higher ratings? +
14 SEER1 is baseline modern efficiency. If utility costs matter or you're in a hot climate, 16-18 SEER units save money over time. For moderate climates and budget-conscious buyers, 14 SEER delivers acceptable performance without excess cost.
How does this single-stage unit compare to two-stage competitors? +
Single-stage runs at full capacity or off, cycling frequently. Two-stage runs part-load more often, cutting noise and wear. This ACiQ costs less upfront but uses more electricity during peak season. Choose based on climate intensity and budget priorities.
Do I need to register online for the warranty to be valid? +
Yes. Goodman requires online registration within a specified period to activate the 10-year coverage. Without registration, warranty terms are limited. Register immediately after installation to protect your investment.
