Review

Goodman's 5-ton combined gas furnace and air conditioner system represents a practical choice for homeowners seeking reliable performance without premium pricing. This upflow configuration fits standard return-air setups in most homes, while the 120,000 BTU heating capacity handles moderate to large residential spaces. The system carries a 10-year warranty with online registration and includes a rebate opportunity, suggesting competitive positioning in the mid-range efficiency market.

The 97% AFUE furnace efficiency is genuinely strong, converting nearly all fuel to usable heat and reducing winter operating costs compared to 95% AFUE alternatives. Cooling performance delivers 54,000 BTU at 16 SEER2 using R410A refrigerant in a scroll compressor design. The modulating burner continuously adjusts flame height rather than cycling full-on or off, while the variable-speed ECM blower delivers up to 2000 CFM. Together, these features provide measured comfort control and lower energy waste.

Installation

Installation requires 208/230V single-phase electrical service with breaker sizing between 29.6 and 50 amps. The PVC exhaust flue simplifies venting compared to traditional metal ducts. The A-coil style with right-side flue location suits standard upflow installations. Suction line insulation at 0.5 inches thickness meets refrigerant line requirements, and standard copper piping sizes match most existing residential setups.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year parts warranty applies upon online registration and covers manufacturing defects. This is standard for mid-tier Goodman equipment but requires proof of registration. Evaporator coil coverage is included separately. No labor coverage is mentioned, so installation and service costs fall to the homeowner after warranty period begins.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between this system's 16 SEER2 and older SEER ratings I see advertised? +
SEER2 is a newer testing standard (since 2023) using more realistic temperatures and conditions. It typically reads 15-20% lower than old SEER, so 16 SEER2 roughly equals 19-20 SEER1. This Goodman actually lists both ratings, with SEER1 shown as 16, making direct comparisons easier when shopping older inventory.
Can I install this in a downflow configuration, or is it truly upflow only? +
The specs list 'Upflow/Horizontal' configuration capability. However, the right-side flue location and A-coil design are optimized for upflow. Downflow conversion requires professional assessment, as exhaust venting and condensate drainage may need repositioning. Contact a licensed installer to confirm your specific space allows downflow.
How much will this system save me on heating compared to a 95% AFUE furnace? +
A 97% versus 95% AFUE saves roughly 2% of heating fuel annually. On a 100 million BTU winter season, that's roughly 2 million BTU gained. Actual dollar savings depend on gas prices and usage patterns, but expect 10-15% lower heating bills versus old 80% AFUE equipment.
Is this a good choice compared to higher SEER rating systems available today? +
At 16 SEER2, this is solid but not premium. Higher-end systems reach 18-20 SEER2 with variable-stage compressors, offering better performance in mild weather. This Goodman suits budget-conscious buyers; premium cooling performance costs 20-30% more upfront but recovers costs faster in hot climates.
What is the 10-year warranty actually cover if something fails after five years? +
The 10-year warranty covers defects in materials and workmanship. If a compressor or heat exchanger fails within that period, Goodman replaces the part. You pay labor and refrigerant charges. Warranty becomes void without online registration proof.
