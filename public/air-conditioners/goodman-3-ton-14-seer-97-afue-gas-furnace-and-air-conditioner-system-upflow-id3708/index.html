Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet list SEER 14 in the product title but SEER 16 in the specifications? +
The title represents a conservative marketing claim, while the actual condenser unit achieves SEER 16 efficiency when paired with compatible indoor coils. The higher SEER 16 rating is what you'll realize in real installations, meaning slightly better cooling energy efficiency than the advertised 14 SEER baseline.
Will this system work in a downflow application, or is it strictly upflow? +
The spec sheet lists configuration as both upflow and downflow, so it adapts to your installation orientation. However, verify with your installer that ductwork design and return air placement support downflow operation effectively, as some homes require specific configurations for proper airflow balance.
How does the 97% AFUE furnace efficiency compare to other gas furnaces at this price point? +
97% AFUE is genuinely excellent in the budget category. Most competitors in this price range offer 90-95% AFUE with single-stage burners. This system's modulating burner technology justifies the higher efficiency rating by avoiding wasteful on-off cycling at partial load conditions.
Is the single-stage cooling acceptable, or should I look for a two-stage system instead? +
Single-stage works fine in moderate climates where outdoor temps rarely exceed 95 degrees. If you live in Arizona, Texas heat, or high-humidity regions, a two-stage compressor reduces energy consumption during part-load cooling. For temperate zones, SEER 16 single-stage delivers sufficient performance.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover if something fails? +
The warranty covers defects in compressor, heat exchanger, and component failures under normal operation. It does not cover neglected maintenance, improper installation, or refrigerant loss from system leaks. Registration is mandatory, so complete that step immediately after installation to protect your investment.
