Review

Goodman's 3-ton gas furnace and air conditioner system occupies the practical middle ground in the residential HVAC market. At $2,142, it undercuts premium brands while delivering legitimate efficiency gains through its 18 SEER cooling and Energy Star certification. The upflow A-coil configuration works well in attic or basement installations where space allows for a 34.5 inch height and 156-pound weight. This system targets homeowners upgrading from 2010-era equipment who want measurable energy savings without the $1,000+ premium of top-tier brands.

The cooling side impresses with its 18 SEER rating, two-stage scroll compressor, and variable speed blower motor working together to maintain consistent temperatures while minimizing electrical draw. The 80,000 BTU furnace runs at 80% AFUE with two-stage burners, handling most residential heating loads through moderate winters. R410A refrigerant and 120V single-phase electrical requirements keep installation straightforward. However, spec inconsistencies appear here, with tonnage listed as both 3-ton and 5-ton while cooling capacity shows only 35,000 BTU, creating ambiguity about true system output.

Installation

Professional installation is mandatory for this gas furnace, and many jurisdictions require licensed technicians to handle both gas connections and venting. The metal exhaust flue requirement and proper propane or natural gas line sizing cannot be DIY projects. Electrical demands are modest at 21.9 to 35 amp service, making it compatible with standard residential panels. Expect 4 to 6 hours for a technician to complete a replacement installation with existing ductwork.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year parts warranty activates with online registration and covers manufacturing defects in the scroll compressor, blower motor, and heat exchanger. This coverage is standard for Goodman systems and protects against catastrophic failures. Labor and refrigerant recharges fall outside the warranty, so factor service calls into your operating budget. Registration takes 10 minutes and should be completed before the system fires up.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does 18 SEER actually mean for my cooling bills? +
SEER measures cooling efficiency as BTU output per watt of electricity. An 18 SEER system uses roughly 40% less energy than a 10 SEER unit from 2000. On a typical $150 monthly cooling bill, expect $85 to $95 with this system, saving $60 to $65 monthly during summer months in moderate climates.
Can I install this myself to save money? +
No. Gas furnace installation requires licensed technicians in most jurisdictions due to safety regulations around gas lines and flue venting. Improper installation risks carbon monoxide poisoning, fires, or explosion. Budget $1,000 to $1,500 for professional installation including labor and startup procedures.
Why does the spec sheet list both 3-ton and 5-ton? +
This appears to be a documentation error from the manufacturer. The 35,000 BTU cooling capacity matches a 3-ton system more closely than 5-ton, which should deliver 60,000 BTU. Contact Goodman directly with the SKU (GMVC800805CN) to clarify before purchasing to ensure it fits your home's cooling needs.
How does 80% AFUE compare to other furnaces at this price? +
80% AFUE is industry standard for mid-range units. Premium systems reach 95%+ AFUE, reducing heating fuel consumption by 15% but costing $2,000 to $3,000 more. For moderate winters, this 80% model provides adequate heat while keeping purchase price accessible.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
The warranty covers defective compressors, motors, and heat exchangers after online registration. It excludes labor, refrigerant, routine maintenance, and damage from improper installation. This protects against major component failure but leaves repair costs in your hands.
