Review

The ACiQ 3-ton horizontal furnace-air conditioner system bridges affordability and efficiency for contractors and homeowners seeking reliable mid-range equipment. Built on Goodman's proven platform and rated at 96% AFUE for heating and 13 SEER for cooling, this unit targets the replacement market where older units operate at 80-82% AFUE. The horizontal A-coil design addresses real-world installation constraints in attics and crawlspaces where vertical units simply don't fit. Energy Star certification and available rebates sweeten the financial case.

Performance specs are straightforward and solid. The furnace delivers 100,000 BTU across two burner stages, while the 36,000 BTU air conditioner condenser operates at single-stage capacity with a reciprocating compressor. Cooling capacity tests at 3.5 tons, providing adequate overlap for oversized comfort. The variable-speed ECM blower motor ramps from idle to 2,000 CFM maximum, reducing runtime electricity draw compared to fixed-speed motors. Operating noise measures 74 dB, acceptable for equipment rooms but notable in sensitive spaces. Electrical requirements are modest at 115V single-phase with 19.1 to 30 amp breaker sizing.

Installation

Horizontal configuration requires careful attention to ductwork connection and support. The unit measures 30 inches in length and weighs 120 pounds, manageable for two-person installation but demanding proper framing. Sweat valve connections on both 3/8 inch liquid and 7/8 inch suction lines require competent brazing. PVC exhaust flue is standard. Refrigerant charging with R454B demands EPA certification. Most installations run 4-6 hours for experienced technicians.

Warranty & Reliability

Ten-year parts coverage with online registration provides strong protection against compressor and heat exchanger failure. Registration is mandatory and takes five minutes online. Coverage excludes wear items like capacitors and blower motors in some circumstances, so review terms carefully. Goodman stands behind these units through a national network of authorized service providers.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 96% AFUE compare to my current furnace? +
Most units installed 10-15 years ago operate at 80-90% AFUE. At 96%, you recover 6-16 percentage points of fuel energy, translating to roughly 10-15% lower heating costs annually depending on climate. In cold regions, payback approaches 5-7 years through energy savings alone.
Can this system replace my existing Goodman GSX130362? +
Yes, this unit explicitly replaces GSX130362, GSX130363, and GSX130365 models. AHRI certificate 7368844 confirms compatibility. Your existing ductwork, refrigerant lines, and electrical service will likely need minor adjustments, but the system is designed as a direct swap.
Is 13 SEER adequate for hot climates? +
13 SEER provides moderate cooling efficiency. In hot climates like Phoenix or Miami, systems rated 14-16 SEER reduce runtime and cycling costs significantly. For moderate climates, 13 SEER balances performance and cost well. Compare annual operating hours in your region.
What's the real-world difference between single-stage and two-stage compressors? +
Single-stage compressors run full-capacity or off, cycling frequently on mild days. Two-stage designs run at 70% capacity during shoulder seasons, reducing electricity consumption and wear. This unit sacrifices some modulation efficiency for lower cost, acceptable in many climates.
Does online warranty registration really matter? +
Yes. Without registration, Goodman may restrict coverage to five years. Registration takes minutes online and activates the full 10-year parts warranty. Do it immediately after installation or purchase.
