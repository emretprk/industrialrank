Review

Goodman's 3-ton combo system represents a practical middle ground in the entry-level HVAC market. This upflow configuration ships as a complete unit combining a gas furnace and air conditioner, simplifying the installation process for contractors. At $647, it undercuts premium brands while maintaining decent efficiency ratings. The system handles both heating with a 100,000 BTU furnace and cooling with 36,000 BTU capacity, making it suitable for average-sized homes in moderate climates.

The 14 SEER cooling efficiency meets minimum federal standards but trails newer systems achieving 16-18 SEER. The 80% AFUE furnace rating means 20% of heating energy becomes flue loss, a significant drawback compared to 90-95% AFUE condensing models. However, the two-stage burner provides operational flexibility, and the multi-speed blower motor optimizes comfort at 2000 CFM maximum output. The single-stage scroll compressor uses R410A or R22 refrigerant, with modest 73 dB noise levels during cooling operation.

Installation

Installation requires standard mechanical skills and HVAC knowledge. The system needs a metal exhaust flue, 120V single-phase 60Hz power, and 18.7-30 amp circuit breaker. Liquid and suction lines measure 3/8 inch and 3/4 inch respectively, with matching valve sizes. The 3/4 inch suction line insulation extends 30 feet, providing adequate coverage for most installations. Upflow configuration suits attic or closet placement in single-story homes.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this unit with a 10-year parts and labor warranty after online registration. This standard coverage protects against manufacturing defects and compressor failure, though coverage varies by retailer and installation method. Always register immediately upon purchase to activate the full term. Extended warranties typically cost $150-300 and add minimal value given the standard coverage length.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 14 SEER compare to newer air conditioners? +
14 SEER meets minimum federal efficiency, but newer systems achieve 16-18 SEER. A 16 SEER unit reduces cooling costs by approximately 12-15% annually. Over 15 years, higher efficiency pays dividends, though this system remains competent for moderate climates where cooling seasons are shorter.
Is 80% AFUE acceptable for gas heating? +
80% AFUE is outdated. Modern 95% AFUE condensing furnaces recover more heat from exhaust. In cold climates, the efficiency difference costs $250-400 yearly in wasted energy. This system suits mild-winter areas where heating isn't the dominant load.
Will this system handle a 3,000 square foot home? +
The 3-ton capacity (36,000 BTU cooling) fits homes around 2,800-3,200 square feet in moderate climates. For southern regions needing extra cooling, consider a 3.5-ton unit. For cold northern climates, ensure the 100,000 BTU furnace meets heating load calculations.
How does this compare to higher-priced Goodman models? +
Premium Goodman units ($1,200-1,800) add variable-capacity compressors, 16 SEER efficiency, and quieter operation. This entry model sacrifices performance for price. Buy premium if staying 15+ years, otherwise budget models recover costs slower.
Does registration really matter for the 10-year warranty? +
Yes. Without registration, warranty often defaults to five years. Registration takes five minutes online and protects your investment. Keep proof of installation and purchase receipts to ensure coverage validity.
