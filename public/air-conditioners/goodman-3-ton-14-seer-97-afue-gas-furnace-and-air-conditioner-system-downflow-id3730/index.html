Installation

Downflow configuration means this unit fits basements or attics with floor or ceiling discharge. You'll need 30-amp breaker service with 115V single-phase power available. Liquid lines measure 3/8 inch while suction lines are 3/4 inch with factory insulation. PVC flue venting is required. At 34.5 inches tall and 120 pounds, two technicians can handle placement easily.

Warranty & Reliability

ACiQ backs this system with 10 years of parts coverage when you register online. Registration takes five minutes and activates protection against compressor failure, coil leaks, and component defects. Labor is not covered, so factor in service call costs if repair is needed. This warranty is solid for the price point but requires quick registration.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does 97% AFUE actually mean for my heating bills? +
AFUE represents fuel converted to heat. At 97%, only 3% escapes as waste. Compared to 80% equipment, you save roughly 17% on heating costs annually. Actual savings depend on climate, insulation, and gas prices, but substantial reduction is realistic over the system's 15-20 year lifespan.
Can I install this in an upflow position instead of downflow? +
No. This system is engineered specifically for downflow operation. Installing it horizontally or upflow compromises refrigerant flow, blower dynamics, and condensate drainage, voiding warranty and risking compressor damage. Choose an upflow-rated unit if your space requires it.
How does 14 SEER compare to newer SEER2 ratings? +
SEER2 uses updated testing standards roughly 15-20% stricter than SEER1. A 14 SEER1 unit typically performs like 11-12 SEER2 under realistic conditions. This remains efficient but expect competition from newer units claiming higher SEER2 numbers marketed at similar prices.
Is the scroll compressor better than reciprocating types? +
Scroll compressors run smoother, quieter, and with fewer moving parts than reciprocating designs, improving reliability. This ACiQ uses a proven scroll design. Trade-off is slightly higher initial cost. For most residential applications, scroll compressors offer better long-term value and lower maintenance needs.
What happens if I don't register for warranty coverage? +
Without online registration, warranty coverage is forfeited. Registration is free and takes minutes through ACiQ's website. You'll need your serial number and proof of purchase. Don't skip this step, as repair costs exceed the registration effort.
