Review

The Goodman 3-ton gas furnace and air conditioner system represents the practical middle ground for residential HVAC replacement. With 100,000 BTU heating capacity and 36,000 BTU cooling output, this upflow unit fits typical single-story homes and smaller two-story properties. Goodman has built a solid reputation for straightforward, maintainable equipment that contractors appreciate and homeowners trust. This SKU arrives ready for upflow or horizontal mounting, making it adaptable to most installation scenarios.

Performance specs align with mid-market expectations. The 80% AFUE furnace efficiency converts fuel to usable heat predictably, though condensing models achieve 95%+ efficiency if operating costs matter most. The 14 SEER air conditioner delivers reliable cooling without ENERGY STAR designation, reflecting its standard rather than premium positioning. Variable-speed blower technology pulls 2000 CFM maximum, allowing the system to match capacity to actual household demand instead of running full-blast constantly. Two-stage burner operation reduces heating overshoots and improves comfort.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 80% AFUE furnace efficiency is respectable for non-condensing units, keeping heating costs moderate
  • 14 SEER cooling delivers solid AC performance at 36,000 BTU output for efficient summer operation
  • Variable speed blower motor adapts to load conditions, improving comfort and reducing runtime electricity use
  • Two-stage burner provides heating flexibility, avoiding constant on-off cycling
  • Lightweight at 35 lbs with standard 3/8 inch and 3/4 inch line sizes, simplifying installation logistics
  • 10-year warranty with online registration covers manufacturing defects and gives reasonable peace of mind

Cons

  • No ENERGY STAR certification means it misses top efficiency tiers that can reduce long-term operating costs
  • Single-stage cooling compressor cannot modulate capacity, leading to less precise temperature control than variable-capacity units
  • 73 dB noise level runs louder than premium models, potentially noticeable during heating cycles
  • 80% AFUE is adequate but trails high-efficiency condensing furnaces that reach 95%+ efficiency

Who Should Buy

Best For

Budget-conscious homeowners replacing 10-15 year old systems in moderate climates who want proven Goodman reliability without premium pricing. This works well for properties where the furnace lives in a garage or utility closet rather than occupied spaces.

Consider Alternatives If

Homeowners in extreme climates, those seeking maximum energy savings through ENERGY STAR equipment, or anyone prioritizing ultra-quiet operation should explore condensing furnaces and variable-capacity systems.

Installation

This unit requires standard HVAC installation skills and common tools. The 35-pound weight moves easily, while 3/8 inch liquid and 3/4 inch suction lines use industry-standard sizes that most contractors stock. Upflow configuration suits basement or utility closet placement. Metal exhaust flue is mandatory for the gas furnace. Electrical requirements are modest, needing 18.7 to 30 amp breaker capacity on standard 120V single-phase service. Professional installation is strongly recommended.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman covers this system for 10 years against manufacturing defects when registered online. This represents solid protection but trails extended warranties some competitors offer. Coverage applies to sealed compressor, heat exchanger, and cabinet components. Registration is required within 60 days of installation. Labor and refrigerant are typically not covered under standard warranty terms, so confirm coverage details with your installer.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size home does this 3-ton system serve? +
The 3-ton cooling capacity (36,000 BTU) and 100,000 BTU heating output suit homes around 1200-1500 square feet efficiently. Exact sizing depends on insulation, climate, and sun exposure. A load calculation performed by your contractor ensures proper fit for your specific property.
Can this unit replace my old 3.5-ton furnace? +
The SKU shows 3.5-ton tonnage despite 3-ton rating, indicating it handles oversized loads slightly better than base 3-ton units. However, if your home needs true 4+ ton capacity, this system may underperform during peak summer cooling. A professional load calculation confirms compatibility.
How does 80% AFUE compare to modern high-efficiency furnaces? +
80% AFUE means 80 cents of every heating dollar becomes usable warmth, with 20 cents lost through the exhaust. Condensing furnaces at 95% AFUE recover additional heat from flue gases. Over 15 years, the difference adds up significantly in cold climates, less so in moderate regions.
Is 14 SEER cooling adequate for hot climates? +
14 SEER provides solid baseline cooling efficiency but trails 16+ SEER units in extreme heat regions. In moderate climates, it handles typical summer loads comfortably. Desert or Southern climates may justify the upgrade to variable-capacity, higher-SEER models for lower peak-season costs.
Does this unit need special electrical work? +
18.7 to 30 amp breaker requirement fits standard residential service panels. Most homes have adequate capacity already. If your breaker panel is full or your service is underrated, electrician upgrades may be needed before installation proceeds.
