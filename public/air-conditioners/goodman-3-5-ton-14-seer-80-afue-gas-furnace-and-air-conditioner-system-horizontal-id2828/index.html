Review

Goodman's horizontal gas furnace and air conditioner combination targets the practical middle ground where budget constraints meet functionality demands. At 3.5 tons cooling and 120,000 BTU heating, this system handles moderate residential loads without oversizing costs. The horizontal configuration makes it valuable for attic and basement installations where space is tight or vertical clearance limited. This isn't a premium system, but Goodman's market reputation and straightforward design appeal to contractors managing replacement projects where upfront cost matters.

The 80% AFUE furnace with two-stage burner operation prevents the efficiency waste of single-stage units that run full-blast regardless of demand. Cooling capacity of 42,000 BTU delivers 14 SEER efficiency, which is solid mid-tier performance that won't command Energy Star certification but outperforms older 10-12 SEER systems. The multi-speed blower motor at 2000 CFM maximum airflow adapts to varying load conditions, reducing runtime waste. The 208/230V single-phase electrical requirement fits most residential panels with 22-35 amp breaker sizing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is this 3.5 ton system listed as 5 tons in the specs? +
The 3.5 ton cooling capacity refers to the air conditioner, while the furnace heating capacity is separate at 120,000 BTU. The 5-ton tonnage reference in specs appears to be a data entry error or refers to a different capacity measurement. Clarify with your supplier before ordering to ensure the 3.5 ton cooling matches your load calculation.
Can I install this in an existing system designed for a different refrigerant? +
This unit accepts both R410A and R22 refrigerant, offering flexibility for replacements. However, verify your existing lineset compatibility before installation. If converting from R22 to R410A, you may need new refrigerant lines, larger capacity oil, and system flushing. Professional assessment is essential to avoid efficiency loss or equipment damage.
How much will the 80% AFUE furnace cost to operate compared to a 95% AFUE unit? +
An 80% AFUE furnace wastes 20% of fuel energy as exhaust, while 95% AFUE wastes only 5%. In heating-heavy climates, upgrading to 95% AFUE can save 3,000 to 5,000 dollars over 15 years. Run an ROI calculation for your region, factoring in installation costs and your natural gas rates to justify the upgrade.
Is 14 SEER good enough, or should I pay more for 16+ SEER? +
14 SEER is adequate for moderate climates but lags behind 16-18 SEER units in high-cooling regions like the South. Energy savings from higher SEER ratings typically exceed upgrade costs within 6-8 years in cooling-heavy areas. For mild climates, 14 SEER provides acceptable performance without premium pricing.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover on this Goodman unit? +
The warranty covers compressor, heat exchanger, blower motor, and refrigerant circuit defects with online registration. It excludes routine maintenance, refrigerant top-ups, electrical components like capacitors, and labor costs. Read the full warranty document through Goodman's site before purchase to understand exclusions and claim procedures.
