Review

The Goodman 4-ton gas furnace and air conditioner system sits squarely in the contractor-grade segment, offering reliable performance without premium pricing. This unit pairs a two-stage gas furnace producing 100,000 BTU with a 4-ton air conditioning component rated at 48,000 BTU and 14 SEER cooling efficiency. The upflow configuration works for standard installations, and the 10-year warranty provides reasonable coverage. Goodman maintains strong market presence through HVAC wholesalers, making parts accessible across most service areas.

Performance-wise, the 80% AFUE heating efficiency and 14 SEER cooling rating represent solid mid-range capability. The high-efficiency multi-speed blower motor and two-stage burner work together to minimize temperature swings, though the single-stage condenser means the cooling system lacks variable-capacity benefits. At 74 dB, noise levels are acceptable for residential settings. The scroll compressor uses standard R410A refrigerant with 3/8-inch and 7/8-inch line sizing, supporting up to 2000 CFM airflow through your ductwork.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 80% AFUE compare to other furnace options? +
80% AFUE means 20 cents of every heating dollar goes up the flue as waste. Modern 95% AFUE systems waste only 5 cents per dollar. Over 15 years, an 80% system costs $2,000-$3,500 more in fuel versus 95% units in cold climates, though upfront cost is $1,500-$2,000 lower here.
Can this unit fit in my existing ductwork? +
The 2000 CFM maximum airflow rating fits standard residential ductwork designed for 4-ton systems. Check your existing furnace CFM rating. If your old system was also 4-ton, this replacement should work without duct modifications. Undersized ducts will restrict airflow and reduce efficiency.
Is 14 SEER adequate for my cooling needs? +
14 SEER provides good cooling for most climates without premium efficiency pricing. In hot regions with long cooling seasons, 16+ SEER systems save more on energy. For moderate climates or part-time cooling use, 14 SEER balances performance and cost effectively, though it lacks variable-capacity features of higher-tier units.
Why isn't this system Energy Star certified? +
Energy Star requires minimum 15 SEER cooling and 95% AFUE heating. This unit meets neither threshold, disqualifying it from federal rebates and many state incentives. However, absence of certification doesn't indicate poor quality, just that efficiency doesn't reach premium-level thresholds.
How long does the warranty actually last after registration? +
The 10-year warranty runs from installation date with online registration completed within 30 days. Coverage extends to parts only, not labor. Most components remain under warranty, though some exclusions apply. Check your registration confirmation for specific coverage details.
