Review

The Goodman 3-ton horizontal A-coil system represents the value-tier option in modern matched HVAC packages. At $2053, this bundle bundles an 80% AFUE two-stage furnace with an 80,000 BTU heating capacity, paired with a 34,800 BTU (3-ton) air conditioner rated at 14 SEER. It's positioned for contractors managing tight budgets and homeowners who need functional climate control without premium efficiency standards. Online Supply's SKU LS38783850 carries Goodman's reputation without the mid-tier pricing that often accompanies their more robust product lines.

Performance specs show a variable-speed blower motor capable of 2000 CFM maximum, with the furnace burning natural gas or propane at two burner stages for improved part-load operation. The air conditioning side uses a scroll compressor with single-stage capacity, R410A refrigerant, and a decibel rating of 73.5 dBA, placing it in the moderate noise range for residential units. Energy Star certification validates that despite the 80% AFUE rating, the combination meets minimum environmental standards, though heating-dominated regions will notice higher seasonal fuel consumption compared to 90%+ efficiency alternatives.

Installation

This horizontal A-coil suits basement or utility closet installations with moderate clearance. The unit measures 38.25 inches tall, 29 inches wide, and 29 inches deep, with a 50-foot maximum suction line run. Requires 208/230V single-phase 60Hz service with 18.7 to 30 amp breaker capacity. Metal exhaust flue mandatory for furnace installation. Refrigerant line sizing includes 3/8 inch liquid and 7/8 inch suction connections with 3/4 inch insulation thickness.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides 10 years of parts coverage when registered online, protecting against compressor failure and heat exchanger defects. Registration is straightforward but mandatory to activate the full term, so documentation upon installation is critical. This warranty matches competitor baseline coverage and reflects Goodman's confidence in scroll compressor longevity. Coverage typically excludes labor, requiring contractor service calls to be billed separately.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 14 SEER cooling efficiency compare to current standards? +
14 SEER is solid baseline efficiency, but modern systems achieve 16-18 SEER regularly. The specs show 16 SEER1 rating, suggesting potential methodology differences. For moderate climates with 500-1200 cooling hours annually, this unit recovers its cost difference in 10-12 years versus higher SEER options.
Can this system handle hot climates or heavy cooling demand? +
The single-stage compressor cycles on/off rather than modulating capacity, making it less efficient in sustained heat. For regions exceeding 2000 annual cooling hours, upgrading to variable-capacity compressor systems reduces energy waste. The 34,800 BTU capacity suits 3-ton homes with average cooling load profiles.
What are the installation requirements for the horizontal A-coil? +
Horizontal A-coils fit tight attics and basements without tall ductwork. Requires 50-foot maximum refrigerant line run between indoor/outdoor units, metal furnace exhaust venting, and 208/230V service with 18.7-30 amp breaker. Upflow/horizontal airflow direction simplifies basement retrofit installations.
How does 80% AFUE furnace efficiency impact heating costs? +
80% AFUE means 20% of fuel energy exits as exhaust. Upgrading to 95% AFUE saves roughly 15% on heating annually in northern climates. Over 10 years, the efficiency gap totals 1500-2500 dollars in natural gas costs, though upfront system cost differences are often larger.
Is the 10-year warranty sufficient coverage for this price point? +
Yes. The 10-year parts warranty matches industry standards and covers major failures. However, parts-only coverage excludes labor, so verify contractor service availability before purchase to avoid expensive emergency repairs outside warranty terms.
