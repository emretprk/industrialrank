Review

The Goodman 2.5-ton split system represents sensible engineering for budget-conscious replacements and new construction. At $732, this unit lands in the sweet spot between bare-bones equipment and premium efficiency tiers. The downflow A-coil configuration handles standard residential installations, and the dual-fuel capability means it runs on natural gas or propane depending on your utility infrastructure. Goodman's reputation for straightforward reliability matters more here than flashy features.

Performance specs show 14.5 SEER1 cooling from a single-stage rotary compressor and 96% AFUE heating from a two-stage burner. That 96% AFUE rating cuts heating waste considerably compared to older 80% units, translating to real savings on monthly gas bills. The variable-speed ECM blower adjusts between 0 and 1200 CFM, preventing the constant cycling that plagues fixed-speed equipment. At 72 dB, noise falls in the typical range for single-stage units, acceptable for most residential settings but louder than premium variable-capacity competitors running at partial load.

Installation

This downflow unit requires standard lineset connections with 3/8-inch liquid line and 3/4-inch suction line, both common residential sizes. The 3/4-inch suction line insulation prevents refrigerant degradation. Electrical demands are modest at 115V single-phase 60Hz with 17.1 to 25-amp breaker sizing. PVC exhaust flue compatibility simplifies venting in most homes. Height of 34.5 inches fits standard furnace closets, though tight attic installations may need careful planning.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does this 14.5 SEER1 rating compare to newer high-efficiency units? +
14.5 SEER1 is solid mid-tier efficiency. Premium units hit 16-18 SEER, potentially cutting cooling costs 20-30% more over fifteen years. However, the 14.5 rating still qualifies for Energy Star rebates and cuts costs versus older 13 SEER equipment. Your payback depends on local electricity rates and usage patterns.
Can this downflow unit work in my horizontal attic installation? +
Yes, the downflow/horizontal configuration handles both orientations. The A-coil style adapts to side-mounted furnace placement common in attics. Ensure suction line runs uphill slightly to prevent oil slugging and plan adequate return air openings.
Is the 96% AFUE heating efficiency good enough to replace my 20-year-old furnace? +
Absolutely. Your old unit probably runs 78-82% AFUE, meaning 18-22% of heating energy is wasted up the chimney. This unit recovers 96%, cutting heating fuel consumption roughly 15-20%. On a $1200 annual heating bill, expect $180-240 annual savings in most climates.
Why is this single-stage unit cheaper than modulating two-stage compressor models? +
Single-stage compressors run full blast or off, no in-between. Modulating units ramp output smoothly, reducing energy waste at partial load and running quieter. You sacrifice that finesse for $732 pricing. It works fine in moderate climates but struggles in extreme heat or cold cycling.
Does the online warranty registration affect coverage if I forget? +
Goodman honors claims without registration but requires proof of purchase at claim time. Registration just streamlines the process. Register immediately after installation to avoid friction if problems arise years later.
