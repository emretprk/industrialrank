Review

The ACiQ ACIQ-24-EHPD-UPG is a horizontal-mount gas furnace and air conditioning combo unit designed for attic and crawlspace installations where traditional upflow orientation won't fit. This 2-ton system pairs a 40,000 BTU furnace with a 24,000 BTU cooling coil, making it suitable for small to mid-sized homes. The unit carries Goodman components and arrives with a 10-year warranty when registered online, positioning it as a practical mid-range choice for contractors seeking reliable performance without premium pricing.

The furnace delivers 96% AFUE efficiency through dual-stage burners that modulate heating output based on demand. Its variable-speed ECM blower motor adjusts CFM between 0 and 1,200 CFM, improving comfort and reducing standby power consumption. The air conditioning side achieves 14 SEER1 efficiency using R454B refrigerant in a single-stage rotary compressor. Electrical specs show 208/230V single-phase operation with a 14.3 to 25 amp breaker range, giving installers flexibility with existing panels.

Who Should Buy

Contractors and homeowners replacing aging horizontal furnace-AC systems in attics, crawlspaces, or utility closets where upflow/horizontal orientation is mandatory. This system works well in mixed climates where both heating and cooling need reliable efficiency without premium pricing.

Installation

Horizontal mounting simplifies installation in space-constrained attics and crawlspaces. The 21.125 inch height, 17.5 inch width, and 26 inch depth fit most framed cavities. Plastic PVC exhaust venting eliminates costly metal flue work. The variable-speed blower requires proper ductwork sizing for the 1,200 CFM maximum output. A-coil configuration integrates with refrigerant lines up to 50 feet, offering flexibility in condenser placement.

Warranty & Reliability

10-year parts and labor coverage applies when you register online within 90 days of installation. This warranty protects against manufacturing defects in compressor, heat exchanger, and coil. Goodman's evaporator coil carries separate warranty documentation. Registration is straightforward but essential, so confirm with your contractor that this step is completed before unit startup.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the actual heating capacity for a 2-ton system showing 40,000 BTU? +
At rated conditions, 40,000 BTU equals approximately 3.33 tons of heating output. This higher BTU relative to 2-ton cooling capacity is intentional for northern climates, allowing strong winter heating independent of cooling capacity. Check local design heating loads before ordering.
Can I use this system with my existing ductwork if it's sized for 1,200 CFM? +
Only if your ductwork is verified for 1,200 CFM maximum airflow without exceeding 0.1 inch water column static pressure. Undersized ducts cause the ECM motor to work harder and reduce efficiency. Have an HVAC contractor perform a duct leakage and friction loss test.
Is 14 SEER the same as 14 SEER2, or are they different ratings? +
SEER1 and SEER2 are different testing protocols. This unit shows SEER1, which typically rates 15 to 20 percent higher than SEER2. For accurate seasonal efficiency comparisons, ask for SEER2 equivalent or request testing data directly from ACiQ.
Why does the spec sheet list both ACiQ and Goodman branding? +
ACiQ is a newer budget brand under the Goodman Corporate umbrella. Core components like the compressor and coil carry Goodman branding, but ACiQ handles sales and support. Both companies honor the 10-year warranty, though verify your service territory includes available ACiQ technicians.
Do I need to register online to activate the 10-year warranty? +
Yes. Online registration within 90 days of startup is mandatory for the full 10-year term. Without registration, coverage may default to a shorter period. Keep your AHRI certificate 7368841 and installation date for quick registration.
