Review

This 5-ton Goodman split system sits in the practical middle of the residential HVAC market. Offered by Online Supply with a 10-year warranty and Energy Star certification, it targets homeowners and contractors balancing performance with cost. The downflow/horizontal configuration suits basements, crawlspaces, and mechanical closets. At 100,000 BTU heating and 54,000 BTU cooling capacity, it handles mid-sized homes efficiently without oversizing complexity.

The furnace delivers 96% AFUE efficiency, placing it in the high-efficiency category and qualifying for rebates in many states. The 16 SEER air conditioner offers solid cooling performance with a scroll compressor and R410A/R22 refrigerant compatibility. Variable-speed ECM blower runs at 2000 CFM maximum, adapting to actual demand. Two-stage heating and single-stage cooling create a hybrid approach: excellent heating modulation paired with straightforward, reliable cooling.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 96% AFUE furnace efficiency reduces heating costs by 10-15% compared to 80% AFUE models, translating to $150-250 annual savings in heating-dominant regions
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor improves comfort by ramping airflow gradually, reducing cold drafts and noise compared to single-stage blowers
  • 16 SEER cooling efficiency and Energy Star certification signal above-average AC performance for a mid-tier 5-ton unit

Installation

This unit requires 208/230V single-phase 60Hz power with 29.6 to 50-amp breaker sizing. PVC exhaust venting simplifies installation compared to metal chimneys. A-coil design connects standard refrigerant lines: 3/8-inch liquid, 1 1/8-inch suction with 1/2-inch insulation. Downflow orientation demands clearance below the unit for return ducting. Professional HVAC installation remains essential for proper refrigerant charging and airflow balancing.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides 10 years of parts coverage with online registration, a standard tier in the industry. Covers compressor, heat exchanger, and blower components. Does not include labor, refrigerant, or dispatch fees. Registration is straightforward but required within 30 days of installation to activate coverage.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much will this furnace reduce my heating bill compared to a standard 80% AFUE unit? +
The 96% AFUE rating captures 16 percentage points more heat energy than 80% AFUE furnaces. In cold climates using 5,000+ heating degree days annually, expect $150-250 annual savings. Actual results depend on gas rates, thermostat settings, and home insulation. New construction or well-sealed homes see better returns.
Can I install this system in an attic or horizontal position? +
Yes. The downflow/horizontal configuration accommodates attic, basement, or horizontal crawlspace mounting. Ensure adequate ventilation around the furnace and support the unit securely. PVC venting must slope properly to avoid condensate backup. Consult the CAPF installation manual for orientation-specific piping and clearance requirements.
Is 16 SEER good enough for summer cooling in hot regions? +
16 SEER is respectable but not premium for hot climates. It performs well in mixed or moderate climates but may lag competitors offering 18+ SEER. High-load cooling seasons will generate higher utility costs. Consider upgrading to a two-stage condenser if budget allows and AC runtime exceeds 2,000 hours yearly.
How does this system compare to a higher-end Goodman or competitor units? +
This mid-tier Goodman balances cost and efficiency effectively. Higher-end units offer two-stage condensers, variable-speed outdoor fans, and 17+ SEER ratings but cost 15-25% more. Budget brands drop below 15 SEER. For contractors, this model delivers good margins and solid customer satisfaction without premium pricing.
What happens to my warranty if I don't register the system? +
Registration within 30 days of installation activates the 10-year parts warranty. Without registration, coverage defaults to the manufacturer's limited warranty, typically 1-5 years. Online registration takes minutes and protects your investment, so do not skip this step.
