Review

The Goodman GCVM970804CN represents a practical middle ground in the residential HVAC market. This 3.5-ton combo system packages an 80,000 BTU gas furnace with an air conditioning condenser into one horizontally oriented unit. Goodman positions this equipment for contractors and homeowners prioritizing efficiency without premium pricing. The system uses R410A refrigerant and operates at 115V single-phase, compatible with standard residential electrical service.

Performance metrics are genuinely impressive for the price tier. The furnace delivers 97% AFUE efficiency with a modulating burner that fine-tunes output rather than cycling on-off, reducing energy consumption during mild weather. The AC side achieves 18 SEER2 with a two-stage scroll compressor, earning Energy Star certification. The variable-speed ECM blower adjusts airflow up to 1,600 CFM, matching demand rather than running continuously. This combination generates measurable utility savings over a five-to-ten-year ownership period.

Installation

Professional installation is mandatory, as gas line connections and venting present serious safety risks if mishandled. The plastic PVC exhaust flue requirement (right-side exit) must be confirmed before purchase to ensure proper ductwork routing. Unit dimensions allow installation in standard attics or closets. Electrical connection requires 21.9 to 35-amp breaker sizing. Most jurisdictions mandate licensed contractors, adding $800-1,500 to system cost.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides 10 years of full parts and labor coverage when you register online within 30 days of purchase. This warranty length is competitive in the mid-range segment. Coverage excludes improper installation, lack of maintenance, and refrigerant recharges after initial startup. Registration is straightforward and handled through Goodman's website.

Frequently Asked Questions

The specs show 35,000 BTU cooling but also 3-ton tonnage. Which is accurate? +
The system is rated 3.5 tons, which equals approximately 42,000 BTU nominal cooling capacity. The 35,000 BTU specification appears to reference minimum staged cooling output. Contact Goodman for clarification before ordering if your load calculation requires exact capacity confirmation.
Can I install this myself if I have HVAC experience but no license? +
No. The safety warning explicitly requires trained technicians, and most jurisdictions legally prohibit unlicensed gas appliance installation. Liability for personal injury or property damage transfers to whoever installs it. Always hire a licensed contractor.
How much will this system reduce my heating and cooling bills? +
Actual savings depend on your current equipment efficiency and local energy rates. A 97% AFUE furnace replaces 80% AFUE units with 17% fuel consumption reduction. The 18 SEER2 AC provides roughly 40-50% lower cooling costs versus 10-year-old systems. Regional variations matter significantly.
Does this unit work with my existing ductwork and thermostat? +
Compatibility requires professional evaluation. Ductwork must handle 1,600 CFM maximum airflow. Thermostats must support modulating burner control and two-stage cooling for optimal efficiency. Your HVAC contractor performs static pressure testing and adjusts dampers during installation.
What happens if I don't register within 30 days for the warranty? +
The warranty is voided. Online registration takes five minutes through Goodman's website. Keep your purchase receipt and AHRI certificate number (7424271) available for the registration process.
