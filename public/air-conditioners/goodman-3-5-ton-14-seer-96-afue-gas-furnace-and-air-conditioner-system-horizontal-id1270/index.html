Review

The ACiQ 3.5 ton system represents a practical middle ground in the HVAC market. Priced at $3136, it targets homeowners tired of paying heating and cooling bills on old equipment but hesitant to spend $5000 plus on premium units. This horizontal A-coil configuration works where traditional units don't fit, and the dual-fuel capability handles both natural gas and propane setups across different regions and property types.

Performance specs matter here. The furnace delivers 120,000 BTU heating output at 96% AFUE, which translates directly to lower winter gas consumption compared to standard 80% units. The 42,000 BTU cooling capacity with 13 SEER rating handles moderate cooling loads efficiently, while the variable-speed ECM blower motor runs between 0 and 2000 CFM depending on demand. Two-stage burner operation reduces short-cycling in shoulder seasons, extending component lifespan and improving temperature consistency.

Installation

This horizontal unit requires careful placement in attic or crawlspace environments. The 30.5 inch length, 24.5 inch height, and 26 inch depth demand measured clearance before ordering. Plastic or PVC exhaust flue is required, simplifying venting compared to metal. Electrical demands sit moderate at 115V single-phase 60Hz operation, though you'll need a 24 to 40 amp breaker depending on final wiring. Suction lines need 3/8 inch insulation thickness, standard for most installers.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year parts warranty applies only after online registration is completed. This is a reasonable coverage period for the price tier, protecting against manufacturing defects through the critical first decade. Labor and refrigerant are not covered, so factor installation costs separately. Compare this carefully with competitors offering 5-year limited warranties or 15-year extended options at higher price points.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much can I expect to save on heating costs with 96% AFUE? +
A 96% unit wastes only 4% of fuel energy, compared to 80% units wasting 20%. Over a heating season, this translates to roughly 15-20% lower gas bills in cold climates, less in mild climates. Actual savings depend on your current system efficiency, usage patterns, and local gas rates. In moderate climates, annual savings typically range from $200-400.
Can this horizontal unit replace my old vertical furnace without major ductwork changes? +
Generally yes, if your existing ductwork accommodates downflow and horizontal airflow patterns. The unit's maximum 2000 CFM output fits most residential systems designed for 3.5 ton equipment. Have your installer verify duct sizing and return air configuration to confirm compatibility before purchase, as undersized returns reduce efficiency and cause airflow problems.
Is 13 SEER adequate for summer cooling in hot climates? +
13 SEER is adequate for mild to moderate climates but may require longer runtimes in hot regions. The single-stage compressor runs continuously once triggered, unlike two-stage units that modulate capacity. In 95+ degree weather with high humidity, expect the system to run frequently. For hot climates, consider 14-16 SEER models with variable-capacity compressors for better efficiency.
How does this compare to Goodman or Lennox units at similar prices? +
ACiQ offers comparable efficiency and reliability to base-model Goodman units at slightly lower cost. This is actually Goodman equipment rebranded and sold direct. Lennox units typically cost 20-30% more but may include better warranties and quieter operation. For straight performance per dollar, ACiQ is hard to beat, though brand recognition favors Goodman and Lennox among some contractors.
What happens if something breaks after the warranty ends? +
After year 10, you pay full replacement or repair costs. A compressor failure typically runs $1200-1800 in labor and parts. Consider extended warranties or service contracts before expiration if the unit is critical to your property. Budget $150-300 annually for maintenance contracts that include annual tune-ups and emergency service.
