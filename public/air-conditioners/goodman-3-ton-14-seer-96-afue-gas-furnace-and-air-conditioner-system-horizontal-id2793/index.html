Review

The Goodman 3-ton horizontal gas furnace and air conditioner system lands squarely in the contractor sweet spot, balancing efficiency, reliability, and installation simplicity. This unit pairs an 80,000 BTU furnace rated at 96% AFUE with a 36,000 BTU cooling section delivering 14 SEER performance. Designed for upflow or horizontal placement, it addresses real space constraints in attics and basements where vertical clearance limits options. The multi-speed blower motor and scroll compressor indicate solid engineering rather than budget-corner-cutting.

Performance numbers tell the practical story. The 96% AFUE rating means 96 cents of every heating dollar converts to warmth, a meaningful improvement over older 80-85% units. On cooling, the 14 SEER rating supports 3-ton capacity through 36,000 BTU output, sufficient for roughly 1,200 to 1,400 square feet in moderate climates. Electrical demands stay reasonable at 18.7 to 30 amps on 208/230v single-phase power. The unit moves up to 1,600 CFM through ducting, and 73 dBA noise levels remain acceptable for basement installations but noticeable indoors.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 96% AFUE heating efficiency translates to roughly 4% less fuel waste than 92% AFUE units over a heating season
  • 14 SEER cooling rating meets current efficiency standards for most U.S. regions without premium pricing
  • Multi-speed blower motor allows variable airflow, improving comfort compared to single-speed designs
  • Plastic PVC exhaust accommodates modern venting codes and reduces installation costs versus metal flue requirements
  • Scroll compressor technology delivers quiet operation at 73 dBA and proven reliability in residential applications

Cons

  • Single-stage burner and condenser mean the system runs at full capacity even during mild weather, reducing off-season efficiency
  • Not Energy Star certified, suggesting it occupies the middle tier rather than top-tier efficiency class
  • 73 dBA operating noise is moderately loud and may be noticeable in quieter homes or bedrooms near equipment

Installation

Horizontal configuration requires ductwork on both sides with proper return and supply plenum connections. The unit accepts PVC plastic exhaust venting, which simplifies routing through walls and roofs. Suction and liquid line sizes measure 3/4 inches and 3/8 inches respectively, with 30 feet of pre-insulated refrigerant line included. Mounting requires stable, level surface in conditioned or semi-conditioned space. Professional installation strongly recommended for proper refrigerant charge and airflow verification.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman covers the entire system for 10 years when you register within 60 days of installation. This covers parts and labor, protecting your investment against compressor failure, heat exchanger cracks, and blower motor defects. Registration is straightforward online and essential, as warranty defaults to one year if skipped. Ten-year coverage ranks as solid mid-market protection, though some premium brands extend to 12 years.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this system Energy Star rated? +
No. While the 96% AFUE and 14 SEER ratings are respectable, Energy Star certification typically requires higher thresholds or variable-capacity features. This unit prioritizes value over premium efficiency status, suitable for cost-conscious replacements.
Can I install this unit vertically or only horizontally? +
The specs list Upflow/Horizontal airflow direction. Vertical upflow is possible, but this particular model SKU is optimized for horizontal placement. Verify with your installer whether vertical conversion impacts warranty or performance.
What size home does 3 tons and 14 SEER cool effectively? +
3 tons typically handles 1,200 to 1,400 square feet in moderate climates. Load calculation based on insulation, windows, and local summer temps is essential. A 3-ton unit oversized for a 900 square foot space wastes money; undersized for 1,800 square feet causes inadequate cooling.
How does single-stage cooling compare to two-stage or variable models? +
Single-stage runs at full 36,000 BTU output whenever cooling is needed, using more energy during mild spring or fall days. Two-stage and variable units ramp down capacity in mild weather, improving off-season efficiency. You trade convenience for lower total SEER impact.
Does the 10-year warranty cover refrigerant or require annual service? +
Warranty covers defects in parts and labor under normal use. Refrigerant leaks from component failure are covered, but refrigerant charges and routine maintenance are your responsibility. Annual professional tune-ups maintain warranty validity and system longevity.
