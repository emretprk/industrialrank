Review

The ACiQ ACIQ-24-EHPD-UPG represents a direct replacement for the popular Goodman GSZ140251 platform, bridging the gap between budget and mid-tier combo systems. This 3 ton horizontal unit combines an 80,000 BTU gas furnace with 24,000 BTU cooling capacity in a single package, appealing to contractors managing mixed heating and cooling loads. The manufacturer positions it as a rebate-eligible upgrade path for homeowners looking to move beyond 10-plus year old equipment without premium pricing.

Efficiency metrics drive the value proposition here. The furnace delivers 92% AFUE, meaning 92 cents of every heating dollar converts to usable warmth, while the AC side achieves 14 SEER for cooling efficiency. The R454B refrigerant represents a significant shift from legacy R410A, offering lower global warming potential without sacrificing cooling performance. Multi-speed blower capability allows better matching to residential ductwork, and the 1200 CFM maximum airflow suits standard residential applications without oversizing concerns.

Installation

Horizontal configuration fits tight basement or attic spaces where vertical furnaces cannot fit. Requires 115V single phase 60Hz power with breaker sizing between 17.1 and 25 amps, manageable with standard home electrical. Sweat valve connections use 3/8 inch liquid and 3/4 inch suction lines, calling for moderate HVAC experience or professional installation. PVC exhaust flue requirement may require new venting infrastructure in older homes with masonry chimneys.

Warranty & Reliability

ACiQ provides 10 year parts coverage when registered online within 60 days of installation, standard industry practice for this tier. Coverage includes compressor, heat exchanger, and blower motor failures. Labor coverage depends on installer network participation, which varies by region. Registration is mandatory, so obtain documentation from your installer immediately after startup.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet list 3 ton tonnage when the product name says 2 ton? +
The 24,000 BTU cooling capacity technically equals 2 tons, but paired with the 80,000 BTU furnace (also 2 ton equivalent), the system is marketed as 3 ton total capacity. This reflects combined heating and cooling tonnage rather than standard AHRI rating methodology used by contractors.
Can I install this unit with my existing R410A refrigerant lines? +
R454B operates at similar pressures to R410A and uses identical 3/8 inch liquid and 3/4 inch suction valve sizes. Existing copper lines are compatible, though professional evacuation and charging are required. Mixing refrigerants is dangerous and illegal, so old lines must be completely flushed if converting from R410A systems.
How does 14 SEER performance compare to newer systems? +
14 SEER meets minimum Energy Star thresholds but trails newer variable-stage units delivering 16-18 SEER. This system targets contractors valuing reliability over peak efficiency. Single stage compression keeps costs predictable but misses modulation benefits on partial load days common in spring and fall.
Is this better than keeping my existing 10 year old Goodman GSZ140251? +
The newer R454B refrigerant, higher AFUE rating, and multi-speed blower justify replacement if cooling efficiency matters. However, if your current unit runs reliably, upgrading purely for 14 SEER instead of 13 SEER saves roughly 5-8% annually on AC costs. The furnace upgrade to 92% AFUE delivers faster savings if heating season is long.
Does this unit qualify for federal tax credits or rebates? +
Energy Star certification makes it eligible for many utility company rebates, typically $300-600 depending on region. Federal tax credits for HVAC expired in 2022, so focus on state and local utility programs. Check with your installer before purchasing to confirm rebate availability in your area.
