Review

Goodman's gas furnace and air conditioner combo system lands squarely in the practical, mid-market segment where contractors and homeowners balance performance against budget. This upflow configuration handles both heating and cooling in one compact footprint, making it suitable for basement installations, utility closets, and retrofit applications. At this price point, the unit targets the replacement market where proven reliability matters more than cutting-edge efficiency. The 100,000 BTU heating capacity and 48,000 BTU cooling output position it for homes in moderate climate zones where seasonal demand fluctuates predictably.

The furnace delivers 92% AFUE, a solid 6 percentage points above the federal minimum 82% requirement, translating to meaningful gas savings over the system's 15 to 20 year lifespan. The 14 SEER cooling efficiency ranks adequately for 2024 standards, though it misses ENERGY STAR status. A multi-speed blower motor adds flexibility for temperature staging and humidity management. The scroll compressor and single-stage burner keep manufacturing costs reasonable but mean the system runs at full capacity rather than modulating for part-load conditions. Maximum airflow reaches 2,000 CFM, appropriate for residential delivery without excessive ductwork stress.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the actual tonnage, and why does the spec show both 4 ton and 5 ton? +
The title states 4 ton, but the spec sheet lists 5 ton tonnage. This discrepancy suggests either a data entry error or a unit configured from different component batches. Contact Online Supply before purchase to confirm which rating applies to your SKU. Tonnage affects which sized ducts and air handlers you can pair with this system.
Can I upgrade the refrigerant from R22 to R410A? +
No, refrigerant type is set during manufacturing. The spec shows compatibility with both R22 and R410A depending on your model batch. Confirm refrigerant type at purchase since R22 is being phased out and costs more. R410A systems offer better long-term availability and regulatory alignment.
How does 92% AFUE compare to high-efficiency furnaces? +
92% AFUE is mid-range. Premium condensing furnaces reach 95 to 98% AFUE, saving approximately 3 to 6 percent more on heating costs annually. The 92% unit suits moderate climates well, but cold-climate homeowners heating six months yearly should consider high-efficiency models for faster payback on equipment costs.
Is 14 SEER competitive with other four-ton systems? +
14 SEER meets current baseline standards but trails premium variable-capacity units rated 18 to 21 SEER. For moderate climates with mild summers, 14 SEER costs less upfront and performs adequately. Hot-humid regions benefit more from higher SEER ratings that reduce cycling and humidity struggles.
Does the 10-year warranty cover labor and refrigerant? +
The 10-year limited warranty typically covers parts only. Labor and refrigerant charge usually require separate service agreements. Review the detailed warranty document before purchase to confirm coverage scope and any exclusions applying to your situation.
