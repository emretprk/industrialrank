Review

The Goodman 2-ton furnace and AC system represents solid mid-market positioning in the HVAC replacement segment. Priced at $643, it delivers certified Energy Star efficiency without the premium costs of variable-capacity equipment. This unit addresses the core needs of contractors managing multiple residential projects and homeowners replacing 10 to 15-year-old systems. The combination of 92% AFUE heating and 14 SEER cooling puts it comfortably above minimum code requirements while remaining accessible to cost-conscious buyers.

Performance specs confirm this system's straightforward capability. The furnace produces 24,000 BTU heating output with 92% efficiency, meaning minimal energy waste through the flue. Cooling delivers matching 24,000 BTU capacity at 14 SEER using R410A refrigerant and a scroll compressor. The multi-speed blower handles up to 1200 CFM airflow, adapting to system demands. Electrical requirements are modest, needing only 15.1 to 25 amps at 208/230V single-phase, making it compatible with standard residential panels without upgrades.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this unit qualify for tax credits or rebates? +
Yes, the Energy Star certification opens eligibility for many utility rebates and some federal tax incentives depending on your location. The specs confirm Energy Star compliance. Check your local utility provider and IRS guidelines for current incentive amounts, as these vary by region and change annually.
Can this system be installed horizontally instead of upflow? +
Yes, the specs indicate this unit supports both upflow and horizontal configuration. However, horizontal installation requires a different coil orientation and ductwork layout. Verify your evaporator coil matches the chosen orientation before purchasing, as mixing configurations reduces efficiency and may void warranty.
How does 14 SEER compare to modern standards? +
14 SEER exceeds the current federal minimum of 13 SEER for most regions and matches mid-range residential systems. While newer variable-capacity units reach 16-18 SEER, this rating delivers measurable energy savings. The difference becomes marginal in mild climates but grows significant in extreme heat or cold regions.
Is this better than budget brands like Ameristar or Comfortmaker? +
Goodman offers superior reliability history and wider contractor availability compared to pure budget brands. This unit costs more upfront but delivers better long-term value through proven durability. Comfortmaker and Ameristar serve budget markets where initial price matters most.
What's the actual tonnage, 2 ton or 3 ton? +
The product title claims 2 ton, but the specifications list 24,000 BTU cooling and heating, which equals exactly 2 tons (12,000 BTU per ton). The spec sheet's 3-ton tonnage entry appears to be a data error. Confirm with your supplier before purchase.
