Review

The ACiQ 3.5 ton system represents Goodman's value strategy in the budget HVAC segment. At $3,136, it undercuts brand-name equivalents while maintaining solid engineering credentials via AHRI certification 7368845. The horizontal configuration with A-coil design targets installers working in space-constrained residential applications, particularly retrofit jobs where vertical equipment won't fit. This is genuine workhorse equipment designed for cost-conscious decision makers.

Heating performance is this unit's strength. The 96% AFUE rating and two-stage burner design deliver measurable efficiency gains, potentially saving $250-350 annually compared to standard 80% units in heating-heavy climates. Cooling at 13 SEER and 42,000 BTU/h capacity handles 3.5 tons adequately but without the modulation benefits of variable-capacity systems. The variable-speed ECM blower motor is a meaningful upgrade at this price, reducing fan energy draw during partial-load operation and improving temperature consistency throughout the home.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 96% AFUE furnace efficiency significantly reduces heating costs, translating to roughly $200-300 annual savings compared to 80% units in cold climates
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor adapts to actual heating and cooling demand, improving comfort and reducing runtime waste
  • Two-stage gas burner allows the furnace to operate at partial capacity during mild weather, reducing cycling and fuel consumption
  • Horizontal configuration with A-coil design fits tight attic and crawlspace installations where vertical units won't work
  • PVC exhaust flue compatibility eliminates expensive masonry chimney modifications and reduces installation labor
  • 3.5 ton cooling capacity delivers 42,000 BTU/h for medium residential applications without over-sizing penalties
  • 10-year parts warranty with online registration provides reasonable coverage at this price tier

Cons

  • 13 SEER cooling efficiency is standard, not premium, meaning higher AC operating costs than 14-16 SEER alternatives
  • Single-stage condenser cannot modulate cooling capacity, leading to more on-off cycling and temperature swings on mild days
  • No Energy Star certification signals it meets baseline standards only, not best-in-class performance
  • 75 dB noise level is moderately loud, audible from adjacent rooms during heating cycles
  • ACiQ branding, while backed by Goodman manufacturing, carries less brand recognition than direct Goodman or Lennox units

Who Should Buy

Best For

Budget-conscious contractors and homeowners replacing older 80-85% AFUE furnaces in climate zones with significant winter heating demands. Ideal for properties with limited space where horizontal configuration is mandatory, such as attics with low clearance or slab-foundation crawlspaces.

Consider Alternatives If

Buyers in mild climates where cooling dominates should look for higher SEER units, and those prioritizing quietest operation should evaluate models rated 70 dB or lower.

Installation

This horizontal unit requires standard HVAC installation expertise. The PVC exhaust flue is a major advantage, eliminating expensive chimney work and simplifying ductwork routing. Electrical connections demand 208/230V single-phase power at 23.9-40 amp breaker sizing. Refrigerant line set sizing uses standard 3/8-inch liquid and 7/8-inch suction lines with 3/8-inch insulation thickness. Weight of 120 pounds and 36.25-inch height require careful positioning in tight attics.

Warranty & Reliability

Ten-year parts coverage with online registration is competitive at this price point, though it does not include labor costs. This aligns with industry standard for value-tier equipment and covers compressor, heat exchanger, and motor failures. Registration is essential for claim eligibility, so documentation should be retained immediately after installation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much will this furnace reduce my heating bill compared to my 20-year-old unit? +
If your current furnace is 78-80% AFUE, expect 16% heating cost reduction. At average regional rates, this translates to $200-350 annual savings depending on climate and usage. Payback occurs within 4-6 years on furnace costs alone, faster when replacing failed units.
Can I install this in an attic with 36 inches of vertical clearance? +
The unit is 36.25 inches tall, so it requires minimal additional space for service access and clearance around the cabinet. Horizontal configuration is specifically designed for this scenario. Ensure adequate space for refrigerant line routing and PVC exhaust flue positioning before ordering.
Why is the cooling efficiency only 13 SEER when other brands offer 14-16? +
This is intentional value-tier positioning. The single-stage condenser and reciprocating compressor prioritize reliability and cost over advanced modulation technology. Step up to premium brands for 15+ SEER if cooling efficiency justifies the additional investment for your climate.
How does ACiQ compare to buying a direct Goodman-branded unit? +
ACiQ is Goodman's value brand using identical compressors, motors, and heat exchangers. Differences are minimal, primarily packaging and marketing. Direct Goodman units cost $400-600 more without functional advantages, making ACiQ the rational choice for budget-conscious buyers.
What happens to my warranty if I don't register online? +
The warranty becomes void without registration. Submit proof of installation within 30 days to activate 10-year coverage. Keep documentation, as manufacturers require it for all claims.
