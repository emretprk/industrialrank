Review

The Goodman CAPT4961D4 represents solid mid-tier efficiency in the combined furnace-condenser market. This upflow unit bundles a 120,000 BTU furnace with a matching air conditioner in one chassis, simplifying installation for contractors and reducing jobsite complexity. Goodman positioned this system for the contractor-friendly segment, offering Energy Star certification and rebate eligibility without enterprise-level pricing. The unit addresses real demand from installers tired of coordinating separate furnace and condenser deliveries.

Performance numbers justify the efficiency ratings. The furnace hits 97% AFUE through a modulating burner that ramps between low and high fire, avoiding the 20-30% energy loss common in single-stage designs. The paired condenser delivers 48,000 BTU cooling capacity with two-stage operation, reaching 16 SEER1 efficiency. The variable-speed ECM blower matches airflow to demand, spinning slowly on mild days and ramping to 2000 CFM maximum during peak load. Real-world savings depend on usage patterns, but homeowners typically see 10-15% heating cost reductions versus 80-90% AFUE equipment.

Installation

This system requires PVC or plastic exhaust venting, simplifying flue installation versus Category IV furnaces needing metal. The right-side flue location works with most standard furnace closets. Electrical demand runs 208-230V single phase at 27.7 to 45 amps breaker sizing, typical for residential applications. Refrigerant lines use 3/8-inch liquid and 7/8-inch suction sizing with sweat connections, requiring certified EPA technicians for proper brazing and evacuation.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this system with a 10-year parts warranty when you register online within 30 days of installation. The warranty covers the furnace heat exchanger, compressor, and condenser coil but excludes wear items like blower wheels. Online registration takes five minutes and is non-negotiable to activate coverage. Ten years beats many competitors at five-year terms, though labor costs fall on the homeowner during years 6-10.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the product description say 4 tons but the specs list 5 tons? +
The title references the furnace BTU capacity while specs show condenser tonnage. The 48,000 BTU cooling output equals 4 tons, but Goodman rates the overall system at 5 tons. Request clarification from your supplier to confirm matched component ratings match your ductwork sizing.
Can I install this in a downflow configuration? +
The specs indicate upflow/downflow configuration capability, but the right-side flue location restricts true downflow installations. Verify with Goodman technical support and your installer whether horizontal/downflow orientation works with your specific ductwork layout.
How does the 97% AFUE compare to older furnaces? +
A 97% AFUE unit converts 97 of every 100 BTUs of gas into usable heat. Older 80% AFUE furnaces waste 20 BTUs. Upgrading saves roughly 17% on heating energy annually, worth 2-3 years of furnace cost difference in savings alone.
Is 16 SEER1 good for cooling efficiency? +
16 SEER1 ranks above average but not premium. High-efficiency units hit 18-21 SEER2. The two-stage design balances comfort and cost, delivering reliable cooling without extreme price premiums or complex installation requirements.
What happens after the 10-year warranty expires? +
After 10 years, repairs are your responsibility. Budget 1,500-3,000 dollars for major compressor or heat exchanger replacement. Most Goodman equipment runs 15-20 years total, making post-warranty repairs occasional rather than constant.
