Review

The Goodman GPCH3 3.5-ton package unit sits comfortably in the mainstream residential cooling market. At $3,835, it undercuts premium brands while maintaining respectable 13.4 SEER2 efficiency ratings and Goodman's established service network. This horizontal configuration replaces the older GPC1442H41 model and targets homeowners seeking straightforward air conditioning without cutting corners on performance or pushing budgets into ultra-premium territory.

Performance specs reveal a competent mid-tier cooler. The 40,500 BTU capacity handles standard 3.5-ton residential loads, while maximum CFM of 1,300 ensures adequate airflow through typical ductwork. Two-stage scroll compressor design reduces the 78-decibel noise signature compared to single-stage units during partial-load operation. The 13.4 SEER2 rating represents meaningful efficiency improvement over legacy 13 SEER units, though newer high-efficiency competitors offer 14-16+ ratings for significantly higher investment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 13.4 SEER2 good enough for my climate? +
13.4 SEER2 represents solid mid-tier performance. For moderate climates with 100-200 cooling degree days, annual energy savings justify the investment. Hotter regions benefit more from 15+ SEER2 units, where the additional efficiency premium pays back faster through reduced cooling runtime and electricity costs.
Will this unit fit my existing horizontal package mounting? +
Likely yes. The 66-inch width, 22-inch height, and 34-inch depth are standard horizontal dimensions. Verify your existing pad dimensions and ductwork connections before ordering. The GPCH3 replaces the GPC1442H41 directly, so existing installations using that model definitely accommodate this unit.
How does 78 decibels compare to other units? +
78 dB is moderate, equivalent to heavy traffic noise. Two-stage operation reduces noise during part-load conditions in spring and fall. Single-stage units run louder constantly. Premium variable-speed models approach 72-75 dB but cost significantly more. For typical residential settings, 78 dB is acceptable but noticeable.
Why choose this over higher SEER2 models? +
Budget and practicality. This unit saves $1,500-2,500 compared to 15+ SEER2 competitors. That premium investment pays back over 8-12 years depending on climate and usage. For homeowners planning 10-year ownership or moderate cooling climates, the value proposition strongly favors this mid-tier option.
Does the 10-year warranty cover labor and refrigerant? +
No. The 10-year term covers parts only. Labor, refrigerant, and service calls are homeowner responsibility. Standard industry practice for residential units at this price tier. Extended service plans are available separately from dealers.
