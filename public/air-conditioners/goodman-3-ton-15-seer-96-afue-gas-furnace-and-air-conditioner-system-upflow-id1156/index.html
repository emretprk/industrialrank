Review

The Goodman 3-ton upflow system is a solid workhorse for HVAC contractors and homeowners managing replacement projects on tight budgets. This configuration combines a gas furnace with an air conditioning condenser into a complete package, eliminating compatibility guesswork. The system is positioned as an entry-level efficient option rather than a premium high-performance solution, making it relevant for residential retrofits across transitional climate zones.

The heating side impresses with its 96% AFUE rating and 100,000 BTU output, meaning you recover nearly all combustion energy as usable heat. The two-stage burner modulates between high and low fire, reducing waste during shoulder seasons. Cooling delivers 34,800 BTU at 16 SEER2 efficiency, adequate for 3-ton loads but delivered through a single-stage compressor that runs full-capacity without partial-load efficiency. The ECM blower motor adjusts from 0 to 1600 CFM, matching airflow to heating or cooling demand.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet show both 15 SEER and 16 SEER2 ratings? +
The product listing contains conflicting data. The 16 SEER2 rating is the current efficiency standard used by EPA since 2023. The older 15 SEER spec predates the test method change. Ask your supplier which rating applies to your specific unit serial number to avoid confusion on warranty and efficiency claims.
Can this system work with my existing downflow ducting? +
The system is manufactured for upflow or horizontal discharge, but not downflow applications. If your home uses downflow ductwork, you'll need a different Goodman configuration or face expensive duct modifications. Verify your ductwork orientation before committing to purchase.
How much will this system reduce my heating costs compared to my old 80% furnace? +
Moving from 80% AFUE to 96% AFUE eliminates 16 percentage points of fuel waste. On a 100,000 BTU system running 1500 hours annually, that saves approximately 2,400 therms per year at typical usage, translating to 300 to 600 dollars in heating costs depending on local gas rates.
Is the 16 SEER2 cooling efficiency competitive with modern units? +
16 SEER2 is mid-range for 2024 standards. Premium units reach 20+ SEER2, but those cost significantly more. This Goodman's single-stage compressor means it cannot provide the efficiency gains of variable-capacity systems during partial-load cooling, limiting real-world performance in mild weather.
What happens if I don't register the warranty online? +
The 10-year parts warranty requires online registration with Goodman. Without registration, coverage drops to a standard manufacturer guarantee, typically 1 to 5 years. Register immediately after installation to secure the full decade of protection.
