Review

The Goodman 3-ton combined gas furnace and air conditioning system represents solid mid-market equipment for residential HVAC applications. At $3,136 for the complete package, it positions itself as a value-conscious option without sacrificing efficiency standards. The horizontal configuration makes it suitable for basement or attic installation where space constraints require a compact footprint. Goodman's market reputation focuses on reliability and contractor support rather than premium features, which aligns with this system's straightforward design and proven component selection.

Performance specifications reveal competent efficiency across both heating and cooling. The furnace delivers 120,000 BTU of heating output at 96% AFUE, meaning 96 cents of every dollar spent on fuel converts to usable heat. Cooling capacity reaches 34,800 BTU with a 16 SEER rating, qualifying the system for ENERGY STAR certification. The variable-speed ECM blower motor adapts to demand up to 2,000 CFM maximum, reducing fan energy consumption during partial-load conditions. Two-stage burner operation further improves heating efficiency by allowing reduced firing rates when full capacity isn't needed.

Installation

This horizontal A-coil unit requires proper ducting integration and supports both downflow and horizontal airflow configurations. The 115V, 60Hz electrical requirement needs a 30-amp maximum breaker with 18.7-amp minimum sizing. PVC or plastic exhaust flue is required, simplifying venting compared to metal systems. Suction and liquid lines use standard 7/8-inch and 3/8-inch connections with 3/4-inch insulation on suction lines. Installation manual and AHRI certificate number 7368845 are available for contractor reference.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between 16 SEER and newer SEER2 ratings I see advertised? +
SEER2 testing uses more realistic conditions including lower outdoor temperatures and higher humidity. A 16 SEER unit typically equals approximately 13 SEER2. Don't directly compare old SEER to new SEER2 ratings. This unit's 16 SEER was tested under older, less stringent standards, so actual efficiency may be slightly lower under SEER2 evaluation.
Can I use this system with my existing ductwork from an older unit? +
Probably yes if your existing ducts accommodate 2,000 CFM airflow and proper return air sizing. Have a qualified contractor measure your ductwork static pressure and compare to the furnace's capability. Undersized ducts will reduce efficiency and cooling capacity regardless of equipment specifications. New ductwork may be necessary for optimal performance.
Does the 96% AFUE furnace really save that much compared to 90% AFUE units? +
Yes, 6 percentage points represents roughly 6% annual fuel savings. On a $1,200 annual heating bill, that's approximately $72 yearly savings. Over 15 years, you recover $1,080 in utility costs, offsetting part of the efficiency premium. Results vary by climate, heating season length, and fuel costs in your region.
How does single-stage cooling affect my comfort compared to two-stage systems? +
Single-stage runs at full capacity whenever cooling is needed, cycling on and off completely. Two-stage systems run at reduced capacity most of the time. You'll feel similar comfort, but two-stage delivers better efficiency on mild days. Single-stage is noisier during operation and slightly less efficient during partial-load conditions. For most homeowners, the difference is marginal but measurable on utility bills.
What does the online registration warranty actually cover? +
The 10-year warranty covers manufacturing defects and component failures under normal operation. It does not cover improper installation, lack of maintenance, refrigerant leaks from external damage, or electrical issues from inadequate breaker sizing. Register immediately after installation to activate coverage and preserve your claim rights.
