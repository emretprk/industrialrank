Review

ACiQ's Goodman-branded 4-ton system combines a gas furnace and air conditioner in one package, addressing a common contractor need for matched-unit simplicity. This downflow configuration positions the 34.5-inch height and A-coil design to fit basements, closets, and attic spaces typical in residential retrofits. The system carries AHRI certification number 7424272, confirming factory-tested performance. Priced at wholesale rates without markup, it appeals to cost-conscious installers and homeowners seeking reliable, efficient equipment.

Performance metrics are genuinely strong. The furnace delivers 100,000 BTU heating output at 97% AFUE, among the highest burn efficiency available in non-condensing equipment. Cooling capacity reaches 48,000 BTU at 14 SEER1, producing Energy Star certification. The variable-speed ECM blower modulates between 0 and 2,000 CFM, cutting idle losses and noise at 74 dB. Modulating burners in the furnace ramp output from 40 to 100 percent, reducing temperature swings and improving comfort versus single-stage operation.

Installation

Downflow installation requires vertical mounting with return air entering the top and supply exiting the bottom. Suction and liquid lines measure 7/8 inch and 3/8 inch respectively, with 30 feet of pre-insulated suction line included. PVC or plastic exhaust flue is mandatory, simplifying venting versus metal. Electrically, the unit needs 115V single-phase 60 Hz service and a 26.4 to 45 amp breaker, compatible with standard residential panels. Technician time typically runs 4-6 hours for experienced crews.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year parts warranty requires online registration and covers compressor, motor, and heat exchanger defects. It excludes wear items like capacitors and contactors unless failed prematurely. This protection is standard for Goodman equipment and reasonable for the price point. Labor costs and refrigerant are homeowner responsibility. Extended warranties are available through most retailers for incremental cost.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much will my heating bill drop switching from an 80% AFUE furnace to this 97% unit? +
A 17-point AFUE jump typically cuts furnace gas consumption by 18-22% annually. On a 1,500 square foot home using 60 million BTU annually, expect 100-140 dollars monthly savings in northern climates. Savings scale with local gas prices and climate severity. Rebates often offset 500-1,500 dollars of the purchase price.
Can this downflow system work in my horizontal attic installation? +
Yes, partially. Downflow units can operate horizontally, but airflow direction reverses, making supply air exit upward instead of down. Ductwork must be redesigned to route horizontal discharge correctly. Consult your installer before assuming compatibility, as some attic layouts require return-to-bottom connection that downflow units lack.
Is 14 SEER cooling strong enough, or should I upgrade to 16+ SEER? +
14 SEER is efficient for mixed climates and moderate cooling loads, typically cooling homes under 2,000 square feet effectively. 16+ SEER units run 1,500-2,500 dollars more but only save 10-15 dollars monthly in cooling-season electricity. Payback spans 10-20 years, so upgrade only if utility rates are high or you live in the South.
How does this single-stage compressor compare to a two-stage unit? +
Single-stage compressors run at full capacity or off, delivering less precise modulation. Two-stage compressors ramp to 60-70 percent on mild days, improving part-load efficiency by 8-12 percent. For areas with moderate temperature swings, two-stage justifies extra 800-1,200 dollars. In harsh climates, the difference narrows.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover if my furnace fails? +
Parts only, after online registration. Compressor, motor, and heat exchanger defects are covered for 10 years. Labor and refrigerant are excluded. Wear items like contactors and capacitors typically fail outside warranty unless defective. Extended warranties add 5-10 year labor coverage for 300-500 dollars additional.
