Review

The ACiQ 2-ton vertical cased coil occupies an interesting market position, serving contractors and homeowners who need straightforward replacement cooling without premium-tier specifications. Designed to replace the legacy Goodman GSX130242, this 24,000 BTU unit brings modern refrigerant compliance while maintaining the accessibility that makes it attractive for standard residential retrofit applications. Its A-coil configuration supports both upflow and downflow installations, adding flexibility to system design.

Performance specs reveal a competent mid-range cooler rather than an efficiency leader. The 13 SEER1 rating sits comfortably above baseline codes but trails newer competitors reaching 14-16 SEER2. Operating at 208/230V single-phase with a 10.3 to 15-amp breaker window keeps electrical infrastructure modest. R454B refrigerant future-proofs the unit against regulatory phase-outs. At 75 dB, noise output runs slightly elevated for residential spaces, though typical for single-stage compressors at this capacity.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will this coil work with my existing Goodman GSX130242 installation? +
Yes, the ACIQ-24-EHPD-UPG directly replaces GSX130242 units. Refrigerant lines, electrical connections, and mounting dimensions align with original specifications, minimizing installation labor and system downtime for contractors.
What's the difference between 13 SEER and newer SEER2 ratings on other units? +
SEER2 testing uses stricter temperature conditions, typically yielding ratings 15-20 percent lower than SEER1. A 13 SEER1 unit roughly equals 10.5-11 SEER2. Newer high-efficiency models at 15-16 SEER2 deliver measurably better utility savings over 10+ years.
Is R454B refrigerant compatible with older Goodman systems? +
R454B requires systems specifically designed for it and cannot be retrofitted into R22 or R410A-only equipment. Verify your condenser unit supports R454B before purchasing. Most modern Goodman condensers manufactured after 2020 accept this refrigerant.
How does this 75 dB noise level compare to premium units? +
Typical variable-stage or scroll compressor systems operate at 70-72 dB. This unit's single-stage rotary compressor runs 3-5 dB louder, noticeably more sound in quiet environments. Not ideal for homes adjacent to sleeping areas or noise-sensitive zones.
Does online registration really matter for the 10-year warranty? +
Yes, manufacturer records require proof of installation date. Registration takes five minutes and protects your warranty claim eligibility. Without it, coverage may be denied or reduced to shorter periods.
