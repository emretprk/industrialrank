Review

Goodman's combination gas furnace and air conditioner system represents a solid mid-to-premium offering for homeowners seeking all-in-one climate control. This upflow configuration fits standard indoor installations, and the modulating furnace with variable-speed blower provides responsive comfort management. The system integrates Goodman's proven scroll compressor technology with modern refrigerant compatibility, positioning it as a practical choice for replacement or new construction across residential markets.

Performance numbers tell the real story here. The furnace delivers 97% AFUE efficiency with 120,000 BTU heating output, meaning 97 cents of every dollar spent on fuel generates usable heat. Cooling capacity registers at 45,500 BTU with a 16 SEER2 rating, translating to measurably lower summer electric bills compared to baseline equipment. The variable-speed blower maxes out at 2,000 CFM, allowing the system to fine-tune airflow for both heating and cooling cycles rather than operating full-blast constantly.

Pros

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this system truly deliver 4-ton or 5-ton cooling capacity? +
The product title claims 4-ton but specifications list 5-ton tonnage with 45,500 BTU cooling output. Contact the retailer to clarify before purchase, as this discrepancy affects system sizing calculations and long-term satisfaction. Proper tonnage matching to your home's cooling load is critical.
What type of ductwork and indoor coil work with this furnace? +
The upflow A-coil configuration fits standard vertical installations typical in basements or utility closets. Suction and liquid lines measure 1 1/8 inches and 3/8 inches respectively, requiring compatible lineset connections. Verify your existing ductwork matches standard five-ton specifications before purchase.
How much will this system save on heating and cooling costs? +
Exact savings depend on your current equipment efficiency and local utility rates. A 97% AFUE furnace typically cuts heating costs 15-25 percent versus older 80% units. The 16 SEER2 rating saves similar percentages on cooling. Use online calculators with your specific rates and climate zone for accurate estimates.
How does single-stage cooling compare to two-stage systems? +
Single-stage compressors run full-capacity or off, less efficient than two-stage models that ramp down during mild conditions. You'll see slightly higher summer bills during partial-load periods. For consistent energy performance, consider two-stage alternatives, though this unit's 16 SEER2 rating remains competitive overall.
Is the 10-year warranty enough protection? +
Ten years provides solid coverage for manufacturer defects and premature failures. Most systems operate reliably beyond this period with proper maintenance. For additional peace of mind, inquire about extended service plans from your installer covering repairs after the warranty expires.
