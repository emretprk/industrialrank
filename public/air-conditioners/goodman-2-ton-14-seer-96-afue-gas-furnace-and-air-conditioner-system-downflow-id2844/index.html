Review

Goodman's 2-ton gas furnace and air conditioner combo represents the practical middle ground in residential HVAC. At $643, this downflow system from Online Supply targets replacement buyers seeking efficiency gains without premium positioning. The package combines a 60,000 BTU furnace with matching 24,000 BTU cooling capacity, making it suitable for smaller homes, additions, or light-load applications. Goodman maintains solid market presence in contractor circles, and this configuration hits the spec sheet requirements for code compliance across most regions.

The furnace delivers 96% AFUE efficiency, translating to measurable savings during heating season compared to 80-85% baseline equipment. Cooling performance of 14 SEER meets current minimum efficiency standards without reaching premium territory. The multi-speed blower motor adds practical value by reducing runtime noise and improving air distribution compared to fixed-speed alternatives. Single-stage compressor and burner mean less operational flexibility during partial-load conditions, but both components perform reliably during design-day demands. Maximum airflow of 1200 CFM handles typical ductwork runs up to 50 feet with proper sizing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this unit qualify for ENERGY STAR rebates? +
No. The system lacks ENERGY STAR certification despite 96% AFUE and 14 SEER ratings. ENERGY STAR typically requires higher SEER thresholds or variable-capacity compressors. Check local utility rebates, as some regions offer incentives for AFUE above 95% independent of ENERGY STAR status.
Can I install this system in my attic with limited headroom? +
Yes, the downflow configuration works in attics. Height of 34.5 inches and width of 17.5 inches fit standard roof cavities. Ensure PVC exhaust venting can route to an exterior wall or roof penetration. Consult installation instructions for minimum clearance around intake and discharge areas.
How does 14 SEER compare to newer systems? +
14 SEER meets 2023 minimum efficiency standards. Newer premium units reach 16-18 SEER with variable-capacity compressors. Higher SEER reduces cooling costs 10-25% in hot climates. This unit balances budget constraints with meaningful efficiency over basic 10-12 SEER alternatives.
Should I buy this instead of a Carrier or Lennox equivalent? +
Goodman competes on price and reliability. Carrier and Lennox typically cost $150-300 more at comparable SEER levels but offer quieter operation and longer-term resale benefits. Goodman's 96% AFUE furnace matches premium brands. Choose Goodman if budget is primary, others if noise or brand reputation matters.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Parts warranty covers compressor, heat exchanger, coils, and electrical components. Excludes refrigerant, labor, and damage from improper installation. Online registration required within 30 days. Refrigerant leaks and normal maintenance remain your responsibility.
