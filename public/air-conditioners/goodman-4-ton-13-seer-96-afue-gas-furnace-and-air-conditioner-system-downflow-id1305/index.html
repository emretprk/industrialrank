Review

The Goodman 4-ton gas furnace and air conditioner system represents the practical middle ground in residential HVAC. This downflow A-coil configuration delivers 100,000 BTU of heating output paired with 48,000 BTU cooling capacity, replacing older GSX130483 through GSX130485 units. Goodman maintains strong brand recognition among contractors and homeowners for straightforward engineering and reliable performance across varied climates.

Efficiency numbers tell the real story here. The furnace achieves 96% AFUE, translating to minimal waste during heating cycles, while the single-stage reciprocating compressor and 13 SEER rating provide solid cooling efficiency without premium costs. Multi-speed blower technology at 2000 CFM maximum adapts to actual heating and cooling demand, optimizing comfort and reducing short-cycling. The system requires 115V single-phase power with breaker sizing between 26.3 and 45 amps, fitting standard residential electrical panels.

Installation

Installation demands proper ductwork sizing for 2000 CFM airflow and downflow configuration placement in attics or utility closets. Refrigerant piping uses standard 3/8 inch liquid and 1 1/8 inch suction lines with 1/2 inch insulation thickness, requiring up to 50 feet of lineset. PVC flue venting with right-side exit simplifies modern code compliance. Professional installation typically runs 4-6 hours for competent technicians.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this system with a 10-year parts warranty upon online registration, protecting major components including the compressor and heat exchanger. Coverage does not include refrigerant or labor costs, which vary by installer. Registration documentation matters for claims, so retain proof of purchase and registration confirmation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size home does this 4-ton system cool effectively? +
Four tons handles roughly 1600-2000 square feet depending on insulation, windows, and climate zone. The 48,000 BTU cooling capacity works well in moderate climates but may struggle in extreme heat or poorly insulated spaces. Have a professional load calculation performed for your specific home.
Can I use this system with my existing ductwork and lineset? +
Possibly, but verify compatibility first. The 2000 CFM maximum airflow and downflow configuration require properly sized ducts. If replacing similar-capacity equipment, existing 3/8 inch and 1 1/8 inch refrigerant lines may work, though new lineset installation is often recommended for reliability.
How does 13 SEER compare to newer air conditioners? +
13 SEER is solid mid-tier efficiency. Modern systems range from 14-16 SEER or higher. The difference translates to roughly 7-15% lower cooling costs versus this unit. For homeowners upgrading from 10 SEER systems, this delivers meaningful savings without premium pricing.
Why isn't this system ENERGY STAR certified? +
ENERGY STAR certification requires 15+ SEER for air conditioners and specific furnace thresholds. This unit's 13 SEER rating falls just below that threshold. You'll miss potential utility rebates, though 96% AFUE furnace efficiency remains excellent regardless of ENERGY STAR status.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Parts coverage includes the compressor, heat exchanger, and major components upon registration. Labor and refrigerant charges are excluded. Coverage typically runs from purchase date or installation, so document everything carefully for potential claims.
