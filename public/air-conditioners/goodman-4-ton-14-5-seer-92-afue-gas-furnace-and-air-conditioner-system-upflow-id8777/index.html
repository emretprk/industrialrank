Review

Goodman's 4-to-5 ton combo system represents a practical middle ground for contractors upgrading split systems or homeowners replacing failed units. This upflow configuration accepts R410A or R22 refrigerant and uses a scroll compressor, proven technology in thousands of installations nationwide. The system ships at 48 pounds and fits standard 21-by-21-by-30-inch equipment closets, making it a straightforward swap for many existing setups.

The furnace delivers 48,000 BTU of heating output at 92% AFUE efficiency, performing respectably for winter comfort without excessive gas consumption. Cooling capacity matches at 48,000 BTU with 14.5 SEER rating and Energy Star certification. The multi-speed blower motor provides flexibility, reaching 1600 CFM maximum, while the single-stage compressor and burner operate at full or idle rather than modulating between speeds. Total system requires 208 or 230 volt single-phase power and appropriate breaker sizing between 26.4 and 45 amps.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this furnace work with existing ductwork from my 15-year-old system? +
Yes, if your current ducts match the upflow configuration and accept 1600 CFM airflow. The multi-speed blower adjusts to existing ductwork restrictions. However, older ducts may have leaks, reducing efficiency gains. Have an HVAC contractor evaluate before purchasing to confirm compatibility and identify duct sealing needs.
What does the 92% AFUE rating actually mean for my heating bill? +
AFUE means 92 cents of every heating dollar heats your home while 8 cents escapes through the flue. Compared to 80% AFUE units, you save roughly 13% annually on gas costs. Actual savings depend on climate, insulation, thermostat settings, and local gas rates. In a cold region, savings could exceed $200-400 yearly.
Is single-stage operation a significant drawback compared to variable-speed models? +
Single-stage runs at full power or idle, consuming more gas during mild weather than modulating units. Two-stage systems reduce this inefficiency. However, at 92% AFUE and 14.5 SEER pricing, this remains a solid budget-conscious choice. Consider variable-stage if you live where heating or cooling seasons are extremely long.
Can I use this with my current R22 system or must I upgrade to R410A? +
This unit accepts both R22 and R410A refrigerant, providing flexibility if you own R22 equipment. R410A is now standard and R22 phasing out, so R410A operation is recommended for future servicing. Clarify refrigerant type with your installer before purchase to match your existing condenser.
Does online registration really matter for the 10-year warranty? +
Yes, registration is mandatory to activate the full 10-year coverage. Without it, warranty coverage drops significantly. Registration takes minutes on Goodman's website with your serial number and proof of purchase. Keep documentation and register immediately after installation.
