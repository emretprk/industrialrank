Review

The Goodman 2-ton 14 SEER 92% AFUE downflow system represents solid middle-ground equipment for residential HVAC contractors and homeowners. This combo unit pairs a gas furnace with an air conditioner in one package, reducing installation complexity and labor costs. Goodman maintains strong market share for good reason: proven compressors, straightforward design, and reasonable pricing without sacrificing core performance. The downflow configuration is practical for homes where indoor equipment space is at a premium.

Performance numbers are honest and competitive without being flashy. The furnace delivers 100,000 BTU heating capacity at 92% AFUE, meaning 92% of fuel energy converts to usable heat. Cooling output sits at 24,000 BTU with 14 SEER efficiency. The rotary compressor handles single-stage cooling with 2,000 CFM maximum airflow. Multi-speed blower motor adjusts to load conditions, reducing runtime on mild days. Operating at 208/230V single-phase, 60Hz with 25-amp maximum breaker requirement, it fits standard residential electrical service. The 71 dB noise level is typical for this class.

Installation

Downflow configuration requires adequate return air clearance below the unit and proper ductwork orientation. PVC plastic flue is essential, standard in most modern homes. The 3/8-inch liquid line and 3/4-inch suction line with 3/4-inch insulation thickness follow industry norms. Dimensions of 27.5 inches by 26 inches by 26 inches make it compact enough for most mechanical closets. Weighing 22 pounds, it's manageable for two technicians. Full installation documentation is available from Goodman.

Warranty & Reliability

Ten-year parts and labor warranty applies when you register online with Goodman, covering both furnace and air conditioner components. This is standard protection for residential combo systems and demonstrates manufacturer confidence. Registration is straightforward and recommended immediately after installation. Warranty does not cover wear items, improper installation, or neglected maintenance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this system Energy Star certified? +
No, it is not Energy Star certified. While 92% AFUE and 14 SEER are respectable, Energy Star models require higher efficiency thresholds. This means missing potential utility rebates or tax credits in certain states, though operating costs remain reasonable.
Can I install this in an attic or crawlspace? +
Yes, the downflow/upflow configuration supports multiple locations. However, adequate space for return air below the unit is critical. Ensure your installation location allows proper ventilation clearance and safe PVC flue routing to the exterior.
How does single-stage cooling compare to two-stage? +
Single-stage runs either fully on or fully off, less sophisticated than two-stage systems that ramp capacity. You'll still get reliable cooling at 24,000 BTU and 14 SEER efficiency, just with more on/off cycling. Two-stage offers quieter, more even temperature control.
Is Goodman reliability competitive with Carrier or Lennox? +
Goodman uses proven rotary compressor technology and maintains reasonable failure rates. They're slightly less expensive than Carrier or Lennox but comparable in durability. Contractor feedback is solid, though premium brands offer more advanced features at higher cost.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Parts and labor on furnace and air conditioner components when registered. Does not include damage from improper installation, neglect, or wear items. Registration is quick and essential to activate coverage.
