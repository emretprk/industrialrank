Review

The Goodman GMEC960303AN pairs a high-efficiency gas furnace with a two-stage air conditioner in a compact A-coil design suitable for upflow or horizontal installation. This 30,000 BTU heating and 24,000 BTU cooling combination targets the middle market where contractors balance efficiency with affordability. The system arrives Energy Star certified, scroll compressor equipped, and backed by Goodman's established distribution network.

Heating efficiency stands at 96% AFUE, delivering measurable savings against baseline 80-85% units, particularly valuable in northern climates running 4,000 to 6,000 heating degree days annually. The cooling side achieves 16 SEER rating through its two-stage compressor and variable-speed ECM blower motor running up to 1,200 CFM. Real-world performance depends heavily on proper refrigerant charging and matched indoor coil, as undersized or mischarged systems lose efficiency quickly.

Pros

Installation

Installation mandates a licensed HVAC technician due to gas line connections and flue venting requirements, non-negotiable in virtually all jurisdictions. The unit requires 115V single-phase 60Hz power with 15.7 to 20 amp breaker sizing. Proper suction line (3/4 inch) and liquid line (3/8 inch) connections are critical, as is venting to PVC/plastic flue material per local codes.

Warranty & Reliability

Coverage includes a lifetime heat exchanger warranty, 10-year full unit replacement, and 10-year parts warranty when registered online. The lifetime heat exchanger coverage is valuable protection against the furnace's most expensive component failure. Ten-year parts coverage is industry standard but registration is mandatory to activate benefits.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet say 3 Ton tonnage when the title says 2 Ton? +
This appears to be a data error in the manufacturer's specifications. The 30,000 BTU heating and 24,000 BTU cooling capacity align with a 2-ton system. Contact the supplier for clarification before purchase, as tonnage mismatch could affect system matching and performance claims.
Can this system work with my existing ductwork designed for a 2-ton unit? +
Yes, if your ductwork is properly sized for 2-ton equipment. The ECM blower motor with 1,200 CFM maximum capacity can adjust to existing static pressure. However, undersized ducts reduce efficiency. A technician should verify CFM requirements match your duct design before installation.
How much will 96% AFUE save on heating bills compared to my old 80% furnace? +
A 16-point AFUE improvement typically saves 16-20% on heating costs, though actual savings depend on climate, insulation, and thermostat usage. In a 5,000 heating degree day area with $1,200 annual heating bills, expect $200-240 yearly savings. Payback occurs in 8-12 years through efficiency gains alone.
How does 16 SEER compare to other mid-market units I should consider? +
16 SEER places this in the upper-mid range above standard 14-15 SEER models but below premium 17-20 SEER inverter systems. For heating-dominant climates, the furnace efficiency matters more than cooling rating. This balances reasonable cooling performance with good heating efficiency at moderate pricing.
What happens if I don't register the warranty online? +
Without online registration, warranty coverage defaults to manufacturer specifications but may exclude certain components or require proof of purchase. Registration is free and takes five minutes. Always register immediately after professional installation to activate the full 10-year parts benefit.
