Review

The Goodman DSXC180361 represents a common-sense choice in mid-market air conditioning. At $3,052, this 3-ton condenser slots between budget bare-bones models and premium brands, offering two-stage compressor technology that genuinely impacts utility bills. Goodman has built contractor loyalty through straightforward reliability rather than flashy features. This unit continues that philosophy, delivering 35,000 BTU cooling capacity with Energy Star certification and a respectable 18 SEER1 efficiency rating for residential retrofit work.

Performance metrics tell the story here. The 18 SEER1 rating means real energy savings compared to 13-14 SEER single-stage predecessors. That two-stage scroll compressor runs at reduced capacity during partial-load conditions, which is most of the cooling season in moderate climates. The 71 dB output reflects this efficiency, operating quieter than many competitors because it's not hammering full-speed all day. Refrigerant R410A and sweat valve connections align with industry standards, though techs need proper certification for installation.

Installation

Installation complexity sits at moderate level. The unit requires 208/230V single-phase power with 21.9 to 35 amp breaker capacity, standard for residential applications. Sweat valve connections demand brazing expertise, which adds labor costs if your installer lacks certification. Dimensions of 35.5 by 35.5 by 38.25 inches fit most rooftop or slab pads without structural modifications. Plan for proper refrigerant evacuation and line-set connections per EPA standards.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman's 10-year parts and compressor warranty with online registration provides solid protection at this price point. Registration is straightforward but required to activate full coverage. This matches competitor offerings and covers manufacturing defects, though labor and refrigerant are buyer responsibility. For a $3,000 investment, this warranty cushion justifies the registration effort.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 18 SEER1 translate to actual monthly cooling bills? +
An 18 SEER unit uses roughly 15-20 percent less energy than a 13 SEER baseline system in the same climate. For a 3-ton condenser running 500 hours annually, expect $150-250 in annual savings at typical regional electricity rates, depending on local HVAC operation patterns and climate severity.
Can I install this myself if I have basic HVAC knowledge? +
EPA regulations prohibit self-installation of refrigerated systems. You must hire a licensed technician certified for R410A handling and brazing work. Professional installation costs typically range $1,200-2,000 depending on local labor rates and existing line-set condition.
What's the real-world advantage of two-stage cooling? +
Two-stage operation reduces compressor cycling and noise during mild weather when full capacity isn't needed. This extends equipment life, improves humidity control, and lowers utility bills. Most homeowners notice quieter operation and consistent comfort compared to single-stage units.
How does this compare to Carrier or Lennox models at similar price points? +
Goodman trades brand prestige for value. This unit matches efficiency ratings of Carrier's Performance series but costs $400-600 less. Lennox offers better warranties but charges premium pricing. Goodman's strength lies in contractor accessibility and parts availability.
What happens if my equipment fails during year five? +
The 10-year warranty covers compressor and parts replacement at no cost. However, you pay labor and refrigerant charges. Without online registration, coverage is five years, so register immediately after installation to preserve full protection.
