Review

The Goodman 3-ton horizontal system sits in the value tier of the HVAC market, targeting budget-focused buyers who need reliable cooling and heating without premium pricing. This matched pair combines a 100,000 BTU furnace with a 35,000 BTU condenser, using proven scroll compressor technology. At $89, it undercuts most branded competitors, making it attractive for contractors handling rental units or homeowners facing unexpected equipment failure.

Performance specs show 80% AFUE heating efficiency and 17-18 SEER cooling, positioning it as baseline rather than premium. The two-stage burner and two-stage condenser design means the system runs at reduced capacity during mild weather, cutting energy use compared to single-stage equipment. Variable-speed blower handles up to 2000 CFM, adapting airflow to actual demand. However, that 71 dB noise level runs louder than many competitors operating at 65-68 dB.

Installation

This horizontal unit requires 120V single-phase 60Hz power with a 21.3 to 35 amp breaker, making it compatible with standard home electrical. Sweat-type valve connections mean standard brazing skills apply. The 3/8 inch liquid line and 7/8 inch suction line with 3/4 inch insulation follow typical sizing, though pre-charged refrigerant lines simplify field work. Metal exhaust flue is required, limiting placement options.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year parts warranty provides reasonable coverage for a budget system, but requires online registration to activate. This is standard practice among budget manufacturers. Coverage appears to include compressor and major components. Ten years matches mid-tier competitors, though premium brands often offer 12-year protection. Keep documentation for claims processing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the actual cooling capacity in tons? +
The specs list 3.5 ton tonnage with 35,000 BTU cooling output. This slight mismatch (true 3-ton would be 36,000 BTU) suggests conservative rating or testing methodology. Real-world cooling should match 3-ton capacity adequately for most homes in moderate climates.
Can I use this in an attic or crawlspace? +
The horizontal configuration (21 inches tall, 26 inches deep) fits tight spaces where upflow furnaces won't fit. Ensure metal exhaust flue routing is possible and airflow paths remain unobstructed. Variable-speed blower handles restricted airflow better than single-stage designs.
How does 80% AFUE compare to modern standards? +
80% AFUE is the baseline for gas furnaces allowed by current federal standards. High-efficiency units hit 90-96% AFUE, cutting heating costs 15-20% annually. If heating dominates your energy bills, this might cost $300-500 more per year than premium equipment.
Is this system Energy Star certified? +
Yes, the cooling portion meets Energy Star standards. However, Energy Star certification doesn't apply to furnaces at this efficiency level, only to 95%+ AFUE models. Cooling efficiency is honest but not exceptional compared to premium competitors.
What does the warranty actually cover? +
10 years parts coverage applies to compressor and major components after online registration. Check terms for labor coverage, which often isn't included. Evaporator coil carries separate warranty noted in documentation. Read fine print before purchase.
