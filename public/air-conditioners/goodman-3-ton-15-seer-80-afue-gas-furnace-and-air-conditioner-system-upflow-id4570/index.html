Review

Goodman's 3.5-ton split system occupies the practical middle ground in residential HVAC. At this price point, you're getting an Energy Star-certified unit that delivers respectable cooling performance without the premium cost of modulating compressors or 95% AFUE furnaces. The system ships as an upflow A-coil configuration suited for basement or closet installation. It's the kind of equipment contractors spec regularly for new builds and equipment swaps where budget clarity matters as much as performance.

Cooling output reaches 34,800 BTU (rated at 3.5 tons), and the 16 SEER2 rating confirms efficient operation across full-season conditions. On heating, you get 100,000 BTU from a dual-burner natural gas or propane furnace rated at 80% AFUE. The variable-speed blower motor automatically adjusts airflow between 0 and 2000 CFM, matching demand without wasting energy on full-blast operation. This hybrid approach, where cooling efficiency is strong but heating efficiency is standard-tier, makes sense for mixed climates where neither season dominates.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 16 SEER2 rating exceeds the baseline 15 SEER requirement, delivering measurable cooling efficiency gains
  • Variable-speed blower motor with 2000 CFM max capacity provides better humidity control and comfort consistency
  • Two-stage burner allows the furnace to run at 50% capacity during mild heating days, reducing short-cycling and energy waste
  • Two-stage gas burner and scroll compressor are Goodman's proven workhorse components with minimal field failure rates
  • 10-year parts warranty with online registration covers both air conditioning and furnace components

Cons

  • 80% AFUE furnace efficiency lags modern high-efficiency units that reach 95%, costing more to operate long-term in heating-dominant regions
  • Single-stage air conditioning compressor cannot modulate capacity, so cooling runs at full output or off, reducing seasonal efficiency in mild weather
  • 73.5 dB noise level is moderately loud for residential applications, especially during compressor operation on hot days

Who Should Buy

Best For

Homeowners and contractors in transition zones (heating and cooling equally important) who want solid efficiency without premium pricing. This system balances reasonable operating costs with upfront affordability for properties that don't justify high-efficiency equipment investment.

Consider Alternatives If

Cold-climate installers should look toward 95% AFUE furnaces, while hot-climate builders may prefer variable-capacity compressors for better part-load efficiency.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 16 SEER2 compare to the old 15 SEER rating mentioned in the title? +
SEER2 is the newer testing standard that reflects real-world conditions more accurately than SEER. A 16 SEER2 unit typically performs about 15 percent better than old 15 SEER equipment, translating to lower cooling costs over the summer months, especially in humid climates.
Will this 80% AFUE furnace handle extreme cold climates? +
The 100,000 BTU output handles most residential heating loads, but 80% AFUE means 20 percent of fuel energy is lost up the flue. In Minnesota or Alberta winters, a 95% AFUE unit saves substantially more on heating bills. This furnace suits temperate zones where heating season is moderate.
Can I upgrade just the air conditioning or furnace later if one component fails? +
Yes. The split-system design lets you replace the condenser (outdoor unit) or furnace independently, though refrigerant line compatibility and coil sizing must align. Most contractors prefer keeping matched units for warranty coverage and performance optimization.
What is the difference between single-stage and two-stage compressor operation? +
This system's single-stage compressor runs full capacity when cooling demand exists or shuts off when satisfied. Two-stage designs modulate to 50 percent capacity on mild days, improving seasonal efficiency. Single-stage is simpler, more durable, and less expensive, but uses more electricity during shoulder seasons.
Do I need to register this unit online for the warranty to be valid? +
Yes. Goodman requires online registration within a set period to activate the full 10-year coverage. Without registration, coverage may be limited to shorter terms. Keep the SKU (LS38783850) and purchase documentation handy for the registration process.
