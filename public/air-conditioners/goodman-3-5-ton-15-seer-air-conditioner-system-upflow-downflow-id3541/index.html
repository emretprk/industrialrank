Review

The Goodman 3.5 Ton split-system condenser occupies the middle market tier, pitched between budget-conscious contractors and premium-efficiency seekers. At 42,000 BTU cooling capacity with a 16 SEER1 rating, it delivers measurable efficiency gains over aging 13-SEER units without straining electrical infrastructure. The Energy Star certification and 10-year registered warranty appeal to homeowners wanting factory-backed peace of mind. Online Supply positions this unit as a straightforward replacement option for residential retrofit jobs and new construction alike.

Performance hinges on the scroll compressor operating at full capacity in single-stage mode, producing 1,600 CFM through a variable-speed ECM blower. At 73 dB, acoustic output trails some quieter competitors but remains acceptable for most residential settings. The 16 SEER1 rating reflects real-world testing, meaning summer cooling bills drop noticeably compared to legacy 10-12 SEER units. R410A refrigerant ensures compliance with current EPA standards, and the 3/8-inch liquid line paired with 1 1/8-inch suction line simplifies connection to standard indoor coils.

Installation

Installation requires standard split-system connectivity: liquid line, suction line, and electrical terminals wired to a compatible indoor coil unit. The 50-foot pre-insulated suction line (1/2-inch foam) ships ready, shortening job time. Upflow and downflow A-coil orientation flexibility handles most attic and basement placements. Electrical setup demands 208/230V single-phase 60Hz service with breaker sizing between 23.5 and 15 amps, confirming adequate panel capacity beforehand. Technicians should verify refrigerant compatibility and perform standard pressure and superheat checks during commissioning.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this unit with a full 10-year parts warranty upon online registration, protecting compressor, coil, and electrical components. Registration is straightforward and adds value without hidden conditions. The Goodman Evaporator Coil Warranty extends coverage to the indoor side, a practical benefit for split-system longevity. Unregistered units default to shorter coverage, making the five-minute registration step worthwhile for long-term asset protection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet list 5 tons when the product title says 3.5 tons? +
This appears to be a data entry error from the distributor. The 42,000 BTU cooling capacity and 16 SEER rating align with a standard 3.5-ton unit, not 5 tons. Verify tonnage with the manufacturer before ordering to ensure compatibility with your indoor coil.
Can this condenser work with my existing air handler and ductwork? +
Yes, if your indoor coil is R410A compatible and properly sized for 42,000 BTU. The upflow/downflow A-coil design fits standard installations. Have a technician verify tonnage matching, refrigerant type, and suction/liquid line sizes before proceeding.
How much will the 16 SEER rating save me on energy bills? +
Savings depend on your current unit's SEER and local electricity rates. Upgrading from a 10 SEER unit typically cuts cooling costs 35-40% in hot climates. A 13 SEER replacement yields roughly 20% savings. Use online SEER calculators with your utility rate for precise estimates.
Is the variable-speed motor worth the extra cost over single-speed blowers? +
Yes. The ECM blower ramps up and down with cooling demand, reducing runtime hours, improving dehumidification, and lowering noise during partial-load operation. Long-term energy and comfort gains outweigh the upfront premium by $300-500 over 10 years.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Compressor, condenser coil, fan motor, and electrical components. Refrigerant, labor, and damage from improper installation are excluded. Registration is required. Review the detailed Goodman warranty document to confirm coverage limits for your specific application.
