Review

The Goodman GMVM970804CN occupies a practical middle ground in the residential HVAC market. At $736, this 3-ton upflow system combines gas furnace and air conditioning in one package, making it attractive for full-system replacements. Goodman's reputation for straightforward, serviceable equipment appeals to both homeowners and contractors. The unit carries Energy Star certification and includes a 10-year warranty with online registration, signaling manufacturer confidence in durability.

Performance specs justify the mid-market positioning. The air conditioning side delivers SEER 18 efficiency through a two-stage scroll compressor and R410A refrigerant, translating to meaningful cooling savings for homes experiencing hot summers. Heating efficiency reaches 97% AFUE with an 80,000 BTU modulating burner that adjusts output rather than cycling on and off. The variable-speed ECM blower motor runs at capacity matching demand, avoiding the energy waste of constant high-speed operation. Maximum airflow of 1,600 CFM suits standard three-ton ductwork.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • SEER 18 cooling efficiency with two-stage compressor delivers measurable energy savings on cooling bills
  • 97% AFUE furnace rating means efficient heating, especially for homes in moderate to cold climates
  • Modulating burner stages optimize gas usage rather than simple on-off operation
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor adjusts to actual heating and cooling demand, reducing runtime waste
  • Energy Star certified with 10-year warranty when registered online, providing reasonable protection

Cons

Who Should Buy

Best For

Homeowners in moderate to cold climates replacing 10+ year old systems, and HVAC contractors stocking reliable mid-range inventory for price-sensitive customers. This system delivers measurable efficiency gains without luxury pricing.

Consider Alternatives If

Installation

Professional installation is mandatory, not optional. Gas connections require licensed technician work in most jurisdictions due to safety codes. The unit requires 115V single-phase power with 21.9 to 35 amp breaker capacity. Upflow configuration with A-coil design fits typical attic or basement installations. PVC exhaust flue is acceptable. Weight of 142 pounds demands proper support, especially if ceiling-mounted.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this system with 10 years parts and labor coverage upon online registration. This is above baseline but below premium manufacturer warranties. Registration is simple and typically costs nothing. Coverage applies to manufacturing defects but excludes neglected maintenance or improper installation. Most contractors include this as standard practice.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet list both 3 ton and 3.5 ton tonnage? +
The cooling capacity shows 35,000 BTU, which is 3.5 tons technically. However, it's marketed as a 3-ton system. Confirm with your contractor which applies to your home's calculated load requirement, as oversizing can reduce efficiency and comfort.
Will this fit my existing ductwork and installation space? +
The unit is 34.5 inches tall, 21 inches wide, and 28.75 inches deep. Verify these dimensions against your attic, basement, or closet space before purchase. Upflow configuration works in most standard installations, but measure twice before ordering.
How much will this system reduce my energy bills compared to a 13 SEER unit? +
SEER 18 versus SEER 13 represents roughly a 28% cooling efficiency improvement. Real savings vary by climate, usage patterns, and local electricity rates. In hot climates running AC six months yearly, expect 15 to 25% total cooling bill reduction.
How does Goodman compare to Lennox or Carrier at this price point? +
Goodman prioritizes value and serviceability over brand prestige. Carrier and Lennox offer similar specs at higher prices. This Goodman delivers comparable performance with less premium pricing and equally available parts at independent suppliers.
Is the 10-year warranty worth registering online? +
Yes. Registration is quick and free. Without it, warranty defaults to one year. Ten years of coverage on a $736 investment protects against compressor failure or heat exchanger cracks, which cost hundreds to thousands to replace.
