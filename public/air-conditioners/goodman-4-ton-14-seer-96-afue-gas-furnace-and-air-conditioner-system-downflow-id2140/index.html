Review

The ACiQ 4-ton downflow furnace and air conditioner combo lands in the practical middle ground of the residential HVAC market. Badged as Goodman equipment, this system targets replacement jobs and new construction where budget constraints matter but efficiency gains are still valued. At $3,136 installed cost, it undercuts premium brands by 20-30 percent while maintaining respectable performance metrics and a decade-long warranty. Contractors appreciate its straightforward specifications and broad fuel compatibility.

Performance numbers reveal a competent, if unspectacular, offering. The furnace delivers 96% AFUE heating efficiency, nearly matching high-end condensing models and cutting heating fuel consumption versus older 80-85% equipment. Cooling capacity reaches 48,000 BTU at 14 SEER, sufficient for moderate climates but trailing 16-18 SEER competitors. The variable-speed ECM blower motor with 2,000 CFM maximum airflow continuously adjusts to actual demand, reducing on-off cycling. Two-stage burner operation provides steady heating at partial load. R454B refrigerant keeps the system current with EPA standards.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does 96% AFUE mean for my heating costs? +
AFUE measures how much fuel converts to usable heat. 96% means only 4% escapes as exhaust waste. Compared to a 90% unit, this system saves approximately 6-8% annually on heating energy, translating to $150-300 savings per year depending on climate and usage patterns.
Can I install this system in a horizontal position? +
Yes. The spec lists downflow/horizontal configuration, making it suitable for attics or crawlspaces with horizontal ductwork runs. Ensure adequate clearance for the 35.5-inch width and depth, and verify proper condensate drainage in horizontal installation.
How does 14 SEER compare to newer air conditioners? +
14 SEER meets federal baseline efficiency but underperforms modern 16-18 SEER units by 12-15% cooling energy consumption annually. For hot climates, upgrading to higher SEER often recovers costs through utility savings and rebates within 4-6 years.
Should I choose this over a true high-efficiency 18 SEER system? +
This unit wins on upfront cost and ECM blower efficiency. An 18 SEER upgrade costs $1,200-2,000 more but delivers superior cooling savings in warm climates. For mild regions, the value proposition of this 14 SEER diminishes. Compare three competing quotes before deciding.
Is the 10-year warranty worth the registration hassle? +
Yes. Registration takes five minutes online and protects you against compressor failure costing $1,500-2,500. Keep receipts and service records accessible. The warranty becomes transferable in some regions, adding resale value to your home.
