Review

The Goodman 3-ton combo system pairs a 120,000 BTU furnace with a 36,000 BTU air conditioner in a single downflow unit, priced at $3,770. This mid-range offering targets homeowners seeking balanced performance without premium costs. The furnace delivers 96% AFUE efficiency, while the condenser achieves 14 SEER1 cooling efficiency with R454B refrigerant. Both components carry Energy Star certification, signaling compliance with current efficiency standards. The downflow configuration suits basements and crawlspaces where upflow isn't practical.

Performance specs reveal a well-thought design for moderate climates. The 96% AFUE furnace burns natural gas or propane through a two-stage burner, meaning it ramps output instead of cycling on-off, reducing temperature swings by roughly 2-3 degrees. The variable-speed ECM blower pulls up to 2,000 CFM and adjusts based on heating and cooling demand. Cooling output of 36,000 BTU with a single-stage scroll compressor operates at full capacity, which works fine for mild days but uses more energy during partial-load conditions compared to modulating compressors.

Installation

This downflow unit measures 30 inches tall by 24.5 inches wide by 21 inches deep, fitting standard basement utility spaces. Refrigerant lines use 3/8-inch liquid and 3/4-inch suction connections with 3/4-inch insulation thickness. The furnace requires PVC or plastic exhaust venting, simplifying ductwork in tight spaces. Electrical demand is straightforward, needing 115V single-phase power with an 18.7 to 30-amp breaker. Most installations take 4-6 hours for experienced technicians.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 3.5-ton cooling capacity the same as 3-ton? +
No, this unit is technically 3.5 tons of cooling (42,000 BTU nominal) but marketed as 3-ton for a 36,000 BTU condenser. The oversizing means you get extra capacity on extremely hot days, but it may reduce part-load efficiency on milder days when the compressor could modulate lower.
Can I install this upflow instead of downflow? +
Yes, the specs list configuration as upflow or downflow. However, you must verify your ductwork connections match the unit orientation. Downflow requires supply ducts below and return above, while upflow reverses this. Your HVAC installer determines feasibility based on your home's layout.
How does 14 SEER compare to newer systems? +
14 SEER1 is solid mid-range performance, delivering about 20-25 percent better cooling efficiency than 11-12 SEER units from the 2010s. However, newer 16-18 SEER systems save an additional 10-15 percent. For moderate climates, 14 SEER offers good value, but high-humidity areas benefit from higher SEER ratings.
Is R454B refrigerant future-proof? +
R454B is EPA-approved and represents current industry standard for new systems. However, regulations may shift within 10-15 years. This unit should operate problem-free for its 15-20 year lifespan, but eventual replacement may use different refrigerants. That's normal equipment lifecycle.
What does online registration actually cover? +
Registering within 30 days of installation activates the full 10-year warranty. Without registration, coverage drops to 5 years parts and 1 year labor. Registration is simple, requires proof of purchase and installation date, and extends protection significantly.
