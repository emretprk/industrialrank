Review

The Goodman GCVM970603BN represents solid middle-market positioning in the dual-fuel HVAC space. This 2-ton combination system pairs a 60,000 BTU gas furnace with a 24,000 BTU air conditioner, delivering year-round coverage for homes between 1,500 and 2,000 square feet. Goodman's reputation for value engineering shows here, offering measurable efficiency gains without the premium pricing of Lennox or Carrier systems.

Efficiency numbers tell the real story. The furnace achieves 97% AFUE, meaning 97 cents of every dollar spent on gas produces usable heat, compared to 80-90% on older units. Cooling delivers 16 SEER1 efficiency through a two-stage scroll compressor and variable-speed ECM blower motor that adapts to demand. The modulating burner stages fine-tune heating output rather than cycling on and off, reducing temperature swings and improving comfort.

Installation

Professional installation is mandatory. Gas line connections demand licensed technician work, and improper installation risks carbon monoxide exposure and equipment failure. The system requires 115V single-phase 60 Hz power with a 15.7 to 20-amp breaker. Plastic PVC venting must be properly routed away from living spaces and graded for condensate drainage.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this unit with a full 10-year parts and compressor warranty following online registration. This equal coverage differs from competitors offering shorter compressor-only extensions. Registration takes five minutes and protects against manufacturing defects across all major components, though homeowner misuse and improper installation voids coverage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the 16 SEER rating mean my cooling bills will drop by 16 percent? +
No. SEER measures efficiency compared to a 13 SEER baseline, not percentage savings. Compared to a standard 13 SEER unit, you'll save roughly 18 to 25 percent on cooling costs depending on your climate and usage patterns. Real savings depend on thermostat settings, insulation, and local electricity rates.
Can I install this myself to save labor costs? +
No. Gas furnaces legally require licensed technician installation in all jurisdictions. Attempting DIY gas work risks death from carbon monoxide and voids all warranties immediately. Professional installation costs $800 to $1,500 but ensures safety and code compliance.
How much will heating costs drop with 97% AFUE versus my old 85% furnace? +
You'll recoup roughly 12 percent of heating expenses. The efficiency gain compounds over 15 to 20 years, delivering $1,500 to $3,000 in total gas savings depending on climate and usage. Payback typically occurs within 5 to 8 years in cold climates.
Is this system compatible with smart thermostats? +
Yes. The modulating burner and variable-speed blower work with modern thermostats like Ecobee and Nest. However, verify that your 115V power supply supports the thermostat's WiFi module. Some smart thermostats require C-wire power that older installations lack.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
All parts and the compressor for 10 years from purchase with online registration. Excludes damage from improper installation, misuse, lack of maintenance, and cosmetic wear. Requires a licensed technician for any covered repairs.
