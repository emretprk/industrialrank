Review

Goodman's 5-ton split system positions itself as a practical middle ground in the residential HVAC market. This upflow A-coil configuration handles both heating and cooling, making it suitable for 2,000 to 3,500 square foot homes. The system carries a strong reputation for durability among contractors and includes Goodman's standard 10-year warranty with online registration. At $2,053, the price point attracts homeowners seeking replacement equipment without premium features.

The cooling side delivers 60,000 BTU across a two-stage compressor with 16 SEER efficiency, which translates to roughly 10-15% lower cooling costs versus 14 SEER baseline units. The heating side produces 80,000 BTU through a two-stage burner operating at 80% AFUE using either natural gas or propane. Variable-speed blower technology maintains up to 2,000 CFM while keeping noise levels at 75 dB, making it quieter than single-stage units. R410A refrigerant is industry standard and widely supported by service technicians.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 16 SEER cooling efficiency exceeds the 15 SEER rating, providing measurable energy savings over 13-14 SEER competitors
  • Two-stage burner and two-stage compressor reduce energy waste and improve comfort during partial-load conditions
  • Variable-speed blower motor runs quietly at 75 dB and delivers up to 2,000 CFM, matching diverse ductwork configurations
  • 80% AFUE furnace is Energy Star certified and qualifies for utility rebates in many regions
  • Upflow/horizontal A-coil design fits tight spaces, making it ideal for retrofits in closets or attics

Cons

  • 80% AFUE is mid-range, not high-efficiency, so fuel savings take 8-10 years to recoup versus 95%+ units
  • 16 SEER is good but not cutting-edge, whereas newer units reach 18-20 SEER with better grid decoupling
  • Metal exhaust flue requirement adds installation cost compared to units supporting plastic venting

Who Should Buy

Best For

Homeowners replacing 15-year-old split systems or upgrading from single-stage equipment will see immediate comfort and efficiency gains. This is the right choice for those who value reliability and price over premium efficiency features.

Consider Alternatives If

High-efficiency seekers in heating-dominant climates should explore 95%+ AFUE modulating furnaces, and those targeting zero-energy homes should consider variable-capacity units rated 18+ SEER.

Installation

This system requires 208/230V single-phase 60Hz power with breaker sizing between 37.2 and 60 amps. A metal exhaust flue is mandatory for the furnace, which adds cost if existing venting won't accommodate it. Liquid and suction line sizes are 3/8 inch and 7/8 inch respectively. Most experienced HVAC installers complete this upflow configuration in 4-6 hours, though ductwork modifications may extend timelines.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman includes a full 10-year parts warranty when you register online within 30 days of installation. This covers both the furnace and air conditioner compressor, offering genuine peace of mind against manufacturing defects. Labor warranty terms vary by installer, so confirm coverage details before signing the contract.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 16 SEER actually better than the rated 15 SEER specification? +
Yes. The spec sheet lists 16 SEER, which exceeds the 15 SEER marketing name. This unit achieves real-world efficiency slightly above its rated threshold, likely due to two-stage compressor optimization. Expect roughly 10-15% lower cooling costs versus older 14 SEER equipment over a 15-year lifespan.
Can I install this in my attic if space is tight? +
Yes, the upflow A-coil design and 38.25 inch height fit most attic installations. The 3,500-pound weight requires solid structural support. You'll need 3/8 inch liquid and 7/8 inch suction lines running downward, plus a metal exhaust flue for safe operation.
What's the difference between two-stage and single-stage operation? +
Two-stage operation runs the compressor and burner at 60% capacity during mild weather, reducing energy waste and cycling. At full load, both stages activate for maximum output. This reduces temperature swings, improves comfort, and saves 8-12% versus constant on-off cycling in single-stage units.
How does this compare to Lennox or Trane equivalents at the same price? +
Goodman matches Lennox XC15 and Trane XV15 on SEER ratings and warranty length but costs $200-400 less. Trane and Lennox command premiums for brand reputation and regional dealer networks. Real-world durability is comparable across all three manufacturers.
What happens if I don't register online for the warranty? +
Goodman reverts to a five-year limited warranty on parts if you skip registration. Always register within 30 days to unlock the full 10-year coverage, as it protects your investment significantly.
