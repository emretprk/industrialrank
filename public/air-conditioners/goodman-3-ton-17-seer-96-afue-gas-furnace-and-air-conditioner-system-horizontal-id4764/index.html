Review

The Goodman 3 Ton 17 SEER 96% AFUE gas furnace and air conditioner system represents a practical middle ground in the HVAC market. Marketed at $89, this horizontal A-coil unit targets homeowners and contractors seeking efficient climate control without the premium pricing of top-tier brands. Goodman's reputation for reliability combined with strong efficiency ratings makes this system a viable option for residential replacements in moderate climate zones.

Performance metrics align well with modern efficiency standards. The furnace delivers 100,000 BTU output at 96% AFUE, meaning 96 cents of every heating dollar converts to usable heat. Cooling reaches 35,000 BTU with an 18 SEER1 rating, exceeding Energy Star thresholds and qualifying for utility rebates. Two-stage burner and scroll compressor stages operation for improved part-load efficiency. Variable-speed ECM blower handles up to 2,000 CFM, adapting automatically to actual heating or cooling demand. The R410A refrigerant and sweat-connection valve design support straightforward installation.

Installation

This horizontal A-coil configuration suits attic, basement, or utility closet placement. Requires 115V single-phase 60 Hz power with breaker capacity between 21.3 and 35 amps. Suction line needs 7/8 inch diameter with 3/4 inch insulation thickness over 50 feet maximum length, while liquid line uses 3/8 inch connections. Plastic or PVC exhaust flue requirement accommodates modern condensing furnace venting. Proper ductwork sizing and airflow verification are essential for stable operation.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides lifetime coverage on the heat exchanger, addressing the most expensive potential furnace failure. Parts warranty extends 10 years with online product registration, protecting compressors, motors, and control boards. This combination delivers solid long-term protection compared to many competitors offering 5-year standard terms. Registration requirement is straightforward and typically completed during installation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does 18 SEER1 actually mean for my cooling costs? +
SEER1 measures cooling efficiency as BTU output per watt-hour input. An 18 SEER1 unit uses roughly 33% less electricity than a 12 SEER baseline. For average cooling seasons, homeowners typically see $200-400 annual savings depending on local electricity rates, usage patterns, and climate zone temperature variations.
Can this furnace run on propane or only natural gas? +
This model supports both natural gas and propane fuel types. Conversion between fuels requires adjusting the burner orifice, a task typically handled during installation by your HVAC contractor. Verify your fuel source availability before purchasing to confirm compatibility with local utility infrastructure.
Is 3.5 ton capacity the right size for my home? +
Tonnage depends on square footage, insulation, climate, and ductwork efficiency. This 3.5-ton unit handles approximately 3,500-4,200 square feet in moderate climates. Oversizing causes short cycling and reduced efficiency. Have an HVAC contractor perform load calculations using ACCA Manual J methodology to confirm proper sizing before purchase.
How does this Goodman system compare to Bryant or Carrier units? +
Goodman shares similar engineering and efficiency with Carrier's lower-tier lines but typically costs 15-25% less. Bryant occupies mid-range pricing. This Goodman delivers competitive SEER1 and AFUE ratings with comparable warranty coverage, making it ideal for budget-conscious buyers prioritizing performance over brand prestige.
What registration do I need for the 10-year warranty? +
Online product registration through Goodman's website within 60 days of installation activates the 10-year parts coverage. Provide your AHRI certificate number 7368836, purchase date, and installation details. Lifetime heat exchanger coverage applies automatically regardless of registration status.
