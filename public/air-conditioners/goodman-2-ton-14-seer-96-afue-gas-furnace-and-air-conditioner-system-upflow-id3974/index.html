Review

The ACiQ 2-ton upflow gas furnace and air conditioner system occupies an important middle ground in the residential HVAC market. This unit combines Goodman's established manufacturing reliability with modern efficiency standards at a competitive price point. The 40,000 BTU heating capacity and 24,000 BTU cooling capacity make it suitable for homes between 1,200 and 1,600 square feet. The variable-speed ECM blower motor and two-stage gas burner reflect current best practices in comfort-focused design.

Performance metrics are straightforward and competitive. The 96% AFUE rating means only 4% of heating energy is lost through the flue, a substantial improvement over 80% baseline furnaces. Cooling delivers 14 SEER1 efficiency with R454B refrigerant, current industry standard. The rotary compressor handles single-stage cooling efficiently for moderate climates. Maximum airflow of 1,200 CFM and 71 dB noise level keep operation reasonably quiet during heating cycles. The plastic PVC exhaust flue requirement simplifies venting through exterior walls.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much will this system cost to operate monthly? +
At 96% AFUE, a 40,000 BTU furnace running 8 hours daily uses roughly 333,000 BTU of gas. In areas with $12 per therm rates, expect approximately $40-50 monthly heating costs. Cooling at 14 SEER1 adds variable expense depending on climate zone and thermostat settings.
Will this fit in my attic space? +
Yes, likely. At 26 inches tall and 17.5 inches wide, it fits most standard attic cavities and closets. Measure your available space and confirm 21 inches of depth clearance. The lightweight 120-pound unit simplifies handling during installation compared to heavier units.
How does 14 SEER1 compare to newer systems? +
14 SEER1 is solid mid-tier cooling performance, suitable for moderate climates where cooling load is moderate. In very hot regions like Arizona or Texas, 16+ SEER2 units justify the premium through lower summer electric bills. For northern climates where cooling matters less, this tier makes financial sense.
Should I choose this over a 13 SEER unit? +
Yes, the efficiency jump from 13 to 14 SEER1 typically recovers the price difference within 3-4 years through lower electric bills. The variable-speed blower adds comfort and efficiency gains beyond raw cooling ratings. The 96% AFUE also outperforms standard furnaces significantly on heating costs.
Is online warranty registration required? +
Yes, the full 10-year coverage requires online registration. Without it, coverage is limited. Register immediately after installation. Keep documentation for claims, though modern systems rarely need warranty service during this timeframe.
