Review

This 5 ton Goodman system represents the practical middle ground in residential HVAC. The 97% AFUE furnace with 100,000 BTU heating capacity and 14 SEER air conditioner delivering 60,000 BTU cooling address both winter and summer comfort. Energy Star certification signals real efficiency gains over baseline equipment. The upflow configuration and compact 30x21x21 inch footprint make it a straightforward replacement for most residential installations without major structural changes.

The furnace's modulating burner stages adjust heat output continuously rather than simple on-off cycling, reducing temperature swings and improving comfort. The variable speed ECM blower motor running up to 2000 CFM optimizes air distribution at multiple comfort levels. However, the air conditioner uses a single stage scroll compressor that cycles rather than modulates, meaning cooling efficiency drops when outdoor temperatures spike. The R454B refrigerant meets current EPA standards and offers environmental benefits over older R410A equipment.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 97% AFUE furnace efficiency significantly reduces heating costs compared to 80-90% units, saving contractors' customers money immediately
  • Modulating burner stages provide consistent comfort by adjusting heat output rather than cycling on and off
  • Variable speed ECM blower motor adapts to demand, improving air distribution and reducing energy waste
  • 14 SEER cooling efficiency earns Energy Star certification, qualifying many installations for utility rebates
  • R454B refrigerant is EPA-approved low-GWP option with forward compatibility
  • Compact 30x21x21 inch dimensions fit standard return plenums without major ductwork modifications
  • PVC/plastic flue compatibility simplifies venting in many residential installations

Cons

  • 14 SEER is mid-tier efficiency, not high-efficiency, so peak cooling performance lags 16+ SEER units in extreme heat

  • Limited to 50 amp maximum breaker size, restricting future upgrades or paired equipment options
  • No two-stage or variable compressor option for those prioritizing quieter operation or superior part-load efficiency

Who Should Buy

Installation

Upflow installation suits basements and utility closets where return air flows upward through the unit. Plastic PVC flue venting simplifies roof or wall penetrations compared to metal ductwork. The 115V single phase 60Hz electrical requirement needs 32.8 to 50 amp dedicated breaker service. Standard 3/8 inch and 7/8 inch line sets with 3/8 inch suction insulation connect to most existing indoor coils without modification. Plan 2 to 4 hours for qualified technician installation.

Warranty & Reliability

ACiQ backs this system with 10 years of parts coverage, extending to the Goodman evaporator coil. Online registration activates full protection, so verify completion with the installer. Compared to 5 year baseline coverage on budget models, the decade coverage reflects confidence in component longevity and provides meaningful peace of mind for homeowners.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much will this system reduce my heating bills compared to an older furnace? +
A 97% AFUE furnace converts nearly all fuel to usable heat versus 80% AFUE units from the 1990s-2000s. Homeowners typically save 15-20% annually on heating costs. Exact savings depend on your climate, natural gas or propane prices, and how much you heat. Areas with long, cold winters see the largest dollar savings.
Can this unit replace my current system without ductwork changes? +
Most likely yes. The upflow 30x21x21 inch dimensions fit standard return plenums in basements and closets. Verify your existing ductwork accommodates 2000 CFM maximum airflow and that line set runs (typically 25-50 feet) reach your outdoor condenser location. Your contractor inspects during the estimate.
Why does the furnace modulate but the air conditioner doesn't? +
The modulating burner continuously adjusts heating output for consistent comfort and efficiency. The single stage compressor cycles completely on or off for cooling, which is standard for 14 SEER units. Variable capacity compressors cost more and appear in 16+ SEER systems. This setup still delivers solid cooling for mixed climates.
Is 14 SEER enough for hot summers, or should I upgrade? +
14 SEER performs well in moderate climates and mixed regions. If outdoor temps regularly exceed 95 degrees Fahrenheit for weeks, consider 16+ SEER units for superior part-load efficiency and reduced cycling. Southern or desert climates benefit more from higher SEER than northern areas.
What does the 10 year warranty cover if something fails? +
Parts replacement for manufacturing defects, including the compressor, motors, and Goodman evaporator coil. Labor is not included, so you pay a technician service call. Online registration is mandatory to activate coverage, so confirm with your installer before they leave.
