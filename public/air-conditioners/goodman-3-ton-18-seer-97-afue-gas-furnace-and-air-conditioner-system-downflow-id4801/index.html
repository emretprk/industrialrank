This Goodman system excels for homeowners upgrading to high-efficiency heating and cooling in moderate climates. The 97% AFUE furnace and 18 SEER air conditioner deliver excellent energy savings, especially in regions with balanced heating and cooling seasons.

Review

The Goodman 3 Ton downflow system represents a solid mid-range option for homeowners seeking efficient heating and cooling in a single package. With a 97% AFUE furnace, 18 SEER air conditioner, and Energy Star certification, this unit positions itself as a modern efficiency upgrade from older R-22 systems. The downflow configuration suits homes where ductwork runs beneath the unit, such as split-level or basement installations. At the current pricing, it competes favorably against comparable two-stage systems from Lennox and Carrier.

Performance specifications tell a compelling story. The furnace delivers 100,000 BTU of heating output with modulating burners that adjust flame intensity automatically, maintaining efficiency across varying outdoor temperatures. The air conditioner produces 35,000 BTU of cooling capacity with two-stage compressor operation. The variable-speed ECM blower motor runs at 2000 CFM maximum while operating at just 71 dBA, keeping noise to acceptable levels. Electrical requirements are straightforward, requiring 115V single-phase 60Hz power with a 21.3 to 35-amp breaker range.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 97% AFUE furnace rating means nearly all fuel converts to usable heat, reducing winter energy bills significantly
  • 18 SEER cooling efficiency qualifies for Energy Star and many rebate programs, lowering upfront cost
  • Modulating furnace burner adjusts output automatically, maintaining comfort while avoiding energy waste
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor runs quietly at 71 dBA and adapts to actual heating/cooling demand
  • Two-stage scroll compressor provides consistent cooling without the abrupt cycling of single-stage units
  • Plastic PVC exhaust flue simplifies venting and reduces installation complexity compared to metal ducts

Cons

  • 5-ton cooling capacity mismatches the advertised 3-ton rating, creating confusion about actual system size and compatibility
  • Refrigerant flexibility (R410A or R22) may complicate future service since R22 is being phased out
  • 71 dBA noise level is moderately loud for some homeowners, though acceptable for most installations

Who Should Buy

Best For

Homeowners replacing aging furnace and air conditioner systems in moderate climates who prioritize energy efficiency and qualifying for utility rebates. This setup works well for homes with balanced heating and cooling loads across spring, summer, and fall seasons.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does this 3-ton system list 5-ton tonnage in the specs? +
This appears to be a specification error from the manufacturer or retailer. The title claims 3-ton capacity while specs list 5-ton. The 35,000 BTU cooling output aligns with 3-ton capacity (12,000 BTU per ton). Contact the seller before purchasing to confirm actual cooling tonnage and system pairing requirements.
What ductwork changes are needed for downflow installation? +
Downflow units require return air drawn from above the furnace and supply ducts exiting below. Your existing ductwork may need reconfiguration if your home was designed for upflow orientation. A qualified HVAC technician must assess your specific installation to determine modification scope.
How much can I save on heating costs with 97% AFUE? +
A 97% AFUE furnace wastes only 3% of fuel as exhaust heat versus 15-20% for older systems. Actual savings depend on your climate, gas rates, and previous equipment efficiency. In cold climates, homeowners typically recover 20-30% of heating costs over ten years.
Is 18 SEER2 equivalent to this unit's 18 SEER rating? +
No. SEER1 (this unit's rating) tests at 82 degrees outdoor temperature, while newer SEER2 standards test at 95 degrees, yielding lower numbers. This 18 SEER unit would likely rate around 14-15 SEER2. Compare apples-to-apples using the same rating standard.
Does online registration really matter for warranty coverage? +
Yes. Goodman requires online registration within a specific timeframe to activate the full 10-year warranty. Register immediately after installation. Without registration, coverage may be limited or denied.
