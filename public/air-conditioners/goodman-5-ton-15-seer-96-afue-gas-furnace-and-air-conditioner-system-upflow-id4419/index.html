Review

The Goodman CAPF4961D6 represents the practical middle ground in residential HVAC systems. This 5-ton upflow unit combines a gas furnace with an air conditioning condenser, designed for homes that need both heating and cooling under one system. Goodman has built its reputation on delivering dependable equipment at competitive prices, and this 96% AFUE furnace reflects that positioning. It's not flashy, but it's engineered for contractors who value straightforward installation and homeowners who want measurable utility savings.

The furnace delivers 120,000 BTU of heating capacity with 96% Annual Fuel Utilization Efficiency, meaning 96 cents of every dollar spent on gas becomes usable heat. The air conditioning side produces 60,000 BTU of cooling at 15 SEER, adequate for moderate climates but not cutting-edge. The variable-speed ECM blower motor, capable of 2000 CFM maximum, adjusts its speed based on heating or cooling demand. Two-stage heating and cooling allow the system to run at partial capacity most of the time, reducing energy waste and noise compared to older single-stage units.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 96% AFUE furnace converts nearly all fuel to usable heat, reducing winter gas bills significantly
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor adjusts airflow from 0 to 2000 CFM, improving comfort and efficiency
  • Two-stage burner and two-stage condenser allow modulated operation for quieter, more consistent temperatures
  • PVC exhaust flue compatible with modern venting standards, simplifying installation in many homes
  • 10-year parts warranty with online registration provides solid protection against early failure

Cons

  • 15 SEER cooling rating is competent but not exceptional compared to newer 18+ SEER models entering the market
  • 75 dB noise level at full capacity is moderately loud, noticeable in open floor plans
  • Requires 37.2 to 60 amp breaker sizing, demanding proper electrical infrastructure in older homes

Who Should Buy

Best For

Homeowners replacing aging furnaces in homes built in the 1990s through 2010s, particularly those in climates with cold winters and moderate cooling seasons. Contractors installing complete systems in new construction or major renovations where upflow configuration fits existing ductwork.

Consider Alternatives If

Anyone prioritizing cutting-edge cooling efficiency or living in hot climates should explore 16+ SEER units, and homes with insufficient electrical service may require costly upgrades.

Installation

This upflow unit requires 30 inches of height, 24.5 inches of width, and 21 inches of depth, fitting standard utility closets or basement alcoves. The PVC exhaust flue requirement simplifies venting compared to older metal duct systems. Electrical connection demands 208/230V single-phase 60Hz service with 37.2 to 60 amp breaker capacity. Most homes with modern electrical panels handle this, though older homes may need panel upgrades.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides a 10-year parts warranty when you register the unit online, covering compressor, heat exchanger, and control components. This warranty is solid for the price point and typical of mid-tier manufacturers. Labor and refrigerant are not included, so factor in service costs if repairs occur after the first year.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does 96% AFUE actually mean for my heating bills? +
AFUE measures how much fuel converts to heat. At 96%, upgrading from an 80% AFUE furnace saves roughly 16% on heating costs annually. Real savings vary by climate and usage, but a home spending $1,500 yearly on heating could save $240. That's meaningful money over a 15-year furnace lifespan.
Can this system work with my existing ductwork? +
The upflow configuration suits homes with basements or crawl spaces and upward ductwork. Maximum airflow of 2000 CFM works well with properly sized ducts. Have an HVAC tech measure your existing ductwork before purchasing to confirm compatibility and ensure proper system performance.
How does 15 SEER cooling compare to other options? +
15 SEER is solid but not exceptional today. Modern systems range from 14 to 20+ SEER. This unit handles moderate cooling needs efficiently, but homes in hot climates or seeking maximum cooling efficiency should explore 16+ SEER alternatives that may cost more upfront but save significantly over time.
Is Goodman a reliable brand compared to competitors? +
Goodman ranks among the most common residential systems installed by contractors, indicating decent reliability. They're owned by Daikin, a global HVAC leader, ensuring parts availability and service support. Goodman trades premium features for price, making them popular for budget-conscious replacements rather than luxury upgrades.
What's included in the 10-year warranty? +
The warranty covers parts like the compressor and heat exchanger when registered online. Labor, refrigerant, and shipping are excluded. This means major failures are protected, but you'll pay for technician time and materials beyond the covered components.
