Review

Performance data shows the system delivers 18,000 BTU cooling output with 16 SEER efficiency, while the furnace produces 60,000 BTU heating at 80% AFUE. Frequently Asked Questions

Is 16 SEER efficient enough for energy savings? +
Yes. 16 SEER meets Energy Star standards and reduces cooling costs versus older units rated 10-13 SEER. You'll see modest savings on summer bills, though premium models at 18-20 SEER provide faster payback in hot climates. For moderate cooling zones, 16 SEER balances efficiency and cost effectively.
Will this system work in my attic or basement upflow configuration? +
Yes. The unit handles both upflow and downflow mounting. However, you'll need adequate headroom for the 26-inch height and clearance for airflow. Verify your space accommodates the 17.5-inch width and 21-inch depth before ordering. Ensure metal exhaust flue can be properly vented.
How does 80% AFUE compare to higher-efficiency furnaces? +
80% AFUE means 20% of heating energy escapes through the flue, standard for mid-range gas furnaces. A 95% AFUE unit saves roughly 15-20% on heating bills but costs significantly more upfront. In moderate climates, the payback period extends beyond 10 years, making 80% practical for budget-conscious buyers.
Can I upgrade this system later to higher SEER? +
Partially. The condenser unit determines cooling capacity and efficiency, so you'd eventually replace it for major upgrades. The furnace is separate and can operate independently if you upgrade only the AC side. Plan for full system replacement rather than piecemeal efficiency improvements.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Full parts and compressor coverage, including labor, when registered online. This protects against defects under normal operation. Excludes misuse, improper installation, or neglected maintenance. Registration is quick and essential to activate coverage.
