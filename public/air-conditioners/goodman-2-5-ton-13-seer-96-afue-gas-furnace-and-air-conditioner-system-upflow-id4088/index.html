Review

The ACiQ ACIQ-30-EHPD-UPG is a no-frills 2.5-ton combo system targeting price-sensitive buyers who won't compromise on core efficiency. As a Goodman-branded unit, it carries the brand's reputation for reliability while undercut typical nameplate pricing. The upflow configuration handles most residential applications, though the plastic PVC flue requirement actually simplifies installation in many homes, cutting labor costs compared to masonry-vented alternatives.

This system pairs an 80,000 BTU furnace rated at 96% AFUE with a 30,000 BTU air conditioner delivering 13 SEER cooling. The 96% AFUE rating positions it in the high-efficiency furnace category, though the single-stage cooling condenser prevents true matched-pair optimization. Maximum CFM of 1,600 with the variable-speed ECM blower provides flexibility for various duct configurations. The reciprocating compressor and 3/4-inch suction line represent industry standards at this tonnage class.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 96% AFUE furnace means 4% more heating efficiency than 92% units, translating to real fuel savings
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor adjusts airflow precisely, improving comfort and reducing energy waste
  • PVC-compatible flue reduces installation costs compared to masonry-only alternatives
  • Two-stage burner provides modulation for better temperature control and efficiency
  • R454B refrigerant is A2L rated, allowing future compliance without system replacement

Cons

  • 13 SEER is middle-tier efficiency, not competitive with 16+ SEER premium units in hot climates
  • Single-stage condenser cannot modulate cooling output, reducing efficiency on mild days

Installation

Installation requires 208-230V single-phase power and a 16.7 to 25-amp breaker. The unit's compact 30-inch height and 21x21-inch footprint fit standard utility closets. PVC flue compatibility simplifies venting without requiring costly chimney repairs. Pre-charged with R454B means minimal field service, though technicians should verify proper linesets, condensate drainage, and thermostat configuration during startup.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year parts warranty applies only with online registration, requiring homeowners to complete registration within 60 days of installation. This is standard industry practice but creates minor friction for warranty claims. Coverage addresses manufacturer defects but excludes wear items, refrigerant loss from leaks, and labor. Registration is straightforward through the Goodman/ACiQ portal.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the actual tonnage of this unit, since the specs list both 2.5 and 3.5 tons? +
The cooling capacity of 30,000 BTU confirms 2.5-ton cooling output. The 3.5-ton specification appears to be a data entry error in the spec sheet. Always verify with the AHRI certificate number 7368835 before installation to confirm proper system sizing for your home.
Can this system handle PVC venting through an outside wall? +
Yes, the plastic/PVC flue requirement explicitly supports this configuration. This is actually an advantage over older masonry-only units, reducing installation costs. Ensure proper slope, condensate drainage, and termination clearance per local code and the CAPT Installation Manual.
How does 13 SEER compare to newer efficiency standards? +
13 SEER meets federal minimums but trails 15-16 SEER premium units by 15-23% cooling efficiency. For moderate climates or moderate cooling seasons, the difference may not justify the $1,500-2,000 premium. Calculate your payback period based on local electricity rates and cooling hours.
Should I choose this over a higher-SEER Lennox or Carrier unit? +
It depends on climate and budget. This system outperforms equivalent Goodman units on heating efficiency (96% AFUE is competitive), but premium brands offer better cooling modulation at higher price points. Get quotes from three contractors before deciding, as installation quality matters more than nameplate brand.
Does registration really matter for the warranty? +
Yes, registration is mandatory within 60 days. Without it, you forfeit the 10-year parts coverage. Register immediately after installation and keep confirmation records. This protects both you and the installer.
