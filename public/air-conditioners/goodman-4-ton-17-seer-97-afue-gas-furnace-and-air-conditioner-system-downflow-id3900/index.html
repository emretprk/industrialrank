Review

Goodman's GCVM970804CN pairs a gas furnace and air conditioning condenser into one downflow system designed for simplicity and balanced performance. This combo appeals to contractors and homeowners who want heating and cooling efficiency without the complexity of premium-tier equipment. The unit targets the middle market, offering solid specs at competitive pricing for regional installers and DIY-minded property owners who hire licensed technicians for setup.

The furnace delivers 80,000 BTU of heating with 97% AFUE efficiency and modulating burner stages, meaning it adjusts flame intensity to match demand rather than cycling on and off. The air conditioner side produces 47,000 BTU cooling with an 18 SEER2 rating and two-stage scroll compressor, earning Energy Star certification. A variable-speed ECM blower handles both functions, running at up to 1600 CFM and keeping noise levels at 72 dB, which is acceptable for residential installations.

Installation

This unit requires a licensed technician because improper gas line connections or venting can cause carbon monoxide hazards or equipment failure. The downflow/horizontal A-coil design needs 34.5 inches of height and fits many existing closet or basement installations. Electrical demand ranges from 29.3 to 50 amps at 115V single-phase 60 Hz. Sweat valve connections and plastic/PVC flue venting are standard, but local codes vary.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year parts and labor warranty activates with online registration, offering genuine peace of mind for most residential failures. This timeframe covers compressor, heat exchanger, and major components, protecting against premature burnout. However, warranty claims require proof of professional installation, so DIY setup voids coverage entirely.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the actual tonnage of this system, 4 or 5 tons? +
The specifications list both 4 ton and 5 ton, creating ambiguity. Contact Goodman or the seller to clarify before purchase. The 47,000 BTU cooling capacity suggests closer to 4 ton, but verify with AHRI certificate 7424271 to confirm actual capacity for your home's square footage.
Can I install this myself if I'm handy? +
No. Federal law requires EPA-certified technicians to handle refrigerant and gas connections. Many jurisdictions require installation licenses. Improper setup risks carbon monoxide poisoning, equipment damage, and voided warranty. Always hire a licensed HVAC contractor.
How much will this lower my energy bills compared to my old furnace? +
A 97% AFUE furnace cuts gas consumption roughly 30% versus units from the 1990s, which averaged 65% to 78% AFUE. The 18 SEER2 air conditioner cuts cooling costs similarly. Exact savings depend on climate, usage patterns, and your current equipment's condition.
Is this system compatible with smart thermostats? +
The specs don't explicitly address smart thermostat compatibility, but most modern Goodman units work with standard thermostats. Verify compatibility with your thermostat model before buying. The ECM blower improves efficiency when paired with demand-controlled thermostats.
What happens if something breaks after year five? +
After 10 years with registration, Goodman covers parts and labor on failures. Before that, coverage applies to manufacturing defects. Normal wear items like blower motors may require owner cost-sharing depending on failure cause and regional labor rates.
