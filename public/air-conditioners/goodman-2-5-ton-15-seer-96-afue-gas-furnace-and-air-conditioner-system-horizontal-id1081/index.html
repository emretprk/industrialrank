Review

The Goodman 2.5-ton horizontal system bridges affordability and performance in the mid-range HVAC market. Designed for tight installations, this unit measures just 21.125 inches tall while delivering 40,000 BTU heating and 29,000 BTU cooling. It's positioned as a practical choice for contractors handling retrofit jobs or new builds where space constraints matter, combining established Goodman reliability with modern efficiency standards that attract both cost-conscious homeowners and builders.

Performance data shows genuine efficiency gains across both heating and cooling. The 96% AFUE rating means nearly all fuel converts to usable heat, while the SEER1 16 rating exceeds typical 2.5-ton expectations. The variable-speed ECM blower adjusts output to match demand, reducing runtime waste and noise. Two-stage burner modulation prevents short-cycling in mild weather. Scroll compressor technology and PVC flue compatibility simplify installation in modern homes. Sound levels at 71.5 dB are competitive for this tonnage class.

Installation

Horizontal configuration requires proper condensate drainage planning, typically sloping toward a floor drain or pump. Supply lines use standard 3/8 inch liquid and 7/8 inch suction connections with 3/4 inch suction insulation for first 50 feet included. PVC plastic flue venting simplifies ductwork versus metal alternatives. Electrical needs 115V single-phase at 17.1 to 25 amps, requiring dedicated breaker. Most experienced HVAC techs complete installation in 6-8 hours.

Warranty & Reliability

Ten-year parts coverage applies with online registration at no additional cost. This standard protection covers compressor, coil, and blower components against manufacturing defects. Note that labor and refrigerant are typically not included in Goodman's base warranty. Registration is straightforward and extends coverage period from installation date. For commercial or rental properties, verify warranty terms as some restrictions may apply.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet list 3 tons but the title says 2.5 tons? +
The tonnage mismatch appears to be a data discrepancy in the product listing. AHRI certificate 7368841 should clarify actual rated capacity. Contact the seller to confirm whether this is 2.5-ton or 3-ton equipment, as this affects pricing, efficiency metrics, and suitability for your home.
Can I install this in an attic with low headroom? +
Yes. At 21.125 inches tall, this horizontal unit fits most attic spaces with 24 inches or more clearance. Ensure adequate return airflow around the unit and verify your electrical panel supports the 17.1 to 25-amp breaker requirement before installation.
How does 96% AFUE compare to newer furnaces? +
96% AFUE is excellent and near the maximum achievable without condensing technology. Most new furnaces max out around 95-98% AFUE. This unit delivers nearly one dollar of heat per dollar of fuel, making it among the most efficient gas furnaces available at standard pricing.
Should I choose this over a 16 SEER2 heat pump? +
Heat pumps excel in moderate climates but struggle below 30-35 degrees. In cold regions, this furnace's 96% AFUE and dual-fuel capability may provide better comfort and lower winter costs. Compare year-round energy bills for your climate zone rather than cooling efficiency alone.
Is the 10-year warranty transferable if I sell my home? +
Goodman typically offers limited transferability on extended warranties, though coverage does pass to new owners in most cases. Register the unit immediately upon installation and retain documentation to ensure valid claims if ownership changes.
