Review

The Goodman 3 Ton dual-fuel system positions itself as the smart middle ground in HVAC equipment, offering legitimate efficiency without flagship pricing. At $3,309, this combination furnace and air conditioner targets contractors and homeowners tired of choosing between performance and budget constraints. The upflow configuration and A-coil design work well in attic or basement installations common across North America, making this a practical replacement for dated equipment.

Performance numbers tell a strong story here. The furnace delivers 80,000 BTU output with 96% AFUE efficiency, meaning minimal heating energy escapes through exhaust. The air conditioning side produces 34,800 BTU cooling with a 16 SEER rating, surpassing the advertised 14 SEER specification. The variable-speed ECM blower reaches 1200 CFM maximum, allowing flexible airflow that adapts to seasonal demands and room layouts rather than cycling at fixed speeds.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 96% AFUE furnace with two-stage burners minimizes fuel waste and reduces heating costs significantly
  • 16 SEER rating exceeds the stated 14 SEER, offering better cooling efficiency than advertised

  • Energy Star certified with rebate availability, lowering net installation cost for qualified buyers

Who Should Buy

Installation

Installation requires standard 115V single-phase 60Hz electrical service with a 30-amp maximum breaker sized for 18.7 amps minimum draw. The unit's compact footprint (34.5 inches tall, 17.5 inches wide, 28.75 inches deep) fits most standard return plenums. PVC exhaust venting is mandatory, requiring proper slope and condensate drainage for safe operation and equipment longevity.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman's 10-year parts and labor warranty activates upon online registration, giving you solid coverage without purchasing extended plans. This standard warranty ranks competitively for this price tier and reflects Goodman's manufacturing confidence. Registration is essential, as unregistered units carry shorter coverage periods.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this unit really deliver 16 SEER when the product name says 14 SEER? +
Yes, the specs confirm 16 SEER rating, indicating Goodman's published ratings are conservative. This gap isn't unusual and works in your favor, delivering better real-world cooling efficiency than the marketing suggests. Always verify AHRI certificates (7368834 for this model) for official performance data.
Can I use this system with propane instead of natural gas? +
The specifications confirm dual-fuel capability with both propane and natural gas options. You'll need a licensed technician to configure and test the appropriate burner settings during installation. Propane conversions require proper orifice sizing and pressure regulation specific to your fuel type.
Is the variable-speed blower worth the extra cost over standard models? +
The ECM blower justifies its cost by reducing energy consumption, improving humidity control, and lowering operational noise compared to single-speed motors. At 1200 CFM maximum, it provides flexibility for larger ducts or multi-zone homes while running more efficiently during partial-load conditions.
How does this compare to other 3-ton units in the $3,000 to $4,000 range? +
This Goodman unit offers strong efficiency (96% AFUE, 16 SEER) at fair pricing. Comparable units from Carrier or Lennox may offer quieter operation or advanced zoning but typically cost 20 to 30 percent more. Budget units sacrifice efficiency or warranty length to undercut this price.
What happens if I don't register the warranty? +
Unregistered units carry limited warranty coverage, typically 5 years instead of 10. Registration is straightforward online and protects your investment. Keep documentation and proof of professional installation for any claims.
