Review

This Goodman 3-ton gas furnace and air conditioner system represents a solid mid-range choice for residential HVAC replacement. The horizontal configuration targets attic and utility space installations where vertical clearance is limited. Goodman's brand reputation for reliability combines here with modulating heating technology and a variable-speed blower motor, positioning this unit as a practical upgrade path for contractors managing budget-conscious retrofit projects.

Heating delivers 60,000 BTU via modulating burners with 97% AFUE efficiency, meaning less wasted fuel and lower winter operating costs. The cooling side produces 23,600 BTU with a 16 SEER2 rating using R410A refrigerant, qualifying for Energy Star certification. The variable-speed ECM blower adapts output up to 1,200 CFM, reducing standby power consumption and improving part-load comfort compared to fixed-speed alternatives.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does 16 SEER2 cooling efficiency actually mean for my electric bills? +
SEER2 rates real-world cooling performance at part-load conditions. A 16 SEER2 unit delivers approximately 16 BTU of cooling per watt-hour consumed. Versus an older 10 SEER system, you'll see roughly 35 to 40 percent lower cooling costs over a season, assuming similar usage patterns and thermostat settings.
Can this horizontal unit work in my attic with limited headroom? +
Yes, if you have minimum 34.5 inches vertical clearance plus service access space. The 17.5-inch width and 28.75-inch depth fit standard attic layouts. Ensure your return and supply plenums clear the unit and that your ductwork supports 1,200 CFM maximum blower output without excessive restriction.
How does the modulating burner improve comfort compared to single-stage units? +
Modulating burners fire at multiple output levels rather than on-off cycling. At 60,000 BTU capacity, this unit ramps heat delivery to match demand, reducing temperature swings and eliminating the comfort dip when burners kick on. Result is steadier indoor temperatures and fewer audible cycling events.
Is this system compatible with my existing R22 air conditioning lines and charge? +
The spec sheet lists R410A or R22 refrigerant compatibility, but mixing is not recommended. If upgrading from R22 equipment, choose R410A and replace all oil, lines, and desiccant to avoid cross-contamination. Consult an HVAC technician to confirm your existing lineset meets pressure ratings for R410A operation.
What happens if I don't register for the warranty online? +
Without registration, you forfeit the 10-year warranty coverage. Goodman may honor claims with proof of purchase, but registration guarantees protection. Complete registration within 30 days of installation activation to lock in full coverage and maintain resale value documentation.
