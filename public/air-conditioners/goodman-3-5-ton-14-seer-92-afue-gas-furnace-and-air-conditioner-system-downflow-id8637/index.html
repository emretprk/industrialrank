// Bottom Line
This Goodman combo system works well for homeowners seeking balanced efficiency and affordability in climates requiring both serious heating and cooling. The 92% AFUE furnace and 16 SEER2 AC unit make it suitable for mixed-use homes where neither heating nor cooling dominates seasonal costs.

Review

The Goodman 3.5-ton combo system pairs a 100,000 BTU gas furnace with a 42,000 BTU air conditioning unit, making it a straightforward replacement for homes needing both seasonal comfort. This downflow configuration suits basements or crawl space installations. The unit ships as a matched pair from Online Supply with rebate availability, targeting budget-conscious homeowners and contractors who value proven Goodman reliability without the premium pricing of top-tier manufacturers.

Efficiency numbers here are genuinely competitive. The furnace delivers 92% AFUE, a meaningful jump from legacy 80% units, while the AC section achieves 16 SEER2 and ENERGY STAR qualification. However, the single-stage scroll compressor runs at full capacity rather than modulating down, which limits efficiency during mild-weather operation. The multi-speed 2000 CFM blower provides better control than fixed-speed designs. At 73 dBA, noise is moderate but audible during operation.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 92% AFUE furnace efficiency reduces winter heating costs by roughly 8% versus older 80% units
  • 16 SEER2 rating on the AC side qualifies for ENERGY STAR certification and many utility rebates
  • Scroll compressor on the condenser is known for durability and quiet operation at 73 dBA
  • Multi-speed blower motor allows flexible CFM output up to 2000 CFM for zoning capability
  • 10-year warranty with online registration provides meaningful protection for furnace and coil

Cons

  • Single-stage compressor means the AC runs at full capacity constantly instead of modulating, reducing part-load efficiency
  • 3.5-ton cooling capacity stated in title conflicts with 5-ton tonnage in specs, creating purchasing confusion
  • 73 dBA noise level is moderately loud for single-stage operation, noticeable in quiet neighborhoods

Who Should Buy

Best For

Homeowners in temperate to cold climates who need balanced heating and cooling without premium modulation features. This system is ideal for those transitioning from 80% AFUE furnaces and wanting real efficiency gains at a reasonable price point.

Consider Alternatives If

Buyers in hot climates where cooling dominates should look for two-stage or variable-speed compressors. Those prioritizing whisper-quiet operation should also consider higher-end alternatives.

Installation

This downflow unit requires right-side flue venting through PVC or plastic ducting, simplifying installation versus masonry chimneys. Electrical requirements are straightforward, 115V single-phase with a 22.4 to 35-amp breaker range. Refrigerant lines come 50 feet insulated. The A-coil design fits tight spaces, though technicians must coordinate suction and liquid valve sizing (7/8-inch and 3/8-inch respectively) with existing copper runs.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman's 10-year parts and labor warranty applies to both furnace and evaporator coil when registered online. This is competitive for mid-tier equipment and protects against compressor failure and heat exchanger issues. Warranty covers manufacturing defects but not installation errors, maintenance neglect, or refrigerant loss from leaks.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between the 3.5-ton title and 5-ton specs listed? +
This appears to be a spec sheet error from the manufacturer or retailer. The product name says 3.5 tons, but internal specs show 5 tons. Verify cooling capacity (42,000 BTU equals roughly 3.5 tons) with your dealer before purchase to ensure it matches your home's load calculation.
Can I pair this furnace with a different brand's AC condenser? +
Technically possible but not recommended. This Goodman furnace is optimized for the paired coil design and refrigerant charge. Mixing brands risks warranty voidance, improper charge, and system instability. Stick with matched pairs for reliability.
How does 92% AFUE compare to newer high-efficiency options? +
92% AFUE is solid but not cutting-edge. Premium models reach 95-98% AFUE with modulating burners and condensing technology. However, 92% represents a strong 12-percentage-point gain over 80% units, translating to roughly 15% lower heating bills for most homes.
Is the single-stage compressor a dealbreaker for efficiency? +
Not necessarily. Single-stage units run full blast, wasting some energy in mild weather. Two-stage systems modulate and save 5-10% in variable climates. For consistent, cold winters with short shoulder seasons, single-stage is acceptable and more affordable.
Does the 10-year warranty cover labor and parts equally? +
Yes, Goodman's registered warranty covers both parts and labor for 10 years on compressor, heat exchanger, and coil. Always register online immediately after installation to activate coverage and maintain proof of purchase.
