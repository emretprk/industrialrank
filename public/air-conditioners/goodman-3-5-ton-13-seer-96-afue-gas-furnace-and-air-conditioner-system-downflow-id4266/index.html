This ACiQ system excels for homeowners in mixed climates who want dependable heating and cooling with strong efficiency ratings. The 96% AFUE furnace and 13 SEER air conditioner deliver measurable energy savings, making it ideal for properties where both heating and cooling demand is substantial.

Review

The ACiQ 3.5 ton combination system pairs a Goodman gas furnace with an air conditioning condenser in one compact package. This downflow-configured unit targets contractors and homeowners seeking an integrated solution that handles both seasonal heating and cooling without installing separate pieces. The 80,000 BTU furnace capacity and 42,000 BTU cooling output serve mid-sized homes, while the dual-fuel design supports natural gas or propane depending on your utility infrastructure.

Efficiency is this system's strongest selling point. The furnace achieves 96% AFUE, meaning nearly all input fuel converts to usable heat rather than escaping through the chimney. The air conditioner delivers 13 SEER cooling efficiency, which qualifies for Energy Star designation and unlocks utility company rebates in most regions. The variable-speed ECM blower motor adjusts fan speed to match heating or cooling demand, reducing unnecessary energy consumption during partial-load operation.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 96% AFUE furnace rating is best-in-class for natural gas and propane heating efficiency
  • 13 SEER cooling efficiency qualifies for Energy Star certification and utility rebates
  • Two-stage burner and variable-speed ECM blower motor optimize comfort and reduce runtime costs
  • Compact footprint of 30 inches by 21 inches by 21 inches fits tight mechanical spaces
  • Plastic PVC exhaust flue reduces installation complexity and material costs versus metal venting

Cons

  • Single-stage condenser limits part-load efficiency during mild weather compared to two-stage units

Who Should Buy

Best For

This system suits homeowners in temperate to cold climates where heating demand justifies the high AFUE rating. It works well for retrofits into existing downflow or horizontal installations where duct configuration is already set.

Consider Alternatives If

Skip this unit if you live in hot climates where cooling dominates, as the single-stage compressor wastes energy in part-load conditions, or if noise concerns matter in your home.

Installation

Installation requires standard HVAC skills and tools. The reciprocating compressor and sweat-type refrigerant valves demand proper brazing during field assembly. Suction and liquid lines measure 7/8 inches and 3/8 inches respectively. The plastic PVC exhaust flue simplifies venting compared to high-temperature metal stacks. Electrical service needs 115V single-phase 60Hz power with 23.9 to 40 amp breaker capacity.

Warranty & Reliability

ACiQ covers this system with a 10-year parts warranty when you register the unit online within 60 days of purchase. This extended coverage reflects manufacturer confidence in component reliability. The Goodman evaporator coil carries separate warranty protection, ensuring your indoor cooling coil stays covered even if primary warranty lapses.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the specs list 5 ton tonnage when the product title says 3.5 ton? +
The furnace rated 80,000 BTU equals approximately 6.7 tons of heating capacity, while the condenser at 42,000 BTU equals 3.5 tons of cooling capacity. The 5 ton tonnage listed likely refers to the furnace alone. Always match the cooling and heating capacities to your home's actual load requirements.
Can this unit fit in my existing horizontal downflow cabinet? +
Yes. The unit measures 30 inches tall by 21 inches wide by 21 inches deep, making it compact for tight mechanical closets. Verify your space accommodates the 3/8-inch and 7/8-inch refrigerant line routing, and confirm the plastic PVC exhaust flue can route safely outside.
How much will this system cut my heating and cooling bills? +
The 96% AFUE furnace saves approximately 15 to 20 percent on heating costs versus older 80% AFUE equipment. The 13 SEER condenser reduces cooling costs by roughly 25 percent compared to 10 SEER units. Actual savings depend on your climate, local utility rates, and system runtime.
How does this compare to split systems with matching efficiency? +
Packaged units simplify installation and reduce field refrigerant connections, lowering leak risks. However, split systems allow you to upgrade furnace and air conditioner independently and often achieve higher SEER ratings through two-stage compressors. Choose packaged systems for retrofit simplicity.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Parts coverage includes compressor, condenser coil, furnace heat exchanger, blower motor, and electrical components. Labor and service calls are your responsibility. Online registration is mandatory within 60 days to activate the warranty period.
