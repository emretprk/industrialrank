Review

The Goodman 3.5 ton system sits squarely in the value segment of the HVAC market, offering contractors and homeowners a straightforward, no-frills approach to heating and cooling. With an 80,000 BTU furnace and 42,000 BTU cooling capacity, this unit handles moderate residential loads competently. The two-stage heating and single-stage cooling design prioritize reliability over sophistication, making it a practical choice for replacement jobs where budget constraints matter.

Performance metrics tell a practical story here. The 80% AFUE furnace rating beats decade-old equipment by meaningful margins, translating to real heating cost reductions. On the cooling side, the 13 SEER rating delivers standard mid-tier efficiency, though it trails premium 16+ SEER models. The high-efficiency multi-speed blower operates at 2,000 CFM maximum, adjusting to load requirements rather than running at constant speed. Operating voltage is 208/230V single-phase at 60 Hz, with electrical requirements between 23.9 and 40 amps depending on configuration.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does this unit show both 3.5 ton and 5 ton tonnage in the specs? +
The tonnage specification appears inconsistent in the provided data. The 42,000 BTU cooling capacity actually classifies this as a 3.5 ton unit (12,000 BTU per ton), not 5 ton. Verify tonnage with your supplier before ordering to ensure proper system matching with your ductwork and home load calculations.
Can this furnace work with my existing 20-amp circuit breaker? +
No. This system requires a minimum 23.9-amp breaker and maximum 40-amp breaker. Your 20-amp circuit is undersized and presents a safety hazard. You'll need to upgrade to at least 25-amp dedicated service, ideally 30-amp, during installation. Electrical upgrades are standard costs in HVAC replacement jobs.
How does 13 SEER compare to modern air conditioners? +
13 SEER meets baseline efficiency standards and beats 10-SEER units by 30 percent. However, current premium systems offer 16+ SEER ratings with variable-capacity compressors. For moderate climates with reasonable cooling loads, 13 SEER provides adequate efficiency without premium pricing. High-humidity regions benefit more from higher ratings.
Should I choose R410A or R22 refrigerant? +
Specify R410A refrigerant without question. R22 supplies are declining, replacement costs exceed R410A by 200-300 percent, and many technicians charge higher service rates for R22 systems. R410A is the modern standard and protects your long-term operating costs over the system's 15-year lifespan.
Does this system qualify for tax credits or rebates? +
No. At 80% AFUE and 13 SEER, this unit doesn't meet Energy Star certification or most federal rebate thresholds. Check local utility programs, as some regions offer modest rebates for replacing 15+ year old systems regardless of efficiency tier.
