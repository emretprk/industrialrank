// Bottom Line
This Goodman system excels for homeowners retrofitting crawlspaces or attics where a horizontal A-coil fits naturally. The 96% AFUE heating and 16 SEER cooling make it a solid match for moderate climates where both seasonal demands matter equally.

Review

The Goodman GMEC960402BN represents the manufacturer's middle-tier offering for homeowners seeking combined heating and cooling in horizontal configurations. Positioned between economy models and premium variable-capacity systems, this unit balances efficiency gains against moderate cost. Its 96% AFUE furnace and 16 SEER air conditioner appeal to contractors working in climate zones where both seasons demand serious equipment performance. The A-coil design and upflow/horizontal flexibility make it practical for retrofit applications where ductwork routing proves challenging.

Performance metrics tell a straightforward story. The furnace delivers 40,000 BTU heating at 96% AFUE, meaning roughly 96 cents of every heating dollar reaches conditioned space rather than exiting the flue. Cooling provides 24,000 BTU at 16 SEER through a two-stage scroll compressor, reducing compressor cycling and noise. The ECM blower motor reaches 800 CFM maximum and adjusts speeds automatically, avoiding the constant on-off behavior of single-stage systems. Operating at 71 dB places it in the normal range, though slightly louder than premium competition.

Installation

Professional installation is mandatory, as improper gas connections create serious safety risks including carbon monoxide hazards. The 115V 1-phase 60Hz electrical requirement fits standard residential circuits with 15.7 to 20 amp breakers. Refrigerant connections use 3/8 inch liquid and 3/4 inch suction valve sizes with 7/8 inch suction line. PVC venting requires proper flue routing and slope. Total unit weight of 112 pounds necessitates adequate support framing.

Warranty & Reliability

Coverage includes lifetime protection on the heat exchanger, 10-year unit replacement, and 10-year parts protection with online registration. The heat exchanger warranty is genuinely valuable since replacement costs exceed $1,500 on most furnaces. Ten-year terms cover most equipment failures within typical ownership periods. Registration requirement is straightforward but mandatory to activate coverage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the listing show both 2 ton and 3 ton ratings? +
This discrepancy appears unresolved in manufacturer documentation. The title states 2 ton, specs list 3 ton, yet cooling capacity measures only 24,000 BTU (true 2 ton equivalent). Request clarification from the supplier before purchase to confirm actual system capacity and ensure proper ductwork sizing.
Can this system work in a standard upflow attic installation? +
Yes, the horizontal A-coil configuration specifically accommodates attic placement. The 34.5 inch height and 17.5 inch width fit most attic trusses. Ensure adequate framing support for the 112-pound unit and confirm clearance for service access around all sides.
How much will this furnace reduce heating bills compared to older equipment? +
The 96% AFUE rating saves roughly 15-20% on heating costs versus typical 80% AFUE units from 15-20 years ago. Actual savings depend on local gas rates, climate, and house insulation, but substantial reductions appear within 5-7 years on most systems.
Is 16 SEER2 comparable to high-efficiency window units or ductless systems? +
16 SEER represents solid mid-tier ducted cooling performance. Ductless mini-splits often reach 20+ SEER but cost significantly more. Window units rarely exceed 12 SEER. For ducted systems, 16 SEER competes favorably against mainstream competition without premium pricing.
What does the lifetime heat exchanger warranty actually protect? +
It covers the furnace's primary heat transfer component against manufacturing defects and normal wear. This protection applies indefinitely to original owners, eliminating one major failure risk over the equipment lifespan.
