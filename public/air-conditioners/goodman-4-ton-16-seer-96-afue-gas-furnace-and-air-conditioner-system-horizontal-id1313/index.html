Review

Goodman's 4 ton horizontal system brings practical efficiency to retrofit and space-constrained installations. The combination of a 96% AFUE furnace and 16 SEER air conditioner positions this unit competitively for residential heating and cooling in moderate climates. At 117 dollars for the furnace component, it delivers solid performance without boutique pricing. Contractors appreciate the horizontal design for attic and crawlspace applications where traditional upflow cabinets create installation headaches.

Performance numbers tell a reliable story. The furnace delivers 120,000 BTU heating output with two-stage burners that modulate based on demand, reducing short-cycling and improving efficiency. The air conditioner side produces 45,500 BTU cooling using a scroll compressor and R410A refrigerant. The ECM blower motor handles airflow from 0 to 2000 CFM, automatically adjusting to match system demand. These specs deliver ENERGY STAR qualification and represent a solid efficiency upgrade from 10 to 15 year old systems.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does 16 SEER actually mean for my cooling bills? +
SEER (Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio) of 16 means this air conditioner uses roughly 20 to 25 percent less energy than a 12 SEER unit over a full cooling season. Actual savings depend on climate, thermostat settings, and insulation, but expect 15 to 20 percent annual reduction versus older systems.
Will this fit in my attic space that's 24 inches tall? +
No, this unit measures 24.5 inches in height, 26 inches wide, and 21.125 inches deep. You need a minimum of 25 to 26 inches of clear vertical space, plus additional room for airflow return and service access on the right side where the flue exhausts.
How does 96% AFUE compare to my current furnace? +
96% AFUE means 96 cents of every heating dollar converts to usable heat, with only 4 cents lost up the flue. Most furnaces from 2005 or earlier rate around 78 to 82 percent AFUE, making this upgrade save 10 to 15 percent on heating bills annually.
Why does this say 4 ton in the title but 5 ton in the tonnage spec? +
This appears to be a data entry discrepancy from the supplier. The cooling capacity of 45,500 BTU divided by 12,000 equals 3.79 tons, closer to 4 ton. Clarify tonnage directly with your supplier before purchase to avoid coil mismatches.
Does the 10-year warranty cover everything? +
The warranty covers major components like the compressor and heat exchanger when registered online. It excludes labor, refrigerant, wear items, and damage from improper installation or maintenance. Registration is required within 30 days of activation.
