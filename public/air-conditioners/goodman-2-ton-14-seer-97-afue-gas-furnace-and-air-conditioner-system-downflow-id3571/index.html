Review

The Goodman 2-ton gas furnace and air conditioner system represents solid mid-market positioning for residential HVAC replacements. At $643, this packaged unit combines an 80,000 BTU heating capacity with 24,000 BTU cooling output, making it suitable for small to medium homes in moderate climates. The system uses modern R454B refrigerant and features both heating and cooling in one integrated package, simplifying installation and reducing connection points.

Performance specs highlight this unit's strengths in heating efficiency. The 97% AFUE rating means nearly all combustion energy converts to usable heat, significantly outperforming older 80-90% furnaces. Cooling efficiency reaches 14 SEER with a single-stage compressor design. The variable-speed ECM blower motor runs at 1,200 CFM maximum, adjusting speed based on demand to optimize both comfort and efficiency. Operating at 71 dB, it produces moderate noise typical for this class of equipment.

Installation

Installation requires standard downflow configuration with right-side flue positioning. The unit accepts 3/8 inch liquid and 3/4 inch suction lines with pre-installed 3/4 inch insulation on the suction line. Electrical connection demands 115V single-phase 60Hz service with 14.3 to 25 amp breaker sizing. PVC plastic exhaust flue is required, and proper gas line sizing for propane or natural gas is essential. Professional installation is strongly recommended for refrigerant charging and gas connections.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this system with a 10-year warranty when you register online after purchase. Coverage includes parts and labor for defects in materials and workmanship. This is standard industry protection for mid-tier equipment. Registration is simple and extends your coverage period compared to unregistered units. Keep documentation and service records to support warranty claims.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this system qualify for energy rebates? +
Yes, the Energy Star certification and 14 SEER rating typically qualify for federal tax credits and many state or utility rebate programs. Check your local utility company's rebate schedule, as incentives vary by region. Some programs offer $300-800 for SEER 14 systems. Rebates are available according to product specs.
Can I install this in an attic or basement? +
The downflow configuration works well for basements and ground-level installations. For attic placement, you would need horizontal installation with proper support and adequate clearance. Check that your space accommodates the 34.5 inch height, 17.5 inch width, and 28.75 inch depth. Professional assessment ensures proper installation.
How much will this reduce my heating and cooling bills? +
Results depend on your current system's efficiency. Upgrading from an 80% AFUE furnace to 97% saves roughly 17% on heating costs. The 14 SEER cooling is 40-50% more efficient than 10 SEER equipment from the 1990s. Actual savings vary by climate, usage patterns, and local energy rates.
Is this better than higher-SEER systems I've seen? +
This 14 SEER unit offers excellent value at competitive pricing. Higher SEER models (16-18) cost 30-50% more upfront but offer 10-15% better cooling efficiency. For moderate climates, 14 SEER provides solid returns within 5-7 years. Hot climates benefit more from premium SEER ratings.
What warranty protection applies after online registration? +
