Review

The Goodman 3.5 Ton split system pairs a 120,000 BTU gas furnace with 42,000 BTU air conditioning capacity in a single upflow-configured package. This mid-market offering targets contractors and homeowners seeking reliable efficiency improvements without premium pricing. Online Supply's listing positions it as a practical upgrade for residential properties in mixed climates where both heating and cooling loads drive annual energy bills.

Performance metrics reveal solid engineering. The furnace delivers 96% AFUE efficiency, translating to roughly 12% better energy conversion than legacy 85% AFUE units common in older homes. The air conditioner achieves 14 SEER rating using R410A refrigerant with a scroll-type compressor rated for 2,000 CFM maximum airflow. Single-stage operation means the compressor runs at full capacity or off, lacking the modulation found in higher-tier systems, but maintaining proven reliability through simpler mechanics.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Furnace achieves 96% AFUE, meaning only 4% energy loss compared to 80-85% on decade-old units
  • 14 SEER cooling efficiency qualifies for many utility rebate programs worth $300-$800

  • Scroll compressor is proven architecture with strong reliability track record across thousands of installations

Installation

Installation requires 115V single-phase 60Hz power with 22 to 35 amp breaker sizing. The upflow A-coil configuration suits basement or utility closet placement in most homes. Suction line sizing at 7/8 inch and liquid line at 3/8 inch follow standard practices. PVC exhaust flue compatibility eliminates masonry chimney dependency, simplifying retrofits. Technicians should budget 4-6 hours for complete installation including refrigerant charge and airflow balancing.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year warranty on registered systems covers compressor, heat exchanger, and coil failures. Parts replacement is included, though labor coverage depends on installer participation agreements. Registration through Online Supply's website is required to activate coverage. This positions the unit competitively against budget lines offering 5-year terms while trailing premium manufacturers offering 12-year enhanced coverage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet list both 3.5 ton and 5 ton? +
Product naming indicates 3.5 ton cooling capacity (42,000 BTU divided by 12,000 BTU per ton), while furnace heating reaches 5 ton equivalent (120,000 BTU). This heating oversizing is common in cold climates but creates imbalance in moderate regions. Verify with your installer which tonnage matches your home's actual load calculation.
Can this replace my old R22 furnace and air conditioner? +
Yes, the system accepts both R410A and R22 refrigerant types, allowing staged replacement. You can retire your old outdoor unit and run this indoor coil with an existing R22 condenser temporarily, though R410A-matched condensers provide optimal performance and warranty coverage.
What energy savings should I expect versus my 20-year-old system? +
Upgrading from 80% AFUE to 96% AFUE furnace saves approximately 16% on heating costs. Combined with 14 SEER cooling replacing older 10 SEER units, total system energy reductions typically reach 20-25% annually. Actual savings depend on local utility rates and climate severity.
How does 14 SEER compare to other mid-market options? +
14 SEER sits in the practical efficiency tier, above budget 13 SEER models but below 16-18 SEER variable-capacity units costing 30-40% more. For mixed climates, 14 SEER offers strong rebate eligibility and ROI payback within 6-8 years on most replacement projects.
Is Online Supply's 10-year warranty transferable if I sell my home? +
Standard Goodman warranties transfer to second owners upon proper registration, though some Online Supply dealer agreements may limit coverage. Confirm transferability with your installer before purchase to understand long-term asset value.
