Review

The Goodman GCVC961205DN combines a gas furnace and air conditioner in one downflow package rated for 4 tons cooling capacity. At $3136, it occupies the practical middle ground between budget equipment and premium brands. This A-coil configuration suits retrofit installations where space constraints matter. The system carries a 10-year parts warranty with online registration, standard for Goodman's residential lineup. Energy Star certification signals that both components meet federal efficiency thresholds.

Performance specs reveal genuine capability. The furnace delivers 120,000 BTU heating output at 96% AFUE, meaning minimal energy waste during winter operation. The air conditioning side produces 47,000 BTU cooling at 18 SEER2 efficiency with two-stage compressor technology that ramps output based on load. The scroll compressor and variable-speed ECM blower motor suggest thoughtful design for balanced temperature swings. Operating at 72 dB at peak capacity places it in the moderate noise range, acceptable for most residential settings.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 96% AFUE furnace efficiency means 96 cents of every heating dollar converts to warmth, reducing winter energy bills significantly
  • 18 SEER2 two-stage compressor provides variable cooling matched to demand, cutting summer AC costs versus single-stage units
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor with 2000 CFM maximum allows precise airflow control and quieter operation at part load
  • Two-stage burner design offers flexible heating output for milder days, improving comfort and efficiency consistency
  • Energy Star certified system qualifies for utility rebates in most regions, potentially reducing net installation cost by $500 to $1500

Cons

  • Spec sheet lists conflicting tonnage information (title says 4 ton, specs show 5 ton), creating confusion about actual capacity
  • 72 dB noise level at full capacity approaches upper limit for bedroom window units, may require sound dampening in noise-sensitive locations
  • Requires licensed technician installation for gas connection and flue venting, adding $1000 to $2000 in labor costs that aren't included in $3136 price

Who Should Buy

Best For

Homeowners in mixed-climate regions replacing 10 to 15-year-old furnace and AC systems who want reliable efficiency without ultra-premium pricing. This system balances reasonable upfront cost against genuine energy savings that recover investment within 5 to 7 years.

Consider Alternatives If

Propane or natural gas compatibility offers flexibility based on local fuel availability. Professional installation typically adds $1200 to $1800 to the equipment cost.

Warranty & Reliability

Ten-year parts coverage applies with online registration, covering compressor, heat exchanger, and major components. This warranty extends beyond many competitors in this price tier. Labor warranty typically runs 1 to 5 years depending on installer, requiring clarification at purchase. Frequently Asked Questions

Does the spec sheet listing both 4 ton and 5 ton affect which system this actually is? +
The title specifies 4 ton capacity, but internal specs state 5 ton. This discrepancy should be clarified with the seller before purchase. Request the AHRI certificate number 7368845 documentation to confirm actual cooling capacity. The refrigerant charge and component sizing hinge on this detail.
Will this system fit in a closet with limited space? +
Dimensions measure 34.5 inches tall, 24.5 inches wide, and 28.75 inches deep. This downflow A-coil unit suits tight utility closets if vertical clearance allows. Confirm ductwork return and supply connections won't obstruct nearby walls or doors before installation.
How much will this system reduce my heating and cooling bills? +
96% AFUE furnace efficiency saves approximately 15 to 20 percent on winter heating versus 80% AFUE equipment. The 18 SEER2 cooling saves 20 to 25 percent versus 13 SEER older units. Exact savings depend on local energy rates, climate, and home insulation. Payback typically occurs in 5 to 7 years through energy cost reductions.
How does this Goodman system compare to comparable Lennox or Trane units? +
Goodman offers similar efficiency at lower upfront cost compared to Lennox XC25 or Trane XR15 systems. Trane and Lennox include better warranty terms and service networks. Goodman reliability is solid but ranked slightly below premium brands. This system makes sense for budget-conscious buyers willing to sacrifice brand prestige for performance per dollar.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover if my system fails? +
Parts coverage includes compressor, heat exchanger, and major components for 10 years with registration. Labor coverage varies by installer, typically 1 to 5 years. Refrigerant charges and routine maintenance are excluded. Review the warranty certificate at installation to understand specific exclusions and regional claim processes.
