Review

This 3-ton Goodman package arrives as a straightforward, middle-of-the-road HVAC solution targeting contractors and homeowners who prioritize reliability over cutting-edge specs. The system combines 36,000 cooling BTU with 100,000 heating BTU in horizontal A-coil configuration, making it adaptable to various installation scenarios. Goodman has long held credibility in the light commercial and residential space, and this unit maintains that brand reputation without pretending to be premium-tier equipment.

Performance numbers tell a practical story: 14 SEER cooling efficiency delivers meaningful improvements over legacy systems, while the 80% AFUE furnace provides adequate heating without premium gas costs. The scroll compressor handles 36,000 BTU cooling, the two-stage burner modulates heating output, and the multi-speed blower pushes 2,000 CFM maximum. At 73 dB, noise levels are moderate for outdoor condenser operation. The system runs on R410A or R22 refrigerant and requires 120V, 1-phase 60Hz service with 18.7 to 30-amp breaker capacity.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 14 SEER rating delivers real cooling efficiency improvements, typically 20-30% better than 10 SEER legacy units
  • Two-stage burner on furnace side provides modulating heat output, improving comfort and reducing short-cycling
  • 80% AFUE efficiency is solid for mid-range gas furnaces, meeting modern minimum standards
  • Multi-speed blower motor optimizes airflow delivery across varying load conditions
  • Scroll compressor is proven workhorse technology with good reliability track record
  • 36,000 cooling BTU and 100,000 heating BTU handle 3-ton loads appropriately

Cons

  • Single-stage condenser is less efficient than two-stage units, limiting part-load efficiency in mild weather
  • 80% AFUE trails high-efficiency models running 90-95%, meaning higher gas bills than premium alternatives
  • No ENERGY STAR certification, indicating it meets base standards but not top-tier recognition
  • 73 dB noise level is moderately loud for outdoor operation, notable in quiet residential areas

Installation

Installation follows standard horizontal/upflow configuration with A-coil design. Line sets include 3/8-inch liquid and 3/4-inch suction copper, with matching valve sizes. Suction line insulation comes pre-wrapped at 3/4-inch thickness. Metal exhaust flue required for furnace venting. Unit dimensions of 34.5-inch height, 21-inch width, and 28.75-inch depth fit typical attic and basement spaces. Total weight is 35 pounds, manageable for two-person installation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 14 SEER cooling efficiency compare to what I have now? +
If replacing a 10 SEER unit, expect approximately 20-40% lower cooling costs depending on climate and usage patterns. If your current system is 13 SEER, savings are more modest around 7-8%. SEER ratings measure steady-state efficiency, so real-world savings vary with seasonal temperature swings and system run time.
Can this replace my existing ductwork and outdoor unit? +
This unit works with compatible existing indoor coil housing and outdoor line set connections. However, older ductwork sized for lower-capacity units may need evaluation. Maximum CFM output is 2,000, so ducts must support that capacity. Have an HVAC tech measure existing ductwork before committing.
Is 80% AFUE adequate for my heating needs? +
80% AFUE is code-compliant and efficient for gas furnaces in most climates. It means 80 cents of heating energy reach your home per dollar spent. Premium models reach 95% AFUE, costing more upfront but lowering gas bills. For moderate climates, 80% balances initial cost against operating expense reasonably well.
How does this Goodman compare to other brands at the same price point? +
Goodman competes directly with Daikin, Lennox, and Carrier models at equivalent SEER/AFUE ratings. Goodman typically costs slightly less while delivering comparable component quality and reliability. You sacrifice brand prestige and marginally faster service response for lower purchase price. Warranty terms are standard across this tier.
What does the 10-year warranty cover if something fails? +
The warranty covers defective parts and labor repairs during the ten-year period from online registration. Normal wear items and refrigerant charges may have exclusions. Read the detailed warranty document carefully, as coverage specifics vary by component type and failure cause.
