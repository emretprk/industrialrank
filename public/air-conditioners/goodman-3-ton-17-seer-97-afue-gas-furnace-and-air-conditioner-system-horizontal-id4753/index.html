Review

This Goodman gas furnace and air conditioner system targets the practical contractor market with solid mid-range efficiency and straightforward horizontal installation. The combination of a 97% AFUE furnace paired with an 18 SEER2 condenser sits squarely in the Energy Star category without reaching premium pricing. However, significant spec inconsistencies in the product listing require clarification before purchase. The unit handles 80,000 BTU heating and 35,000 BTU cooling capacity with modulating burner stages and two-stage condenser technology.

Performance data shows this system prioritizes even heating and cooling over raw capacity. The variable-speed ECM blower motor runs at up to 1600 CFM, allowing the furnace to cycle modulation efficiently rather than constantly running full blast. Scroll compressor architecture in the condenser provides reliability, while the two-stage approach reduces runtime during partial-load conditions. R410A refrigerant compatibility meets current standards, and PVC exhaust flue requirements simplify venting in horizontal applications.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this unit work with both natural gas and propane? +
Yes. The furnace accepts both natural gas and propane fuel, making it flexible for homes with either supply. However, ensure the unit is factory-configured or professionally adjusted for your specific fuel type before installation to avoid combustion efficiency loss.
What electrical service do I need for this system? +
The unit requires 115V single-phase 60Hz power with breaker sizing between 21.3 and 35 amps. Most homes have adequate 115V circuits. Your electrician should confirm existing panel capacity and install appropriate breaker size based on actual compressor amperage.
How does the two-stage condenser improve efficiency? +
Two-stage compressors modulate between low and high capacity based on cooling demand. On mild days, the compressor runs at reduced output, consuming less power while maintaining comfort. The 18 SEER2 rating reflects this part-load efficiency advantage over single-stage units.
Can I use this with my existing ductwork? +
Compatibility depends on your current system configuration. The 1600 CFM blower and horizontal mounting work with most standard residential ductwork, but undersized ducts can bottleneck performance. Have a contractor verify CFM requirements match your duct design before proceeding.
Is the 10-year warranty adequate? +
Yes, this covers typical furnace and compressor lifespan for standard residential use. Scroll compressors and modulating furnaces typically reach 15 to 20 years with proper maintenance, so the warranty protects the most critical replacement periods.
