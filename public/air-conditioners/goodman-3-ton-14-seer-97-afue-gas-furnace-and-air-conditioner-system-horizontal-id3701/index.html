Review

This 3-ton Goodman gas furnace and air conditioner system sits squarely in the middle of the residential HVAC market, offering solid efficiency without flagship pricing. The combination of a modulating furnace rated at 100,000 BTU and a 3.5-ton condenser coil provides reasonable capacity for homes between 1,400 and 2,000 square feet. Goodman maintains strong market presence among contractors for reliable mid-tier equipment, and this horizontal downflow configuration works well for basements or attics where space is tight.

Performance specs reveal thoughtful engineering across both sides of the system. The furnace achieves 97% AFUE, meaning nearly all fuel converts to usable heat, while the condenser pulls 34,800 cooling BTU with 16 SEER2 efficiency. The modulating burner means heating output adjusts in steps rather than cycling on and off at full capacity, reducing temperature swings. The variable-speed ECM blower manages 2,000 CFM maximum, supporting both efficient part-load operation and strong delivery on demand days.

Installation

Installation complexity sits at moderate difficulty. The unit requires standard 115V single-phase power with a 30-amp breaker maximum, manageable for most residential panels. Suction lines need 3/4 inch insulation up to 50 feet, while liquid and suction connections use standard 3/8 inch and 7/8 inch ports respectively. Plastic PVC exhaust venting simplifies flue routing compared to metal ductwork. Professional installation remains strongly recommended for proper refrigerant charging and airflow balancing.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this system with a solid 10-year warranty on parts when you register online after purchase. This covers compressor failure, heat exchanger cracks, and component defects equally across heating and cooling. Real-world claims experience suggests Goodman honors these warranties fairly, though homeowners should keep receipts and registration documentation accessible. This protection level matches industry midpoint expectations and adds meaningful risk reduction.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 97% AFUE compare to standard furnaces in actual heating bills? +
A 97% AFUE furnace uses roughly 15-20% less fuel annually compared to 80% AFUE models, translating to $200-400 annual savings depending on your local gas rates and heating needs. Over a furnace's 15-year lifespan, this adds up to thousands in fuel cost recovery.
Does this system work with existing ductwork from older equipment? +
Yes, if your current ducts accommodate 2,000 CFM airflow and connect to downflow configuration. However, many older systems undersized for modern efficiency gains, so a technician should measure actual CFM delivery before installation to ensure proper temperature and humidity control.
What makes modulating burner stages different from standard on-off heating? +
Modulating burners adjust output in stages matching your home's heating load, preventing overshooting temperatures and maintaining steadier comfort. This reduces energy waste compared to full-capacity on-off cycling and eliminates those abrupt temperature swings many homeowners experience.
Is 16 SEER2 competitive with higher-efficiency units, or should I spend more? +
16 SEER2 sits in the 85th percentile for residential condensers, offering solid efficiency without premium pricing. Units rated 18-20 SEER2 cost significantly more and add incremental cooling savings of 5-10%, so your climate and electricity rates determine payback periods. For most moderate climates, 16 SEER2 hits the value sweet spot.
Does the 10-year warranty cover labor or only parts? +
Goodman's warranty covers parts only, not installation labor. You'll need separate service agreements for technician visits, though many contractors offer extended labor plans for reasonable fees when equipment is installed.
