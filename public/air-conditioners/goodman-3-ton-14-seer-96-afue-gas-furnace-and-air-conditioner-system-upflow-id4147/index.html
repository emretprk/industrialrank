Review

The Goodman 3-ton gas furnace and air conditioner system represents a pragmatic middle ground in the HVAC market. At $643, it targets cost-conscious homeowners and contractors seeking dependable equipment without luxury pricing. This upflow configuration with PVC exhaust compatibility makes it suitable for many residential installations. The system combines a 60,000 BTU furnace with 34,800 BTU cooling capacity, delivering year-round climate control for typical single-family homes.

Performance metrics tell a compelling efficiency story here. The 96% AFUE rating means nearly all fuel converts to usable heat, translating to meaningful savings on winter utility bills. The multi-speed ECM blower motor delivers up to 1200 CFM, adapting to heating and cooling demands throughout the day. SEER1 efficiency of 16 qualifies for ENERGY STAR certification, though it trails premium systems hitting 18-22. The two-stage furnace burner works with a single-stage condenser, providing straightforward operation without complex controls.

Installation

Installation requires 208/230V single-phase 60 Hz power with 18.7 to 30 amp breaker sizing. The unit measures 38.25 inches tall by 29 inches wide and deep, fitting most standard equipment closets and utility spaces. PVC flue venting on the right side simplifies routing compared to metal alternatives. The 3/8-inch liquid line and 7/8-inch suction line with 3/4-inch insulation are standard refrigerant connections. Technicians should allow for proper upflow clearance above the unit.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman's 10-year parts warranty with online registration provides solid protection, though registration is required to activate full coverage. This warranty covers manufacturing defects and component failures, excluding normal wear and seasonal maintenance. At this price point, the 10-year term matches or exceeds many competitors, offering peace of mind without extended service plan costs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this system right for my climate? +
This 3-ton system works best in moderate climates where winter heating demands reach 60,000 BTU and summer cooling needs stay near 34,800 BTU. Homes in extreme cold regions benefit from higher-capacity furnaces, while hot climates may need greater cooling capacity. Check your current system's tonnage as a baseline for proper sizing.
What electrical upgrades do I need? +
The system requires 208/230V single-phase 60 Hz service with a breaker sized between 18.7 and 30 amps. Most homes have adequate existing electrical service, though older panels may need evaluation. A licensed electrician should verify panel capacity and install proper breaker protection before equipment startup.
How much will this save me on heating costs? +
The 96% AFUE rating cuts heating waste to just 4%, versus 20% loss in 80% AFUE systems. Annual savings depend on fuel prices and heating hours, but upgrading from an older furnace typically saves 15-25% on heating bills. ENERGY STAR certification confirms these efficiency gains meet federal standards.
How does this compare to higher-SEER systems? +
This unit's SEER1 rating of 16 is solid for budget-friendly equipment, but premium systems reach 18-22 SEER. Higher ratings offer 10-15% additional cooling efficiency, justifying higher upfront costs only if you run heavy air conditioning. For moderate cooling use, this system delivers compelling value without premium pricing.
Does online registration really matter for warranty? +
Yes, the 10-year warranty requires online registration to activate. Without registration, coverage drops significantly. Register immediately after installation to secure full protection. Keep registration confirmation and purchase documentation for any future claims.
