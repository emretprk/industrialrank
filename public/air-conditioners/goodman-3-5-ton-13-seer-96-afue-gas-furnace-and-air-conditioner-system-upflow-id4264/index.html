Review

The ACiQ 3.5-ton gas furnace and air conditioning system pairs proven Goodman engineering with straightforward, installer-friendly design. This upflow unit delivers 100,000 BTU heating capacity and 42,000 BTU cooling across dual-fuel capability (natural gas or propane), making it a practical choice for mixed-climate regions. At mid-market pricing, it competes against other contractor-grade combos by offering modern efficiency without feature creep.

Performance numbers tell a solid story. The furnace reaches 96% AFUE, which translates to lower heating costs over a season compared to 80-90% units. The cooling side manages 13 SEER efficiency with a reciprocating compressor and single-stage condenser, adequate for moderate cooling loads in temperate zones. The variable-speed ECM blower motor (2000 CFM max) distributes air more evenly than fixed-speed alternatives, improving comfort during heating and cooling cycles.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can this furnace run on propane instead of natural gas? +
Yes. The dual-fuel design handles both propane and natural gas. Your installer must configure the burner and fuel supply lines correctly during setup. Propane typically costs more per BTU but provides an option for off-grid or rural properties lacking natural gas lines.
What electrical service do I need for this unit? +
You need 208-230V single-phase 60Hz service with a minimum 23.9-amp breaker and maximum 40-amp breaker. Most homes have this standard residential electrical panel. Verify your current panel capacity before purchase to avoid expensive upgrades.
How does 96% AFUE efficiency compare to standard furnaces? +
A 96% AFUE furnace converts 96 cents of fuel into heat per dollar spent, losing only 4% up the flue. Older units at 80% AFUE waste 20%. Over a heating season, this translates to 15-20% lower heating bills in northern climates, recouping cost differences in 5-8 years.
Is 13 SEER good cooling efficiency for the price? +
13 SEER represents solid mid-tier cooling, adequate for temperate climates. High-efficiency alternatives reach 16-18 SEER but cost significantly more upfront. Choose 13 SEER if budget matters; choose higher ratings if you run AC heavily or live in hot, humid regions.
Does this unit qualify for utility rebates? +
The listing notes rebates are available, though specific amounts depend on your local utility company and state programs. Contact your utility before purchasing to confirm eligibility and claim deadlines, as some rebates require registration within 30 days.
