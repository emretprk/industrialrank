Review

The Goodman 2-ton system combines furnace and air conditioning into one competitively priced package at $643, positioned squarely in the value segment of the HVAC market. This ACiQ-branded unit pairs a 96% AFUE gas furnace with 14 SEER cooling, delivering above-average efficiency without stretching contractor or homeowner budgets. The downflow configuration offers flexible installation options, making it viable for both horizontal and vertical placements in homes where space is limited.

Performance specs show 24,000 BTU heating output and matching 24,000 BTU cooling capacity with an 800 CFM blower motor running at multi-speed operation. The 96% furnace efficiency is genuinely competitive, recovering nearly all fuel energy into conditioned air, while the 14 SEER cooling rating reflects single-stage operation that maintains consistency without variable capacity. Electrical requirements are straightforward at 208/230V single-phase 60Hz with a 17.1 to 25-amp breaker window, keeping installation wiring typical for residential applications.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 96% AFUE furnace efficiency significantly reduces heating costs compared to 80-90% units
  • 2-ton 14 SEER cooling qualifies as Energy Star, matching modern efficiency standards
  • Single-stage scroll compressor is proven technology with low failure rates
  • Downflow/horizontal configuration accommodates both basement and attic installations
  • 10-year warranty with online registration covers manufacturing defects
  • PVC exhaust flue requirement eliminates costly masonry venting upgrades

Cons

  • 24,000 BTU heating capacity insufficient for homes above 2,000 square feet in cold climates
  • Single-stage cooling and heating cannot modulate capacity, leading to wider temperature swings
  • 70 dBA noise level at peak operation may concern noise-sensitive homeowners
  • R454B refrigerant is newer with limited technician familiarity in some markets

Who Should Buy

Best For

Homeowners in USDA zones 5-8 replacing failed 10-20 year old systems who prioritize upfront cost savings over variable-speed efficiency. Contractors installing budget-friendly replacements in small to mid-sized homes where single-stage operation is acceptable.

Consider Alternatives If

Anyone in zone 3-4 heating climates, homes over 2,500 square feet, or buyers seeking modulating compressors for comfort should explore higher-tonnage or multi-stage alternatives.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 24,000 BTU heating enough for my house? +
For homes under 1,800 square feet in moderate climates, yes. Larger homes or zone 3-4 heating areas typically need 3 to 5-ton systems. Calculate your load: roughly 40 BTU per square foot in cold climates. This unit works well in zones 5-8 for average-sized homes.
Can I install this in my upflow attic setup? +
Yes, it's designed for both upflow and downflow. The unit weighs 120 pounds and measures 38 inches tall, so verify attic access clearance. Horizontal attic runs need proper return air plenum design and condensate drainage below the evaporator coil.
How does 14 SEER compare to newer 16+ SEER models? +
14 SEER saves roughly 12-15% on cooling costs versus 13 SEER baseline equipment, but upgrading to 16 SEER saves an additional 12% more. The payback depends on climate; air-conditioning-heavy regions justify higher SEER, while heating-dominant areas may not recoup the cost difference.
What's the difference between this ACiQ unit and standard Goodman? +
ACiQ is Goodman's value brand using identical compressors and heat exchangers with streamlined features. You lose smart controls and variable-speed options, but gain 96% efficiency and reliable single-stage operation at significant savings versus full Goodman product lines.
Will my existing ductwork fit this system's 800 CFM output? +
Older homes with undersized ducts may struggle. Standard residential ducts handle 400-600 CFM comfortably. If upgrading from a smaller unit, verify duct sizing with Manual D calculations or request contractor load analysis before installation.
