Review

The Goodman 3-ton gas furnace and air conditioner combination sits comfortably in the middle market, offering contractors and homeowners a proven, no-frills approach to HVAC replacement. This upflow A-coil system is manufactured through Online Supply and rated at 100,000 BTU heating capacity paired with 42,000 BTU cooling. With a 92% AFUE furnace rating and 14 SEER air conditioning efficiency, it delivers respectable performance without premium pricing, making it a practical choice for homes in moderate climates where efficiency gains beyond this tier don't justify the cost differential.

The system runs on R410A refrigerant with a scroll compressor and single-stage cooling cycle, producing a maximum 2,000 CFM airflow. The multi-speed blower motor allows technicians to tune performance for specific ductwork and comfort zones. Heating efficiency at 92% AFUE means roughly 92 cents of every heating dollar reaches conditioned space, which trails premium condensing furnaces but exceeds many older units significantly. For cooling, the 14 SEER rating provides solid performance without the noise and complexity of variable-displacement compressors.

Installation

Installation requires 208/230 volt single-phase 60 Hz electrical service with a 22 to 35 amp breaker. The right-side flue location accepts PVC piping, simplifying exhaust routing in retrofit situations. At 21 by 21 by 30 inches and weighing 30 pounds, the compact footprint fits standard closets and utility spaces. The A-coil style connects directly to existing ductwork with 3/8-inch liquid and 7/8-inch suction lines, both requiring standard refrigeration fittings.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this system with a 10-year parts warranty when you complete online registration after installation. This covers compressor, heat exchanger, and most mechanical components, though labor and refrigerant are typically excluded. The 10-year term is competitive for mid-market equipment and provides meaningful protection against unexpected failures during the critical first decade of operation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this system Energy Star certified? +
No, this model does not carry Energy Star certification. While its 92% AFUE and 14 SEER ratings are respectable, they fall slightly short of Energy Star thresholds. This may disqualify you from some utility rebates, so verify your local programs before purchasing.
Can this system work with my existing downflow installation? +
Yes, Goodman lists this unit as compatible with both upflow and downflow configurations. However, confirm the right-side flue exit works with your home's venting setup. Some downflow installations require left-side or bottom exits, so verify before ordering.
How does 14 SEER compare to newer SEER2 standards? +
This system was rated under the older SEER metric. SEER2 ratings are roughly 15 percent lower numerically, so expect this unit to measure around 11.8 SEER2 equivalent. Actual cooling performance remains unchanged, just measured differently.
Should I choose this or a higher-SEER variable-speed system? +
Variable-speed units cost 20 to 30 percent more but reduce noise and offer 16 to 18 SEER performance. Choose this Goodman if upfront cost matters and your heating load is moderate. Premium systems justify themselves mainly in very hot or cold climates.
What happens if I don't register the warranty online? +
Without registration, warranty coverage drops to 5 years instead of 10. Register immediately after installation to secure the full decade of protection.
