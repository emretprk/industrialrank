Review

The Goodman 5 Ton 14 SEER system sits squarely in the mid-market segment where affordability meets acceptable performance. This isn't a premium unit, nor is it a budget basement model. ACiQ/Goodman positions this downflow A-coil configuration for contractors who need dependable equipment that installs cleanly in standard 35.5-inch width spaces. It replaces the GSZ140611 legacy model and brings modern R454B refrigerant while maintaining the single-stage simplicity contractors have trusted for decades.

Cooling performance reaches 57,000 BTU with a 14 SEER rating, meaning you're looking at roughly $0.85-$1.10 per cooling dollar depending on regional rates. The heating side delivers 58,000 BTU at 80% AFUE, which translates to 80 cents of heat output per dollar of gas consumed. That's respectable for non-condensing furnaces but lags condensing models by 10-15 points. The multi-speed PSC blower can push 2,000 CFM maximum, enough to condition modest to moderately sized homes without excessive static pressure.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 14 SEER rating delivers solid cooling efficiency at modest price point, competitive for non-ENERGY STAR systems
  • 57,000 BTU cooling and 58,000 BTU heating capacity properly matched for true 5-ton operation
  • Multi-speed PSC blower motor with 2,000 CFM maximum allows some comfort tuning across seasons
  • R454B refrigerant meets modern environmental standards while maintaining proven cooling performance

Cons

Installation

Installation involves standard refrigerant line connections using 3/8-inch liquid and 1 1/8-inch suction lines with 1/2-inch suction insulation over 50 feet of run. The unit requires top-mounted metal flue exhaust and 208/230V single-phase power with 34.6 to 60-amp breaker sizing. Downflow orientation simplifies placement in utility closets, though contractors must verify adequate return airflow and proper condensate drainage from the coil.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year parts and labor warranty activates through online registration, providing solid coverage against manufacturing defects. This matches many mid-tier HVAC offerings and beats budget models, though doesn't extend to neglected maintenance or installer error. Goodman's service network remains broad, making warranty claims relatively straightforward across most regions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 14 SEER compare to newer SEER2 ratings? +
SEER2 testing uses stricter conditions and generally runs 15-20% lower than SEER1. This unit's 14 SEER likely translates to roughly 11-11.5 SEER2 equivalent. It's mid-range, not premium, so compare against other SEER1 14-rated units when shopping directly.
Can this system work in cold climates where heating matters most? +
The 80% AFUE furnace runs acceptably but isn't optimized for high heating demand. In zones requiring 5,000+ heating degree days annually, a 95%+ AFUE condensing furnace recovers exhaust heat better and reduces gas bills substantially over 15 years.
What's the actual runtime electrical demand? +
Unit requires 208 or 230V single-phase at 60 Hz with minimum 34.6-amp breaker and maximum 60-amp capacity. Verify existing service before installation. Most standard home electrical panels handle this easily.
Does this cool better than a Lennox or Carrier equivalent at the same price? +
At 14 SEER, it matches most mid-market competitors directly. Real-world differences come from installation quality, ductwork design, and thermostat controls, not the coil itself. Contractor reputation matters more than brand nuances here.
What if I register online for the warranty? +
Online registration activates the full 10-year coverage on parts and labor. Skip this and you're typically limited to 5 years. Registration takes minutes and protects your investment.
