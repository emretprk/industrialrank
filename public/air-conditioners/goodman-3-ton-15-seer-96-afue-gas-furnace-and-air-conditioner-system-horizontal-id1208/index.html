Installation

Horizontal installation requires clear attic or crawlspace routing with proper ductwork support and condensate drainage sloped away from the unit. Plastic PVC exhaust flue must vent to exterior with correct rise and horizontal runs per Goodman spec sheets. The 30-amp maximum breaker size fits standard residential panels. Pre-installation review of CHPF manuals prevents common mistakes with refrigerant line sizing (3/8 inch liquid, 7/8 inch suction) and evaporator coil compatibility.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this 3.5-ton unit really match a typical 2,500 sq ft home? +
Manual J sizing is essential. Many homes that size need 2.5 to 3 tons, making this unit slightly oversized. Oversizing reduces part-load efficiency and causes short-cycling where the system runs briefly then shuts down, wasting startup energy. Professional load calculation determines if downsizing to 3 tons improves your efficiency profile.
Can I install this myself if I have HVAC experience? +
Possible but risky. EPA Section 608 certification is legally required to handle R410A refrigerant in most states. Improper evacuation or charging voids warranty and causes compressor failure. Hiring a licensed technician costs $500-1,000 but protects your 10-year coverage and ensures proper airflow commissioning.
How much heating cost savings should I expect versus my 15-year-old furnace? +
Older furnaces average 78-82% AFUE. This 96% unit cuts fuel consumption roughly 15-18 percent. Regional natural gas rates vary, but most homeowners save $250-400 annually. Over 15 years, that totals $3,750-6,000 in heating cost reduction, easily offsetting the $3,136 purchase price.
Why is SEER1 listed as 16 when the product title says 15 SEER? +
SEER2 represents newer EPA testing that penalizes part-load efficiency more heavily than legacy SEER ratings. SEER1 (older metric) measures 16, while SEER2 would rate lower. Both metrics apply to this unit depending on whether sellers use legacy or current EPA standards for marketing.
Is the 10-year warranty truly comprehensive? +
Parts coverage is solid, excluding labor, refrigerant, and electrical work. Damage from improper installation or refrigerant overcharging voids coverage. Registration is mandatory. Read the full warranty document before purchase to confirm what components and failure modes your coverage truly includes.
