Review

The ACiQ ACIQ-4X-EHPD-UPG is a solid mid-tier split system combining a gas furnace and air conditioning condenser designed for downflow installation. At $647, it undercuts comparable Goodman-branded equipment while maintaining core efficiency standards. This 3.5-ton unit is engineered for residential applications in homes ranging from 1,400 to 1,800 square feet. The system carries a 10-year warranty with online registration and Energy Star certification, positioning it as a practical choice for homeowners balancing budget constraints with genuine efficiency requirements.

Performance specs are straightforward and honest. The furnace delivers 100,000 BTU with 96% AFUE efficiency using natural gas or propane, paired with a two-stage burner and variable-speed ECM blower motor capable of 2,000 CFM maximum airflow. Cooling output reaches 42,000 BTU at 13 SEER using R410A refrigerant with a single-stage reciprocating compressor. Electrical requirements are modest, needing 115V single-phase 60Hz service with a 23.9 to 40-amp breaker range. These specifications deliver measurable efficiency without reaching top-tier performance tiers.

Installation

Installation requires standard split-system procedures with refrigerant line sets up to 30 feet maximum. The condenser uses 3/8-inch liquid lines and 7/8-inch suction lines with sweat valve connections. Downflow configuration works in attics or crawlspaces with plastic or PVC exhaust flue venting. The unit weighs 120 pounds and measures 34.5 inches tall, 21 inches wide, and 28.75 inches deep. Standard ductwork and electrical work apply. AHRI certificate number 7368844 ensures proper matching with indoor coils.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year parts warranty requires online registration and covers manufacturing defects. This is above-average coverage for equipment at this price point but below premium brands offering lifetime compressor protection. The warranty does not include labor costs or damage from improper installation, so hiring a certified technician is recommended. Check your local regulations for any emissions or efficiency rebates that may apply.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the 13 SEER rating compare to newer air conditioners? +
SEER 13 is solid but not cutting-edge. Current premium units hit SEER 16-18. However, SEER 13 still qualifies for most utility rebates and Energy Star incentives. The real question is whether paying extra for SEER 15+ makes sense in your climate. In moderate cooling zones, SEER 13 delivers reasonable payback within 7-10 years.
Can I install this in an attic if my home doesn't have a basement? +
Yes. The downflow/horizontal A-coil configuration is specifically designed for attic and crawlspace placement. The 34.5-inch height and 21-inch width fit most standard framing. Ensure adequate clearance for airflow and access for future maintenance. Plastic PVC venting makes attic installation straightforward compared to older metal duct systems.
Is 100,000 BTU enough heat for a cold climate? +
For most homes in zones 5-6, yes. A properly insulated 1,500-square-foot home typically needs 60,000-80,000 BTU. The extra 20,000 BTU buffer handles poor insulation and extreme cold snaps. Get a Manual J load calculation done to confirm. Oversizing costs more to operate; undersizing won't keep you warm.
Why is the price $647 when Goodman branded systems cost $1,200+? +
ACiQ is Goodman's budget subsidiary using the same compressors and burners but fewer premium features. You lose variable-capacity cooling, quieter operation, and extensive warranty add-ons. The core heating and cooling components are proven. This pricing model works for budget-conscious buyers willing to trade convenience for savings.
Does the 10-year warranty cover refrigerant leaks? +
Manufacturing defects yes, but not improper installation or normal wear. Refrigerant itself is not covered. Online registration is required to activate warranty coverage, so complete that immediately after purchase. Labor costs always fall on the homeowner.
