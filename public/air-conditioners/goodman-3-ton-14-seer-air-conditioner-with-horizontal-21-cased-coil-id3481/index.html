Review

The ACiQ 3 Ton 14 SEER horizontal cased coil represents a pragmatic choice in today's mid-tier AC market. Built around Goodman's trusted platform with modern R454B refrigerant, this unit delivers credible efficiency without premium pricing. The horizontal 21-inch configuration specifically targets retrofit applications where attic height and space constraints eliminate vertical coils. At 36,000 BTU capacity with a scroll compressor, it's engineered for durability rather than cutting-edge performance.

The 14 SEER rating puts this squarely in the reliable-efficiency zone, representing approximately 40% better performance than older 10 SEER models. The scroll compressor, operating at single-stage capacity, cycles fully on and off rather than modulating, which simplifies mechanics but sacrifices part-load comfort compared to variable-capacity alternatives. Electrical requirements remain straightforward, needing just 208/230V single-phase 60 Hz service with a 30-amp breaker maximum. The 73 dB noise level is competent but audible compared to quieter premium units.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 14 SEER compare to the competition at this price point? +
14 SEER is competitive for budget-focused units but trails premium options reaching 16-17 SEER. Expect approximately 10-15% lower annual cooling costs versus 13 SEER equipment. The trade-off is worth it for most homeowners, though energy-conscious buyers in hot climates should consider 15+ SEER alternatives with variable compressors.
Will this horizontal coil fit my attic installation? +
The 21-inch height, 21.125-inch width, and 26-inch depth fit most attic framings. Measure your joists and available headroom, accounting for the 3/4-inch suction line insulation. Verify ductwork connections don't exceed line set angles, as tight bends reduce efficiency.
How efficient is single-stage operation compared to variable-stage? +
Single-stage means the compressor runs at full output or shuts off, reducing efficiency during mild weather. Variable-stage units modulate capacity, maintaining efficiency across temperature ranges. For intermittent use climates, this unit performs well. High-use regions should explore variable-capacity options.
Is R454B refrigerant available if I need service? +
Yes, R454B is increasingly stocked at major HVAC distributors as EPA transitions away from R410A. Supply will improve through 2025. Verify your local technician stocks R454B before purchase, particularly in rural areas where phasedown adoption lags.
Does the 10-year warranty cover refrigerant leaks? +
Manufacturing defects in the coil are covered. Leaks from improper installation, physical damage, or normal wear are excluded. Online registration is required within 30 days of installation to activate coverage.
