Review

The Goodman 3-ton horizontal system represents a practical mid-range choice in the HVAC equipment market, blending solid efficiency with straightforward installation. At $3225, this unit sits in reasonable territory for homeowners upgrading from 10+ year old equipment or contractors standardizing on reliable Goodman platforms. The horizontal configuration specifically targets installations where vertical space is limited, such as attics with low clearance or basement installations where headroom above a furnace is restricted.

Performance numbers tell a compelling story for heating-focused climates. The furnace achieves 96% AFUE with dual-stage burners, meaning nearly every dollar spent on gas translates to usable heat. The cooling side delivers 35,000 BTU from a two-stage scroll compressor with 18 SEER1 rating, though the 71 dB noise level indicates moderate compressor activity during peak cooling. Variable-speed ECM blower technology modulates up to 2000 CFM, avoiding the energy waste of oversized constant-speed motors and improving dehumidification on humid days.

Installation

Installation requires standard HVAC practices: refrigerant line connections use 3/8 inch liquid and 7/8 inch suction lines with 3/4 inch foam insulation included for 50 feet of lineset. Electrical demands fall within typical home service specifications at 115V, 1-phase, 60Hz with 21.3 to 35 amp breaker sizing. PVC exhaust flue installation is mandatory and straightforward in new construction but may require venting modifications in homes with existing masonry chimneys. Sweat valve connections demand soldering skills or professional installation.

Warranty & Reliability

Online Supply backs this unit with a 10-year parts and labor warranty upon registration, which is competitive for equipment at this price point. The registration requirement is standard but non-negotiable for claim eligibility. Goodman's evaporator coil carries separate warranty coverage detailed in supplied documentation. Frequently Asked Questions

Does this unit actually cool 3 tons or 5 tons? +
The product title claims 3 ton capacity, but specifications list 5 ton tonnage with 35,000 BTU cooling output. This discrepancy suggests potential labeling error or refers to matched furnace capacity versus standalone condenser rating. Verify AHRI certificate 7368845 and contact Online Supply before purchase to clarify actual cooling tonnage for your application.
Can this horizontal coil work with my existing upflow furnace? +
No. This A-coil is specifically designed for downflow/horizontal airflow orientation. Installing it on an upflow furnace causes refrigerant distribution problems and voids warranty. You must either replace the furnace with a horizontal model or select a vertical upflow coil configuration designed for your current equipment.
How much will this system save on heating costs compared to an 80 AFUE furnace? +
The 96% AFUE rating versus 80% AFUE saves approximately 16,000 BTU per 100,000 BTU of gas burned. On a $1.20 per therm natural gas rate, annual savings reach roughly $190 for every 100,000 BTU of annual heating demand, typically $200 to $400 for cold-climate homes.
Is 18 SEER1 enough or should I wait for higher ratings? +
18 SEER1 cooling efficiency is solid for mid-range equipment. Top-tier systems reach 21+ SEER1 but cost 40% more. For moderate climates and average cooling loads, 18 SEER1 delivers payback within 8 to 10 years. Wait for higher ratings only if your budget permits and cooling costs are your primary concern.
What happens if I don't register the warranty? +
Warranty coverage becomes void without online registration. Online Supply requires registration proof for any claims. Register immediately after installation to activate the full 10-year protection period and ensure claim eligibility for compressor or heat exchanger failures.
