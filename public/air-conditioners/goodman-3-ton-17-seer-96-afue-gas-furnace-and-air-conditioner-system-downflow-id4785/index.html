Installation

This system requires standard refrigerant lines (3/8-inch liquid, 7/8-inch suction with 3/4-inch insulation) and basic electrical work at 115V single-phase 60Hz. The 21.3 to 35-amp breaker range fits most residential panels. PVC flue capability simplifies venting compared to older masonry chimney requirements. Downflow orientation needs adequate space below the unit for condensate drainage and blower intake. Licensed HVAC installer strongly recommended for proper evacuation, charging, and startup.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year comprehensive warranty covers parts and labor when you complete online registration after installation. This timeline protects against early compressor failures and component defects but excludes wear items like blower motors and capacitors after 5 years. Registration immediately after commissioning ensures the coverage window starts correctly and prevents documentation delays if problems emerge.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet say both 3-ton and 3.5-ton? +
The system is rated 3.5-ton capacity but marketed as suitable for 3-ton applications, providing 500-pound capacity headroom. This prevents oversizing penalties while handling peak summer loads without strain. Verify cooling load calculations before ordering to ensure proper sizing for your home.
Can I install this myself or upgrade from my existing system? +
Federal refrigerant handling regulations require EPA-certified technicians for any work touching refrigerant lines. Most homeowners can handle electrical connections and ductwork modifications, but compressor evacuation, charging, and pressure testing demand professional licensing. Labor costs typically run $1,500 to $2,500 depending on existing infrastructure condition.
How much will this actually save me compared to a basic 13 SEER unit? +
The 18 SEER2 versus 13 SEER represents roughly 30-35% better cooling efficiency. On heating, 96% AFUE versus 80% AFUE (typical for older units) recovers an additional 16% of fuel energy. Annual savings in moderate climates range from $400 to $800 depending on local electricity and gas rates.
How does this compare to higher-SEER variable-refrigerant models? +
Premium systems reach 20+ SEER2 but cost $5,500 to $7,500 installed. This Goodman delivers 85-90% of that efficiency at 60-70% of the price. The two-stage approach offers better part-load performance than single-stage units without the complexity and cost of fully variable systems.
Is the 10-year warranty actually useful if parts fail? +
Yes, provided you register online immediately after installation. The warranty covers compressor replacement and refrigerant leaks, which are the most expensive failures. Exclusions apply to wear items after 5 years, but core components remain covered through year 10.
