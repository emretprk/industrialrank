Review

The Goodman GMVM970603BN combines a 2-ton air conditioner with a gas furnace in one compact horizontal package. This Energy Star certified system targets homeowners wanting efficiency improvements without entering the premium equipment category. At $115 wholesale pricing, it competes directly with other mainstream brands in the builder and mid-market contractor channels. The dual-fuel configuration makes it suitable for regions requiring both consistent cooling and winter heating capacity.

Cooling delivers 24,000 BTU at 16 SEER efficiency, while heating provides 60,000 BTU at 97% AFUE. The two-stage compressor and modulating furnace burner both reduce cycling losses by running at partial capacity during mild weather. Variable-speed ECM blower adjusts to demand, maintaining 1,200 CFM maximum while saving energy during lighter loads. This modulating approach particularly benefits homeowners in transitional seasons when heating and cooling needs fluctuate throughout the day.

Installation

Installation requires a licensed HVAC technician due to natural gas and propane connections. The horizontal A-coil configuration fits tight attic spaces, and plastic PVC venting simplifies exhaust routing. Electrical demand stays modest at 115V single-phase with breaker requirements between 15.7 and 20 amps. Sweat-type valve connections demand careful soldering to prevent leaks and refrigerant loss.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides 10 years of comprehensive parts coverage when you register online after purchase. This non-prorated warranty covers compressor, heat exchanger, and coil failures. Online registration takes minutes and activates immediately, offering solid protection for systems lasting 15-20 years with proper maintenance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet say 3-ton tonnage when this is marketed as 2-ton? +
The cooling capacity rates at 24,000 BTU (2-ton standard), but the system is engineered to handle modest oversizing scenarios. This rating appears to reflect AHRI certificate matching. Always confirm with manufacturer documentation that your home's load calculation aligns with actual installed capacity.
Can I install this myself to save money? +
No. Gas appliance installation requires state-licensed technicians in most jurisdictions. Improper gas line or venting connections create carbon monoxide and explosion hazards. The manufacturer explicitly disclaims responsibility for DIY installation. Budget $800-1,500 for professional installation.
How much will this reduce my heating and cooling bills? +
Compared to 13 SEER and 80% AFUE equipment from 2005, expect 20-30% annual utility savings depending on climate. The modulating burner and variable blower optimize part-load efficiency when temperatures are mild. Actual savings depend on local energy rates and thermostat programming discipline.
How does this compare to Lennox or Carrier mid-tier systems? +
Goodman trades quieter operation and brand cachet for lower upfront cost. The 16 SEER and 97% AFUE ratings match competitors' mid-tier offerings. Goodman parts availability and service network rival major brands in most markets, though Lennox typically runs 2-3 dB quieter.
What happens after the 10-year warranty expires? +
Coverage ends, but the system typically continues operating reliably for 5-10 additional years. Plan for eventual compressor or heat exchanger replacement around year 15-18. Extended warranties are available but rarely justify their cost for mid-tier equipment.
