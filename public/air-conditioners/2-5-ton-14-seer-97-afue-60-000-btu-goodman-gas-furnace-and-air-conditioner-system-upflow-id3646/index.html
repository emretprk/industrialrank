Review

The ACiQ 2.5-ton system represents the practical middle ground in residential HVAC equipment. At $732, it delivers capable heating and cooling without the complexity or cost of premium brands. This upflow A-coil configuration suits most residential installations, and the Energy Star certification signals legitimate efficiency credentials rather than marketing hype. Contractors favor ACiQ for straightforward builds where homeowners want dependable performance without premium pricing.

Where this system earns respect is the heating side, where the 60,000 BTU furnace achieves 97% AFUE through modulating burners that adjust output between full and partial fire. Cooling delivers 30,000 BTU with 14 SEER efficiency, though the single-stage compressor cycles fully on or off rather than ramping capacity. The variable-speed ECM blower running to 1,200 CFM maximizes air distribution, and R454B refrigerant future-proofs against upcoming regulations. Real-world performance expectations: heating bills 5-8% lower than 90% AFUE units, cooling performance solid but not premium.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet list 3-ton cooling but this is marketed as 2.5-ton? +
The 30,000 BTU cooling capacity technically equals 2.5 tons, but the condenser unit itself is rated 3-ton class hardware. Sizing reflects the condenser's physical cooling envelope, not system design. Verify with your installer that your home's load calculation matches 2.5-ton requirements before purchase.
Can this system work with my existing ductwork and breaker panel? +
The 17.1 to 25-amp electrical requirement fits most homes with 150-amp service, though older houses with 100-amp panels may need upgrades. Upflow design works with existing ductwork in most attic/closet installations. Have a licensed electrician and HVAC tech evaluate your specific setup before ordering.
How does 14 SEER compare to modern standards? +
14 SEER meets federal minimum efficiency standards and qualifies as Energy Star certified. It's not cutting-edge, higher-end units hit 16-20 SEER with variable-capacity compressors. Real-world difference in cooling bills runs 10-15% compared to budget 13 SEER models.
Is the modulating furnace worth the extra cost versus single-stage? +
Yes, modulating burners at 97% AFUE reduce runtime and fuel consumption versus 90-95% fixed-stage units. You'll see 3-5% yearly heating savings depending on climate. Over ten years, that pays back the equipment difference on most utility bills.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Parts and compressor failures are covered after online registration, excluding refrigerant, labor, and maintenance items. Coverage runs from installation date, so keep paperwork. ACiQ's support infrastructure through Goodman parent company is solid for claims processing.
