Review

This Goodman 2-ton combo system combines a 97% AFUE furnace with 80,000 BTU heating capacity and a 24,000 BTU air conditioner in one upflow package. At $643, it sits in the practical middle ground between bare-minimum efficiency and premium high-end equipment. The system earns ENERGY STAR certification and comes with a 10-year warranty when registered online. It's designed for contractors and homeowners who want modern efficiency standards without luxury pricing.

The furnace's modulating burner and variable-speed ECM blower represent real operational advantages. Rather than simple on-off cycling, the modulating burner adjusts flame intensity to match heating demand, reducing standby losses. The 1200 CFM maximum airflow and variable blower adapt cooling and heating distribution to actual room temperatures. Cooling efficiency rates at 14 SEER1, qualifying for most state rebate programs. The R454B refrigerant meets current environmental standards. Electrical requirements are straightforward, 115V single-phase with a 14.3 to 25-amp breaker range.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 97% AFUE furnace efficiency means 97 cents of every heating dollar converts to usable heat, significantly outperforming standard 80-85% models
  • Modulating burner adjusts output automatically rather than cycling on and off, reducing energy waste and improving comfort consistency
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor adapts airflow to demand, avoiding the constant full-blast operation of older single-speed designs
  • ENERGY STAR certified cooling with 14 SEER1 rating meets modern efficiency standards for tax credit eligibility in many states
  • A-coil configuration and upflow design work in basements, closets, or utility rooms with standard ductwork
  • Competitive $643 price point for a complete 2-ton system makes it accessible for budget-conscious contractors and homeowners

Cons

  • 14 SEER1 on the cooling side is solid but not top-tier, with premium units reaching 16-18 SEER1
  • Single-stage condenser means outdoor unit runs at full capacity rather than modulating, slightly less efficient than two-stage alternatives
  • 71 dB noise level is moderate but some homeowners notice the blower during operation at maximum CFM

Installation

Upflow configuration installs cleanly in basements, attics, or utility closets where vertical ductwork flows upward. The 34.5-inch height and 17.5-inch width fit standard equipment closets. Plastic PVC exhaust flue requirement simplifies venting compared to older metal duct systems. Suction line arrives pre-insulated at 3/4-inch thickness. Standard refrigerant connections use 3/8-inch and 3/4-inch fittings. Most HVAC contractors complete installation in four to six hours.

Ten-year parts warranty applies when registered online, a straightforward process taking five minutes on Goodman's website. Frequently Asked Questions

How much does this system reduce heating costs compared to an older 80% AFUE furnace? +
The 97% AFUE versus 80% AFUE difference saves approximately 17% on furnace heating costs annually. On a $1,200 winter heating bill, that translates to roughly $200 savings per season. Actual savings vary by fuel costs, insulation quality, and thermostat settings in your specific climate zone.
Can this system be installed horizontally in an attic or closet? +
The upflow/horizontal configuration supports both orientations. Horizontal attic installation requires adequate support framing and longer ductwork runs. Consult your contractor about attic temperature extremes, as furnaces perform better in conditioned spaces. Horizontal installation typically adds 1-2 hours to labor time.
Is 14 SEER1 cooling efficiency good enough for energy savings? +
14 SEER1 meets current ENERGY STAR minimums and qualifies for most state/utility rebates. Higher-SEER units (16-18) cost 30-50% more and save approximately 15-25% on cooling electricity. The payback period justifies upgrading only if cooling runs 4-6 months annually in your climate.
How does this Goodman system compare to Carrier or Lennox at similar price points? +
Goodman competes on efficiency and value without brand premium pricing. Carrier and Lennox equivalents cost $800-1,200 for identical capacity and AFUE ratings. This system delivers identical heating/cooling output at lower cost. Warranty and parts availability are equally strong across all three manufacturers.
What happens if I don't register the warranty online? +
Without registration, you retain the manufacturer's standard one-year warranty. The extended ten-year coverage only activates after online registration. Registration is free and takes approximately five minutes. Keep your proof of purchase and registration confirmation together.
