Review

The Goodman 3.5-ton horizontal gas furnace and air conditioner system occupies a specific but important niche in residential HVAC. Designed primarily for horizontal installation in attics, crawl spaces, and other confined locations, this unit combines serious heating muscle with practical space-saving engineering. At under 48 pounds and measuring just 21 by 21 by 26 inches, it solves installation problems that plague contractors working in homes without dedicated mechanical rooms or utility closets.

Performance numbers tell a compelling heating story. The 96% AFUE rating and 80,000 BTU output from a two-stage burner deliver efficient, responsive warmth in most climates. Cooling capabilities are honest but secondary, with 42,000 BTU and 15 SEER ratings reflecting its single-stage condenser design. The variable-speed ECM blower motor adjusts from near silence at part load to full 1,600 CFM operation, adapting intelligently to demand and reducing runtime energy consumption. Energy Star certification confirms these efficiency claims.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Exceptional 96% AFUE heating efficiency reduces natural gas bills significantly compared to 80-90% AFUE alternatives
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor automatically adjusts to home demand, improving comfort and reducing energy waste
  • Compact horizontal design fits tight spaces, attics, and crawl spaces where traditional vertical units won't work
  • Two-stage burner provides better temperature control and quieter operation during low-demand periods
  • Energy Star certified with 10-year warranty when registered, protecting investment against premature failure

Cons

  • Single-stage condenser limits cooling efficiency compared to two-stage units, capping cooling at 42,000 BTU despite 3.5-ton capacity
  • 73 dB noise level is moderate but noticeable, particularly during heating cycles in quiet homes
  • Requires plastic PVC exhaust flue, limiting installation flexibility in older homes with masonry chimneys

Who Should Buy

Best For

Contractors replacing furnaces in attics, crawl spaces, or tight mechanical rooms where standard vertical units are impossible to fit. Homeowners in moderate climates prioritizing heating efficiency over cooling capacity and willing to accept single-stage AC performance.

Consider Alternatives If

Buyers in hot climates needing maximum cooling output should look at two-stage condenser systems, and those without PVC exhaust infrastructure should explore alternatives compatible with masonry venting.

Installation

Installation hinges on your space constraints and existing infrastructure. The horizontal orientation and A-coil design fit readily into attics and crawl spaces where upflow furnaces cannot. You'll need proper ducting connections, 208/230V single-phase 60 Hz electrical service with 23.5 to 40 amp breaker sizing, and critically, a plastic PVC exhaust flue. Refrigerant lines come in standard 3/8 inch liquid and 1 1/8 inch suction sizes with recommended 1/2 inch insulation.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides a substantial 10-year warranty when you register the unit online, covering parts and labor against manufacturing defects. This extended protection is valuable for horizontal installations where accessibility sometimes adds service costs. Registration takes minutes and directly impacts your coverage period and claim experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can this unit handle both propane and natural gas? +
Yes, the furnace accepts both propane and natural gas interchangeably. Your installer will configure the burner jets and pressure settings for your fuel type during commissioning. Verify fuel availability in your area before purchase.
Will this fit in my attic if I have limited headroom? +
The 21-inch height makes horizontal installation practical in most attics, but measure your actual space including return plenum and ductwork. Some crawl spaces with 24-inch clearance work; others with 20-inch clearance will not. Account for service access.
Why is cooling capacity lower than typical 3.5-ton units? +
The single-stage condenser limits cooling to 42,000 BTU, roughly matching 3.5-ton capacity. If cooling is your priority, two-stage or three-stage condenser models deliver higher SEER ratings and better hot-climate performance.
How does this compare to Lennox or Carrier horizontal units? +
Goodman's 96% AFUE matches premium competitors, but carries lower cooling SEER and single-stage compressor design. This units cost less upfront, suiting budget-conscious contractors, while premium brands offer more options and faster service networks.
Do I need to register for the full warranty? +
Yes. The 10-year coverage requires online registration within 30 days of installation. Without registration, coverage defaults to shorter factory terms. Keep documentation for claim purposes.
