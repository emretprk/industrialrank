Review

The 2.5-ton Goodman gas furnace and air conditioner combo occupies the practical middle ground of residential HVAC. At $2,466, it delivers current-generation efficiency without flagship pricing, making it attractive for contractors specifying systems on competitive bids. The downflow configuration suits attic, closet, and utility room installations common in remodels and new construction where space and duct routing matter.

Performance specs read solidly across the board. The furnace achieves 96% AFUE with a two-stage burner and 60,000 BTU heating capacity, while the 2.5-ton condenser handles 30,000 BTU cooling at 13 SEER. The variable-speed ECM blower reaches 1200 CFM maximum and adapts output intelligently, reducing runtime noise and part-load electricity draw. Energy Star certification confirms these ratings meet EPA thresholds. Single-stage compressor design keeps costs down but sacrifices the modulation efficiency of pricier units.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 96% AFUE furnace efficiency means lower heating bills, particularly valuable in northern climates with long winter seasons
  • 13 SEER rating and Energy Star certification provide solid cooling efficiency for mid-range residential applications
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor adapts airflow from 0-1200 CFM, improving comfort and reducing energy waste during partial-load operation
  • Two-stage burner reduces short cycling and maintains steadier indoor temperatures compared to single-stage alternatives
  • PVC exhaust flue compatibility allows flexible ductwork routing, simplifying retrofit installations in existing homes
  • 10-year warranty with online registration covers compressor and parts, providing meaningful protection for the investment

Cons

  • Single-stage compressor limits cooling modulation, meaning it runs at full capacity until temperature setpoint is reached, less efficient than variable-capacity models
  • 73 dB noise level is moderately loud during operation, potentially noticeable in quiet rooms or open-concept layouts
  • R454B refrigerant requires technician certification and specialized charging equipment, increasing service costs compared to legacy refrigerants
  • 13 SEER is competent but not exceptional; newer systems reach 16-18 SEER, delivering meaningfully lower cooling costs over 15-year equipment life

Who Should Buy

Best For

Contractors and homeowners seeking a balanced heating and cooling system for moderate-sized homes in mixed climates, particularly those replacing 15+ year old equipment where the efficiency jump justifies ROI. This unit works well in downflow applications, attics, or closets where horizontal discharge improves installation flexibility.

Consider Alternatives If

Homeowners prioritizing lowest operating costs over budget should explore 16+ SEER systems, and those in hot climates with extended cooling seasons should consider variable-capacity compressors for better part-load efficiency.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the 96% AFUE rating compare to older furnaces in actual heating bills? +
A 20-year-old 78% AFUE unit wastes 22% of fuel as heat loss. This 96% AFUE system cuts that waste to 4%, delivering roughly 18 percentage point efficiency gain. On a $1,200 annual heating bill, expect $180-216 annual savings, recovering the system cost in 12-14 years through efficiency alone.
Is the downflow configuration suitable for my basement installation? +
Downflow works best in attics and upper-story applications where gravity aids conditioned air delivery. Basement installs typically require upflow models to push warm air upward against gravity. Confirm your specific layout with CAPT Installation Manual specifications before purchase to ensure proper system orientation and ductwork design.
What's the real-world cooling difference between this 13 SEER and a 16 SEER unit? +
13 SEER means 13 BTU cooling output per watt input over a season. A 16 SEER system delivers roughly 23% more efficiency. On a $500 annual cooling bill, that gap costs $115 yearly. Over 15 years, the premium for higher SEER pays back if electricity rates stay flat, but single-stage limits modulation benefit here.
How does this Goodman compare to Lennox or Trane equivalents at similar price points? +
Goodman emphasizes reliability over brand prestige. This unit matches Lennox merit series and Trane XR13 specs nearly identically, but Goodman pricing undercuts both by 15-20%. All three earn contractor respect. Choose Goodman for budget consciousness, Lennox for advanced controls, Trane for regional dealer support strength.
Does online registration for the 10-year warranty require annual steps or just one-time signup? +
Registration is one-time upon installation. Submit proof of purchase and installation date via Goodman's website to activate coverage. No annual renewals required. Keep documentation accessible; claims require proof of professional installation and model/serial verification.
