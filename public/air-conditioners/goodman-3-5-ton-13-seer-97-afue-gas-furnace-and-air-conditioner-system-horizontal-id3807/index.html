Review

The ACiQ 3.5 Ton Goodman Gas Furnace and Air Conditioner System combines efficient heating with flexible installation options in a horizontal configuration. This unit targets the mid-market contractor and homeowner segment, positioning itself as a reliable workhorse rather than a premium system. With a 120,000 BTU heating output and 42,000 BTU cooling capacity, it handles typical residential loads across moderate climates effectively. The horizontal orientation specifically solves installation challenges in attics and crawlspaces where traditional upflow models won't fit.

Performance metrics reveal a genuinely efficient heating system. The 97% AFUE furnace rating means nearly all fuel converts to usable heat, translating to real money saved on natural gas or propane bills. The modulating burner adjusts flame intensity automatically, preventing the energy waste and comfort dips caused by full-on-or-off cycling in single-stage units. A variable-speed ECM blower runs at lower speeds when full capacity isn't needed, reducing electrical consumption. Cooling efficiency at 13 SEER is respectable but middle-tier compared to 15+ SEER competitors, making this better for heating-focused regions.

Installation

Installation requires 208/230V single-phase 60Hz power with breaker size between 23.9 and 40 amps. The unit weighs 120 pounds and measures 24.5 inches high by 26 inches wide by 21.125 inches deep, fitting compact spaces. PVC plastic exhaust flue is required, which is standard in most homes. Refrigerant lines use 3/8 inch liquid and 7/8 inch suction, with 3/8 inch insulation thickness on the suction side.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this system with a 10-year parts warranty when registered online, protecting against manufacturing defects. This is solid mid-tier coverage, though premium brands sometimes offer 12-year warranties. Online registration is mandatory to activate the full term, so documentation matters. Coverage includes the compressor, which is the most expensive component to replace.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the actual difference between 97% AFUE and standard 80% AFUE furnaces? +
The 97% unit converts 97 cents of every dollar spent on fuel to heat, versus 80 cents on older models. On an average $1,200 annual heating bill, the 97% model saves roughly $200 yearly. Over a 15-year lifespan, that's $3,000 in fuel savings before accounting for rising energy costs.
Can this horizontal unit be installed in a standard upflow cabinet? +
No. The horizontal configuration is fixed. If you need upflow capability, choose a standard vertical furnace. However, horizontal orientation excels in attics, crawlspaces, and utility closets where vertical units simply don't fit or create ducting complications.
How does the modulating burner improve efficiency and comfort? +
Single-stage burners run at full capacity until reaching set temperature, then shut off completely. Modulating burners adjust flame intensity continuously, matching heat demand precisely. This reduces on-off cycling stress on components, eliminates temperature swings, and cuts fuel waste significantly.
Is 13 SEER cooling good enough for my home? +
13 SEER meets minimum standards and works fine in cold and moderate climates where cooling isn't heavily used. In hot southern climates, units with 15+ SEER provide better efficiency and lower operating costs. Calculate your region's cooling degree days to assess true value.
Do I need online registration to keep the 10-year warranty? +
Yes. Registration is mandatory to activate full 10-year coverage. Without it, you may lose warranty protection. Register immediately after installation with your AHRI certificate number 7424268 and proof of purchase.
