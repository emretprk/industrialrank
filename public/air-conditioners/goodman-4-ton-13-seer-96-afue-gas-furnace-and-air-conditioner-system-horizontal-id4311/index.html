Review

The ACiQ 4-ton horizontal furnace and air conditioner system occupies a practical middle ground in the HVAC market. This combo unit delivers 120,000 BTU heating capacity with a 48,000 BTU cooling coil, sized appropriately for mid-range residential applications. At $0 pricing (rebate available), it targets cost-conscious contractors and homeowners who need solid performance without premium equipment costs. The horizontal orientation and compact 24.5 by 26 by 21.125 inch footprint make it ideal for tight installation spaces.

Efficiency numbers tell the real story here. The 96% AFUE furnace rating means minimal heat loss during the heating season, significantly outperforming 80-90% equipment still common in older homes. The variable-speed ECM blower motor runs at up to 2000 CFM and adjusts automatically to match heating and cooling demands, reducing runtime energy consumption. On the cooling side, 13 SEER1 efficiency is respectable mid-tier performance. Two-stage burner operation allows the furnace to modulate during partial-load conditions, improving comfort and efficiency during shoulder seasons.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 96% AFUE furnace efficiency reduces heating costs significantly compared to 80-90% models
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor optimizes airflow and minimizes energy waste at 2000 CFM maximum
  • Horizontal A-coil design fits attics and crawlspaces where upflow units cannot
  • Two-stage burner provides better part-load heating efficiency during mild weather

Cons

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does this use R454B refrigerant instead of R410A? +
R454B is the EPA-approved replacement for R410A, with lower global warming potential. It performs similarly to R410A in most applications, though it requires careful handling during service. Fewer technicians are deeply familiar with it yet, so verify your service provider's training before purchasing.
Can I install this in an attic with limited headroom? +
Yes, the horizontal configuration is designed specifically for this. At 24.5 inches tall and 26 inches wide, it fits most attic spaces. Ensure adequate return and supply ductwork clearance, and verify your attic can support the 120-pound weight on floor joists or blocking.
How does 13 SEER compare to newer units I've seen advertised? +
13 SEER is respectable mid-tier cooling efficiency. Newer high-end systems reach 16-20+ SEER through dual-stage compressors and variable capacity. You'll use more cooling energy with 13 SEER, but the cost difference versus premium units often exceeds the 10-year energy savings.
What models does this replace from Goodman? +
ACiQ lists this as a replacement for GSX130483, GSX130484, and GSX130485 Goodman condenser units. Cross-reference your existing equipment before ordering to ensure compatibility with your furnace and evaporator coil.
Do I need online registration to get the 10-year warranty? +
Yes, ACiQ requires online registration to activate the full 10-year coverage. Without registration, coverage may be limited. Register immediately after installation to protect your investment.
