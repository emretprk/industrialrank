// Bottom Line
This Goodman system works well for homeowners in mixed climates who want solid heating and cooling efficiency without premium pricing. The 96% AFUE furnace and 14 SEER air conditioner deliver measurable energy savings, especially in regions with moderate heating and cooling seasons.

Review

The Goodman 4 Ton 14 SEER 96% AFUE combination system is a workhorse offering legitimate efficiency credentials at accessible pricing. Goodman occupies a practical middle ground in the HVAC market, respected by contractors for reliability without the premium tag of high-end brands. This upflow A-coil configuration suits most residential installations, particularly replacement projects where existing ductwork already supports horizontal air delivery.

Performance metrics show real capability. The 96% AFUE rating means 96 cents of every heating dollar actually heats your home, with only 4% lost through the exhaust. Cooling output reaches 48,000 BTU at 14 SEER efficiency, delivering solid seasonal performance. The scroll compressor and multi-speed blower provide flexibility, while the single-stage design keeps mechanical complexity low. Maximum airflow of 2,000 CFM handles proper air distribution across typical residential square footage.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 96% AFUE furnace efficiency means minimal heating fuel waste, translating to real savings on winter bills
  • 14 SEER cooling rating offers solid air conditioning performance, above the 13 SEER baseline for modern systems
  • 80,000 BTU heating output handles most residential applications in temperate to cold climates
  • Multi-speed blower motor provides flexibility for different comfort and efficiency settings
  • PVC/plastic flue compatibility simplifies venting requirements and reduces installation complexity
  • 10-year warranty with online registration provides reasonable coverage for furnace and AC components

Cons

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this system use R410A refrigerant or the older R22? +
The specs indicate compatibility with both R410A and R22, but R410A is the modern standard. Verify with your installer which refrigerant the specific unit contains. R410A is non-ozone-depleting and preferred by EPA regulations. Most new units ship with R410A, but clarify before purchase.
Can this upflow unit work in a basement or attic installation? +
Yes. The upflow/horizontal A-coil configuration works in both scenarios. Basement placement suits gravity return paths, while attic mounting requires proper structural support for the 30-pound weight. Ensure adequate clearance for the 34.5-inch height and proper return air ducting. Consult local building codes for attic placement.
How does 14 SEER compare to other systems available today? +
14 SEER represents solid mid-tier efficiency. Baseline modern units meet 13 SEER; premium systems reach 16-18 SEER with variable-speed compressors. This unit offers better-than-minimum performance without the 20-30% price premium of high-efficiency models. For moderate climates, 14 SEER delivers excellent payback.
Is the 5-ton cooling capacity or 4-ton capacity the correct specification? +
The specs show conflicting data. The title states 4 tons while the specification sheet lists 5 tons and 48,000 BTU capacity. Contact the seller for clarification. This discrepancy should be resolved before purchase to ensure proper load matching with your home's requirements.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Coverage includes furnace and AC components against manufacturing defects when online-registered. Typical exclusions are labor, refrigerant, normal wear, and installation errors. Extended warranty periods make this attractive, but maintenance records matter for claims. Read the full warranty document before installation.
