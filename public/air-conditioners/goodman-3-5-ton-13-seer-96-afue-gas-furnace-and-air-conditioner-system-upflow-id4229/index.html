Review

The ACiQ 3.5 Ton Goodman gas furnace and air conditioner system represents solid mid-market equipment that balances heating and cooling capability without premium pricing. This upflow configuration suits single-story homes and standard attic installations where ductwork runs above living spaces. The 120,000 BTU heating capacity and 42,000 BTU cooling output deliver adequate capacity for most residential applications in zones 4 through 6. ACiQ positions this unit as a value-oriented alternative to higher-cost premium brands while maintaining Goodman's reputation for equipment longevity.

Performance specs reveal competent engineering across both functions. The 96% AFUE furnace rating means only 4% of fuel energy escapes as waste, delivering strong winter efficiency and qualifying for many regional utility rebates targeting heating upgrades. The 13 SEER air conditioner rating sits comfortably in the mid-range, providing meaningful savings over older 10 SEER units while stopping short of high-efficiency 15+ SEER models. Two burner stages and an ECM blower motor allow the system to modulate output rather than cycling on and off constantly, improving comfort and reducing energy consumption during partial-load operation.

Installation

This system requires standard residential HVAC installation with PVC plastic venting, simplifying retrofit projects in homes with modern exhaust systems. The upflow configuration demands 30 inches of vertical height clearance and fits most utility closets or basement mechanical rooms measuring 24.5 inches wide by 21 inches deep. Electrical requires 208/230 volt, single-phase 60 Hz service with breaker sizing between 23.9 and 40 amps, standard for most homes. Refrigerant lines use 3/8 inch liquid and 7/8 inch suction ports with 3/8 inch insulation thickness.

Warranty & Reliability

ACiQ backs this system with a 10-year warranty covering parts and labor when registered online within 30 days of installation. This protection ranks above the industry minimum but trails extended warranties offered by premium manufacturers. Registration is straightforward and essential, as unregistered units receive shorter coverage periods. The warranty covers compressor, heat exchanger, and coil defects, providing meaningful protection during the critical early ownership years.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 96% AFUE compare to standard furnaces? +
96% AFUE means the furnace converts 96 cents of every energy dollar into heat, with only 4 cents lost through the exhaust flue. Most homes replace older 78-80% AFUE units, so this upgrade saves 15-20% on annual heating costs. High-efficiency condensing furnaces reach 95-98.5% AFUE, placing this unit at the excellent tier.
Is this system compatible with my existing ductwork? +
Compatibility depends on your current setup. The upflow design suits homes with basement or closet furnace placement and ducting routed upward. If you have downflow or horizontal installation requirements, ACiQ offers downflow configurations. Have an HVAC contractor evaluate your current duct sizing and layout before purchase.
How much will the 13 SEER rating reduce my cooling costs? +
13 SEER systems use roughly 15-20% less cooling energy than 10 SEER units under identical conditions. Actual savings depend on your climate, thermostat settings, and home insulation. In hot climates, homeowners typically recover the efficiency premium within 5-7 years through lower electric bills.
Should I choose this over a higher SEER model? +
That depends on budget and climate. For moderate climates, 13 SEER delivers solid value. Hot climates benefit more from 15+ SEER systems. Compare total installed cost against expected energy savings in your area. This unit excels as a balanced option, not as a maximum-efficiency choice.
What happens to my warranty if I don't register? +
Unregistered units receive reduced warranty coverage compared to the full 10-year protection. Register online within 30 days of installation to activate complete coverage. Keep your receipt and registration confirmation for warranty claims.
