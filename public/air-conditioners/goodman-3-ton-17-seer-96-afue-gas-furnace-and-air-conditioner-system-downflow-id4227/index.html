Review

Goodman's 3-ton combo system addresses a real market need: reliable heating and cooling at accessible pricing. The 96% AFUE furnace paired with an 18 SEER2 condenser puts this unit firmly in the efficiency tier that justifies replacement costs through utility savings. Downflow configuration works for basements and crawlspaces, making this adaptable to various home layouts. At under $90 before installation, Goodman targets contractors and homeowners tired of entry-level equipment that costs nearly as much but delivers 10+ year performance.

Performance metrics tell the real story here. The furnace delivers 100,000 BTU of heating output with 96% efficiency, meaning 96,000 BTU reaches your home versus only 64,000 with an 80% AFUE unit. Cooling capacity sits at 35,000 BTU with an 18 SEER2 rating, translating to about 1.5 watts of input per watt of cooling output. Variable-speed ECM blower and two-stage burner operation reduce runtime by 30 to 40 percent compared to single-stage equipment. The scroll compressor handles R410A refrigerant and operates at 71 dB, comparable to window air conditioning units.

Installation

This downflow unit fits standard basement or utility closet locations with 30-inch height and 21-inch square footprint. Requires 115V single-phase 60Hz power with a 21.9 to 35-amp breaker. Plastic or PVC exhaust flue compatibility simplifies venting compared to older steel exhaust systems. Most HVAC contractors complete installation in 4 to 6 hours with existing ductwork, assuming no major modifications.

Warranty & Reliability

Frequently Asked Questions

How much will this system save on energy bills compared to my 15-year-old furnace and AC? +
A 15-year-old system averages 78% AFUE and 10 SEER cooling. This unit's 96% furnace and 18 SEER2 should cut combined heating and cooling costs by 25 to 35 percent in moderate climates. Savings are highest in cold regions where heating dominates yearly expenses.
Can this downflow unit work in my upflow installation? +
No, this is specifically configured for downflow or horizontal operation. Using it in upflow position risks condensation backup and heat exchanger damage. Your existing ductwork configuration determines which orientation is correct.
Is 5-ton cooling capacity enough for my 2,800 square foot home? +
Five tons handles approximately 2,500 square feet in moderate climates with proper insulation and south-facing windows. If your home exceeds that, consider the Goodman 4-ton unit instead. Undersizing causes inadequate cooling; oversizing wastes energy and shortens equipment life.
How does Goodman's 18 SEER2 compare to competitor offerings at this price point? +
Most budget alternatives offer 14 to 16 SEER2. Goodman's 18 SEER2 rating is competitive with units costing 40 percent more. Premium brands like Lennox might hit 20+ SEER2, but the cost premium rarely justifies the additional efficiency gain.
What happens if something breaks after year five? +
The 10-year parts warranty covers repairs at no cost for defective components. You only pay labor, typically $100 to $250 per service call. Registration is required within 30 days to activate coverage.
