Review

Goodman's 2.5-ton horizontal system combines gas heating and air conditioning in one package, marketed as a complete solution for homeowners upgrading from aging split systems. At $115, this unit sits in the affordable-to-mid-range for combination systems. The horizontal configuration works best in attics or utility closets with limited headroom. Goodman maintains solid market presence among contractors due to straightforward design and parts availability, though they're not premium-tier like Lennox or Carrier.

Heating delivers 80,000 BTU through a modulating burner that continuously adjusts flame intensity, achieving 97% AFUE. Cooling pulls 29,000 BTU at 16 SEER1 with R410A refrigerant and a scroll compressor. The variable-speed ECM blower reaches 1,200 CFM maximum and runs at 71.5 dBA, noticeably quieter than fixed-speed alternatives. Electrical draw sits between 17.1 and 25 amps at 115V single phase, simplifying residential wiring. ENERGY STAR certification confirms real-world efficiency gains compared to baseline models.

Installation

Horizontal A-coil configuration requires floor or attic mounting with clearance for the 34.5-inch height and 28.75-inch depth. Suction line uses 7/8-inch piping with 3/4-inch insulation, while liquid line is 3/8-inch. Plastic exhaust venting exits right-side flue location. Installation demands proper refrigerant recovery, brazing for line connections, and electrical work within 17.1 to 25-amp breaker limits. Most contractors complete this in 4 to 6 hours for straightforward replacements.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides 10 years of parts coverage when you register online after purchase. This covers compressor, heat exchanger, and blower motor failure. Labor costs remain your responsibility, making extended service plans worthwhile in years 6 through 10. Registration typically takes minutes through Goodman's website using serial number and proof of purchase.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this unit right for my 2,500 square foot home? +
The 3-ton capacity handles approximately 1,800 to 2,100 square feet comfortably in moderate climates. Homes larger than 2,200 square feet in hot regions will struggle to reach set temperatures. Get a manual J load calculation from your contractor before ordering to confirm proper sizing.
Can I install this myself if I'm handy? +
Absolutely not. This requires EPA-certified technician work for refrigerant handling, plus gas line connections demand licensed plumbers or HVAC pros in most states. Improper installation voids the 10-year warranty and creates safety hazards. Budget $1,500 to $2,500 for professional installation.
How much will this save me compared to my 15-year-old system? +
The 97% AFUE furnace saves roughly 15 to 20 percent on heating costs versus 80% AFUE models. The 16 SEER1 air conditioning saves 20 to 25 percent on cooling compared to 10 SEER units. Actual savings depend on your local energy rates and climate.
Why does this have 16 SEER1 when the title says 15 SEER? +
Title wording reflects older SEER ratings before the SEER2 standard. This unit actually achieves 16 SEER1 according to specs, which is above typical 15 SEER baseline. Always verify actual spec sheets rather than relying on product titles alone.
What if the furnace fails after 10 years? +
Warranty expires at year 10, leaving you responsible for all repairs. After a decade, replacement typically costs $3,000 to $5,000 installed. Goodman units often last 15 to 20 years with maintenance, so replacement won't be immediate.
