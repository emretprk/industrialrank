Review

The Goodman 3-ton gas furnace and air conditioner system represents the pragmatic middle ground in residential HVAC replacement. This upflow-configured unit pairs an 80,000 BTU furnace with a 34,800 BTU cooling condenser, creating a complete solution for homes averaging 1200 to 1800 square feet. Online Supply positions this equipment as the sensible choice for contractors and homeowners balancing performance, durability, and budget constraints in today's competitive market.

Performance specs reveal a system engineered for reliable operation rather than cutting-edge efficiency. The furnace delivers 80% AFUE with two-stage burner control, while the outdoor condenser achieves 16 SEER2 rating with a scroll compressor and single-stage operation. The high-efficiency multi-speed blower reaches 2000 CFM maximum airflow, supporting proper distribution across ductwork. These numbers confirm Energy Star certification but indicate this equipment targets contractors managing replacement budgets rather than efficiency-first applications.

Installation

Installation requires standard 120V single-phase 60Hz electrical with breaker sizing between 18.7 and 30 amps, fitting most residential panels without upgrades. The unit accepts R410A or R22 refrigerant, with 3/8 inch liquid and 7/8 inch suction line connections featuring 3/4 inch insulation rated to 50 feet. Metal exhaust flue is required for the two-stage gas furnace operation. Upflow configuration suits most basements and utility closets.

Warranty & Reliability

Coverage spans 10 years on compressor, coil, and parts when registered online following purchase. This standard commercial warranty reflects Goodman's reputation for durable equipment but lacks the extended protections offered by premium manufacturers. Registration is straightforward and strongly recommended to activate full coverage from the purchase date.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the 16 SEER2 rating compare to other mid-range systems? +
At 16 SEER2, this condenser outperforms the stated 15 SEER specification and sits solidly in the efficient-but-practical category. High-end systems reach 20-22 SEER2, but this unit's scroll compressor design balances efficiency with proven reliability, making it competitive for replacement applications.
Is the 80% AFUE furnace adequate for northern climates? +
For heating-heavy regions, 80% AFUE generates substantially higher annual fuel bills than 95%+ condensing models. If you experience consistent winters below 20 degrees Fahrenheit, upgrading to a high-efficiency condenser furnace could recover costs within 8-10 years through reduced heating cycles.
What does the two-stage burner accomplish? +
Two-stage burner modulation reduces on-off cycling by running at reduced capacity during mild outdoor temperatures, cutting gas consumption and improving comfort consistency. This feature particularly benefits partial heating loads during spring and fall shoulder seasons.
Can this system work with existing ductwork from my old unit? +
Yes, provided your ducts are sized appropriately for the 2000 CFM maximum airflow. Existing R22 systems can transition to this equipment, though many contractors recommend ductwork evaluation to ensure proper CFM delivery and static pressure handling.
Does online registration really matter for warranty coverage? +
Yes. Online registration is mandatory to activate the full 10-year coverage period. Without registration, coverage typically defaults to one year, making this simple step essential for protecting your investment.
