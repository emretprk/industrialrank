Review

The Goodman GSX140481 represents the practical middle ground of residential air conditioning condensers. At $1,720, it occupies the sweet spot between budget equipment and premium systems, making it a favorite spec choice for contractors managing customer budgets. This 4-ton, single-stage condenser uses R410A refrigerant and houses a scroll compressor, delivering 48,000 BTU of cooling capacity. It's built for standard residential installations where straightforward performance and proven reliability matter more than cutting-edge features.

The 14 SEER efficiency rating is respectable without being exceptional, placing this unit ahead of older standards but behind high-efficiency alternatives that reach 16 to 18 SEER. The scroll compressor is the workhorse of mid-range equipment, offering durability with lower maintenance than reciprocating designs. Electrical requirements are modest at 208/230V single-phase 60Hz, requiring breakers between 26.4 and 45 amps. The 74 dB noise output is typical for this capacity class but worth noting if the installation site borders noise-sensitive areas.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 14 SEER rating provides good efficiency for the price point, delivering 48,000 BTU of cooling capacity
  • Scroll compressor design is proven reliable with fewer moving parts than reciprocating alternatives
  • 10-year warranty with online registration offers solid coverage for parts and labor peace of mind
  • Single-stage operation simplifies controls and reduces maintenance complexity on installation
  • Compact footprint at 35.5 inches wide and deep fits most standard condenser pads without modifications

Cons

  • 74 dB noise level is notably loud, comparable to heavy traffic, which may concern noise-sensitive homeowners
  • Not Energy Star certified, meaning it misses out on potential utility rebates available in many regions
  • Single-stage operation cannot modulate capacity, leading to less efficient part-load performance in mild weather

Who Should Buy

Best For

Contractors performing straightforward residential replacements in temperate climates where budget constraints are real will appreciate this unit's value. Homeowners in 4-ton applications who prioritize reasonable efficiency and reliability over premium features will find solid performance here.

Consider Alternatives If

Skip this model if you need Energy Star rebates, live in a noise-sensitive area, or want the variable capacity that two-stage condensers provide for improved efficiency during mild seasons.

Installation

Installation is straightforward for experienced technicians. The unit requires standard 3/8 inch liquid line and 7/8 inch suction line connections, matching common residential lineset sizes. Weighing 242 pounds, it needs two people or light equipment to position on the pad. The square footprint (35.5 by 35.5 inches) fits most existing condenser pads without concrete work, and the 36.25 inch height accommodates typical clearance requirements.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year parts and labor warranty, activated through online registration, provides meaningful coverage in the mid-range segment. This matches or exceeds many competitor offerings at this price level. Registration is essential, as coverage defaults to a shorter period without it. The warranty covers compressor failure, which is the costliest potential issue, giving buyers solid financial protection.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 14 SEER compare to newer efficiency standards? +
14 SEER is solid but not cutting-edge. Newer systems reach 16 to 18 SEER, potentially saving 15 to 25 percent on cooling costs. However, 14 SEER still exceeds older 10 to 12 SEER equipment significantly, making this a meaningful upgrade for replacements. The real-world difference depends on climate and usage patterns.
Will this condenser work with my existing indoor unit? +
Yes, if your indoor coil is designed for R410A and matched to 4-ton capacity. Check your current system documentation. Most installs require a complete system replacement rather than condenser-only swaps due to refrigerant type and efficiency rating mismatches. Consult your contractor before purchasing.
Is the 74 dB noise level a problem? +
For most residential settings, 74 dB is acceptable. It's louder than conversation but quieter than a lawnmower. If the unit sits near a bedroom window or near neighbors, consider requesting a quiet-rated alternative. Noise-sensitive installations warrant the premium for variable-speed condenser fans.
Why isn't this Energy Star certified? +
Energy Star requires 15 SEER minimum, and this unit rates 14 SEER. While one SEER point seems minor, it represents real efficiency gains. Missing Energy Star means no utility rebates in many markets, potentially costing buyers $200 to $500 in incentives they'd otherwise qualify for.
Is the 10-year warranty transferable? +
Goodman's standard warranty transfers to second owners with registration, which is a plus for resale value. However, you'll need proof of purchase and original registration. Check the specific warranty document, as conditions and coverage may vary slightly between original and subsequent owners.
