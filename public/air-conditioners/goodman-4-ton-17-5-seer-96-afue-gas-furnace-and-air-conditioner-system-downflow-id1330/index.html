Review

The Goodman CAPT4961D4 represents a solid mid-market choice in the combined furnace-AC market. Priced at $3136, it targets homeowners seeking meaningful efficiency upgrades without high-end system costs. This downflow configuration suits basements and crawlspaces, making it flexible for various home layouts. Goodman maintains strong contractor relationships and reasonable availability across most regions, supporting its market position as a reliable workhorse system.

Performance specs impress for the price point. The furnace delivers 120,000 BTU output at 96% AFUE, meaning nearly all fuel converts to usable heat. The variable-speed ECM blower handles airflow up to 2000 CFM, optimizing comfort across heating and cooling seasons. Two-stage operation on both furnace and cooler reduces short-cycling, extending component life and stabilizing indoor temperatures.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does 96% AFUE actually mean for my heating bills? +
AFUE measures how much fuel energy becomes heat delivered to your home. At 96%, nearly all natural gas or propane converts to heating. Upgrading from an 80% AFUE system saves roughly 20% on heating costs, translating to $300-500 annually depending on climate and fuel prices. Over 15 years, this compounds to significant savings.
Can I install this downflow system myself? +
No. Licensed HVAC technicians should handle installation because it involves gas connections, electrical work, refrigerant handling, and permit compliance. DIY installation voids warranty, creates safety hazards, and violates most building codes. Professional installation costs $1500-2500 typically, but ensures proper operation and longevity.
How does 17.5 SEER compare to newer high-efficiency systems? +
17.5 SEER is strong but not bleeding-edge. Premium systems reach 18-20 SEER at higher cost. For moderate climates with 800-1200 cooling hours annually, the efficiency gap translates to $50-100 yearly difference. This unit balances real efficiency gains against reasonable price increase over 14-15 SEER baseline models.
Is Goodman reliable compared to Carrier or Lennox? +
Goodman maintains solid reliability ratings around 92-95% mean-time-between-failures. Carrier and Lennox average slightly higher at 94-96%, but differences are marginal. Goodman's strength lies in value and parts availability through independent contractors. Professional installation and maintenance matter more than brand for long-term performance.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Parts only. Covers compressor, heat exchanger, motors, and electronics failures. Does not cover installation labor, refrigerant, or damage from improper maintenance. Online registration activates coverage immediately. Some dealers offer extended labor warranties separately for additional cost.
