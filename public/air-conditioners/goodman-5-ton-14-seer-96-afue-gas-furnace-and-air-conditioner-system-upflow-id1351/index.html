Review

The ACiQ 5 Ton system bridges the gap between budget and high-end HVAC equipment. This furnace and air conditioner combo delivers 120,000 BTU heating capacity and 60,000 BTU cooling in a compact 5 ton package. It's branded as Goodman but sold under ACiQ's label, offering OEM quality at direct pricing. The upflow A-coil configuration fits traditional attic and basement installations where space planning favors vertical equipment.

The 96% AFUE furnace converts nearly all natural gas to usable heat, ranking in the high-efficiency category without requiring condensing drain management beyond PVC piping. The 14 SEER cooling unit pairs a scroll compressor with single-stage operation and R454B refrigerant, delivering consistent performance across moderate temperature swings. Two-stage burner control and multi-speed ECM blower operation minimize on-off cycling and maintain comfort during partial-load operation. Maximum CFM reaches 2,000, supporting typical duct systems in 2,000 to 3,500 square foot homes.

Who Should Buy

Homeowners replacing 15+ year old systems who value proven efficiency and want a balanced furnace/AC package without spending on premium variable-capacity equipment. Contractors serving moderate climate zones will appreciate the straightforward two-stage design and PVC venting compatibility.

Installation

Installation requires 208/230V single-phase 60 Hz power with 32.8 to 50 amp breaker sizing. PVC exhaust flue vents right-side from the furnace, simplifying routing compared to metal vent requirements. Standard refrigerant lineset uses 3/8 inch liquid and 7/8 inch suction lines with 3/8 inch insulation thickness, requiring 30 feet maximum run length. Most installers complete setup in 4 to 6 hours for replacement applications.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year parts warranty applies with online registration and covers both the furnace and Goodman evaporator coil. This extended protection is solid for the price point but requires registration within 30 days of purchase. Labor warranty depends on your HVAC contractor's service agreements, not the manufacturer. ACiQ provides documentation through PDF resources requiring Adobe Reader.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this system qualify for federal HVAC tax credits or rebates? +
The lack of ENERGY STAR certification may disqualify it from federal incentives, though state or utility rebates sometimes apply to 96% AFUE furnaces and 14 SEER AC units separately. Check with your local utility first. Specific eligibility varies by location and changes annually.
Can this furnace operate in downflow configuration for basement installations? +
Yes, the specs list configuration as upflow and downflow capable. However, the flue location defaults to right-side, so confirm flue routing feasibility before installation. Your contractor should verify ductwork configuration during the estimate.
How does 14 SEER compare to current market efficiency standards? +
14 SEER falls between mid-range (13-14 SEER) and premium (16-20 SEER) equipment. It provides meaningful energy savings over older 10-12 SEER systems while costing significantly less than variable-capacity alternatives. Expect 20-30% cooling cost reduction compared to 2005-era air conditioners.
What size home does a 5 ton system properly cool and heat? +
Five tons typically serves 2,000 to 3,500 square feet depending on insulation, climate zone, and window efficiency. Oversizing beyond your actual load wastes money on equipment and energy. Your contractor should perform load calculations using ACCA Manual J sizing methods.
Is online registration required for the 10-year warranty to be valid? +
Yes, ACiQ requires online registration within 30 days of purchase to activate the 10-year warranty. Without registration, coverage may default to a shorter period. Keep your receipt and complete registration immediately after installation.
