This Goodman 5-ton system is ideal for homeowners replacing an aging furnace and AC unit without breaking the bank. The 80% AFUE heating and 15 SEER cooling efficiency put it solidly in the practical sweet spot for mid-range residential applications.

Review

The Goodman CAPF4860D6 represents a practical workhorse in the mid-tier HVAC market. This 5-ton combined gas furnace and air conditioning system hits the price-to-performance ratio that appeals to cost-conscious homeowners and contractors alike. At $2,071, it's competitive against comparable Lennox and Carrier units without demanding premium dollars. The upflow and downflow flexibility means installers can adapt it to varied home configurations without expensive retrofitting.

Performance metrics show competent rather than cutting-edge efficiency. The 80% AFUE heating rating delivers 80,000 BTU output, adequate for most residential spaces but trailing condensing furnaces that reach 95%+ efficiency. Cooling capacity hits 60,000 BTU with a 15 SEER rating and two-stage condenser operation. The variable speed blower motor at 2,000 maximum CFM adjusts output to minimize runtime and reduce utility costs. AHRI certificate number 204633307 confirms tested compatibility, and Energy Star qualification enables utility rebate eligibility in many areas.

Installation

This upflow system requires minimal ductwork modifications in standard applications. The 30-inch height, 24.5-inch width, and 21-inch depth fit standard utility closets without special framing. Metal exhaust flue is mandatory for safe combustion venting. Electrical demand ranges from 37.2 to 60 amps, potentially requiring circuit upgrades in older homes. Refrigerant lines use standard 3/8-inch and 7/8-inch sweat connections, familiar to most technicians.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman's 10-year parts warranty applies upon online registration after purchase. This covers compressor, coil, and component failures but typically excludes refrigerant and labor costs. Registration is straightforward and protects your investment significantly, though savvy buyers should verify coverage specifics with their installer to understand what repairs qualify.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does this system perform in cold climates? +
The 80% AFUE rating means 20% of heating energy is lost, making it less efficient than condensing furnaces in sustained freezing temperatures. Northern buyers operating systems 6+ months annually should budget higher utility costs compared to 95%+ AFUE alternatives. This system suits moderate climates better than deep cold regions.
Can I install this myself, or do I need a professional? +
Professional installation is essential. Improper refrigerant charging, electrical connections, or gas line setup creates safety hazards and voids manufacturer warranties. Most HVAC licenses require certified installation for warranty validity and local code compliance.
What's the real-world cooling difference between 15 SEER and 17 SEER? +
A 17 SEER unit runs approximately 12-15% more efficiently than this 15 SEER model. Over a 10-year lifespan in a moderate climate, that translates to roughly $400-600 in energy savings. The premium paid upfront usually recovers within 5-7 years in cooling-heavy regions.
How does this Goodman compare to Lennox or Carrier equivalent models? +
Goodman offers bottom-tier pricing with solid reliability. Lennox and Carrier systems at this capacity cost $2,800-3,500 but deliver 16-17 SEER efficiency and quieter operation. Goodman excels for budget replacements, while premium brands justify costs through durability and comfort features.
What happens after the 10-year warranty expires? +
You're responsible for all repairs and replacement parts. Service calls typically cost $150-300. Extended service plans are sometimes available through your installer, though most homeowners simply pay as needed after warranty expiration.
