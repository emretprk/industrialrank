This ACiQ unit is ideal for homeowners replacing older systems who want meaningful efficiency gains without premium pricing. The 92% AFUE and 14 SEER combination delivers real energy savings while the multi-speed blower ensures comfort in most climates.

Review

The ACiQ CAPF furnace and coil combo occupies the practical middle ground of residential HVAC equipment. It's not a budget basement unit, nor is it a premium variable-capacity system. Instead, it targets contractors and homeowners who understand that 92% AFUE and 14 SEER represent genuine efficiency improvements over 1990s and 2000s equipment, with simpler controls and fewer failure points than advanced designs. ACiQ positions itself as the accessible choice for straightforward residential replacements.

Performance specs reflect this philosophy. The furnace delivers 42,000 BTU heating with 92% efficiency, while the 5-ton coil paired with a scroll compressor produces 41,000 BTU cooling at 14 SEER. The multi-speed blower varies airflow up to 1,600 CFM, matching house loads better than fixed-speed motors. Electrical demand sits reasonable at 208/230V single-phase, requiring a 24 to 40-amp breaker depending on installation specifics. This configuration runs quieter than basic units but louder at 74 dB than premium alternatives.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 92% AFUE furnace efficiency exceeds federal minimums by 2%, reducing heating costs meaningfully over 15+ years
  • 14 SEER cooling efficiency qualifies for ENERGY STAR, supporting lower monthly electric bills year-round
  • Multi-speed blower motor adapts to heating and cooling demands, improving comfort versus fixed-speed alternatives
  • Compact 21x21x30 inch footprint fits tight basement or attic spaces that limit installation options
  • PVC exhaust allows easier venting through exterior walls without masonry chimneys

Cons

  • 74 dB noise level runs louder than premium units, noticeable in open floor plans or quiet zones
  • 5-ton capacity exceeds stated 3.5-ton system rating, creating potential matching complications with outdoor condensers

Installation

Installation favors experienced technicians. The upflow configuration suits basements and attics, while the A-coil style integrates with most ductwork. Plastic PVC exhaust venting eliminates chimney requirements, opening retrofit options. Sweat-connection refrigerant ports demand proper brazing skills. The 1 1/2 inch suction line and 3/8 inch liquid line require correct sizing and 1/2 inch foam insulation to prevent efficiency loss and compressor damage.

Warranty & Reliability

ACiQ provides a 10-year comprehensive warranty when you register online, covering parts and defects. This ranks above entry-level equipment but below premium brands offering 12+ years. Registration is mandatory, so factor that into customer communication. Parts availability through established HVAC supply chains supports long-term service.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet list 5 tons when this is marketed as 3.5 tons? +
The coil is rated at 5-ton capacity for flexibility pairing with various outdoor units. When matched with a proper 3.5-ton condenser, the system operates at its intended 41,000 BTU cooling output. Verify condenser tonnage matches your furnace selection during installation.
Can this furnace retrofit into my existing ductwork from a 1995 unit? +
Likely yes, if ductwork handles 1,600 CFM without excessive noise. The compact 21x21x30 inch dimensions fit most mechanical spaces. However, older ducts often leak significantly, so sealing them improves efficiency gains from the new 92% AFUE furnace substantially.
How does 14 SEER compare to modern standards in 2024? +
14 SEER meets current federal minimum standards for most US regions and qualifies for ENERGY STAR. It's respectable but not cutting-edge, two to three SEER points below premium variable-capacity systems that reach 18+ SEER in ideal conditions.
Is ACiQ a reliable brand, or should I choose Goodman or Lennox instead? +
ACiQ is HVAC Holdings' budget brand, sharing component suppliers with Goodman but with simpler designs and lower price points. Reliability is comparable across brands when properly installed. Choose ACiQ for value, Lennox for premium features, Goodman for middle ground.
Does online registration for the warranty actually work? +
Yes, HVAC supply distributors handle registration routinely. Keep your receipt and serial number, then register through ACiQ's website or have your contractor manage it. Warranty claims process smoothly through established dealer networks.
