Review

Goodman remains a trusted equipment manufacturer for budget-conscious homeowners and contractors seeking solid efficiency without luxury pricing. This 3-ton system pairs a 100,000 BTU furnace with a 35,000 BTU air conditioning condenser in a horizontal A-coil configuration. The combination delivers both heating and cooling solutions in single equipment strategy, simplifying replacement projects and reducing installation complexity for experienced technicians.

The 97% AFUE furnace rating ranks among the highest efficiency gas furnaces available, converting nearly all fuel energy into usable heat. Its modulating burner adjusts output continuously rather than cycling full on and off, reducing temperature swings and improving comfort. The 18 SEER2 air conditioner with two-stage compressor and variable-speed ECM blower matches modern efficiency expectations while maintaining the scroll compressor reliability Goodman is known for. These ratings translate directly to lower utility bills, particularly in climates with extended heating seasons.

Installation

This horizontal unit requires attic or crawlspace installation with minimum 21-inch clearance. Technicians must run 3/8-inch liquid line and 7/8-inch suction line with 3/4-inch insulation to outdoor condensers. The unit accepts both natural gas and propane, requiring proper fuel line connection and venting through plastic or PVC exhaust flues. Electrical connection needs 115V single-phase 60Hz service with 21.3 to 35-amp breaker capacity.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this system with a 10-year parts warranty following online registration at purchase. This protection covers defects in materials and workmanship, providing meaningful security for component failures. Registering immediately after installation ensures no coverage gaps and establishes documentation for future service claims or warranty transfers if you sell the home.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much will this system save on monthly heating bills compared to older furnaces? +
A 97% AFUE furnace uses 97 cents of every dollar for heating versus 78-80% efficiency in 15-year-old units. On average heating costs of $1,200 annually, you'll save roughly $200-240 per year depending on local gas rates and climate zone, paying for itself within 5-6 years.
Can I install this system in a standard attic space without major modifications? +
Yes, if your attic has 21-inch minimum clearance and adequate support framing. You'll need clearance around the unit for airflow and service access. Horizontal units work best in attics with flat framing. Crawlspaces work equally well if moisture-controlled and properly sealed.
Is 18 SEER2 a significant upgrade from my current air conditioner? +
If replacing a 10-13 SEER system, 18 SEER2 delivers 30-45% greater cooling efficiency. Your air conditioning costs will drop noticeably in summer months. The two-stage compressor reduces power draw during partial-load conditions when full cooling capacity isn't needed.
What's the difference between R410A and R22 refrigerant compatibility? +
R410A is the modern standard and more efficient. R22 is legacy refrigerant being phased out. This unit's dual compatibility provides flexibility if paired with existing outdoor equipment temporarily, though pairing matching refrigerants always optimizes performance and efficiency ratings.
Does the 10-year warranty cover labor costs if something fails? +
No, the 10-year warranty covers parts only. Labor costs for repairs or replacement service calls are separate expenses you'll pay to your HVAC contractor. Extended warranty plans are sometimes available at additional cost for labor coverage.
