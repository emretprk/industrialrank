Review

This Goodman 3.5-ton system combines a high-efficiency gas furnace with a mid-tier air conditioner in a downflow configuration designed for tight installation spaces. The unit targets homeowners seeking better heating efficiency without stepping into premium price brackets. With an AHRI certificate on file (7424271) and Energy Star qualification, it represents a mainstream choice among contractors specifying Goodman equipment for moderate-climate residential applications.

The furnace delivers 80,000 BTU heating output at 97% AFUE using a modulating burner that adjusts flame intensity rather than cycling on and off, reducing temperature swings and energy waste. Cooling capacity reaches 34,800 BTU with a 16 SEER1 rating and scroll compressor running on R410A refrigerant. The variable-speed ECM blower moves up to 1,600 CFM and automatically adapts to heating and cooling demand, improving comfort and reducing runtime hours.

Installation

This downflow system requires proper ductwork clearance below the unit since conditioned air flows downward. The compact 21-inch width and A-coil design fit most attic spaces. Electrical needs are straightforward, 115V single-phase 60Hz with 18.7 to 30-amp breaker sizing. PVC venting to the exterior is mandatory, and pre-fab plastic flue kits simplify venting configuration. Field refrigerant charge adjustment may be needed depending on line set length.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides a 10-year parts warranty when you register the unit online after installation. This covers compressor, coil, and gas valve failures but typically excludes damage from improper installation or maintenance neglect. Registration takes minutes and extends coverage substantially beyond the standard 5-year baseline, making it worthwhile for any homeowner retaining the system.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 97% AFUE compare to older furnaces in real-world heating bills? +
A 97% AFUE furnace converts 97 cents of every fuel dollar to heat, versus 80 cents for an 80% AFUE unit. Replacing a 15-year-old furnace with this model typically saves $15-25 monthly in heating costs during winter months, depending on climate and usage patterns.
Can this downflow unit work in a basement or crawlspace instead of an attic? +
Yes. Downflow configuration means air exits below the unit, making it suitable for basements, crawlspaces, and utility closets where ducts drop to lower levels. Just ensure adequate space for the 34.5-inch height and 28.75-inch depth, plus room for service access.
Is 16 SEER1 good enough for hot, humid summers? +
16 SEER1 is solid mid-tier cooling but not best-in-class. The single-stage compressor runs at full capacity even on mild days, limiting dehumidification. In very humid climates, a two-stage or variable-capacity unit may provide better moisture removal during partial-load operation.
How does the modulating burner save energy compared to standard two-stage burners? +
Modulating burners adjust flame size continuously across a range, not just high-low. This reduces temperature overshoot, minimizes short cycling, and cuts energy consumption 5-15% versus two-stage units that run at full fire until satisfied, then shut off completely.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover if something fails? +
Parts and labor for compressor, evaporator coil, and gas furnace heat exchanger failures are covered. You must register online within a timeframe to activate extended coverage. Damage from poor installation, lack of maintenance, or refrigerant contamination is excluded.
