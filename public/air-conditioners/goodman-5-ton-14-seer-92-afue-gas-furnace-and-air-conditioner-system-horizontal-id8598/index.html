Review

The Goodman 5 Ton 14 SEER 92% furnace and AC system (badged as ACiQ for commercial channels) addresses a specific market gap: contractors needing solid efficiency in unconventional layouts. This horizontal unit weighs 120 pounds and measures 24.5 inches tall, 26 inches wide, and 21.125 inches deep, making it genuinely functional for tight installations. The R454B refrigerant and 5-ton capacity handle moderate to larger residential loads without oversizing, positioning it as a practical mid-market choice rather than an entry-level or high-end solution.

Performance metrics show this system delivers balanced heating and cooling: 58,000 BTU heating output pairs with 57,000 BTU cooling capacity, creating a well-matched combo. The 92% AFUE furnace efficiency significantly outperforms baseline equipment, while the 14 SEER AC rating sits comfortably in the mainstream range. Maximum airflow reaches 2000 CFM through a multi-speed blower, adapting to ductwork and load conditions. Single-stage burner and compressor design prioritizes reliability over maximum modulation, meaning less nuanced efficiency at part-load but fewer complex components to maintain.

Installation

Horizontal mounting requires careful ductwork planning, as the right-side flue location demands proper venting clearances. The unit accepts PVC exhaust flue, simplifying installation in non-traditional settings. Electrical requirements demand 208/230V single-phase 60Hz service with a breaker between 34.6 and 60 amps. Refrigerant line sizing uses 3/8-inch liquid and 1 1/8-inch suction lines with 1/2-inch insulation, demanding competent brazing and evacuation procedures from EPA-certified technicians.

Warranty & Reliability

ACiQ backs this unit with a 10-year parts warranty activated through online registration. This covers the compressor, blower motor, and most major components, providing solid mid-tier protection. Registration is required to activate coverage, so buyers must complete this step promptly. Labor and service calls fall outside coverage, but extended availability of OEM parts over a decade supports long-term serviceability.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 14 SEER compare to modern efficiency standards? +
14 SEER1 sits above minimum standards but below premium 18+ SEER systems. For moderate climates, this delivers noticeable utility savings versus 10 SEER units while avoiding premium pricing. Energy Star certification requires 15+ SEER, so this misses that threshold, affecting some utility rebate programs.
Is this unit suitable for attic installation? +
Yes. The horizontal configuration and 24.5-inch height fit most attic spaces. Right-side flue placement requires proper venting routing. Ensure adequate support framing for the 120-pound unit and plan ductwork layout before installation to maximize effectiveness.
What makes R454B refrigerant different from older R410A? +
R454B is lower-GWP (global warming potential) and required by EPA for new equipment. It operates similarly to R410A but demands precise charging and evacuation. Technicians unfamiliar with R454B should obtain proper training, as charging errors reduce system efficiency and capacity.
How does this compare to higher-SEER Goodman units? +
Higher-SEER models often feature modulating compressors and two-stage heating for deeper part-load efficiency. This single-stage design prioritizes reliability and affordability. For constant-runtime applications, modulating compressors reduce utility bills, but upfront costs increase significantly.
Do I need to register for warranty coverage? +
Yes, online registration is mandatory to activate the 10-year warranty. Register immediately after installation. Without registration, coverage defaults to 5 years, so this step is critical for protecting your investment.
