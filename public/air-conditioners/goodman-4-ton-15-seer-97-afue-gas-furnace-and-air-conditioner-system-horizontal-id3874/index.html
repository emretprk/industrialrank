Review

The Goodman TX5N4 is a practical choice for contractors and homeowners managing real-world installation constraints. This 4-ton horizontal furnace delivers 120,000 BTU heating capacity in a compact 24.5 inch by 26 inch footprint, making it genuinely useful for tight attic and crawlspace applications where standard upflow units won't fit. Goodman positions this unit in the midmarket, offering solid efficiency without premium branding costs. It's ENERGY STAR certified and backed by a 10-year warranty with registration.

Performance specs demonstrate genuine efficiency gains. The 97% AFUE rating means nearly all fuel converts to heat, minimizing waste compared to older 80-85% units. A modulating burner fires at varying capacities rather than cycling on and off, reducing temperature swings and maintaining comfort. The variable-speed ECM blower motor adjusts CFM automatically, using less electricity during partial-load conditions. The air conditioning side provides 48,000 BTU cooling with R410A refrigerant, though the 15 SEER cooling rating trails higher-end competitors.

Installation

Horizontal installation requires right-side flue location and PVC exhaust venting, which contractors appreciate for routing flexibility. The unit accepts 208/230V single-phase power at 27.7 to 45 amps, matching most residential electrical panels without upgrades. Compact dimensions fit difficult spaces where conventional furnaces fail. Proper horizontal ductwork clearance and support framing are essential for reliable operation and longevity.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides 10-year parts coverage upon online registration, a solid commitment for a midmarket unit. This covers compressor failure and component defects but excludes routine maintenance and wear items. Registration is straightforward and critical, as coverage requires proof of activation. Compare this against premium brands offering 12-year warranties, though repair costs typically run lower.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet show both 15 SEER and 16 SEER1? +
Cooling efficiency testing switched from SEER to SEER2 methodology, making direct comparisons difficult. The 16 SEER1 represents the newer standard. When comparing to other units, verify whether they use old or new testing. This system's cooling efficiency is solid but not premium-class performance.
Can I install this in an upflow position if I modify the flue? +
No. Goodman specifically configured this unit for horizontal operation only. The internal airflow path, burner arrangement, and flue design optimize for horizontal mounting. Installing it vertically risks improper combustion, safety failures, and warranty voidance.
How much will this reduce my heating bills compared to my old furnace? +
If replacing an 80% AFUE unit, expect 15 to 20 percent heating cost reductions depending on usage patterns and local gas prices. The 97% AFUE and modulating burner minimize idle cycling. Real savings vary significantly by climate and thermostat habits.
How does this compare to Lennox or Carrier horizontal furnaces at similar price? +
Goodman offers better efficiency-to-price ratio here. Lennox and Carrier charge 20 to 30 percent more for comparable AFUE ratings. However, those brands often provide quieter operation below 70 dB. Choose Goodman for value, competitors for noise sensitivity.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Parts and labor for manufacturing defects, including compressor and heat exchanger failures. Excludes wear items, refrigerant, and routine maintenance. You must register online within 30 days. Keep your receipt and registration confirmation for claims.
