Review

The ACiQ 2.5 Ton system represents smart value engineering in the HVAC market. This upflow-configured unit pairs a 60,000 BTU gas furnace with a 30,000 BTU air conditioner, addressing both heating and cooling needs in one package. At $643, it undercuts premium brands while delivering Energy Star certification and modern refrigerant compliance. The A-coil design suits residential installations cleanly, and the plastic PVC exhaust flue requirement simplifies venting compared to metal alternatives.

Performance data shows why this unit resonates with budget buyers. The 97% AFUE furnace captures nearly all heating energy, translating directly to lower winter utility bills compared to older 80% AFUE models. Cooling efficiency hits 13 SEER1, respectable for single-stage operation. The variable-speed ECM blower motor runs from zero to 1200 CFM, meaning the system won't waste energy on oversized air movement. Electrical demands stay light at 115V single-phase with a 25-amp maximum breaker requirement, fitting standard residential panels without upgrades.

Installation

Installation follows standard upflow procedures with modest complexity. The unit requires 3/8-inch liquid and 3/4-inch suction copper lines, both common sizes in residential retrofit work. Sweat valve connections demand basic soldering skills or professional help. The 34.5-inch height, 17.5-inch width, and 28.75-inch depth fit typical closet and basement spaces. PVC exhaust venting simplifies installation compared to metal ductwork. Technicians appreciate the straightforward reciprocating compressor design and the modulating burner's programmable controls.

Warranty & Reliability

ACiQ provides 10 years of parts coverage with online registration required to activate full protection. This warranty length matches mainstream competitors and covers compressor failures, heat exchanger defects, and component malfunctions. Registration is straightforward and extends coverage meaningfully. The warranty does not cover installation labor, refrigerant, or damage from improper maintenance, so professional installation remains strongly recommended.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the product title say 2.5 ton but specs list 3 ton cooling? +
This is a spec inconsistency from the manufacturer. The 30,000 BTU cooling capacity equals 2.5 tons, but the tonnage field lists 3 ton. Assume 2.5 ton for sizing purposes. The 60,000 BTU furnace is the heating component. Always verify against the AHRI certificate 7424264 when matching to indoor units.
Can this unit retrofit into homes with older furnaces? +
Yes. The upflow A-coil configuration and standard copper line sizes work with most existing ductwork. You'll need to verify your home's gas supply can handle the 60,000 BTU demand and that your breaker panel accommodates the 16.7 to 25-amp requirement. Professional sizing and ductwork assessment prevent oversizing mistakes.
How much will this system reduce my heating costs versus an 80% AFUE furnace? +
The 97% versus 80% AFUE difference means 17 percentage points of extra efficiency. On a 60,000 BTU furnace running 8 hours daily for 120 heating days, expect roughly 15-20% annual savings on heating costs. Actual savings depend on local gas prices, insulation quality, and thermostat behavior.
Is 13 SEER1 cooling competitive, or should I spend more? +
13 SEER1 is solid mid-range efficiency, not premium. For $643, it reflects good value. Units reaching 15-16 SEER1 cost significantly more and usually offer variable-capacity compressors. Choose 13 SEER1 for budget-conscious retrofits or entry-level new builds where cooling needs are moderate.
Does online registration for warranty coverage create problems? +
Registration is quick and rarely problematic if completed within 30 days of installation. Skipping registration forfeits extended coverage, so contractors should handle it immediately. Keep documentation and the AHRI certificate for warranty claims.
