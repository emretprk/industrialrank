Review

This Goodman 3-ton system represents a solid mid-market choice for homeowners balancing efficiency gains against installation costs. At $3,398, it sits above entry-level equipment but well below premium brands. The unit combines a high-efficiency gas furnace with a matching air conditioner in a single package, available in upflow or downflow configuration. Goodman holds strong brand recognition among contractors, backed by AHRI certification number 7368834 and a 10-year warranty with registration.

Performance numbers justify the price. The furnace delivers 80,000 BTU output with 96% AFUE efficiency using either natural gas or propane, paired with two-stage burner modulation for better part-load performance. The air conditioner side produces 35,000 BTU cooling with an 18 SEER2 rating and two-stage compressor. Variable-speed ECM blower runs at 1,200 CFM maximum but scales down during moderate weather, reducing energy consumption compared to single-speed motors. Operational noise measures 71 dBA, typical for this tonnage class.

Installation

Installation requires standard HVAC skills and equipment. The unit measures 30 inches high by 17.5 inches wide by 21 inches deep, fitting most furnace closets and basement spaces. Electrical demands are modest at 115V single-phase 60Hz with breaker requirements between 21.3 and 35 amps. The 10-year parts warranty requires online registration to activate, a standard Goodman requirement that some buyers miss. Coverage includes the compressor and major components but does not cover labor or refrigerant. Unregistered units may receive shorter coverage periods. The evaporator coil carries separate warranty documentation. Always register immediately after installation to protect your investment.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does 18 SEER2 actually mean compared to older SEER ratings? +
SEER2 uses updated test standards more reflective of real-world conditions, making it roughly 15% lower than legacy SEER ratings. This unit's 18 SEER2 approximately equals a 21 SEER under old testing, confirming genuine high efficiency. You'll see measurable cooling cost reductions of 20-30% versus 13 SEER baseline units in the same tonnage.
Can I pair this outdoor condenser with an existing furnace I already own? +
Not recommended without professional assessment. HVAC systems must be matched by manufacturer and capacity. Mismatched indoor and outdoor units void warranties and reduce efficiency by 10-15%. Contact a local contractor for proper sizing and compatibility checks if you only need to replace one component.
How much money will I actually save with 96% AFUE heating? +
Annual savings depend on climate and usage. In a cold climate heating 6,000 degree-days, upgrading from 80% to 96% AFUE saves roughly $300-400 yearly on gas costs. Over 15 years of operation, total savings reach $4,500-6,000 before considering compounding utility rate increases.
Is Goodman equipment as reliable as more expensive brands like Carrier? +
Goodman compressors and furnace components meet similar reliability standards as premium brands, though some contractors report slightly higher service call rates. The two-stage design on this model increases longevity by reducing stress during part-load operation. Proper installation and maintenance matter more than brand name.
What happens if I don't register the warranty online? +
Coverage likely reduces from 10 years to 1-5 years on parts depending on Goodman's current policy. Registration takes five minutes online and protects your investment substantially. Always register immediately after professional installation to ensure maximum protection.
