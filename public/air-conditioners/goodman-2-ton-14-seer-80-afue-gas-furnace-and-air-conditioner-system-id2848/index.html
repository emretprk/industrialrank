Review

The Goodman 2-ton gas furnace and air conditioner system represents a practical middle ground in the residential HVAC market. With 24,000 BTU cooling capacity and 60,000 BTU heating output, it targets homeowners replacing 1980s-2000s equipment without committing to premium-efficiency models. Goodman's reputation for straightforward engineering and solid customer support, combined with Online Supply's competitive pricing, makes this unit attractive to cost-conscious contractors and homeowners tackling replacements themselves.

Efficiency metrics tell the real story. The 14 SEER cooling performance sits comfortably above minimum code requirements, while the 80% AFUE furnace delivers acceptable but unspectacular heating efficiency. The single-stage compressor and single-stage burner cycle on and off rather than modulating to load, resulting in more frequent startups and wider temperature swings than variable-capacity competitors. Noise output of 71 dB ranks louder than variable-speed alternatives but remains acceptable for most residential settings. The multi-speed PSC blower motor at 1,200 CFM maximum provides flexibility for ductwork adjustments.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this 2-ton unit or 3-ton unit? The specs show conflicting tonnage. +
The product title lists 2 tons, but the specs sheet states 3-ton tonnage. This discrepancy needs clarification from the seller. Request documentation showing actual cooling capacity. The 24,000 BTU rating matches 2-ton equipment, suggesting the title is correct and specs contain an error.
Can I install this myself, or do I need a licensed technician? +
Most jurisdictions require EPA certification for refrigerant handling and pressure testing. You can handle ductwork, electrical, and gas line connections if qualified, but have a licensed tech manage the refrigerant charge and system startup. Improper refrigerant levels void warranty and damage the compressor.
How does 14 SEER compare to newer air conditioners? +
14 SEER is solid mid-tier efficiency. Current high-efficiency units reach 16-18 SEER, reducing cooling costs 10-20% more annually. The tradeoff is higher upfront cost. For moderate climates with mild summers, 14 SEER payback is reasonable. Hot climates favor newer 16+ SEER units despite premium pricing.
Will this qualify for energy rebates or tax credits? +
The lack of Energy Star certification disqualifies it from most federal tax credits and utility rebates targeting high-efficiency equipment. Some basic state programs may apply. Check your local utility for 14 SEER thresholds. This cost difference can exceed $500-1,000 versus Energy Star models.
What's the lifespan of this system? +
Expect 15-20 years with proper maintenance. Single-stage components endure frequent cycling, which accelerates wear versus variable-capacity equipment. Annual service, clean filters, and refrigerant checks extend life. The 10-year warranty covers major failures during peak usage years.
