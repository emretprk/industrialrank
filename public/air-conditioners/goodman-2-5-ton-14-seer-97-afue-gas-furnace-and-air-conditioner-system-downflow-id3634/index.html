Review

Goodman has earned trust in the HVAC market by delivering solid performance at competitive prices, and this 2.5-ton gas furnace and air conditioner system exemplifies that formula. At just 643 dollars, you're getting a complete heating and cooling package with genuine efficiency credentials, not a stripped-down budget model. The unit ships ready for downflow or horizontal installation, making it adaptable to most residential applications across North America.

The heating side impresses with 97% AFUE efficiency and a modulating burner that adjusts flame intensity across multiple stages, reducing short-cycling and keeping utility bills down. Cooling delivers 29,000 BTU of capacity at 16 SEER2 through a scroll compressor running on R410A or R22 refrigerant. The variable-speed ECM blower motor manages up to 1,200 CFM, adapting airflow to actual demand rather than running full speed constantly. Both capabilities earned this unit an Energy Star designation.

The downflow A-coil design fits compact spaces like attics with a 34.5-inch height and 17.5-inch width footprint. Frequently Asked Questions

Is this unit truly a 2.5-ton system or 3-ton as the specs suggest? +
The tonnage discrepancy appears in listing specs showing 3-ton condenser capacity. The 29,000 BTU cooling output equals approximately 2.4 tons (12,000 BTU per ton). Verify exact capacity with the AHRI certificate number 7424269 before installation to ensure proper furnace matching and system balance.
Can I install this furnace in my basement crawlspace with existing metal ductwork? +
Yes for ductwork, no for exhaust venting. The unit's downflow configuration works well in basements and crawlspaces, but the furnace requires plastic or PVC exhaust flue. Metal venting from older systems cannot be reused, so budget for new PVC exhaust venting during installation.
How much will my heating bills drop with 97% AFUE compared to my 80% AFUE furnace? +
A 17-percentage-point jump in efficiency translates roughly to 17-21% lower heating costs, depending on local natural gas rates and thermostat habits. Your actual savings depend on climate severity and usage patterns. A home heating 8,000 degree days annually could save 300 to 500 dollars yearly at typical rates.
How does this Goodman compare to mid-tier Carrier or Lennox models at similar price points? +
Goodman competes on efficiency specs and warranty length. Carrier and Lennox often feature quieter operation and smoother modulation but command 20-30% price premiums. For contractors valuing parts availability and technician familiarity, Goodman's ecosystem edges ahead. Performance differences are marginal for most residential uses.
Does the 10-year warranty cover labor or just parts? +
The advertised 10-year warranty covers parts only with online registration. You are responsible for service labor costs if repairs are needed. Confirm whether your installing contractor offers any labor coverage options, as these vary widely between dealers.
