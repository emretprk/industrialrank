Review

The Goodman 3 Ton Gas Furnace and Air Conditioner System sits squarely in the practical middle of the residential market. At $736, this upflow A-coil configuration combines modulating heating with two-stage cooling, targeting homeowners and contractors who want measurable efficiency gains without luxury pricing. Goodman's reputation for straightforward engineering rather than bells-and-whistles features makes this unit popular for replacement work and new construction where budgets matter but performance cannot slip.

Performance numbers tell the real story. The system delivers 80,000 BTU heating output at 97% AFUE, meaning nearly all fuel converts to usable heat. On the cooling side, 35,000 BTU capacity with SEER 18 efficiency beats many competitor base models. The variable-speed ECM blower motor works with both stages to optimize airflow across your home, while the scroll compressor runs at 71 dB, keeping noise reasonable. R410A refrigerant compatibility ensures you avoid obsolescence if regulations shift further.

Installation

This unit needs standard residential electrical service: 115V single-phase 60Hz with breaker sizing between 21.3 and 35 amps. The upflow configuration fits most closets or utility spaces with its 34.5-inch height and 21-inch width. You will need to run PVC exhaust flue to the exterior, a straightforward addition if your home has adequate venting routes. Suction and liquid lines come sized at 7/8-inch and 3/8-inch respectively, which most contractors can accommodate without special fabrication.

Warranty & Reliability

Coverage runs 10 years when you register the unit online after installation. This matches industry standard for mid-tier equipment and protects against compressor failure, heat exchanger defects, and parts replacement. Registration is typically fast, often completed at point-of-sale or through Goodman's website. Keep your proof of purchase and registration documents, as manufacturers enforce activation deadlines.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much can I actually save on heating and cooling costs? +
Real savings depend on your current system and local energy rates. A 97% AFUE furnace uses roughly 8 to 12 percent less gas than equipment from the 1990s, while SEER 18 cooling saves 15 to 25 percent versus older single-stage units. Monthly dollars vary by climate and usage, but most homeowners see payback in 5 to 8 years.
Can I install this myself or do I need a licensed tech? +
HVAC work requires EPA certification for refrigerant handling and local licensing in most areas. Hire a licensed contractor. The system comes with detailed installation instructions, but improper charge, ductwork connections, or electrical work voids warranty and creates safety hazards.
What's the difference between modulating and two-stage heating? +
This unit uses modulating burners, which vary heat output continuously rather than cycling on-off. That means smoother temperature control, less comfort swings, and lower standby gas consumption. Two-stage cooling works similarly, letting the compressor run at partial capacity during mild weather.
Is SEER 18 better than SEER 16 units from competitors? +
SEER 18 is respectable, roughly 12 percent more efficient than SEER 16. Premium systems hit SEER 19 to 21, but cost significantly more. This unit delivers real savings without the highest-tier price tag, making it smart for budget-conscious buyers.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Registration protects parts, compressor, and heat exchanger for 10 years against defects. Labor and service calls are not included. Register immediately after installation to activate full coverage and keep documentation for claims.
