Review

The Goodman 3 Ton 13 SEER gas furnace and air conditioner system occupies the practical middle ground that most contractors actually sell. At $3,770, it's not a premium package, but it's also not a bare-bones budget unit. This downflow configuration suits basements and crawlspaces across residential builds, and the ACiQ branding indicates Goodman's reliable engineering. The system handles 120,000 BTU heating and 36,000 BTU cooling, making it suitable for medium-sized homes across mixed climates.

Where this system earns its rating is in actual performance numbers. The furnace's 96% AFUE rating means you're losing almost nothing to exhaust, while competitors at this price often sit at 92 to 94 percent. The 13 SEER air conditioner carries ENERGY STAR certification, and the variable-speed ECM blower motor adjusts output to your home's real demand rather than cycling on and off like older fixed-speed designs. A two-stage burner prevents temperature swings by modulating heat output. Real homeowners upgrading from 1990s-era furnaces typically see 20 to 30 percent annual heating cost reductions.

Pros

Installation

This downflow unit requires 115V single-phase 60 Hz power with a breaker between 19.1 and 30 amps, standard for most homes. The system accepts plastic PVC exhaust flue, which simplifies venting compared to metal ductwork. Standard sweat valve connections use 3/8-inch liquid lines and 3/4-inch suction lines. Total weight of 120 pounds is manageable for two technicians. Most installations take a full day for experienced crews familiar with Goodman equipment.

Warranty & Reliability

Ten years parts coverage with free online registration is competitive for this price tier. Goodman honors it well, and the AHRI certificate number 7368845 confirms system matching. Note that labor is not covered, only parts. Registering immediately after installation ensures coverage activation, and you'll want to document the purchase receipt.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the 96% AFUE rating actually affect my heating bills? +
A 96% AFUE furnace converts 96 cents of every energy dollar into usable heat versus older 80% units converting 80 cents. For a home heating for 6 months annually, upgrading typically saves 2,000 to 3,000 dollars over the system's 15-year lifespan depending on gas prices and climate zone.
Can I use this in upflow configuration if my tech prefers it? +
Yes, the specs list both upflow and downflow capability. Your HVAC installer can orient the A-coil configuration to match your ductwork return path. Just confirm this before purchase to ensure the coil direction matches your existing setup.
Is 13 SEER considered good efficiency for 2024? +
It's solid middle-market performance. SEER2 ratings published now are lower than old SEER ratings, making comparison tricky. At 13 SEER, this unit performs 2 to 3 points above basic Goodman models but trails premium two-stage units sitting at 16 to 18 SEER.
How does this compare to Lennox or Carrier models at the same price? +
Goodman generally prices 10 to 15 percent lower than Lennox or Carrier with nearly identical component quality. You're trading brand prestige for cost. Reliability across all three is strong, so contractor familiarity often drives the real difference.
What if my existing ductwork is undersized for the 2,000 CFM maximum? +
The variable-speed ECM blower will ramp down automatically, but you'll lose efficiency and cooling capacity. Undersized ducts are a common installation issue that reduces real-world performance by 15 to 20 percent, so have your ducts verified before commitment.
