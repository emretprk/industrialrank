Review

The Goodman DSXC160601 is a workhorse 5-ton air conditioning condenser positioned at the practical middle ground of the residential cooling market. At 3,410 dollars, it undercuts premium brands while delivering solid efficiency through its two-stage design. This unit targets contractors and homeowners prioritizing reliability and straightforward installation over cutting-edge features. Goodman's reputation in commercial and residential sectors provides confidence that this condenser will perform consistently across typical American climates.

Performance centers on the 16 SEER1 efficiency rating and 60,000 BTU cooling capacity, meeting mid-sized home cooling demands reliably. The two-stage scroll compressor adjusts output based on load, running at reduced capacity during partial-load conditions to minimize energy waste. At 75 dB during full operation, noise sits above quieter premium units but remains acceptable for typical residential settings. Electrical requirements of 208/230V single-phase with 37.2 to 60-amp breaker compatibility fit standard residential service.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 16 SEER1 rating qualifies for Energy Star, reducing seasonal cooling costs by 15-20% versus single-stage units
  • Two-stage compressor operation allows low-stage cooling on mild days, improving part-load efficiency and comfort consistency
  • Scroll compressor is proven reliable in Goodman equipment, with lower failure rates than reciprocating designs
  • 10-year warranty with online registration provides solid protection comparable to mid-tier competitors
  • Standard R410A refrigerant widely available and familiar to most HVAC technicians

Cons

  • 75 dB noise level places this unit in the louder category; some homeowners notice vibration during full-load operation
  • 16 SEER1 (roughly 12.5-13 SEER2 equivalent) trails premium brands offering 18-20 SEER ratings by 20-30%
  • Sweat valve connections require experienced soldering, adding labor costs if technician skill is limited

Who Should Buy

Best For

Contractors managing budget-conscious residential replacements and homeowners in moderate cooling zones (AHRI regions 1-3) where 16 SEER provides good ROI within 5-7 years. Ideal for retrofits where existing ductwork and indoor units are compatible.

Consider Alternatives If

Anyone in extremely hot climates (Arizona, southern Texas) or with high daily cooling loads should explore 18+ SEER units to maximize long-term savings, even at higher upfront cost.

Installation

Installation requires standard HVAC skills and tools. The 3/8-inch liquid and 7/8-inch suction valve connections are sweat (solder) connections, demanding proper torching and nitrogen purging during brazing. Unit dimensions of 35.5 by 35.5 by 38.25 inches fit standard concrete pads; verify outdoor clearances per local code before delivery. Most installations take 4-6 hours for experienced technicians.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman offers 10 years of parts warranty upon online registration, providing meaningful protection for compressor and major components. This mid-tier coverage matches most competitors in the 3,500 dollar price range. Extended labor warranties are available separately through dealer networks. Registration is mandatory to activate full protection, so document the purchase and complete registration immediately.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this unit qualify for tax rebates or utility incentives? +
Yes, the Energy Star certification and 16 SEER1 rating often qualify for federal tax credits (currently up to 600 dollars) and state utility rebates. Check EnergyStar.gov and your local utility to confirm current programs, as incentives change annually.
Can I install this myself if I'm experienced with HVAC tools? +
Absolutely not. Refrigerant handling, nitrogen purging, and electrical setup require EPA certification and licensing in most states. Hire a licensed technician to ensure warranty validity and safe operation.
How does two-stage operation improve efficiency compared to single-stage? +
Two-stage compressors run at 60-70% output on mild days, reducing unnecessary cycling and energy consumption. This part-load efficiency improvement compounds over a season, delivering roughly 5-8% better SEER than single-stage units in the same class.
Is 16 SEER competitive with brands like Lennox or Carrier at this price? +
Goodman's 16 SEER is solid but falls short of Lennox or Carrier offerings at similar prices, which often feature 17-18 SEER. However, Goodman costs 300-500 dollars less, making it the better value for budget-conscious buyers accepting slightly lower efficiency.
What if the unit fails during the warranty period? +
Parts including compressor and condenser coil are covered for 10 years with registration. Labor warranty varies; check your specific agreement. Contact Goodman directly or your installing contractor to initiate claims.
