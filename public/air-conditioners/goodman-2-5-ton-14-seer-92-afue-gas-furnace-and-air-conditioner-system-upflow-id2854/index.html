Review

The 2.5 ton Goodman gas furnace and air conditioner system at $643 represents honest middle-market equipment for homeowners and contractors seeking reliable heating and cooling without premium costs. Goodman built its reputation on straightforward, dependable systems, and this upflow A-coil configuration follows that formula. It's designed for homes needing integrated heating and cooling in a compact footprint measuring 34.5 inches tall and 17.5 inches wide.

Performance specs indicate a 14 SEER cooling rating with 30,000 BTU capacity and 92% AFUE heating delivering 60,000 BTU output. The single-stage rotary compressor operates at 72 dB, which is moderate for the tonnage class. Multi-speed blower motor reaches 1,200 CFM maximum, supporting proper air distribution through typical residential ductwork. Refrigerant compatibility with R410A or R22 offers flexibility for retrofit or new installations, though R410A is the modern standard.

Installation

Upflow configuration suits attic or basement installations where air moves vertically upward through ductwork. The 3/8-inch liquid line and 3/4-inch suction line with 3/4-inch insulation thickness require standard refrigerant piping practices. PVC plastic exhaust flue requirement simplifies venting compared to metal options. Electrical demand runs 115V single-phase 60Hz with 17.1 to 25 amp breaker sizing, typical for residential circuits.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman's 10-year parts warranty activates upon online registration, protecting against compressor and component failures during this period. Registration is straightforward but mandatory to unlock full coverage. This duration is competitive for systems in this price range, though premium brands sometimes extend to 12 years. Labor and refrigerant typically fall outside warranty scope.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this system right for my 2,000 square foot home? +
The 2.5 ton cooling capacity handles approximately 2,000-2,500 square feet in moderate climates, assuming proper insulation and single-story construction. The 60,000 BTU heating output works similarly. Have an HVAC contractor perform a Manual J calculation to confirm sizing for your specific location and home characteristics.
Can I upgrade this system later if my cooling needs increase? +
Yes, the A-coil design allows future compressor upgrades, though replacing the entire outdoor unit is typically more economical than component swaps. The furnace section operates independently, so heating upgrades remain separate from air conditioning work. Plan accordingly during initial installation.
How does the 14 SEER rating compare to newer standards? +
14 SEER meets federal minimum efficiency standards and beats many budget models substantially. However, newer SEER2 ratings (which replaced SEER in 2023) show different performance curves. This unit's real-world efficiency is likely 10-11 SEER2 equivalent, still respectable but not premium-tier performance.
What makes single-stage different from variable-capacity systems? +
Single-stage compressors run at full output or off, creating more temperature swings and slightly higher energy bills versus systems that modulate capacity. Variable-speed systems cost more upfront but deliver quieter, steadier comfort. This unit prioritizes affordability over precision temperature control.
Does the warranty cover refrigerant and labor? +
The 10-year warranty covers compressor and sealed components with registration, but excludes refrigerant costs and service labor. You pay separately for technician visits and refrigerant recharges if leaks occur after the first year.
