Review

The Goodman 1.5-ton gas furnace and air conditioner system represents the entry-level segment of the HVAC replacement market. At $1,909, this horizontal A-coil configuration targets budget-conscious contractors and homeowners prioritizing installation cost over premium efficiency. Manufactured by Goodman and distributed through Online Supply, this unit combines cooling and heating in one package, eliminating the need for separate component purchases and simplifying logistics on smaller residential jobs.

Performance specs show 18,000 BTU cooling output with 14 SEER efficiency and 60,000 BTU heating with 80% AFUE across two burner stages. The variable-speed blower reaches 1,600 CFM maximum airflow, while the rotary compressor operates at 208/230V single-phase with a 30-amp maximum breaker requirement. At 71 decibels, noise output is typical for single-stage units but noticeable in quiet environments. The R410A refrigerant and 3/4 inch suction line indicate proper cooling capacity for 1.5-ton systems.

Installation

This horizontal unit requires metal exhaust flue, upflow ductwork configuration, and standard 208/230V electrical service. The 22-pound weight suits one-person installation in attics or basements. Pre-charged refrigerant lines simplify field connections. Contractors should verify 1,600 CFM duct sizing and provide adequate clearance around the 27.5 x 26 x 26 inch footprint. Frequently Asked Questions

Is 14 SEER adequate cooling for a 1.5-ton system? +
Yes. 14 SEER means the system removes 18,000 BTU per hour while using moderate electricity. This rating suits homes under 1,500 square feet in climates with less than 200 cooling degree days annually. Larger homes or hot climates should consider 16+ SEER units for lower summer bills.
Can I install this in a horizontal attic position? +
Absolutely. The A-coil horizontal configuration is specifically designed for attic or basement mounting. The 27.5-inch height and 26-inch depth fit standard joist spacing. Ensure metal ductwork connects properly and verify the variable-speed blower has adequate return airflow.
How does two-stage heating improve efficiency? +
Two-stage burners run at lower fire rates during mild weather, using less gas than single-stage units that fire at full capacity regardless of demand. Combined with the variable-speed blower, this reduces energy waste and provides quieter operation on partial-load heating days typical in spring and fall.
Is this system cheaper than separate coil and furnace units? +
Yes, combo systems reduce labor and component costs. However, if one component fails later, you must replace the entire unit unless you disconnect and replace only the failed part, which may void warranty and create efficiency mismatches.
Do I need to register the warranty immediately? +
Yes. Online registration activates the 10-year coverage. Without registration, you may lose warranty eligibility despite purchase. Keep the serial number and registration confirmation accessible for any future service claims.
