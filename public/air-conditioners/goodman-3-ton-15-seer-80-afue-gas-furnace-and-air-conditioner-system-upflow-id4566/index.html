Review

The Goodman 3 Ton 15 SEER 80% AFUE system represents practical, no-frills heating and cooling for residential contractors and budget-conscious homeowners. As a matched pair, furnace and condenser ship from Online Supply at $2,705, significantly undercutting premium brands like Carrier or Lennox. The upflow configuration suits typical basement and attic installations across most residential builds. Its two-stage furnace and single-stage condenser prioritize straightforward operation over advanced comfort features, making it familiar territory for service technicians.

Cooling output reaches 34,800 BTU from the 3.5-ton condenser, which actually carries a 16 SEER2 rating rather than the headline 15 SEER figure, suggesting conservative marketing. The furnace delivers 80,000 BTU across two burner stages, paired with a variable-speed blower that adjusts airflow from under 500 CFM to a maximum of 2,000 CFM. Electrical demands are modest at 120 volts, 60 Hz, requiring a 30-amp breaker. The scroll compressor and R410A refrigerant are field-proven technologies, and 50-foot suction line insulation supports up to moderate-distance indoor unit placement.

Installation

Upflow installation requires vertical ductwork in basements or utility closets, with 30 inches of height and 24.5 inches of width needed for clearance. The metal exhaust flue is mandatory, adding rigid ducting cost if the home lacks existing flue infrastructure. Suction and liquid lines come pre-sized at 7/8 inch and 3/8 inch respectively. Typical installation takes 4 to 6 hours for experienced crews, including electrical connections and refrigerant charge verification.

Warranty & Reliability

Coverage spans 10 years on parts and compressor when you register online, which is standard for Goodman but doesn't include labor. Registration is straightforward but required, making it owner responsibility to document purchase within the activation window. No extended warranties are offered through Online Supply, so budget for service calls beyond year one.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this system actually deliver 15 SEER as advertised? +
The condenser carries a 16 SEER2 rating, which exceeds the listing. SEER2 is the newer metric reflecting lower outdoor temperatures. The headline 15 SEER is conservative. Actual performance depends on indoor coil matching and proper installation, but the condenser specifications support efficient cooling.
Can I install the outdoor unit far from the indoor coil? +
Yes, the 50-foot suction line with 3/4-inch insulation supports distance installation. However, going beyond 50 feet requires larger refrigerant lines and may trigger AHRI decertification. Verify with a contractor that extended runs maintain proper charge and superheat.
Is 80% AFUE acceptable, or should I buy a 95% unit instead? +
80% AFUE is baseline modern efficiency, not high-efficiency. A 95% condensing furnace saves approximately 15% on heating fuel but costs $1,500 to $2,500 more. Payback in mild climates exceeds 10 years, making this unit fine for moderate regions or shorter ownership timelines.
How does this compare to a Lennox or Carrier system at the same price? +
Goodman competes on price and parts availability, not features or noise levels. Lennox and Carrier offer variable-stage compressors and lower decibels at higher cost. Goodman's scroll compressor is reliable but less sophisticated. Choose Goodman for budget and simplicity, not premium comfort.
What happens if I don't register for the warranty? +
Online registration is required to activate the 10-year warranty. Without it, you lose compressor coverage and may only have implied baseline protection. Register immediately after installation to avoid disputes during claims.
