Review

Goodman's 3.5-ton system represents the pragmatic middle ground in residential HVAC, combining the manufacturer's proven track record with no-nonsense engineering. At $647, this upflow A-coil configuration targets contractors and homeowners tired of premium pricing for features they won't use. The SKU LS38783830 unit ships at 30 pounds, compact enough for standard attic installations across most single-story homes and ranch-style properties.

Performance specs reveal a workhorse design rather than a high-efficiency showpiece. The furnace delivers 80,000 BTU heating through two-stage burners, while the AC coil handles 42,000 BTU cooling at 13 SEER efficiency. The high-efficiency multi-speed blower achieves 2000 CFM maximum airflow, meaning the system modulates capacity better than fixed-speed alternatives. However, 80% AFUE and single-stage condenser operation keep this unit in the mid-tier category, trading some efficiency gains for lower acquisition cost.

Installation

The upflow configuration suits attic placement in most homes, requiring standard 208/230V single-phase power. A 40-amp breaker handles peak electrical demand, while the 36.25 inch height fits most attic joist spacing. Technicians need to provide metal exhaust flue, R410A refrigerant charge, and proper line insulation with 3/8 inch minimum thickness on the suction line. Standard ductwork connections use conventional sweat-valve fittings.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman includes a 10-year parts and labor warranty upon online registration, standard for the brand's mid-market offerings. This covers compressor, coil, and furnace components but requires proof of professional installation. Registration takes minutes at Goodman's website, and technicians familiar with the brand report straightforward claims processing through Online Supply's support network.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will this system qualify for energy rebates in my state? +
The 13 SEER rating meets federal efficiency standards and qualifies for many state programs, though 80% AFUE furnaces miss rebates in high-efficiency markets. Check your utility company's specific requirements, as some programs now target 14+ SEER or 90%+ AFUE. You may receive partial rebates depending on local incentive structures.
Can I install this myself, or do I need a technician? +
The 10-year warranty requires professional installation, and refrigerant handling legally demands EPA certification. Goodman provides detailed installation manuals, but the complexity of charging systems, electrical connections, and flue venting makes DIY installation risky and unwarranted given the $647 unit cost.
How does the two-stage burner improve heating performance? +
Two-stage operation runs at 70% capacity during mild weather, reducing short cycling and improving comfort stability. Full 80,000 BTU output engages only during deep cold snaps, lowering utility costs during shoulder seasons. This modulation is a solid upgrade over fixed single-stage burners common in budget units.
How does 13 SEER compare to newer systems I've seen? +
13 SEER meets current DOE minimum standards but trails modern 16-18 SEER units by 20-40% in cooling efficiency. The gap widens in hot climates with extended cooling seasons. However, payback on upgrading purely for SEER improvement typically exceeds 10-12 years unless existing AC is failing.
What if something breaks after purchase? +
The 10-year warranty covers compressor, coil, and furnace failures after registration. Contact Online Supply or an authorized Goodman dealer with proof of installation date. Warranty claims are processed through local supply houses, usually resulting in parts replacement within 3-5 business days.
