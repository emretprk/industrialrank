Review

The Goodman 3.5 Ton 14 SEER 92% 100,000 BTU furnace and AC system, branded through ACiQ, occupies the practical middle ground of residential HVAC equipment. It delivers reasonable efficiency gains without the premium pricing of modulating or variable-speed systems. The combination of 92% AFUE heating and 14 SEER cooling provides measurable energy savings compared to 10-13 year old equipment, and Energy Star certification signals compliance with national efficiency standards. This downflow configuration suits retrofit installations where space is limited.

Performance specs reveal a straightforward design optimized for reliability over sophistication. The furnace generates 42,000 BTU heating output at 92% efficiency, while the AC side delivers 41,000 BTU cooling capacity at 14 SEER. The scroll compressor operates at single stage, which means it runs at one speed rather than modulating capacity with demand. The multi-speed blower motor at 2,000 CFM maximum provides three-stage airflow adjustment, helping balance comfort and efficiency. Electrical requirements are modest at 208/230V single-phase with 24-40 amp breaker sizing.

Installation

Installation favors contractors and experienced DIYers. The downflow/horizontal configuration fits tight spaces like basements or attics. PVC exhaust flue requirements eliminate costly stainless steel venting, reducing material expenses and simplifying ductwork design. The unit dimensions (30 inches height, 21 inches width and depth) require standard roughin spacing. Refrigerant lines accommodate the supplied 50-foot suction line with 1/2-inch insulation, adequate for most residential runs without additional field insulation.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year parts and labor warranty activates upon online registration, providing solid baseline coverage. This matches mid-tier competitor offerings but trails premium brands offering 12-year protections. Registration is straightforward and recommended immediately after installation to establish the coverage timeline. Extended warranties are sometimes available through dealers, worth evaluating for properties with limited service access.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the actual difference between the listed 3.5 ton and 5 ton tonnage specifications? +
This spec sheet shows conflicting data, listing both 3.5 ton and 5 ton. Based on the 41,000 BTU cooling output, this is a 3.5 ton unit. Verify tonnage with your ACiQ dealer before ordering to ensure proper evaporator coil sizing and system matching.
Can this furnace handle downflow and upflow installation, or is it locked to one direction? +
The configuration lists both upflow and downflow capability, but the primary spec sheet emphasizes downflow operation. Consult installation manuals for your specific model to confirm whether conversion requires flue relocation or air intake repositioning.
How does 14 SEER compare to modern high-efficiency systems I'm seeing advertised? +
14 SEER represents solid mid-tier efficiency and qualifies for Energy Star rebates. Premium systems reach 16-21 SEER through variable-speed compressors and advanced controls, but cost significantly more. For moderate climates, 14 SEER delivers 20-30% savings versus 10 SEER legacy equipment.
Is R410A refrigerant still the right choice, or should I wait for newer alternatives? +
R410A remains standard and widely serviced, with abundant supply and technician familiarity. Newer low-GWP refrigerants are emerging but increase system costs. R410A is appropriate for new installations through 2030 based on current EPA regulations.
What happens after the 10-year warranty expires, and are parts still available? +
ACiQ, a Goodman-backed brand, maintains parts availability for 10-15 years post-production. After warranty expiration, repairs become out-of-pocket but remain affordable since components are common across Goodman product lines.
