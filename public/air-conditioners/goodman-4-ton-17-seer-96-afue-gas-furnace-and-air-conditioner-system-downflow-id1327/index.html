Review

The Goodman CAPF4961C6 is a traditional split system pairing a gas furnace and air conditioner coil designed for downflow or horizontal installation. This is solid, mid-market equipment trusted by HVAC contractors for residential retrofits and new builds. Goodman has established a reputation for reliability and affordability, making systems like this popular in competitive markets where upfront cost matters but efficiency cannot be ignored.

The furnace delivers 80,000 BTU of heating output at 96% AFUE, meaning 96 cents of every dollar spent on fuel converts to usable heat. The air conditioning side produces 47,000 BTU at an advertised 17 SEER rating. The variable-speed ECM blower motor adjusts output from 0 to 1,600 CFM based on demand, reducing runtime and energy consumption compared to single-speed models. Two-stage operation on both heating and cooling keeps the system from overshooting comfort targets.

Installation

Installation requires 208/230 volt, single-phase 60 Hz electrical service with a 29.3 to 50 amp breaker. The unit accepts either natural gas or propane and exhausts through PVC or plastic flue pipe. Downflow configuration demands proper return air ducting beneath the unit. Refrigerant lines use 3/8-inch liquid and 7/8-inch suction connections with sweat valve fittings. Most contractors complete installation in one day on straightforward applications.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides 10 years parts coverage when you register the unit online after installation. This is a standard industry offering that protects against compressor failure, motor burnout, and component defects. Registration is simple and extends protection beyond the standard one-year coverage. Some installers may offer additional labor warranties separately.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does this product list both 17 SEER and 18 SEER1 ratings? +
The title specifies 17 SEER (older rating standard), while the specs show 18 SEER1 (newer EPA standard introduced 2023). SEER1 is stricter and more realistic for actual climate conditions. Verify which rating applies to your region's incentive programs, as they may reference either standard.
Can I install this in an upflow position instead of downflow? +
The spec sheet lists upflow and downflow capability in the configuration field, but the title emphasizes downflow. Contact Goodman or your installer to confirm upflow compatibility for your specific ductwork. Improper orientation affects blower performance and condensate drainage.
How does the ECM blower motor affect monthly utility bills? +
Variable-speed ECM motors typically reduce blower electricity use 30 to 50 percent versus fixed-speed designs by running at lower speeds during partial-load conditions. Combined with 96% AFUE heating and two-stage cooling, expect 15 to 20 percent lower annual heating and cooling costs on average.
Is this system compatible with smart thermostats? +
Two-stage heating and two-stage cooling make this system compatible with standard WiFi thermostats from Ecobee, Nest, and others. However, verify that your therstat supports two-stage equipment before purchase. Some basic thermostats only work with single-stage systems.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
The 10-year coverage protects parts like the compressor, blower motor, and heat exchanger against defects. You must register online to activate it. Labor and refrigerant are typically not included, so clarify coverage with your installer before service is needed.
