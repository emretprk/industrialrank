Review

The Goodman GSX130481 is a workhorse 4-ton air conditioning condenser built for contractors and homeowners who need dependable cooling without premium pricing. At $1,536, it sits squarely in the budget-to-mid-range segment where value matters more than cutting-edge efficiency. This single-stage unit handles typical residential cooling demands with proven reciprocating compressor technology that's been refined over decades. It's the kind of equipment you see on job sites regularly because it balances performance with affordability.

Performance-wise, the unit delivers 48,000 BTU of cooling capacity at a respectable 13 SEER rating. That efficiency level is solid without being exceptional, placing it ahead of older equipment but below premium systems. Operating at 76 dB, noise output is moderate and acceptable for most residential installations, though not as refined as high-end models. The single-stage design means the compressor runs at full capacity when cooling is needed, lacking the modulation that provides finer temperature control on premium units.

Installation

Installation is straightforward for experienced technicians. The unit accepts standard 3/8-inch liquid line and 1 1/8-inch suction line connections with sweat valves, matching typical residential line sets. Electrical requirements are 208/230V single-phase at 60 Hz, with breaker sizing between 26.3 and 45 amps. The compact 29 by 29 by 36.25-inch footprint fits most standard installation spaces without modification.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides a 10-year parts warranty when you register online, which is competitive for this price tier. Online registration is simple and protects your investment against compressor failure and component defects. This warranty length suggests the manufacturer stands behind the equipment, though you should register promptly to activate coverage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 13 SEER good efficiency for this price point? +
Yes. At $1,536, a 13 SEER rating is solid performance for the budget segment. Premium units hit 16-18 SEER but cost significantly more. This unit balances efficiency gains against upfront investment, making it attractive for homeowners without maximum energy savings as their priority.
What home size does the 4-ton, 48,000 BTU capacity serve? +
A 4-ton unit typically handles 1,600 to 2,000 square feet in moderate climates, depending on insulation and sun exposure. In hot climates like Arizona or Texas, sizing may need adjustment. Always have a professional calculate load requirements before purchasing.
How does the 76 dB noise level compare to other condensers? +
76 dB is moderate. Budget units often range 75-78 dB, while premium systems run 70-73 dB. For reference, 76 dB is audible conversation distance. If noise concerns you, test units in person or upgrade to a higher-tier model.
Can this replace older Goodman units like the GSX130483? +
Yes, this unit replaces GSX130483, GSX130484, and GSX130485 according to Goodman specifications. However, verify your indoor coil compatibility before purchasing, as mismatched components reduce efficiency and void warranties.
Does online warranty registration really matter? +
Yes. Goodman requires online registration to activate the 10-year coverage. Without registration, warranty periods are shorter. Registration takes five minutes on their website and protects you.
