Review

The ACiQ 2.5-ton furnace and air conditioning system sits firmly in the value category of HVAC equipment. Priced at $732, it positions itself as an accessible option for contractors managing multiple job sites and homeowners facing unplanned replacements. While ACiQ is less recognized than Goodman or Daikin in some markets, this unit carries the engineering fundamentals that make it a practical choice for straightforward heating and cooling applications.

Performance specs reveal a competent system with real-world implications. The furnace delivers 96% AFUE efficiency, burning less gas than units rated 80% to 90%, translating to meaningful savings over ten heating seasons. The air conditioner side produces 30,000 cooling BTU at 14 SEER efficiency using R454B refrigerant, which meets Energy Star criteria. Multi-speed blower technology allows the motor to adjust fan speed based on demand, reducing the constant 74 dB noise typical of single-speed designs.

Installation

Installation straightforward for licensed technicians familiar with upflow configurations. The unit measures 30 inches tall by 17.5 inches wide by 21 inches deep, fitting standard closet and basement alcoves. Requires 208/230V single-phase power with 18.7 to 30 amp service. PVC plastic exhaust flue is standard for modern homes but may require ductwork modifications in older installations with masonry chimneys.

Warranty & Reliability

Ten-year parts warranty activates upon online registration, covering compressor, heat exchanger, and component failures. Registration is quick and essential, as it doubles coverage duration versus the standard five-year baseline. No labor coverage is included, so plan for technician service calls outside the parts guarantee.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 30,000 BTU enough heating for my home? +
Adequate for homes under 1,500 square feet in moderate climates like ASHRAE zones 4-5. Colder regions or larger spaces need 40,000 to 60,000 BTU systems. Calculate required BTU at roughly 20-30 BTU per square foot depending on insulation and outdoor winter temperatures.
Can this furnace connect to my existing ductwork? +
Yes, if your ducts accommodate upflow airflow at 800 CFM maximum. Verify duct sizing with a technician using Manual D standards. Most homes with previous furnaces transition easily, though some systems may need supplemental return air pathways.
How much will this system reduce my heating bills compared to an 80% AFUE unit? +
Expect 16% annual heating cost reduction versus 80% AFUE systems. Over ten years, a home spending $1,200 yearly on heating saves approximately $1,920 in fuel costs alone, offsetting premium purchase prices quickly in colder climates.
Is ACiQ quality comparable to Goodman or Lennox? +
ACiQ uses similar scroll compressors and heat exchangers but offers less brand recognition and dealer support. Reliability is solid for basic applications. However, premium brands typically provide faster warranty claims and more regional service network availability.
What happens if the system fails before the warranty expires? +
Contact your installing contractor to file a claim under the ten-year parts warranty. Compressor and major component replacement is covered after online registration. Labor charges and service calls fall outside warranty scope.
