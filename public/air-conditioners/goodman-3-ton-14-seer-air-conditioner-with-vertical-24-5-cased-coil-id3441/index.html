Review

ACiQ's 3-ton air conditioner condenser sits in the sweet spot between budget equipment and mid-range units. At $601, this 36,000 BTU unit uses modern R454B refrigerant and delivers straightforward, reliable cooling without premium frills. The vertical cased coil configuration makes it adaptable to various home layouts, and the scroll compressor is industry-standard technology that HVAC techs know well. For homeowners tired of paying $1,200 to $2,000 for name-brand condensers, this represents genuine value.

Performance specs are honest and competitive. The 14 SEER rating meets minimum efficiency standards and keeps operating costs reasonable, though it won't match high-efficiency models at 16+ SEER. At 73 dB, it's noticeably audible from 10 feet away but typical for single-stage units at this price point. The single-stage scroll compressor runs full-blast rather than modulating, which simplifies design but means slightly higher energy use during partial-load conditions. For routine cooling in mild-to-moderate climates, these trade-offs are acceptable.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 14 SEER rating meets current efficiency standards while keeping equipment cost accessible
  • R454B refrigerant is EPA-approved and future-proofs against refrigerant phase-outs affecting older units

Who Should Buy

Best For

This unit works best for homeowners replacing aging air conditioners in homes under 2,000 square feet, or contractors stocking inventory for maintenance calls in moderate climates. It's particularly attractive for budget-conscious buyers in regions without aggressive Energy Star rebate programs.

Consider Alternatives If

Skip this model if you need Energy Star certification for rebate eligibility, require ultra-quiet operation below 70 dB, or live in extremely hot climates where modulating compressors deliver measurable efficiency gains.

Installation

This unit needs 208/230V single-phase power with a 30-amp maximum breaker and 18.7-amp minimum rating. The vertical cased coil measures 24.5 inches wide by 22 inches tall, fitting standard return plenums in most homes. Line set comes pre-insulated at 3/4 inches thick with 30 feet of copper, reducing field work. Standard refrigeration connections use 3/8-inch liquid and 3/4-inch suction lines, compatible with most existing coils.

Warranty & Reliability

ACiQ provides a 10-year parts warranty when you register the unit online, which is standard in this price range. This covers compressor and component failures but typically excludes wear items. Registration is straightforward and adds no cost. Ten years is respectable coverage, though premium brands sometimes offer longer terms on sealed components.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this unit compatible with my existing coil? +
Compatibility depends on your evaporator coil specifications. This 36,000 BTU condenser works with matched coils using identical refrigerant (R454B), liquid and suction line sizes (3/8-inch and 3/4-inch), and tonnage rating. Mismatched equipment reduces efficiency and voids warranty. Consult your existing coil documentation or have a technician verify compatibility before purchasing.
Will this fit in my outdoor unit pad? +
At 24.5 inches wide, 22 inches tall, and 21 inches deep with 120 pounds weight, this unit is compact for a 3-ton condenser. Most outdoor pads accommodating older 3-ton units will handle it. Measure your available space and confirm clearance for service access on all sides, typically 24-30 inches around the unit.
How much will this cost to run annually? +
Operating cost depends on your local electricity rate and cooling hours. A 36,000 BTU unit running 1,200 annual cooling hours at 14 SEER consumes roughly 3,086 kWh yearly. At $0.13 per kWh average, expect approximately $401 annually. Actual costs vary significantly by climate and thermostat settings.
Should I choose this or a higher SEER model? +
A 16-18 SEER unit saves 12-28 percent on cooling energy but typically costs $200-400 more upfront. In mild climates with moderate cooling demand, payback takes 5-8 years. In hot climates with extended seasons, payback accelerates to 2-4 years. Lower SEER saves cash upfront if you plan to move within five years.
What does online registration include? +
Online registration activates your 10-year warranty coverage. It typically takes 5-10 minutes and requires proof of purchase. Registration validates eligibility for manufacturer support and component replacement claims, though some contractors report the process is straightforward.
