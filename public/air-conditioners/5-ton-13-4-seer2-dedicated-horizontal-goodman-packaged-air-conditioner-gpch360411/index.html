Review

The Goodman 5-ton GPCH36041 package unit represents solid mid-market positioning in the residential cooling sector. Priced at $4,717, it addresses contractors replacing aging equipment and homeowners seeking straightforward cooling solutions without premium costs. This horizontal configuration delivers 57,500 BTU cooling capacity with R410A refrigerant, fitting applications from moderate to large homes across diverse climates.

Performance metrics show this unit delivers practical real-world cooling. The 13.4 SEER2 efficiency rating meets current regulatory standards and provides measurable improvements over older 10-11 SEER equipment. Two-stage compressor design enables modulation between 50 and 100 percent capacity, reducing runtime on mild days and improving equipment longevity. Maximum airflow reaches 1,600 CFM with manageable 80 decibel operation, though this runs slightly louder than premium-tier competitors.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 13.4 SEER2 efficiency rating exceeds baseline SEER2 13 requirement, reducing monthly cooling costs approximately 5-8% compared to minimum standard units
  • Two-stage scroll compressor adjusts capacity based on demand, improving comfort and extending equipment lifespan versus single-stage competitors
  • Horizontal configuration fits tight spaces where vertical units won't work, expanding installation possibilities in attics and mechanical closets
  • Single-phase 208/230v operation simplifies electrical requirements for standard residential service panels

Cons

Installation

This horizontal unit requires proper placement in attics, basements, or mechanical closets with adequate clearance for airflow and service access. Electrical connection demands a 60-amp breaker on standard 208/230v single-phase service, eliminating three-phase complications common in commercial equipment. Professional installation with proper refrigerant charging remains essential for warranty coverage and optimal efficiency.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides a 10-year parts warranty when registered online within 30 days of installation. This covers compressor and major components, offering reasonable protection for equipment at this price point. Registration is straightforward and must be completed promptly, so contractors should guide homeowners through the process immediately.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 13.4 SEER2 compare to other 5-ton units in this price range? +
At 13.4 SEER2, this unit meets 2023 federal minimums and ranks mid-tier for packaged units. Budget units hit 13.0 SEER2 while premium options reach 15-16 SEER2. The difference translates to roughly $100-200 annually in cooling costs for typical 4-ton homes in moderate climates.
Can this horizontal unit retrofit my existing horizontal setup? +
Yes, the GPCH36041 replaces older GPH1460M4E and GPC1460H41 models directly. Verify your existing ductwork accommodates 1,600 CFM maximum airflow and confirm electrical service supports 60-amp breaker requirements before ordering.
Will the 80 decibel noise level bother neighbors or homeowners? +
At 80 dB, operation registers as loud as heavy traffic or lawnmowers. In quiet suburban settings, this becomes noticeable during peak summer days. Quieter models exist in the 73-75 dB range if noise sensitivity matters significantly.
Is R410A refrigerant still the best choice or should I wait for newer options? +
R410A remains EPA-approved and widely serviced, but the industry shifts toward R-454B and similar low-GWP alternatives. This unit will remain serviceable for 15-20 years, though technician availability for older refrigerants may eventually decline.
What happens to my warranty if I don't register within 30 days? +
Registration within 30 days is required for the full 10-year coverage. Without registration, you likely default to manufacturer's limited warranty terms. Always register promptly after installation to activate full protection.
