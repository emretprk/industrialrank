Review

The Goodman CHPF4860D6 represents the practical middle ground in residential HVAC systems. This 4-ton horizontal unit combines an 80,000 BTU furnace with a 47,000 BTU air conditioner, earning ENERGY STAR certification without commanding premium prices. Manufactured by Goodman, a brand with decades of contractor relationships, it's built for replacement jobs where space is tight and budgets are tight. The horizontal configuration makes it ideal for attic or crawlspace installations across climate zones.

Performance-wise, the 80% AFUE furnace delivers moderate heating efficiency with two-stage burner control that reduces short-cycling and energy waste. The cooling side operates at 17 SEER with two-stage compressor modulation and a scroll-type compressor. Variable speed blower technology ranges from 0 to 2,000 CFM maximum, allowing the system to match load demand instead of running constantly at full power. Electrical requirements are reasonable at 208/230V single phase, with breaker sizing between 29.3 and 50 amps depending on installation specifics.

Installation

This unit requires standard furnace ductwork, a metal exhaust flue for combustion venting, and proper refrigerant line sizing. The horizontal configuration suits attics and crawlspaces better than vertical installations. Sweat valve connections on the 3/8-inch liquid line and 7/8-inch suction line require competent soldering or professional installation. Total unit weight of 77 pounds is manageable for two technicians.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this system with a 10-year parts warranty following online registration, which is standard for this price tier but not exceptional. Homeowners transferring ownership receive five-year coverage, making this less attractive for speculative investors. The warranty covers compressor and scroll components, core strengths of Goodman's manufacturing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 80% AFUE sufficient for my climate, or should I upgrade? +
80% AFUE is adequate for moderate climates but wasteful in cold regions. If you heat 6+ months yearly, consider 90-95% AFUE systems that recover exhaust heat. Your energy savings over 15 years could offset higher initial cost by $3,000 to $5,000 in many northern states.
Will this fit my attic space and existing ductwork? +
At 26 inches wide and 24.5 inches tall, it fits most residential attics. However, verify your existing ductwork matches 4-ton capacity. Undersized ducts restrict the 2,000 CFM maximum airflow, reducing both heating and cooling performance. Professional duct evaluation before purchase prevents costly problems.
How does 17 SEER compare to newer units I'm seeing at 20+ SEER? +
17 SEER is respectable but moderate. A 20 SEER system costs $300 to $600 more but uses 15% less cooling energy. In hot climates with 8+ month cooling seasons, the higher-rated unit typically pays back within seven to ten years through reduced electricity bills.
Can I use this with my current thermostat, or do I need an upgrade? +
Compatibility depends on your existing thermostat. Two-stage heating and cooling require a programmable or smart thermostat with sufficient terminals. Older single-stage thermostats limit the system to full-capacity operation, defeating energy-saving modulation. Budget $150 to $300 for thermostat replacement if needed.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover if something fails? +
The 10-year warranty covers manufacturing defects in compressor, scroll mechanism, and core components after online registration. It does not cover labor, refrigerant recharging, or installation errors. Read the AHRI certificate and full documentation before purchase to understand exclusions.
