Review

This Goodman horizontal gas furnace and air conditioner combination addresses a specific market need: compact installations where traditional upright equipment simply won't fit. With 100,000 BTU heating output and 92% AFUE efficiency, it bridges the gap between budget-conscious contractors and homeowners seeking reliable performance without flagship pricing. The 2.5-ton cooling capacity with 14 SEER rating places it squarely in the mid-market segment, where most residential replacements happen.

Performance data shows solid fundamentals for the price point. The 92% AFUE furnace efficiency beats government minimum standards by a meaningful margin, translating to approximately 8 percent lower heating costs versus 80% AFUE models. The 14 SEER cooling rating delivers decent seasonal efficiency, though single-stage compressor operation means the system runs at full capacity rather than modulating output. Multi-speed blower capability and PVC exhaust venting streamline installation in modern homes lacking traditional chimneys or adequate vertical space.

Installation

This horizontal configuration requires minimal ductwork reconfiguration, making retrofits simpler than swapping to vertical models. Installation demands 208/230V single-phase power, 17.1 to 25 amp breaker sizing, and standard refrigerant line connections using 3/8-inch liquid and 3/4-inch suction lines. PVC flue venting from the right-side exhaust simplifies exhaust routing compared to traditional metal vent pipes, reducing material costs and installation time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the SKU show 3.5 tons when the title says 2.5 tons? +
This specification discrepancy appears in the manufacturer data, suggesting a potential cooling capacity mismatch. The 30,000 BTU cooling output listed aligns with 2.5-ton nominal capacity, while specifications reference 3.5-ton equipment. Verify exact tonnage with your supplier before ordering to ensure proper system sizing.
Will this furnace fit in my attic with limited headroom? +
At 21 inches tall, 21.125 inches wide, and 26 inches deep, this horizontal unit works in confined spaces where vertical furnaces cannot fit. Confirm your available space dimensions and that joists can support the 22-pound weight before installation.
How does 14 SEER compare to modern air conditioners? +
14 SEER represents solid mid-range cooling efficiency. Current premium units reach 18-20 SEER ratings, but those cost significantly more. For most homeowners, 14 SEER provides adequate savings versus older 10-12 SEER systems without premium pricing, typically reducing cooling costs by 15-20 percent.
Is single-stage operation going to waste energy compared to two-stage systems? +
Yes, single-stage burners run at full 100,000 BTU output constantly, cycling on and off rather than modulating. Two-stage units run at partial capacity most of the time, reducing energy waste and wear. The efficiency gap typically costs $100-200 annually in heating expenses versus comparable two-stage models.
Does the 10-year warranty cover labor costs? +
The warranty description specifies 10 years with registration but doesn't explicitly state labor coverage terms. Contact the seller or Goodman directly to confirm whether labor costs are included, as many warranties cover parts only.
