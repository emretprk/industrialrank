Review

The Goodman 2-ton combo system represents the practical middle ground in HVAC purchasing. At $643, this unit pairs a 92% AFUE furnace with a 14 SEER air conditioner in a straightforward upflow configuration. The R454B refrigerant reflects current environmental standards, and Energy Star certification qualifies buyers for utility rebates in most regions. This system targets cost-conscious homeowners and rental property investors who prioritize reliability and reasonable efficiency without premium pricing.

Performance numbers are solid across the board. The furnace delivers 24,000 BTU heating output with single-stage burner operation, while the AC condenser matches at 24,000 BTU cooling. The 14 SEER rating sits above entry-level units and the 92% AFUE furnace efficiency beats standard 80% equipment by a meaningful margin on heating bills. A multi-speed blower motor allows better airflow management than fixed-speed alternatives, and the 70 dB noise level keeps operation relatively quiet despite the single-stage compressor design.

Installation

Installation requires standard upflow/horizontal mounting with PVC exhaust flue, both common in residential retrofits. The scroll compressor and sweat-valve connections use industry-standard tooling. Electrical demand sits between 17.1 and 25 amps at 208/230V single-phase, fitting most residential panels without upgrades. The 30-inch height and compact footprint work well in basements and utility closets. Field technicians should reference the CAPF installation manual provided.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year parts and labor warranty activates upon online registration, which is typical for this price tier. Coverage includes the compressor, blower motor, and all major components. Registration is straightforward and extends protection meaningfully compared to 5-year baseline warranties on cheaper alternatives. This represents fair protection for a system expected to last 15-20 years with proper maintenance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet list both Goodman and ACiQ branding? +
ACiQ is Goodman's value-oriented sub-brand used for direct-sale and contractor-specific inventory. The units share identical manufacturing standards and warranty coverage. This particular SKU appears in both catalogs but delivers the same equipment and support regardless of labeling approach.
Will this fit in my basement if I have limited space? +
At 30 inches tall, 17.5 inches wide, and 21 inches deep, it fits most residential utility spaces. The upflow configuration works for basement or closet installation with adequate clearance for service access. Verify your current furnace footprint and allow 6 inches on sides for airflow and maintenance.
How much will this save on utility bills compared to my 15-year-old equipment? +
Older furnaces typically run 75-80% AFUE and AC units at 8-10 SEER. This unit's 92% AFUE and 14 SEER should reduce heating costs 15-20% and cooling costs 25-35% annually, depending on climate and usage patterns. Actual savings vary by local energy rates and thermostat habits.
How does this compare to higher-priced Goodman two-stage models? +
Two-stage units add 2-4 SEER points and run more efficiently at partial load, but cost $1,000-1,500 more. Single-stage works fine in moderate climates. Choose two-stage if you experience extreme temperature swings or need premium comfort control during shoulder seasons.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Parts and labor on compressor, blower, heat exchanger, and coil defects. Does not cover wear items like filters or refrigerant loss from leaks outside manufacturing defect. Online registration is required within 30 days of installation to activate full coverage.
