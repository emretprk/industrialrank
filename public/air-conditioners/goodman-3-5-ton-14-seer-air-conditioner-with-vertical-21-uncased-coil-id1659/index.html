Review

The ACiQ 3.5 Ton 14 SEER air conditioner with vertical 21-inch uncased coil fills a specific niche in the residential HVAC market. This is a straightforward, no-frills condenser designed for retrofit applications where space constraints make traditional horizontal coil mounting impractical. ACiQ positions this unit as a value-conscious alternative to name-brand equipment, targeting contractors who need reliable cooling without premium pricing.

Performance specs tell a practical story here. The unit delivers 42,000 BTU of cooling capacity with a SEER rating of 14, placing it solidly in the mid-efficiency range. It runs on 208 or 230V single-phase power at 60 Hz, requiring a 22 to 35 amp breaker depending on circuit configuration. The scroll compressor operates single-stage, meaning it cycles on and off rather than modulating capacity, which simplifies operation but reduces efficiency during partial-load conditions common in spring and fall.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Vertical 21-inch uncased coil design fits tight attic and closet spaces where standard horizontal coils won't work
  • 42,000 BTU cooling capacity at 14 SEER efficiency meets Energy Star standards for mid-range residential applications
  • Single-stage scroll compressor is proven, reliable, and requires less complex refrigerant tubing than variable-stage units
  • 208/230V single-phase electrical requirement works with standard residential wiring, no three-phase conversion needed
  • 10-year compressor warranty with online registration provides solid coverage for homeowner peace of mind

Cons

  • 73 dB noise level is noticeably louder than many modern units, especially during peak cooling season
  • Single-stage compressor runs at full capacity constantly rather than modulating, reducing efficiency during partial-load conditions
  • Not Energy Star certified, meaning it misses out on utility rebates available in some regions

Who Should Buy

Best For

Contractors upgrading older systems in homes with limited mechanical space, particularly those with tight attics or utility closets where a vertical coil orientation is essential. Also suitable for homeowners in moderate climates where 14 SEER efficiency provides good value without premium pricing.

Consider Alternatives If

Skip this unit if you need Energy Star certification for rebate eligibility, live in a noise-sensitive area, or want variable-capacity cooling for better part-load efficiency.

Installation

Installation complexity is moderate. The vertical 21-inch coil design requires downflow or upflow mounting in confined spaces, which actually speeds installation compared to traditional horizontal arrangements. The 3/8-inch liquid line and 7/8-inch suction line use standard refrigerant tubing with 3/8-inch insulation. Most experienced contractors can complete rough-in and final connections within standard labor windows. Pre-charged coil requires careful handling to avoid refrigerant loss.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this unit with a 10-year compressor warranty when you register online, which is competitive for equipment at this price point. The warranty covers parts and labor on the scroll compressor and internal components. Full system coverage details depend on your installer's workmanship warranty. Registration is straightforward but required to activate the longer term.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is this unit cheaper than typical Goodman equipment? +
ACiQ is Goodman's value brand, cutting costs through simpler single-stage compressor design and skipping Energy Star certification. You lose variable capacity and rebate eligibility, but gain reliable equipment at lower upfront cost. Perfect for price-conscious contractors managing margins.
Will this vertical coil work with my existing furnace? +
Yes, the A-coil vertical design mounts in upflow or downflow positions. Verify your ductwork clearance is 20 inches wide by 20 1/8 inches deep. The 30-foot suction line insulation provides flex for most installations, though longer runs may require additional insulation.
How does 14 SEER compare to modern units? +
14 SEER is mid-range efficiency in 2024. Modern units reach 16-21 SEER, but those cost 30-50% more. This unit delivers solid efficiency gains over pre-2010 equipment, typically reducing cooling costs by 20-30% compared to older single-stage systems.
Is the 73 dB noise level acceptable? +
At 73 dB, this unit is noticeably louder during operation, equivalent to a typical lawnmower at distance. For noise-sensitive locations, consider variable-speed units rated 68-70 dB. Placement away from windows and bedrooms helps minimize noise complaints.
Does online registration automatically activate the warranty? +
Yes, registration through Goodman's portal activates the 10-year compressor coverage. Complete registration within 30 days of installation. Without registration, coverage drops to the standard manufacturer's limited term, so don't skip this step.
