Review

The ACiQ 2-ton split system represents Goodman's entry-level offering for homeowners balancing budget constraints against efficiency gains. At $2,466, this unit delivers solid performance in replacement scenarios where the existing infrastructure supports downflow installation. The combination of a 60,000 BTU furnace and 24,000 BTU air conditioner targets small homes and condos in moderate climates. Its Energy Star certification signals compliance with current federal standards, making it a practical choice for contractors and DIY replacements.

Performance metrics justify the mid-market positioning. The furnace's 96% AFUE efficiency ranks above basic 80-92% models, translating to measurable annual savings on natural gas or propane heating. On the cooling side, 13 SEER1 meets Energy Star thresholds but represents baseline efficiency rather than premium performance. The variable-speed ECM blower adjusts from idle to 1,200 CFM, reducing compressor cycling on mild days. Two-stage burner control prevents the temperature swings common in single-stage furnaces, though the single-stage compressor cannot similarly modulate cooling.

Installation

This downflow A-coil unit requires 115V single-phase power with 10.3 to 15-amp breaker sizing, typical for residential installations. Dimensions of 34.5 inches tall, 17.5 inches wide, and 28.75 inches deep fit standard closet or attic spaces. Sweat valve connections and 3/8-inch liquid line plus 3/4-inch suction line interface with standard copper runs. PVC/plastic exhaust flue requirement simplifies venting versus metal pipe in condensing furnaces. Most HVAC technicians complete installation in 4-6 hours on straightforward changeouts.

Warranty & Reliability

ACiQ backs this unit with 10 years parts coverage upon online registration, matching or exceeding typical Goodman warranty terms. Registration is straightforward and crucial, as unregistered units default to 1-year coverage. The 10-year window covers compressor failure, blower motor defects, and heat exchanger cracks. Labor costs and refrigerant charges fall outside warranty scope. This duration protects against premature component failure but doesn't cover wear items like capacitors or contacts.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this system qualify for federal tax credits or utility rebates? +
Energy Star certification opens doors for some utility company rebates, typically $200-400. Federal HVAC tax credits require 16+ SEER or 8+ HSPF ratings, which this 13 SEER unit does not meet. Check your local utility and state programs for incentives on 96% AFUE furnaces specifically.
Can I install this myself if I'm handy with tools? +
EPA refrigerant handling laws require EPA Section 608 certification for anyone touching R454B lines. Electrical and gas connections also demand licensed work in most jurisdictions. Hire a licensed HVAC contractor to avoid warranty voidance and legal liability.
How does 13 SEER compare to newer equipment I'm seeing advertised? +
13 SEER meets federal minimum standards but lags premium units at 16-20 SEER. In moderate climates, the cost gap ($3,000-5,000 more) takes 8-12 years to recover through energy savings. This unit suits cost-conscious buyers prioritizing upfront budget over maximum efficiency.
Why is the air conditioner single-stage when the furnace is two-stage? +
Two-stage compressors add $500-800 to manufacturing cost. Single-stage models cycle fully on or off, less efficient on 70-degree days but sufficient in moderate climates. Cold regions rarely stress cooling, justifying Goodman's cost-cutting here.
What happens if I lose my online registration proof for warranty? +
Contact ACiQ directly with your serial number and proof of purchase (receipt, invoice). Replacement registration is possible but slower. Keep receipts and digital confirmations accessible for the full 10-year period.
