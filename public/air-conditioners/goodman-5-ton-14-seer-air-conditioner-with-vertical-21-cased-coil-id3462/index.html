Review

The ACiQ 5-ton air conditioner with vertical cased coil occupies a practical middle ground in the residential cooling market. This unit pairs established Goodman reliability with modern R454B refrigerant, avoiding phase-out pressures while maintaining compatibility with most existing systems. At 14 SEER efficiency, it satisfies baseline performance standards without commanding premium pricing, making it attractive to contractors managing multiple installations and homeowners balancing cooling needs against upfront costs.

Performance specs reveal a straightforward workhorse. The 60,000 BTU output handles typical 5-ton loads, powered by a single-stage scroll compressor running at 208/230V single-phase. At 75 dBA, it's moderately quiet but noticeably louder than variable-capacity competitors. The vertical 21-inch A-coil design excels in tight spaces, measuring 30 inches tall with 3/8-inch suction line insulation. Electrical demands stay reasonable, requiring minimum 32.8 amps and maximum 50 amps breaker capacity.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does 14 SEER mean for my cooling costs? +
14 SEER indicates cooling efficiency where the unit removes 14 BTU per watt-hour. For a 5-ton system, expect seasonal cooling costs approximately 20-30% higher than 16+ SEER units. In moderate climates, this translates to roughly $200-400 additional annual electricity expense versus premium-efficiency equipment.
Will this coil fit my existing furnace cabinet? +
The vertical 21-inch A-coil measures 30 inches tall and 21 inches wide. Verify your cabinet accepts A-coil vertical orientation in upflow or downflow configuration. Most standard residential cabinets accommodate this size, but measure before ordering. The included CAPT installation manual specifies exact clearance requirements.
Why use R454B instead of older refrigerants? +
R454B replaces R410A as EPA regulations phase out high-GWP refrigerants. It delivers nearly identical performance while reducing environmental impact. Most modern compressors, including this scroll unit, run efficiently on R454B. Existing R410A systems require conversion work, but new installations specify R454B exclusively.
How does single-stage cooling compare to variable-capacity systems? +
Single-stage runs full capacity or off, cycling on-and-off as needed. Variable systems modulate output smoothly, delivering superior temperature control and efficiency. Single-stage works fine for stable climates but produces more temperature swings in extreme heat. This unit suits moderate regions better than desert or humid subtropical climates.
Is online registration required for the 10-year warranty? +
Yes, registering the unit online at Goodman's portal activates the full 10-year coverage. Without registration, warranty periods may be significantly reduced. Registration takes minutes with proof of purchase and installation date. Most contractors handle this immediately post-installation.
