Review

The Goodman 2-ton gas furnace and air conditioner system represents a practical mid-tier solution for homeowners balancing efficiency upgrades with realistic budgets. With 60,000 BTU heating capacity and 24,000 BTU cooling output, it handles typical two to three-ton cooling loads in moderate climates. The unit combines a 96% AFUE furnace with an Energy Star qualified 14 SEER air conditioner, delivering noticeable savings over 10-year-old equipment while maintaining straightforward installation and service requirements.

Performance specifications emphasize efficiency without premium features. The furnace operates at 96% AFUE with two-stage burners, reducing cycle losses and improving comfort during partial-load heating periods. The air conditioner achieves 14 SEER1 efficiency using R454B refrigerant and a rotary compressor in single-stage configuration. The multi-speed ECM blower motor provides 1200 CFM maximum airflow, enabling better air distribution than basic single-speed alternatives while consuming less electricity during operation.

Installation

This horizontal A-coil unit requires PVC exhaust flue and fits upflow or horizontal configurations. Dimensions of 34.5 inches high, 17.5 inches wide, and 28.75 inches deep work in confined spaces. At 120 pounds, it requires two technicians for safe handling. Suction line needs 3/4-inch insulation over 50 feet maximum distance. Electrical requirements are straightforward, 115V single phase 60 Hz with 14.3 to 25-amp breaker sizing.

Warranty & Reliability

Coverage includes lifetime heat exchanger warranty, 10-year unit replacement, and 10-year parts coverage with online registration. The lifetime heat exchanger protection is significant because this component typically costs 40-60 percent of furnace replacement. Remaining warranties are industry-standard for mid-tier equipment. Online registration requirement is simple but essential for full coverage activation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 14 SEER cooling compare to older systems, and what savings can I expect? +
A 14 SEER air conditioner uses approximately 35-40 percent less electricity than 10 SEER models from 2010-2012. Annual cooling cost savings depend on local electricity rates and runtime, but homeowners typically see $200-400 annual reductions in moderate climates, paying back equipment cost in 8-12 years.
Is this system compatible with my existing ductwork and thermostat? +
Yes, this unit works with standard ductwork and any conventional or programmable thermostat. The 1200 CFM airflow rate fits most residential systems. Verify your current ducts are sized for 2-ton capacity, minimum 5-inch equivalent diameter main trunk recommended.
What's the difference between 14 SEER and SEER2, and does this unit meet current efficiency standards? +
14 SEER1 converts to approximately 11.2 SEER2 under newer test procedures. This system meets 2023 DOE standards for non-cold-climate zones and earns Energy Star certification, though SEER2 was the official metric beginning January 2023.
How does single-stage cooling affect efficiency and comfort compared to variable-capacity systems? +
Single-stage units cycle on and off rather than modulating capacity, using more energy during mild weather and creating temperature swings. Variable-capacity models maintain steadier temperatures and achieve 16-18 SEER ratings, but cost 30-40 percent more, making them better for humid climates.
What does the lifetime heat exchanger warranty actually cover, and how do I activate it? +
This warranty replaces the furnace heat exchanger at no cost if failure occurs from manufacturing defects. Register online within 30 days of installation activation. Labor costs for replacement are your responsibility, but the exchanger itself, typically 40-60 percent of furnace cost, is fully covered.
