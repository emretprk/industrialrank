Review

The ACiQ 5-ton horizontal slab coil condenser represents a practical entry-level solution for residential cooling in the competitive 13 SEER segment. Designed as a drop-in replacement for six Goodman legacy models, this unit eliminates compatibility guesswork for contractors managing fleet replacements. Operating at 60,000 BTU cooling capacity with a single-stage reciprocating compressor, it delivers consistent performance without the complexity of variable-stage systems. The horizontal slab configuration addresses a real installation challenge, making it suitable for basements, crawl spaces, and clearance-restricted locations where vertical condensers won't fit.

Performance metrics confirm solid mid-range capability. The 13 SEER1 efficiency rating translates to approximately 13 BTU of cooling output per watt-hour consumed, positioning it favorably against older R410A units while meeting current minimum efficiency standards. Running on R454B refrigerant with a 3/8-inch liquid line and 7/8-inch suction line, the unit integrates smoothly into standard residential systems. At 77 dB, noise output sits slightly above quiet-operation thresholds but remains acceptable for most residential applications. Electrical requirements of 208/230V single-phase with a 32.8 to 45 amp breaker range align with typical residential service panels.

Installation

Installation is straightforward for experienced HVAC technicians. The 120-pound unit weighs less than comparable vertical condensers, simplifying logistics and reducing crew strain. Sweat-valve connections using standard 3/8-inch and 7/8-inch line sizes require basic brazing skills and proper nitrogen purging. The horizontal slab design means careful leveling during placement, preventing oil accumulation in the compressor. Pre-charge verification and refrigerant line insulation (3/8-inch thickness specified) are essential for system efficiency. Allow 30 feet of lineset on the included tubing for typical residential routing.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year parts warranty, activated through free online registration, provides solid coverage against manufacturing defects on compressor and major components. This extended protection is valuable given reciprocating compressor reliability in single-stage applications. However, the warranty excludes labor costs and field service, so budget separately for technician time if repairs become necessary during the coverage period.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will this condenser work with my existing Goodman system? +
Yes, if your current unit is one of these models: GSX130603, GSX130604, GSX130605, GSX130611, GSX130613, or GSX130614. The ACiQ replacement maintains identical refrigerant connections and mounting footprints. Verify your model number on the nameplate before ordering to ensure proper compatibility.
Is the 30-foot lineset included with the condenser? +
The specifications list 30 feet of suction line insulation thickness (3/8-inch), indicating standard lineset is provided. However, confirm with your supplier whether additional lineset beyond 30 feet incurs extra charges for longer installations.
How does 13 SEER1 compare to newer high-efficiency units? +
13 SEER1 represents baseline modern efficiency (meets 2023 federal minimums). Premium units reach 16-18 SEER2. For climates with moderate cooling demand, the 13 SEER1 provides acceptable annual operating costs while keeping upfront equipment investment lower than high-efficiency alternatives.
Can I use this unit to replace an older R410A condenser? +
Yes, but you'll need to replace the entire refrigerant system. R454B requires different service procedures and is not interchangeable with R410A. Plan for system evacuation, flushing, and a complete refrigerant changeover with proper EPA certification and documentation.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Parts and manufacturing defects on compressor and sealed components. Labor, service calls, and field repairs are excluded. Register online after installation to activate coverage and retain proof of purchase for warranty claims.
