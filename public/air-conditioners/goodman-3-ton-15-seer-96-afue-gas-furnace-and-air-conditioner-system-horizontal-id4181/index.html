Review

Goodman's horizontal furnace targets the contractor segment that installs in attics, basements, and tight utility closets where conventional upflow cabinets cannot fit. This 100,000 BTU unit delivers solid mid-range efficiency and dual-fuel flexibility at a direct-supply price point. The horizontal configuration with right-side flue exit suits retrofit applications where ductwork already runs horizontally. Energy Star certification and two-stage burner technology position this model competitively for homeowners upgrading aging 80% AFUE systems.

The 96% AFUE rating represents a meaningful jump in heating efficiency, translating to roughly 20-25% lower winter fuel bills than standard 80% units over a five-year span. The multi-speed ECM blower motor adjusts output between 600 and 1600 CFM based on heating demand, reducing overshooting and maintaining more consistent room temperatures. Two-stage burner operation runs at reduced capacity during mild weather, cutting short-cycle waste. However, spec sheets list both SEER 16 and a mismatched 3.5-ton cooling capacity alongside the 3-ton furnace designation, creating uncertainty about matched system sizing.

Installation

Horizontal mounting requires secure bracket support on joists or beams to handle the 30-pound weight and vibration loads. PVC/plastic flue venting simplifies roof or wall termination without requiring expensive masonry work. The unit connects to standard 208/230V single-phase 60Hz electrical service with breaker sizing between 18.7 and 30 amps. Refrigerant lines come in 3/8-inch liquid and 7/8-inch suction sizes with 3/4-inch insulation, standard for residential coil pairs.

Warranty & Reliability

A 10-year parts and compressor warranty applies with online registration, a straightforward protection plan common among Goodman equipment. Online registration is required, so buyers must complete this step within the specified timeframe to activate coverage. The warranty covers manufacturing defects but excludes damage from improper installation or maintenance neglect. This coverage aligns with contractor expectations for mid-range residential furnaces.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much can I save annually with 96% AFUE compared to my old 80% furnace? +
A 96% AFUE unit recovers 16% more heat from fuel than 80% equipment. For a typical home burning 60,000 BTU annually, you'll save roughly 10,000 BTU worth of gas yearly. Actual savings depend on regional gas prices and heating degree days, but expect 15-25% monthly winter bill reductions in moderate climates.
Can I install this horizontally in my attic, and what clearance do I need? +
Yes, the horizontal design fits attics with 26-inch depth clearance. Ensure secure joist support for the 30-pound unit, allow 6-12 inches around flue and ductwork, and verify attic ventilation prevents backdrafting. Most jurisdictions require 1-inch clearance from combustibles, so confirm local codes before installation.
Does this furnace work well in cold climates, or is it limited to mild regions? +
The 100,000 BTU output suits homes in ASHRAE zones 4-6 (moderate climates). Two-stage operation optimizes efficiency during shoulder seasons. Colder regions (zones 1-3) may require larger tonnage or supplemental heating. Consult a load calculation to match furnace size to your climate zone and home envelope.
What's the difference between SEER 16 and SEER2, and does the spec sheet say SEER1 or SEER2? +
The spec lists SEER1 16, an older rating standard. SEER2 values are roughly 20% lower than SEER1 under stricter testing. This unit's actual SEER2 rating likely sits around 12-13. Check the Goodman specification sheet to confirm the cooling coil pairing supports this efficiency rating.
Does the 10-year warranty include labor, or is it parts only? +
The warranty covers parts and compressor for 10 years with online registration. Labor costs fall on the homeowner or contractor. Some retailers offer extended labor warranties separately, so ask your installer about additional coverage options before signing.
