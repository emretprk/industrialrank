Review

This ACiQ combo system represents a straightforward approach to home climate control by bundling a gas furnace and air conditioner into one integrated package. Goodman designed this unit to appeal to contractors and homeowners who prefer single-vendor solutions with matching AHRI certification (7424268). The 5-ton capacity handles most homes efficiently without oversizing, and the upflow configuration works in basements and crawlspaces. It's a practical choice when you need both heating and cooling without chasing cutting-edge features.

The efficiency specs justify the name. The furnace delivers 97% AFUE, which converts nearly all natural gas or propane into usable heat, translating to measurable savings on winter bills. On the cooling side, 14 SEER performance meets ENERGY STAR standards and qualifies you for utility rebates in many regions. The modulating burner and variable-speed 2000 CFM blower work together to avoid the energy waste of old on-off systems that cycle constantly.

Installation

Installation requires standard HVAC practices with no unusual complexity. The unit accepts 3/8 inch liquid lines and 7/8 inch suction lines, both common residential sizes. PVC or plastic flue venting simplifies ductwork changes compared to older metal vents. Electrical setup is straightforward at 115V, 1-phase 60 Hz, requiring a 32.8 to 50 amp breaker. Most contractors complete installation in one day.

Warranty & Reliability

Ten years of coverage applies when you register online after installation. This protects parts and labor for both the furnace and air conditioner components. Frequently Asked Questions

Why is this system cheaper than separate furnace and AC units? +
Combined units share electrical components, ductwork connections, and installation labor, reducing total cost by 15-25%. You buy one thermostat, one warranty, and one service agreement. Contractors prefer bundled systems for cleaner installations and simpler troubleshooting.
Can this furnace work with propane instead of natural gas? +
Yes, the specs explicitly list propane as a fuel option. Your installer converts the burner during setup by changing the orifice plate, a ten-minute job. Both fuels achieve the same 97% AFUE efficiency and 120,000 BTU output.
Is 14 SEER enough for modern cooling standards? +
14 SEER meets ENERGY STAR requirements and qualifies for rebates. It's not the absolute highest rating, but it represents solid efficiency for its price point. Homes with extreme heat or large square footage may benefit from 16+ SEER models instead.
How does this compare to a high-end two-stage system? +
High-end units run quieter and maintain temperatures more precisely through multi-stage operation. This single-stage model cycles on-off more frequently, using slightly more electricity but costing 20-30% less upfront. It's better value than premium if you prioritize budget over perfect comfort.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Parts and labor for manufacturing defects in the furnace and condenser. Excludes wear items, refrigerant leaks from poor installation, and damage from improper maintenance. Online registration is required, so register immediately after installation.
