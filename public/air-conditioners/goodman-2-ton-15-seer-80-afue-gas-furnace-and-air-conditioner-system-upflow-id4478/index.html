Review

Goodman's gas furnace and air conditioner system represents the practical middle ground for HVAC upgrades. This unit combines a 60,000 BTU heating capacity with 23,600 BTU cooling output, configurable for upflow or downflow installation. At the 2-3 ton cooling range with Energy Star qualification, it appeals to contractors working on standard residential replacements where customers want efficiency gains over legacy equipment without paying for premium features.

The heating side delivers 80% AFUE through a two-stage burner design, meaning partial-load efficiency during shoulder seasons outperforms single-stage competitors. On cooling, the 16 SEER1 rating with R410A or R22 refrigerant compatibility provides respectable seasonal performance. The high-efficiency multi-speed blower motor adjusts airflow from base to 1,200 CFM maximum, reducing temperature swings. At 71.5 dBA operating noise, the scroll compressor runs comparatively quiet for residential settings.

Pros & Cons

Installation

Installation requires 120V single-phase 60Hz power with 12.4 to 20-amp breaker sizing. The unit accepts 3/8 inch liquid lines and 3/4 inch suction lines with 3/4 inch insulation thickness supplied (up to 50 feet). Metal exhaust flue is mandatory. Upflow configuration fits standard vertical ductwork layouts. Licensed HVAC technicians handle refrigerant charging and electrical integration in most jurisdictions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the actual tonnage capacity, and does the 2-ton title match the specs? +
Specifications list 3-ton tonnage with 23,600 BTU cooling and 60,000 BTU heating. The product title stating 2-ton appears to be an error or model variant discrepancy. Confirm tonnage with your supplier before ordering to ensure proper sizing for your ductwork.
Can this unit retrofit into existing systems with older refrigerants? +
Yes, it accepts both R410A and R22 refrigerant, allowing flexibility for replacement scenarios. However, EPA regulations may restrict R22 availability in some regions. Discuss refrigerant strategy with your technician during the retrofit assessment.
How does 80% AFUE compare to newer high-efficiency furnaces? +
80% AFUE means 20% of fuel energy escapes as exhaust waste. Modern units reach 95%+ AFUE, saving 15 to 20% on heating costs annually. The 80% model suits replacements where maximum efficiency gains don't justify premium pricing.
Why is the 16 SEER1 rating different from traditional SEER measurements? +
SEER1 uses updated testing standards reflecting real-world variable-speed conditions. While 16 SEER1 is respectable, it's roughly equivalent to 14-15 traditional SEER. Energy Star still recognizes it, confirming efficiency leadership in its category.
Does the 10-year warranty cover everything, or are there exclusions? +
Coverage includes compressor and heat exchanger defects after registration. Labor is typically included for first five years; parts only thereafter. Review the complete warranty document with Online Supply for specific exclusions.
