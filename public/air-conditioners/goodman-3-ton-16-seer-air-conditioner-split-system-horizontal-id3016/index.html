Review

The Goodman 3-ton horizontal split system occupies a solid middle ground in the residential HVAC market. At $89 with 16 SEER efficiency and ENERGY STAR certification, it targets budget-conscious contractors and homeowners who prioritize operational cost savings over premium features. The horizontal A-coil configuration makes it practical for attic installations where space constraints limit vertical options. Goodman's reputation for reliability, while not premium-tier, remains respectable among contractors handling volume installations.

Performance metrics reveal a capable but not exceptional unit. The scroll compressor delivers 36,000 BTU cooling capacity with two-stage operation, meaning it runs at reduced capacity during mild weather for better efficiency. The variable-speed ECM blower motor adapts between 0 and 1,600 CFM maximum output, eliminating the on-off cycling that reduces comfort and efficiency. At 70.4 dBA, it's moderately audible but acceptable for most installations. The R410A refrigerant and 208/230V single-phase electrical design fit standard residential applications.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 16 SEER1 efficiency rating qualifies for ENERGY STAR, reducing operating costs significantly
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor maximizes comfort by modulating airflow based on actual cooling demand
  • Two-stage scroll compressor handles partial-load cooling efficiently, extending equipment lifespan
  • Sweat valve connections simplify installation for experienced techs without special fittings
  • 10-year warranty with online registration provides solid coverage for mid-range equipment
  • Horizontal A-coil configuration works well in attic and closet spaces with limited height

Cons

  • Tonnage discrepancy exists: labeled as 3-ton with 36,000 BTU but specs list 3.5-ton capacity, creating confusion

  • Entry-level Goodman brand lacks the longevity reputation of premium manufacturers like Carrier or Lennox

Who Should Buy

Installation

Installation suits contractors comfortable with standard split-system procedures. The sweat valve connections use 3/8 inch liquid lines and 7/8 inch suction lines, eliminating flange fittings. Electrical requirements demand a 21.8 amp minimum breaker rated for 208/230V single-phase at 60 Hz. The unit weighs 2,500 pounds, requiring proper rigging for attic placement. Horizontal configuration simplifies mounting but demands careful condensate drainage design.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides a 10-year parts warranty when you register online. This covers compressor, components, and refrigerant, though labor is excluded. Registration is mandatory to activate full coverage, so homeowners must complete this immediately after installation. The 10-year window aligns with industry midpoint expectations, offering adequate protection without premium coverage extending beyond typical equipment lifespan.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the specs list both 3-ton and 3.5-ton capacity? +
The product listing shows 36,000 BTU (equivalent to 3-ton) with a tonnage field stating 3.5-ton. This appears to be a data entry error. Contact Goodman directly or verify the installation manual to confirm actual rated capacity, as oversizing or undersizing affects system performance and efficiency.
Can this handle three-phase power in commercial applications? +
No. This unit operates exclusively on 208/230V single-phase at 60 Hz. Commercial installations requiring three-phase power need different equipment. Verify your electrical service before purchasing to avoid compatibility issues and potential project delays.
How does 16 SEER1 compare to newer SEER2 ratings? +
SEER1 ratings typically translate to roughly 15 to 20 percent lower SEER2 values. A 16 SEER1 unit performs approximately 13-14 SEER2 equivalent. While still efficient, compare against newer units using SEER2 standards for accurate efficiency comparison and long-term energy projections.
Is the variable-speed ECM blower worth the premium over single-speed models? +
Yes. Variable-speed operation reduces energy consumption by 10-20 percent versus fixed-speed alternatives, eliminates startup noise, and improves temperature consistency. Over 10 years, energy savings typically recoup the higher initial cost, making it excellent value.
What backup heat options are available? +
Goodman offers optional electric auxiliary heat in 3, 5, 6, 8, 10, and 15 kilowatt stages. Select based on winter demand and electrical capacity. Heat pump operation alone provides moderate heating in mild climates but requires backup for cold regions.
