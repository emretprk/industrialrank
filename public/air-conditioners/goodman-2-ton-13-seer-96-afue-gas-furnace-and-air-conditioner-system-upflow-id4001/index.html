Review

The ACiQ 2-ton combo system pairs Goodman furnace and condenser components into a cohesive heating and cooling solution. Rated at 96% AFUE for heating and 13 SEER1 for cooling, it targets the mid-efficiency segment where builders and contractors balance performance against budget constraints. This upflow configuration fits standard installation spaces, and the 60,000 BTU heating capacity with two-stage burner covers moderate climate zones adequately.

The furnace delivers 60,000 BTU output across two burner stages, allowing it to ramp capacity based on demand rather than cycling on and off constantly. The air conditioner produces 24,000 BTU cooling with a single-stage rotary compressor, which maintains steady output but lacks the efficiency gains of variable-capacity designs. The variable-speed ECM blower reaches 1200 CFM maximum, providing responsive airflow control and reducing unnecessary fan runtime when full capacity isn't needed.

Installation

Installation requires 115V single-phase 60Hz power with a 10.3 to 15-amp breaker, keeping electrical work straightforward for most homes. The upflow design accepts PVC exhaust venting, eliminating costly masonry modifications. Refrigerant lines use 3/8-inch liquid and 3/4-inch suction connections with 50 feet of insulated line included, though professional HVAC installation is strongly recommended for proper charge and commissioning.

Warranty & Reliability

The 10-year parts warranty activates upon online registration and covers manufacturing defects across the furnace, condenser, and blower motor. This is a solid mid-range warranty matching most Goodman-badged systems. Customers should retain receipts and registration documents, as warranty claims require proof of purchase and proper installation by licensed technicians.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between 13 SEER1 and older SEER ratings I've heard about? +
SEER1 is the newer testing standard (as of 2023) reflecting real-world conditions more accurately than legacy SEER. A 13 SEER1 rating is roughly equivalent to a 15-16 SEER under old testing, so this unit is moderately efficient but not premium-tier. It still qualifies for Energy Star.
Can I install this myself or do I need a licensed contractor? +
HVAC systems require EPA-certified technicians for refrigerant handling and proper charge verification. DIY installation voids warranty and risks safety hazards. Hire a licensed HVAC contractor, which typically costs $1500 to $3000 for labor on replacement systems in your market.
Will the two-stage furnace burner actually save money compared to single-stage? +
Yes, two-stage burners run at 60-70% capacity during mild conditions, cutting gas consumption significantly. The 96% AFUE rating already assumes this efficiency gain. Homeowners typically see 15-25% lower heating bills versus old single-stage furnaces, though results vary by climate and thermostat programming.
How does this system compare to a Bryant or Lennox equivalent at similar SEER ratings? +
This Goodman-badged system is competitively priced and reliable, but single-stage compressors lag behind two-stage Lennox or variable-capacity Bryant units in cooling precision. Goodman's strength is affordability and parts availability, not cutting-edge technology. Choose Lennox if comfort matters most, choose this if budget is tight.
What happens after the 10-year warranty expires? +
You're responsible for repair costs, though parts availability remains good due to Goodman's market share. Consider extended warranties from your installer at purchase time, typically $500-$800 for 5-10 additional years of coverage.
