Review

The Goodman 4-ton system reviewed here is a workhorse combination furnace and air conditioner built for residential applications where dependable performance matters more than cutting-edge features. This 5-ton outdoor condenser paired with a 100,000 BTU furnace covers most homes between 1,500 and 2,500 square feet. It replaces the GSX16S481 model and arrives as a complete matched system, simplifying ordering and ensuring component compatibility from day one.

Performance numbers tell a clear story. The 96% AFUE rating on the furnace means 96 of every 100 fuel dollars convert to usable heat, with only 4 percent lost through venting. The 16 SEER2 cooling efficiency rating qualifies for Energy Star certification, delivering approximately 20-25 percent better cooling efficiency than 13 SEER baseline units. Two-stage heating and variable-speed blower operation prevent short-cycling and temperature swings, while the scroll compressor operates at a reasonable 73 decibels.

Installation

This upflow A-coil configuration fits vertical installations common in attics and basements. The 34.5-inch height and 21-inch width pass through standard framing. Suction lines require 1-inch insulation with 50-foot maximum length, so indoor location within 50 feet of the outdoor unit is necessary. Plastic PVC flue venting is required, and the system needs 23.9-40 amp electrical service. Professional installation is highly recommended.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this unit with lifetime coverage on the heat exchanger, 10 years on unit replacement, and 10 years on parts when registered online. The lifetime heat exchanger warranty is substantial and covers the furnace component most likely to fail catastrophically. Registration is straightforward and extends coverage terms significantly compared to non-registered baseline warranties.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 16 SEER2 compare to older 13 SEER units? +
SEER2 uses stricter testing standards than the older SEER rating, so a 16 SEER2 unit typically performs like a 19-20 SEER by legacy measurements. Real-world cooling cost reductions average 20-25 percent compared to 13 SEER equipment, translating to roughly 200-300 dollars annual savings in moderate climates.
Can I install this myself or do I need a contractor? +
Professional installation is essential. Refrigerant charging, electrical connections, gas line work, and flue venting all require licensed technician expertise and local permits. DIY installation voids warranty and creates safety hazards. Budget 1,500-2,500 dollars for professional installation labor.
What's the difference between single-stage and two-stage cooling? +
This unit has single-stage cooling, meaning the compressor runs full capacity or off. Two-stage models modulate capacity on mild days, improving efficiency. For moderate climates where temperatures rarely hit extremes, single-stage performs adequately at lower cost, though variable-capacity units cost more upfront.
Is this system compatible with smart thermostats? +
Yes. The multi-speed ECM blower motor and two-stage burner are fully compatible with modern Wi-Fi thermostats. Installation requires matching the thermostat wiring to the furnished control board connections, a task contractors handle routinely.
What does the lifetime heat exchanger warranty actually cover? +
It covers defects in the heat exchanger itself, including cracks or corrosion, for the equipment's life. Labor is typically not covered unless purchased separately. This protects against the most expensive furnace failure scenario, estimated at 1,500-2,500 dollars in repair costs.
