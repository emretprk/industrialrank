Review

The ACiQ 3.5-ton horizontal cased coil sits in the practical middle of the residential HVAC market. Built to Goodman specifications and SKU'd as ACIQ-4X-EHPD-UPG, this A-coil delivers 42,000 BTU cooling in a compact 24.5-inch height that fits most attic framings without modification. At 13 SEER1 efficiency, it represents a real step up from aging 10-SEER stock, making it the go-to choice for contractors doing bread-and-butter replacements where customers want proven reliability over cutting-edge tech.

Performance specs confirm this is straightforward equipment. The reciprocating compressor operates single-stage, meaning it runs at full capacity or cycles off, with no variable modulation. That approach keeps cost down and service simple, though it sacrifices the fine-tuning efficiency of inverter-driven alternatives. At 75 dB, noise output is moderate and typical for this class. The unit runs on standard 208/230V single-phase 60 Hz residential power, requiring a 23.9 to 40-amp breaker depending on final installation configuration.

Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly is a horizontal cased coil, and where does it go? +
A horizontal cased coil is an evaporator that mounts flat in attic spaces, crawlspaces, or utility closets above the furnace. The 24.5-inch height and A-frame design fit standard joist spacing. Cased means the aluminum fins are fully enclosed in a protective cabinet, ideal for dusty installations.
Can I use this coil with any 3.5-ton condenser, or does it need to be Goodman? +
This coil works with any properly sized R410A condenser rated for 3.5 tons, not just Goodman units. However, matching brands ensures optimal refrigerant charge and superheat tuning. Always verify tonnage and refrigerant type before pairing with aftermarket condensers.
How much does this coil improve energy bills compared to my 10-SEER system? +
Moving from 10 SEER to 13 SEER cuts cooling energy use roughly 23 to 30 percent on average, depending on climate and runtime. In hot regions, expect 50 to 100 dollars monthly savings during peak summer months. Actual results vary by local electricity rates and cooling load.
Why isn't this unit Energy Star certified if it has 13 SEER? +
Energy Star requires 14+ SEER in most climate zones. This 13 SEER coil meets baseline efficiency standards but falls just short of the premium certification threshold. It's still highly efficient for residential use, just not badge-eligible for certain rebate programs.
What happens to my warranty if I don't register online? +
Unregistered units drop from 10 years to 5 years parts and labor coverage. Online registration takes five minutes and is critical to protecting the customer's investment. Most installers complete this at commissioning.
