Review

The Goodman 3.5-ton furnace and AC combo represents intelligent value engineering for homeowners and contractors tired of premium pricing. This upflow configuration with R410A refrigerant checks the boxes for modern HVAC requirements while staying accessible. Online Supply's $647 price point positions it competitively against carrier mid-range equipment. The system comes pre-configured for straightforward installation, making it attractive for retrofit applications where existing ductwork and electrical infrastructure already exist.

Performance metrics show a 92% AFUE furnace delivering 42,000 BTU of heating output, paired with 42,000 BTU cooling capacity and a claimed 14-16 SEER rating. The multi-speed blower motor maxes out at 1,600 CFM, providing flexibility across load conditions. The scroll compressor is proven technology, reliable across millions of units. Energy Star certification and PVC exhaust flue compatibility simplify installation. However, the single-stage burner and condenser mean the system runs in on-off cycles rather than modulating, which can affect comfort consistency during mild weather conditions.

Installation

This upflow unit measures 30 inches high by 21 inches wide by 21 inches deep, fitting standard utility closets and basements. The 208/230V single-phase 60Hz electrical requirement needs a 22.4 to 35-amp breaker. Refrigerant line sizing requires 3/8-inch liquid and 1 1/8-inch suction lines with 1/2-inch insulation, pre-measured to 50 feet. Professional HVAC installation is essential for proper refrigerant charging and electrical setup.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the 92% AFUE rating actually mean for my heating bills? +
AFUE (Annual Fuel Utilization Efficiency) of 92% means 92 cents of every gas dollar becomes usable heat, with 8 cents lost through exhaust. This is solid mid-range efficiency, offering meaningful savings versus older 80% units. Your actual savings depend on climate, insulation, and usage patterns, typically 15-20% lower heating costs versus baseline equipment.
Can I install this myself, or do I need a licensed technician? +
Professional installation is mandatory. You'll need EPA certification to handle R410A refrigerant, licensed electrician work for the 208/230V connection, and proper airflow testing. DIY installation voids warranties and creates safety risks. Budget $1,500-2,500 for professional installation labor in most markets.
The specs show both 14 SEER and 16 SEER ratings. Which is correct? +
This discrepancy requires clarification from the seller before purchase. SEER1 regulations changed recently, so older marketing materials showed 14 SEER while newer standards reflect different test conditions. Confirm the exact cooling efficiency rating with technical documentation before committing to ensure it matches your expectations.
How does single-stage cooling compare to variable-capacity systems? +
Single-stage compressors run at full capacity or off, cycling on-and-off to maintain temperature. Variable-capacity systems modulate output, reducing cycles and improving comfort during mild weather. Single-stage is less expensive upfront but uses slightly more energy. For moderate climates with stable temperatures, the difference is minimal.
Is the 10-year warranty transferable if I sell my home? +
Goodman typically restricts the 10-year parts warranty to the original owner. The new homeowner receives reduced coverage, usually 5 years on parts. Verify transfer terms in warranty documentation, as policies vary by region and registration method.
