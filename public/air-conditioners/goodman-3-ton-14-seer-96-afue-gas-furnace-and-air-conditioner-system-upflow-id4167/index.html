Review

The ACiQ 3-ton system lands in the practical middle ground of the HVAC market. It pairs a 96% AFUE gas furnace with 36,000 BTU cooling capacity in a compact upflow/downflow configuration. Built on Goodman's platform, this unit targets homeowners and contractors who need respectable efficiency ratings without the complexity or cost of premium variable-capacity systems. The Energy Star certification confirms its credentials, and the dual-fuel capability (natural gas and propane) adds flexibility to installation scenarios.

Performance-wise, the numbers tell a straightforward story. The 96% AFUE furnace rating is near the top tier for gas equipment, translating to 80,000 BTU of heating with minimal waste. The 14 SEER1 cooling efficiency is solid for 2024 standards, handling 36,000 BTU at moderate demand levels. The variable-speed ECM blower motor delivers up to 1,600 CFM, adjusting output based on load rather than running at constant speed. This matters because it reduces wear, lowers electricity use, and maintains steadier indoor temperatures compared to single-speed alternatives.

Installation

Installation is straightforward for certified technicians. The system uses standard 3/8-inch liquid and 3/4-inch suction lines with matching valve sizes, sizing that aligns with typical residential runs up to 30 feet. The upflow/downflow configuration works in both orientations, and the plastic/PVC flue requirement keeps venting simple. Electrical demands are moderate at 18.7 to 30 amps, fitting standard residential service. Weight of 120 pounds requires two technicians for safe handling.

Warranty & Reliability

ACiQ provides a 10-year parts warranty when you register the system online after installation. This covers compressor, heat exchanger, and coil defects at no cost during the coverage period. Labor is not included, so factor in service call costs if repairs become necessary. Registration is straightforward and extends coverage, making it a worthwhile 10-minute task after startup.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the 96% AFUE rating affect monthly heating costs compared to an older 80% AFUE system? +
A 96% AFUE furnace captures an extra 16 percentage points of fuel energy versus 80% AFUE equipment. On a $100 heating bill from an older system, the ACiQ saves roughly $16 monthly, totaling $160 to $320 annually depending on local gas prices and heating degree-days. Payback accelerates in colder climates.
Can this system work in a downflow installation, and are there ductwork modifications needed? +
Yes, the ACiQ supports both upflow and downflow configurations. Downflow requires modified return and supply ductwork layouts but no changes to the equipment itself. Work with your installer to verify basement or crawlspace headroom and flue routing, as the right-side flue location matters for clearances.
What makes the variable-speed ECM blower different from a standard single-speed blower? +
The ECM blower ramps speed up and down based on heating or cooling demand, running slower during mild weather and full speed during extreme conditions. This cuts electricity consumption by 30 to 50 percent annually, reduces noise, and provides more consistent comfort. Single-speed blowers run at one fixed speed regardless of load.
Is 14 SEER1 enough cooling efficiency, or should I look at 16+ SEER2 systems? +
14 SEER1 is solid for moderate climates with 100 to 200 cooling degree-days annually. Higher ratings (16+ SEER2) cost significantly more and use variable-capacity compressors that shine in hot climates. For temperate regions, 14 SEER1 delivers good savings without premium pricing.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover, and what is not included? +
The warranty covers defects in compressor, heat exchanger, condenser, and evaporator coil for 10 years with online registration. Labor, refrigerant, filters, and routine maintenance are excluded. You pay for technician visits, but parts replacement is free.
