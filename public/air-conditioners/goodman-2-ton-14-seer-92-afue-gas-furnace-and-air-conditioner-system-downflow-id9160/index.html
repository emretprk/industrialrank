Review

The Goodman 2-ton system lands squarely in the mainstream HVAC market where price and practicality trump cutting-edge features. Priced at $643, this downflow configuration bundles 60,000 BTU gas heating with 24,000 BTU cooling capacity, targeting contractors and homeowners who need functional climate control without premium costs. Goodman's reputation for straightforward, serviceable equipment supports this unit's positioning as a workhorse replacement system rather than a luxury installation.

Performance numbers tell a familiar story for this segment. The 92% AFUE furnace rating sits above baseline federal requirements, while the 14 SEER cooling efficiency provides reliable air conditioning without variable-speed technology. A single-stage rotary compressor and single burner mean operation stays simple but less responsive to partial-load conditions. The multi-speed blower motor at 1200 CFM maximum airflow gives installers flexibility in ductwork balancing. Refrigerant R410A and standard line sizes ensure compatibility with most existing installations.

Installation

Installation requires standard electrical service: 208/230V single-phase 60 Hz with breaker sizing between 14.3 and 25 amps. The downflow A-coil configuration with right-side flue location suits basements and utility closets. PVC exhaust venting reduces installation cost compared to metal flue systems. Suction and liquid lines use common 3/4 inch and 3/8 inch sizes respectively, with 50 feet of pre-insulated suction line included. This straightforward setup appeals to experienced installers handling quick turnarounds.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs the unit with a 10-year parts warranty upon online registration, a solid commitment at this price point. Registration typically takes minutes and protects against compressor and heat exchanger failures. Unlike extended warranties from some competitors, this coverage focuses on core component reliability. Homeowners should register promptly after installation to activate full protection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this 2-ton system cool homes larger than 1200 square feet effectively? +
24,000 BTU cooling capacity suits homes up to roughly 1200 square feet in moderate climates. Larger spaces may see slower cooling or inadequate temperature control, especially during peak summer heat. Regional humidity and insulation quality significantly impact actual performance boundaries.
Can I install this downflow unit in an upflow position? +
Yes, the specs list configuration as upflow/downflow capable. However, flue location becomes critical when changing orientation. The right-side flue placement may conflict with horizontal ductwork in some homes. Consult Goodman's installation manual for your specific layout before proceeding with non-standard mounting.
How does 14 SEER compare to modern high-efficiency units? +
14 SEER is solid mid-range performance, roughly 30-40% above federal minimum standards. Premium units reach 16-20 SEER, costing significantly more but delivering better cooling efficiency in hot climates. For moderate regions, 14 SEER strikes a practical balance between comfort and operating costs.
Is Goodman's single-stage compressor a drawback against variable-speed competitors? +
Single-stage operation runs at full capacity whenever cooling demand exists, increasing noise (71 dBA) and electricity use at part load. Variable-speed units modulate smoothly, operating quieter and more efficiently overall. The trade-off here is simplicity and lower upfront cost versus premium comfort and long-term savings.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover if something breaks? +
The registered warranty covers defects in compressor, heat exchanger, and other major components for 10 years. Labor costs and non-covered items like blower motors may require separate payment. Review Goodman's warranty document carefully to understand specific exclusions and claim procedures.
