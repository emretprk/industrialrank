Review

The ACiQ 3-ton combo system positions itself as the entry point for homeowners seeking balanced performance without flagship pricing. At $643, this Goodman-manufactured unit combines a 60,000 BTU furnace and 36,000 BTU condenser in a compact upflow A-coil configuration. It's designed for existing ductwork retrofits and new construction where budget constraints matter. The unit carries ENERGY STAR certification and includes manufacturer documentation spanning installation, service, and technical specs.

Performance metrics show real-world relevance. The furnace achieves 98% AFUE with modulating burner stages, meaning it ramps output down on mild heating days instead of cycling on-off. The condenser's 13 SEER rating reflects single-stage operation, delivering 36,000 cooling BTU through reciprocating compression. The variable-speed ECM blower reaches 1200 CFM maximum while running quietly at lower speeds during partial-load conditions. Suction line insulation measures 3/8 inch thick, meeting modern field standards.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 13 SEER compare to newer SEER2 ratings I see advertised? +
13 SEER converts to approximately 10.5 SEER2 under current DOE testing. Newer 16 SEER units claim roughly 12.5 SEER2. The gap widens in humid climates. However, at this price point, SEER2 upgrades cost $300-400 more, offsetting payback periods beyond 7 years for most homeowners.
Can this unit replace my old Goodman GSX130362 system? +
Yes, the specs confirm this ACiQ model replaces GSX130362, GSX130363, and GSX130365 units directly. Your existing ductwork, flue venting, and refrigerant lines will require adapter work if suction/liquid sizes differ, but same-capacity replacement typically installs in 4-6 hours.
Is the modulating furnace worth the complexity over single-stage? +
In heating seasons exceeding 5,000 degree-days (most northern climates), modulating burners reduce energy consumption by 15-25% through less on-off cycling. Mild climates see lower savings, around 8-12%. The modulating mechanism adds minimal failure risk compared to two-stage compressors.
How does ACiQ pricing compare to full-line Goodman models? +
ACiQ is Goodman's value subsidiary, eliminating decorator cabinets and extended warranties but keeping core equipment identical. You sacrifice 2-year additional coverage and faster warranty processing for $150-250 savings, making sense for budget buyers accepting manufacturer phone support delays.
Will R454B refrigerant availability be an issue if I need service? +
R454B adoption is still ramping. Major metro areas now stock it routinely, but rural technicians may require 3-5 day special orders. If service flexibility matters, verify local availability before purchase.
