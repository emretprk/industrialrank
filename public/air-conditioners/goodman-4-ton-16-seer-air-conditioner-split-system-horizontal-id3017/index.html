Review

The Goodman 4-ton 16 SEER1 split system represents solid mid-tier cooling performance for residential applications. Positioned between economy units and premium brands, this horizontal configuration appeals to contractors and homeowners tackling attic or crawlspace installations where traditional vertical coils simply don't fit. Energy Star certification and a reasonable price point make it competitive in the popular 4-to-5-ton market segment.

Performance data shows this unit delivers 48,000 BTU of cooling with an efficient 16 SEER1 rating, translating to lower operating costs compared to older 13-14 SEER equipment. The two-stage compressor and variable-speed ECM blower motor work together intelligently, modulating between 0 and 2,000 CFM to match actual cooling demand. This staging approach reduces wear, extends lifespan, and keeps your system from running at full power during mild days.

Installation

The horizontal A-coil design installs easily in attics or crawlspaces where height is limited. Standard sweat-type refrigerant ports use 3/8-inch liquid and 7/8-inch suction lines. However, verify your electrical panel can handle the 27.7-amp minimum requirement before committing. Installation typically requires certified HVAC technicians familiar with R410A refrigerant.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this unit with a 10-year parts warranty when you register online, a standard approach in the industry. This covers compressor, coil, and other components against manufacturing defects. Online registration is straightforward and essential to activate full coverage. Labor costs are your responsibility.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does 16 SEER1 actually mean for my monthly cooling costs? +
SEER1 measures cooling efficiency across varying conditions. A 16 SEER1 unit uses roughly 15-20% less electricity than a 13 SEER baseline system. Monthly savings depend on climate, usage patterns, and local electricity rates, but most homeowners see $10-25 monthly reductions in moderate climates.
Can I install this in my attic myself? +
No. HVAC installation requires EPA certification for refrigerant handling, electrical expertise for the 27.7-amp circuit, and system evacuation equipment. Hire a licensed contractor. DIY attempts void warranties and risk safety hazards.
Why does the spec sheet list both 4-ton and 5-ton? +
The unit delivers 48,000 BTU, which is technically 4-ton capacity. The 5-ton tonnage reference appears to be a specification inconsistency from Goodman. Confirm with your contractor which rating applies to your installation.
How does this compare to Lennox or Carrier units at the same SEER rating? +
Goodman competes on price and reliability rather than brand prestige. Carrier and Lennox typically cost 20-35% more for comparable SEER ratings. Performance differences are minimal at 16 SEER. Choose based on local service availability and installer preference.
Does the 10-year warranty cover my condenser unit too? +
The warranty covers the evaporator coil (indoor unit) when registered. You'll need a separate condenser warranty. Check your contractor's quote to confirm the outdoor unit coverage separately.
