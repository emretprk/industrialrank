Review

The Goodman GSX140251 sits squarely in the mainstream residential condenser market, targeting homeowners and contractors seeking dependable cooling without premium pricing. At $1,452 for a 2-ton unit, it positions itself as the practical choice between budget minimums and high-efficiency alternatives. Goodman's market presence and parts availability make this condenser a familiar sight in contractor trucks and HVAC warehouses across North America.

Performance metrics align with mid-tier expectations. The unit delivers 24,000 BTU cooling capacity using R410A refrigerant and achieves a 14 SEER rating, placing it above baseline 13 SEER units but below emerging 15-plus SEER standards. The scroll compressor operates at 71 dB in single-stage mode, meaning full on or full off with no modulation. Electrical requirements are straightforward: 208/230V single-phase 60Hz service with breaker range 14.3 to 25 amps, fitting standard residential panels.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 14 SEER efficiency rating meets modern minimum standards for tax incentives in many states
  • Scroll compressor design is more reliable than rotary types, with lower failure rates historically
  • 24,000 BTU cooling capacity handles typical 2-ton residential applications effectively
  • Single-stage operation keeps noise at manageable 71 dB, quieter than many competing models
  • 10-year warranty with online registration provides meaningful protection against defects

Cons

  • Not Energy Star certified, meaning it misses the top 15% efficiency tier that some rebate programs require
  • Single-stage compressor cannot modulate capacity, cycling on and off rather than adjusting output continuously
  • 71 dB noise level is acceptable but louder than variable-speed alternatives for noise-sensitive locations
  • R410A refrigerant is being phased down, though units remain serviceable for 10-15+ years

Who Should Buy

Budget-conscious homeowners replacing older 2-ton systems or contractors building affordable residential HVAC packages will appreciate the balance of efficiency, reliability, and cost. Consider Alternatives If

Installation

Installation complexity is moderate. The 146-pound unit measures 27.5 inches tall by 26 inches wide and deep, fitting standard pad spaces. Liquid and suction valve sizes (3/8 inch and 3/4 inch respectively) are industry-standard connections. Technicians will need proper R410A recovery equipment, evacuation pump, and brazing capability. The included installation manual covers typical procedures, though this remains a licensed-technician task in most jurisdictions.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides 10 years of parts coverage with online registration, a solid offering in the budget-to-mid tier. This protects against manufacturing defects, compressor failures, and component wear. Registration is straightforward online and critical to activate. Note this is parts coverage only; labor costs for service calls fall to the homeowner. Ten years aligns with typical system lifespan expectations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 14 SEER rating good for a 2-ton condenser in 2024? +
14 SEER is acceptable but no longer leading edge. It exceeds baseline 13 SEER by about 8 percent efficiency, qualifying for some state rebates. However, 15-16 SEER units are now standard in premium markets. For moderate climates with reasonable electric rates, the 14 SEER performance saves money versus older 10-12 SEER units without jumping to costlier alternatives.
Will this condenser work with my existing furnace or air handler? +
Compatibility depends on your indoor unit's matching tonnage and refrigerant type. This 2-ton R410A condenser pairs with 2-ton R410A coils and handlers. If your existing equipment is R22 or different tonnage, you need a complete system replacement. A technician should verify coil specifications before purchasing this condenser alone.
What does single-stage compressor operation mean for my bills? +
Single-stage means the compressor runs at full capacity or not at all, without adjusting output. This creates more on-off cycling than variable-capacity units, potentially increasing energy use in partial-load conditions. In stable 80-90 degree weather, it performs efficiently. In mild spring or fall, frequent cycling can waste power. Two-stage or inverter models modulate better but cost significantly more.
How does this compare to Carrier or Lennox 2-ton condensers at similar prices? +
Goodman typically undercuts Carrier and Lennox by 15-25 percent while delivering comparable SEER ratings and compressor reliability. Trade-offs include fewer variable-capacity options and less premium cabinet design. For budget-focused replacements, Goodman offers better value. For whole-home comfort features or smart integration, premium brands justify higher cost.
Does the 10-year warranty cover refrigerant leaks or accidental damage? +
The 10-year warranty covers manufacturing defects and component failures under normal operation. Refrigerant leaks from installation or accidental damage are typically excluded. Registration is required to activate coverage. Review Goodman's full warranty document before purchase to understand exclusions and claim procedures.
