Review

The Goodman 3-ton downflow system pairs a high-efficiency gas furnace with a two-stage air conditioner, targeting homeowners seeking reliable performance without flagship pricing at $3,198. Online Supply positions this as an Energy Star unit, and the specs largely support that claim. The furnace delivers 60,000 BTU heating output with 96% AFUE efficiency, while the condenser unit handles 35,000 BTU cooling capacity. However, that cooling BTU number creates confusion around true tonnage rating, which we'll address throughout this review.

Efficiency credentials impress on both sides. The furnace's two-stage burners and variable-speed ECM blower motor work together to maintain comfort while avoiding the energy waste of constant on-off cycling. The 18 SEER1 cooling rating reflects the scroll compressor's two-stage operation, which modulates capacity in moderate conditions rather than running flat-out. AHRI Certificate 7368838 confirms matched component pairing. At 115V single-phase 60Hz with a maximum 35-amp breaker requirement, this unit integrates into standard residential electrical service without upgrades.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet list 35,000 BTU cooling when this is marketed as a 3-ton unit? +
Standard 3-ton air conditioning should deliver 36,000 BTU. The 35,000 BTU rating is slightly undersized, suggesting either a spec error or that Online Supply rounded down. Request clarification from the seller before purchasing to confirm actual capacity against your load calculation.
Will this furnace work with my existing ductwork designed for a downflow system? +
Yes, the downflow configuration specifically supports basement installations. Verify your return and supply plenum sizes match the 1,200 maximum CFM blower output. Have an HVAC contractor confirm duct sizing, as undersized ducts create pressure problems and reduce efficiency gains.
How much can I expect to save on heating costs with 96% AFUE compared to an old 80% furnace? +
A 16-point AFUE improvement typically cuts heating bills by 15-20 percent, depending on climate and usage. In a northern climate burning $1,500 annually for heat, expect savings of $225-$300 yearly. Payback depends on installation costs and local energy prices.
Is this system comparable to higher-priced Goodman or Lennox models at $4,500 plus? +
This unit covers the core performance needs for mixed climates, but lacks some premium features like modulating gas valves or scroll compressors with enhanced efficiency. Premium units deliver SEER2 ratings of 20-22 and AFUE near 98 percent, worth considering if you plan 15 plus years ownership.
Do I need to register the warranty immediately, or can I do it later? +
Register during installation to activate the full 10-year coverage. Most manufacturers require registration within 30-60 days of purchase. Delaying risks losing warranty protection if the unit fails before you register.
