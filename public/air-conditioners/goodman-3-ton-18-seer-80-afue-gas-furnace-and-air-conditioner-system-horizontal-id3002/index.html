Review

Goodman's horizontal CHPF4860D6 combo system bridges affordability and performance in a crowded market. At $2160, it undercuts many competitors while delivering respectable 18 SEER cooling and 80,000 BTU heating capacity. The unit earned Energy Star status and includes a 10-year warranty with online registration. This system targets homeowners seeking legitimate efficiency upgrades from older single-stage equipment without committing to premium pricing that often exceeds $3000.

The cooling side impresses with an 18 SEER rating, two-stage scroll compressor, and 35,000 BTU output. Two-stage burner operation on the heating side keeps the 80% AFUE furnace from constant cycling, improving comfort during partial-load conditions. The variable-speed blower runs up to 2000 CFM and operates at a reasonable 71 dB. Electrical requirements are modest at 120V single-phase, 60Hz, with a breaker range of 21.9 to 35 amps, making retrofit installations straightforward in most homes.

Installation

Installation complexity is moderate. The horizontal configuration fits attics or basements easily, and sweat valve connections with standard 3/8-inch liquid line and 7/8-inch suction line follow industry norms. Metal exhaust flue is required for the furnace. Technicians should verify ductwork sizing matches the variable-speed blower's capability. Most installations take 4 to 6 hours including refrigerant charging and commissioning.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides 10 years of parts coverage when you register online within 60 days of installation. This is solid mid-market coverage that protects against compressor failure, heat exchanger cracks, and component defects. Labor coverage typically requires extended plans purchased separately. Registration is straightforward and extends protection considerably beyond standard one-year coverage.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the actual tonnage of this system, given the confusing 3-ton versus 5-ton specs? +
The 35,000 BTU cooling capacity and specs align with 3-ton rating. The 5-ton notation in specs appears to be a data entry error. Always verify with the AHRI certificate (204633307) when sizing. Contact Goodman directly if spec uncertainty affects your decision.
Can I install this horizontally in my attic with standard ductwork? +
Yes, the horizontal A-coil configuration works well in attics. Ensure ductwork supports 2000 CFM maximum airflow and that return air is unrestricted. Have your contractor verify existing ducts can handle the variable-speed blower output without pressure issues.
How much will my heating costs drop compared to my old 60% AFUE furnace? +
An 80% AFUE unit delivers roughly 25 to 30 percent fuel savings versus 60% equipment. Actual savings depend on climate, gas prices, and thermostat settings. In mild climates where you run heat sparingly, annual savings may only be $150 to 200.
Is 18 SEER still competitive in 2024, or should I spend more for higher ratings? +
18 SEER remains efficient and cost-effective. 20+ SEER units cost significantly more but save perhaps 10 to 12 percent in cooling energy. The payback on premium SEER ratings often exceeds 10 years, making 18 SEER sensible for most buyers.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover if my compressor fails? +
Parts are covered fully at no cost with registration. Labor costs are your responsibility unless you purchased an extended service agreement. Most contractors charge $300 to 600 for compressor replacement labor separately.
