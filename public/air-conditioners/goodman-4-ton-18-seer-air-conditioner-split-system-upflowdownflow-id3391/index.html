Review

The Goodman 4 Ton 18 SEER split system represents mainstream commercial HVAC equipment positioned between budget and premium market segments. This A-coil, upflow/downflow configuration handles most residential retrofit and new construction projects without specialized ducting requirements. Goodman has built reputation on straightforward engineering and field serviceability, making this unit a contractor staple across North America.

Performance specs show 47,000 BTU cooling capacity with an 18 SEER1 rating, yielding approximately 2,600 BTU per watt of energy input. The variable-speed ECM blower delivers up to 2,000 CFM while modulating runtime based on load, reducing energy waste during shoulder seasons. Two-stage compressor technology enables the unit to operate at partial capacity during mild weather, further improving seasonal efficiency over single-stage competitors.

Installation

This unit requires standard 208/230V single-phase 60Hz power with 29.3 to 50 amp breaker sizing depending on backup heat configuration. Sweat connections use 3/8 inch liquid and 7/8 inch suction lines, calling for competent refrigeration brazing. Weight at 50 lbs demands secure mounting; upflow/downflow orientation flexibility eases installation in confined spaces or utility closets.

Warranty & Reliability

Ten-year parts coverage activates upon online registration, providing comprehensive protection against manufacturer defects. This warranty extends to compressor, coil, and motor components. Registration requirement is simple but non-negotiable, so document the serial number and complete enrollment immediately after installation to avoid coverage gaps.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the title say 4 Ton but specifications show 5 Ton? +
This appears to be a product database inconsistency. Verify tonnage with Goodman directly using SKU CAPF4961D6 before ordering. The 47,000 BTU rating suggests nominal 4-ton capacity, but confirm with your supplier to prevent coil mismatch or installation delays.
Can this unit work with both R410A and R22 refrigerants? +
The specs state R410A or R22 compatibility, but verify your coil matches the specified refrigerant. Field conversion between refrigerants is costly and reduces efficiency. Confirm refrigerant type with your equipment supplier at time of purchase to ensure proper compressor oil and component matching.
How much energy will the variable-speed motor actually save? +
Variable-speed ECM motors typically reduce annual energy consumption 10-20% compared to single-speed PSC motors during moderate seasons. Your savings depend on climate, setpoint consistency, and duct design. In consistently hot climates, benefits diminish since the compressor runs near full capacity most operating hours.
How does this compare to Carrier or Lennox equivalents? +
Goodman delivers comparable SEER ratings at lower initial cost but may require slightly more field experience to achieve rated efficiency. Carrier and Lennox units often include premium diagnostics and tighter quality control, justifying 15-25% higher pricing for similar capacity and efficiency ratings.
Is the 10-year warranty worth registering for? +
Yes. Compressor replacement alone costs 800-1,500 dollars. Registration is five minutes online. Without it, coverage defaults to one year, making registration a no-risk decision protecting your significant equipment investment.
