Review

The Goodman 2.5-ton gas furnace and air conditioner system targets the mid-market HVAC buyer seeking balanced heating and cooling without premium pricing. This horizontal configuration operates at 97% AFUE on the heating side with modulating burners, positioning it squarely against Lennox and Carrier equivalents. Online Supply's offering includes full documentation, AHRI certification (7424271), and Energy Star qualification, signaling mainstream contractor acceptance.

Performance specs reveal a system optimized for moderate climates. The furnace delivers 80,000 BTU heating output with variable-speed ECM blower reaching 1600 CFM, while the cooling side produces 29,000 BTU at SEER 16, SEER1 efficiency. The scroll compressor uses R410A refrigerant in a single-stage configuration. Electrical requirements are modest: 115V single-phase 60Hz with 17.1 to 25-amp breaker sizing, making integration into existing home panels straightforward for most installations.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 97% AFUE with modulating burners saves 10-15% annually on natural gas compared to single-stage furnaces in typical use
  • SEER 16 cooling efficiency exceeds most competitor offerings in the 2.5-ton class by 2-3 SEER points
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor with 1600 CFM maximum adapts to load, reducing runtime noise and energy consumption
  • Energy Star certified with rebate eligibility through many utility providers, offsetting initial system cost

Cons

Installation

The horizontal A-coil design suits attics and crawlspaces with limited headroom, measuring 34.5 inches tall and 50 feet in suction line length. PVC flue compatibility eliminates metal venting costs. Refrigerant lines are 3/8 inch liquid and 7/8 inch suction with 3/4 inch insulation included. Variable-speed blower wiring is standard for modern thermostats. Downflow/horizontal configuration requires proper return air plenum design for optimal CFM delivery.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this system with a 10-year compressor and parts warranty upon online registration, typical for branded equipment at this price point. Coverage includes labor in most cases during the first year through authorized dealers. This warranty mirrors competitors like Carrier Comfort and Lennox systems, offering adequate protection for homeowners planning 15-plus year equipment lifecycles.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is the condenser rated 3.5 tons when the furnace is 2.5 tons? +
This spec mismatch indicates Online Supply bundled a popular 3.5-ton condenser with a 2.5-ton furnace. For proper operation, verify your cooling load requires 3.5 tons and your heating load requires 2.5 tons. Mismatched capacity reduces efficiency and can cause short-cycling in cooling mode.
Can I install this myself as a DIY homeowner? +
No. Furnace installation requires licensed gas plumbing, EPA Section 608 refrigerant certification, and local permit compliance. Improper installation voids the warranty and creates safety hazards including carbon monoxide leaks and electrical fires.
How much will this system save on energy bills compared to my 15-year-old furnace? +
Expect 20-30% heating savings from 97% AFUE versus older 78-80% units, plus 25-35% cooling savings from SEER 16 versus 10-SEER window units. Actual savings depend on local gas and electricity rates, thermostat programming, and insulation quality.
Is SEER 16 rating better than the SEER2 standards contractors mention now? +
Yes, but they measure differently. SEER 16 uses legacy testing; SEER2 is the 2023 EPA standard and typically 15-20% lower numerically. This unit's SEER2 rating would approximate 12.8-13.6, still above the 13 SEER2 minimum efficiency standard.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Parts and compressor failure, but not routine maintenance, refrigerant recharges, or damage from improper installation. Online registration is mandatory to activate coverage.
