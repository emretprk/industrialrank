Review

The Goodman 2-ton combo system packages a gas furnace and air conditioner into one compact unit, targeting cost-conscious homeowners who want both heating and cooling efficiency without buying separate equipment. At $115 wholesale, this represents a budget-friendly entry point to high-efficiency HVAC, though the actual installed cost will be substantially higher due to labor and ductwork. Goodman's reputation for reliable mid-range equipment makes this a practical choice for moderate climates where extreme performance isn't required.

Performance specs show a 97% AFUE furnace producing 60,000 BTU heating output paired with a 24,000 BTU air conditioner rated at 16 SEER1. The modulating burner adjusts heating output continuously rather than cycling on and off, reducing temperature swings and improving comfort. The variable-speed ECM blower motor delivers up to 1,200 CFM while consuming far less electricity than fixed-speed alternatives. For homeowners paying $150 to $200 monthly for heating and cooling, this efficiency combination could save $20 to $35 monthly compared to 80% AFUE and 13 SEER equipment.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 97% AFUE furnace efficiency means minimal heat loss compared to older units, translating to lower winter gas bills
  • 16 SEER cooling rating qualifies for Energy Star and matches mid-to-upper tier air conditioner efficiency
  • Modulating burner adjusts heating output in real time, reducing short-cycling and improving comfort
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor optimizes airflow while cutting electricity consumption by 50% versus fixed-speed units
  • Two-stage scroll compressor handles partial-load cooling more efficiently during mild weather
  • Compact A-coil design fits tight installation spaces, downflow configuration works in closets or attics

Cons

  • SKU lists 3 ton tonnage but product title states 2 ton, creating specification confusion for buyers
  • 71 dB noise level is moderately loud, comparable to older window units and may be noticeable in quiet homes

Installation

Installation requires a licensed HVAC technician certified for gas work in most jurisdictions. The unit needs proper gas line connections using appropriate fittings, plastic or PVC exhaust venting to the exterior, refrigerant line connections with sweat valves, and electrical hookup to a 15.7 to 20 amp breaker. Downflow configuration requires adequate vertical clearance of 34.5 inches. Installation typically takes 6 to 10 hours including ductwork modifications and system testing.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides 10 years of parts warranty with online registration, which is competitive for mid-range equipment. This covers internal compressor failure, heat exchanger cracks, and component defects but excludes normal maintenance items. Registration is straightforward and extends coverage from the default 1 year to the full 10 years. Labor and service calls are not included, so buyers should budget for technician fees during the warranty period.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet say 3 ton tonnage when the product title says 2 ton? +
This appears to be a data entry error on the manufacturer's side. The 24,000 BTU cooling capacity and 60,000 BTU heating output confirm 2-ton cooling capacity. Verify tonnage with Goodman or your installer before purchasing to ensure proper system matching.
Can I install this myself to save money? +
No. Gas furnace installation requires a licensed technician in most jurisdictions. Improper gas line connections risk carbon monoxide leaks and fire hazards. The safety warning explicitly states that improper installation may result in death. Always hire a licensed professional.
How much will my energy bills actually decrease? +
Upgrading from an 80% AFUE furnace and 10 SEER unit to 97% AFUE and 16 SEER saves approximately 15 to 20% on combined heating and cooling costs. Actual savings depend on local utility rates, climate, and home insulation. Expect $300 to $600 in annual savings in moderate climates.
How does the 16 SEER rating compare to newer equipment? +
16 SEER is solid mid-tier performance. Top-tier systems reach 20+ SEER but cost significantly more. For homeowners replacing 10 to 13 SEER units, this represents a meaningful efficiency jump without premium pricing. Compare total installed cost, not just efficiency ratings.
Is the 10-year warranty worth registering for? +
Yes. Registration is free and extends coverage from 1 year to 10 years, protecting against expensive compressor and heat exchanger failures that can cost $1,500 to $3,000 to replace. Register immediately after installation.
