Review

Goodman's dual-fuel system pairs a 96% AFUE furnace with a 16 SEER1 air conditioner in a downflow configuration rated for propane or natural gas. This SKU LS381183850 targets contractors and homeowners balancing heating and cooling needs without stretching budgets. The combination earns Energy Star certification and includes a scroll compressor with two-stage burner technology, positioning it as a mid-tier performer in the residential HVAC market.

Heating output reaches 80,000 BTU across two burner stages, while cooling delivers 42,000 BTU in single-stage operation. The variable-speed ECM blower pushes up to 1,600 CFM and maintains a quiet 73 dB sound level. Electrical requirements are modest at 115V, 1-phase, 60 Hz with a 40-amp maximum breaker. These specs deliver solid efficiency without exotic refrigerant demands, using standard R410A or R22.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this truly 3.5 tons or 5 tons? +
The spec sheet shows conflicting tonnage ratings. The 42,000 BTU cooling capacity aligns with 3.5-ton systems, but the listing also references 5-ton specs. Contact the supplier for clarification before purchase to ensure indoor coil compatibility and proper system design.
Can this furnace work with an older air conditioner? +
Possibly, but matching matters. The furnace's 80,000 BTU heating and two-stage operation require compatible ductwork and controls. Most upgrades pair new furnaces with new condensers to ensure refrigerant type, airflow, and electrical alignment. Mixing old and new often voids warranties.
How does 16 SEER1 compare to typical air conditioners? +
16 SEER1 exceeds the federal minimum of 13 SEER for most regions and beats average 14 SEER equipment. However, single-stage compressors cannot modulate, so efficiency gains come from the blower and coil design rather than variable cooling capacity. Two-stage condensers typically achieve higher SEER ratings.
What's the difference between this Goodman and a Lennox or Carrier system? +
Goodman competes on price and simplicity. Lennox and Carrier often offer modulating compressors and higher SEER ratings, but cost significantly more. Goodman's two-stage furnace and variable-speed blower bridge the efficiency gap without premium pricing, making it popular with cost-conscious contractors.
Does the 10-year warranty cover labor? +
No, the warranty covers parts only. Labor, refrigerant, and ductwork modifications are the homeowner's responsibility. Contractor installation warranties vary, so confirm coverage details with your HVAC installer before signing.
