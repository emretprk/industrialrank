Review

The ACiQ 3-ton gas furnace and air conditioner system represents solid engineering at an aggressive price point. Goodman's ACiQ division targets price-conscious contractors and homeowners who refuse to sacrifice core efficiency for budget positioning. At $643, this unit undercuts most 3-ton combos while delivering 96% AFUE heating and 13 SEER cooling, specifications that typically command premium pricing. The system qualifies as AHRI certified (certificate 7368833) and maintains Goodman's reputation for reliable compressors and durable construction.

Performance metrics justify the value proposition. The 60,000 BTU two-stage furnace uses variable-speed ECM blower technology to deliver between 0 and 1,200 CFM, optimizing both heating efficiency and comfort. The 36,000 BTU cooling capacity maintains 13 SEER through a single-stage reciprocating compressor using R454B refrigerant. Operation runs at 74 dBA, acceptable for utility closets but noticeable in living spaces. Electrical requirements are modest, needing 19.1 to 30-amp circuits at 208/230V single phase, fitting most residential panels without upgrades.

Installation

Installation demands standard HVAC skills and basic tools. The upflow configuration suits most furnace closets with its 30-inch height and 17.5 by 21-inch footprint. PVC exhaust venting simplifies routing compared to metal flue requirements. Liquid and suction lines require 3/8-inch and 7/8-inch connections respectively, with 3/8-inch insulation thickness on the suction line to prevent condensation. Technicians should budget 4 to 6 hours for complete commissioning including airflow verification.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides a 10-year parts and compressor warranty upon online registration, standard coverage for this product class. The warranty covers manufacturing defects but excludes wear items, improper installation, and refrigerant losses. Registration is required within 30 days of startup. This protection is adequate for residential applications but notably shorter than premium 12-year warranties from top-tier brands.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why choose 96% AFUE over standard 80% efficiency? +
A 96% AFUE furnace converts 96 cents of every fuel dollar into usable heat, versus 80 cents with older units. On a 60,000 BTU system running 1,000 hours annually, the difference saves approximately 200 therms per year, worth $150 to $300 in heating costs depending on fuel prices.
Is this system compatible with existing ductwork? +
Yes, if your current system is 3-ton and properly sized ducts exist. The upflow configuration matches most existing installations. However, have a technician verify static pressure and CFM delivery, as undersized ducts waste efficiency gains despite the 1,200 CFM maximum blower capacity.
How does 13 SEER compare to newer standards? +
13 SEER meets current federal minimum requirements and beats 10 to 12 SEER systems. However, 15+ SEER variable-capacity units outperform this single-stage condenser in partial-load conditions, particularly during spring and fall when cooling demand is moderate.
What's the difference between this ACiQ unit and higher-priced Goodman models? +
This entry-level ACiQ focuses on core efficiency without variable-capacity compressors, advanced controls, or premium refrigerants. Mid-tier Goodman GSX units add variable-stage cooling for quieter operation. You're paying for proven reliability and 96% AFUE efficiency, not luxury features.
Does online registration truly matter for warranty coverage? +
Yes. Without registration within 30 days of startup, Goodman limits coverage to one year. Registration is simple, takes 10 minutes, and must be completed to activate the full 10-year protection.
