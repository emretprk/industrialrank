Review

The Goodman 2-ton gas furnace and air conditioner system targets contractors and homeowners seeking affordable efficiency without unnecessary frills. At $647, this downflow-configured unit combines an 80,000 BTU furnace with 24,000 BTU cooling capacity in a single, space-efficient package. The A-coil design and variable-speed ECM blower motor place this firmly in the value category, delivering mainstream performance at entry-level pricing.

Efficiency numbers tell the real story here. The furnace hits 97% AFUE through modulating burner technology, which means it adjusts flame intensity rather than cycling on and off, reducing standby losses. The 13 SEER air conditioner meets ENERGY STAR thresholds, qualifying for rebates in most states. With a 1600 CFM maximum airflow and 115V single-phase operation, this system runs on standard residential electrical service. These specs translate to 15-25% lower heating bills versus 80s-vintage equipment, depending on your heating season.

Pros & Cons

Installation

This downflow unit requires overhead ductwork clearance and standard refrigerant line runs to an outdoor condenser unit. The plastic PVC exhaust flue goes right-mounted, needing 50-foot line length capacity. ECM blower motor hookup is straightforward on existing furnace wiring. Installation complexity is moderate; any licensed HVAC technician familiar with modern systems completes this in 4-6 hours. R454B refrigerant handling requires EPA certification.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this system with a 10-year parts warranty activated through online registration. This is industry-standard coverage for equipment at this price tier. No labor warranty is included, so service calls after year one fall on the homeowner. Frequently Asked Questions

Is 13 SEER good enough for long-term savings? +
Yes, 13 SEER represents a 30-40% efficiency jump over pre-2015 equipment and qualifies for federal tax credits in some years. Payback typically occurs within 7-10 years through lower cooling bills, longer if you live in mild climates where AC runs infrequently.
Can I install this myself, or do I need a professional? +
EPA refrigerant certification is legally required to handle R454B, so DIY installation isn't an option. Licensed contractors handle refrigerant charging and system testing. Expect $1,500-2,500 in labor costs on top of the $647 equipment price.
How does 97% AFUE furnace efficiency compare to heat pumps? +
Gas furnaces at 97% AFUE are more efficient than most air-source heat pumps in harsh winters. However, heat pumps save more in mild climates where heating needs are light. For cold-climate homes, gas furnaces win on efficiency; heat pumps win in moderate zones.
Why single-stage cooling when the furnace is modulating? +
Cost. Two-stage compressors add $300-500 to equipment price. The modulating furnace alone delivers noticeable efficiency gains. Single-stage works fine in most climates; two-stage matters most in regions with prolonged 85-95 degree summers.
What if something breaks after the 10-year warranty expires? +
You pay full service cost. A failing compressor runs $1,200-1,600 to replace. Consider extended warranties during installation if system longevity concerns you, though Goodman's reliability record is solid for this product tier.
