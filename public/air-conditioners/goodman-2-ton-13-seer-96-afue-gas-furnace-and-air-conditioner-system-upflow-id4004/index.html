Review

The Goodman 2-ton gas furnace and air conditioning system is a solid mid-market choice for homeowners seeking an all-in-one heating and cooling solution. Built on Goodman's reputation for straightforward reliability, this unit combines an 80,000 BTU furnace with a matching 24,000 BTU condenser in an upflow configuration. It targets the replacement market where older equipment occupies tight spaces and budget-conscious buyers value efficiency gains without luxury features.

Performance-wise, the 96% AFUE heating efficiency and 13 SEER cooling efficiency deliver real savings on monthly utility bills. The two-stage burner adjusts heating output to match demand, while the multi-speed ECM blower motor fine-tunes airflow between 0 and 1,200 CFM. This system runs at 75 dB, comparable to window units, and operates on standard 115V single-phase power with a 10.3 to 15 amp breaker requirement, making it straightforward for most residential installations.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 96% AFUE furnace efficiency with two-stage burners reduces heating costs significantly compared to 80s-era units
  • 13 SEER cooling paired with R454B refrigerant meets current EPA standards and Energy Star certification
  • Multi-speed ECM blower motor delivers up to 1,200 CFM with better part-load efficiency than single-speed alternatives
  • Compact A-coil design (34.5 inches tall, 17.5 inches wide) fits tight attics and vertical installations
  • Lifetime heat exchanger warranty plus 10-year unit replacement and parts coverage with online registration

Cons

  • 2-ton capacity limits this to smaller homes or single-zone applications, not suitable for larger residences above 2,000 square feet
  • Single-stage cooling compressor cannot modulate, so it runs at full capacity and cycles on/off rather than adjusting to load
  • 75 dB noise level is acceptable but not whisper-quiet compared to variable-speed units in the market

Who Should Buy

Best For

Homeowners with 1,200 to 1,800 square feet looking to replace aging split systems or upgrading from window units. Contractors installing in retrofit scenarios where space constraints make standard horizontal coils difficult to position.

Consider Alternatives If

Anyone needing cooling capacity above 24,000 BTU or wanting the efficiency benefits of variable-speed compressors should explore higher-tonnage or two-stage cooling models instead.

Installation

Installation is straightforward for licensed HVAC technicians familiar with gas furnace hookup and refrigerant line work. The A-coil design requires minimal ductwork modification in upflow/horizontal positions, and the plastic PVC flue exhausts directly through exterior walls. Suction line insulation is included at 3/4 inch thickness, and standard sweat valve connections simplify indoor coil mounting. Most installations complete within one day on retrofit jobs.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this unit with a lifetime warranty on the heat exchanger against defects, plus 10-year unit replacement and 10-year parts coverage provided you register online. This tiered approach is competitive for the price point and protects against major component failures during the critical first decade of operation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this 2-ton system replace the Goodman GSX130242 unit? +
Yes, the ACiQ ACIQ-24-EHPD-UPG directly replaces the GSX130242 model. It maintains the same tonnage and mounting footprint while upgrading to R454B refrigerant and improving SEER ratings to align with current efficiency standards.
What refrigerant does this system use and why does it matter? +
This unit uses R454B refrigerant, a low-GWP blend that phases out older R410A systems. R454B reduces environmental impact, meets EPA requirements for new equipment, and performs identically in cooling applications without requiring special servicing.
Is 13 SEER sufficient for cooling efficiency, or should I look for higher SEER ratings? +
13 SEER is respectable and qualifies for Energy Star certification. Higher SEER ratings require variable-speed compressors and cost significantly more. For modest tonnage like 2 tons, 13 SEER offers good balance between efficiency gains and return on investment.
Can this system be installed in a horizontal attic position or only upflow? +
The unit handles both upflow and horizontal configurations thanks to its A-coil design. Horizontal installation works well in attics where ductwork flows perpendicular to the unit, though vertical upflow positioning is standard for basements and utility closets.
What does the lifetime heat exchanger warranty actually cover? +
Goodman covers the heat exchanger against manufacturing defects for the life of the unit with no time limit. The 10-year parts warranty covers other components. This protects against costly furnace failures while parts coverage shields you from most service expenses early on.
