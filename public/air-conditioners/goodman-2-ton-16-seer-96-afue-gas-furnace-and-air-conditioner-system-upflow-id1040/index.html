Review

The Goodman GMVC960803BN is a 2-ton combo system pairing a two-stage air conditioner with an 80,000 BTU furnace. At $2,666, it occupies the practical middle ground between budget equipment and premium brands like Lennox. It's Energy Star certified, appeals to cost-conscious contractors, and offers enough efficiency gain to justify replacement on a 15-year-old unit. The upflow A-coil design fits standard attic or closet installations common in residential HVAC retrofits.

Performance specs are solid: 16 SEER cooling efficiency and 96% AFUE heating. The scroll compressor cycles between full and partial capacity, reducing runtime waste during shoulder seasons. The variable-speed ECM blower motor ramps output based on demand rather than running constant-speed, which cuts fan energy consumption noticeably. Refrigerant is R410A, requiring trained service technicians. Electrical requirements are minimal at 115V single-phase with a 15.7 to 20-amp breaker, fitting standard residential panels.

Installation

This unit requires licensed technician installation due to gas connections and exhaust venting. The system weighs 120 pounds and measures 34.5 inches tall by 17.5 inches wide by 28.75 inches deep, fitting standard attic or utility closet spaces. PVC venting is required for the furnace flue. Refrigerant lines use 3/8-inch liquid and 3/4-inch suction sweat connections, so ductwork and line sets may need replacement or modification if upgrading from older tonnage.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides a 10-year parts and labor warranty when registered online after purchase. Coverage includes the compressor, heat exchanger, and furnace components. This is standard for the brand and protects against manufacturing defects. Labor coverage varies by region, so confirm details with your installing contractor before signing paperwork.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does 16 SEER actually mean for my electric bill? +
SEER measures cooling efficiency. A 16 SEER unit uses roughly 25% less electricity than a 13 SEER baseline model for the same cooling output. On a typical $1,200 annual cooling cost in a hot climate, expect $200-$300 annual savings. Payback depends on local electricity rates and cooling season length.
Can I install this myself to save money? +
No. Federal EPA regulations require certified technicians to handle R410A refrigerant. Gas line connections also require licensed installers in most jurisdictions. Improper installation voids warranty and creates safety hazards. Budget $3,500-$6,000 for professional installation including labor and materials.
Is this the same as the listing showing 3-ton tonnage? +
There's a spec inconsistency. The product title and cooling BTU (24,000) confirm 2-ton capacity, but one field lists 3 tons. Always reference the 24,000 cooling BTU figure. Contact the seller to clarify before ordering to ensure it matches your existing ductwork.
How does this compare to a high-efficiency Carrier or Lennox unit? +
Premium brands offer variable-speed compressors and sound damping for quieter operation and 18-20 SEER efficiency. This Goodman trades noise levels and peak efficiency for lower upfront cost. For budget-conscious buyers replacing working systems, the difference in yearly energy savings often doesn't justify $2,000+ premium pricing.
What if something breaks after the warranty expires? +
Goodman parts are affordable and widely available through HVAC supply houses. Service calls typically cost $150-$250 for diagnostics. Compressor replacement runs $1,000-$1,500 labor plus parts. Extended warranties are available at purchase but often represent poor value versus self-insuring.
