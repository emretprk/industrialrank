Review

The Goodman GSX140301 occupies the practical middle ground of residential air conditioning condensers. At $1252, this 2.5-ton single-stage unit targets contractors and homeowners who want straightforward cooling performance without premium pricing. Goodman's positioning in the value segment means this condenser prioritizes dependable operation and installation simplicity over advanced features like variable-stage compression or smart controls.

Performance specs are respectable for the category. The unit delivers 30,000 BTU cooling capacity with a 14 SEER rating, indicating adequate seasonal efficiency for moderate climates. The rotary compressor and R410A refrigerant are industry standard choices. At 72 dB, noise output is acceptable but noticeably louder than premium units. Electrical requirements are straightforward, requiring 208/230V single-phase power with a 17.1 to 25-amp breaker range, fitting most residential service panels without upgrades.

Installation

Installation is contractor-friendly. The 172-pound weight is manageable for two technicians, and the compact 29" x 29" footprint accommodates standard concrete pads. Frequently Asked Questions

Is 14 SEER adequate for modern installations? +
Yes, 14 SEER meets current minimum efficiency requirements and qualifies for most jurisdictions without special approvals. However, newer 16 to 18 SEER units can reduce utility costs by 12 to 28 percent over time. Choose 14 SEER when budget constraints outweigh long-term energy savings.
Can this condenser pair with any indoor coil? +
This is a condenser-only unit. It requires a matching 2.5-ton indoor coil and air handler. Consult Goodman compatibility charts or your supplier, as mismatched systems reduce efficiency and void warranties. Always verify tonnage ratings match exactly.
What does single-stage compression mean for performance? +
Single-stage means the compressor runs at full capacity whenever cooling demand triggers it, then cycles off completely. Unlike variable-capacity compressors, it cannot modulate output for partial-load conditions, resulting in slightly higher energy consumption during mild weather operation.
How does this unit compare to higher-SEER Goodman models? +
Goodman's 16 to 18 SEER units cost $200 to $500 more but deliver 12 to 28 percent better efficiency. For homeowners staying more than eight years, higher SEER usually pays for itself through utility bill reductions. This 14 SEER model suits shorter-term ownership or tight budgets.
Is online warranty registration mandatory? +
Yes, the 10-year warranty requires registration within a specific timeframe after purchase. Without registration, coverage defaults to a shorter period. Registration is free and takes minutes on Goodman's website.
