Review

The Goodman 3 Ton Upflow Gas Furnace and Air Conditioner System represents the midrange efficiency sweet spot for residential HVAC. At $736, it positions itself as an accessible upgrade path for homeowners tired of aging equipment but unwilling to jump to premium brands. This matched pair carries genuine engineering: a 96% AFUE furnace paired with an 18 SEER2 air conditioner, both certified by AHRI. Online Supply's retail pricing makes this competitive against local contractor quotes.

Performance specs tell a practical story. The furnace delivers 100,000 BTU of heating output with two-stage burner operation, meaning it modulates down to part capacity most of the time. The air conditioner produces 35,000 BTU cooling with a scroll compressor and two-stage design, hitting that 18 SEER2 rating with R410A refrigerant. The variable-speed ECM blower hits 2000 CFM maximum, adapting airflow rather than running full-blast constantly. This approach cuts utility bills 15-25% versus single-stage equipment.

Installation

Installation demands standard HVAC skills and materials. The unit requires 115V, 1-phase power with 21.3 to 35 amp breaker sizing, necessitating panel upgrades in older homes. Plastic PVC exhaust venting is required for the furnace. Suction and liquid lines measure 7/8 inches and 3/8 inches respectively, with 50-foot insulated line sets available. The A-coil measures 34.5 inches tall, 21 inches wide, and 28.75 inches deep, fitting most attics and closets.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs the heat exchanger for the life of the system, meaning zero cost if internal corrosion occurs, the number-one furnace failure point. Parts carry 10-year coverage with online registration required. This two-tier structure protects your investment while encouraging proper maintenance. Labor remains the buyer's responsibility after year one.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this system qualify for tax credits or rebates? +
The 96% AFUE and 18 SEER2 ratings qualify for Energy Star designation. Many states and utilities offer $300-800 rebates for systems meeting these thresholds. Register online with Goodman and contact your local utility to confirm eligibility in your area.
Will this fit my attic if I have limited vertical space? +
The 34.5-inch height fits most attics, but measure first. The unit operates in both upflow and horizontal configurations, providing flexibility. Contractors can rotate the A-coil to accommodate tight spaces, though this requires field engineering.
How does 18 SEER2 compare to older equipment I might replace? +
A 10-year-old unit likely operates at 10-13 SEER. This 18 SEER2 system reduces cooling electricity consumption by 30-40%. In warm climates, expect $30-60 monthly cooling savings, recovering the equipment cost in 10-14 years.
Should I buy this or a higher-tier Goodman or different brand? +
Premium units hit 21-23 SEER2 and 98% AFUE, cutting annual energy use 5-8% further but costing $1500-2200 more. This model represents 85-90% of maximum efficiency at 60% of premium pricing. Choose based on climate severity and budget.
What happens if the heat exchanger cracks? +
Goodman replaces it free under lifetime warranty. You pay only labor and refrigerant recharge, typically $500-900. This protection is uncommon at this price point and addresses the most expensive failure mode.
