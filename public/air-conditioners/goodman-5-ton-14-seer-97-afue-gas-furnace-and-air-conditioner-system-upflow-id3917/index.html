Review

The ACiQ-branded Goodman 5-ton split system sits squarely in the practical middle ground of residential HVAC. This 120,000 BTU furnace paired with 60,000 BTU cooling capacity addresses real contractor demand for dependable equipment that performs without exotic features or collector pricing. Goodman's reputation for straightforward engineering and field serviceability carries through here, supported by a 10-year warranty when registered.

Heating efficiency is the standout spec: 97% AFUE means nearly all fuel converts to usable heat, with minimal stack loss. The modulating burner fine-tunes output across its range, avoiding the constant on-off cycling that plagues single-stage furnaces. Cooling delivers 14 SEER performance using R454B refrigerant and a scroll compressor rated to 50-amp breakers. The variable-speed ECM blower motor reaches 2000 CFM maximum and adjusts quietly between calls, lowering parasitic electrical draw compared to fixed-speed alternatives.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 97% AFUE furnace efficiency means minimal wasted heat and lower natural gas or propane bills year-round
  • Modulating burner adjusts flame continuously rather than cycling on-off, reducing temperature swings and improving comfort
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor ramps airflow intelligently, using 25-40% less electricity than standard motors
  • PVC/plastic exhaust flue simplifies installation in retrofit and new construction scenarios
  • R454B refrigerant meets current EPA regulations and future compliance standards
  • 14 SEER cooling provides solid mid-tier performance at this price point

Cons

  • No Energy Star certification despite strong efficiency ratings, which may affect rebate eligibility in some regions
  • 5-ton capacity limits this unit to homes under approximately 2,500 square feet with moderate insulation
  • Single-stage condenser cannot modulate cooling capacity, so it cycles rather than varying output like the furnace
  • 75 dB noise level is audible during operation, louder than quieter premium units on the market

Who Should Buy

Best For

Contractors upgrading rental properties or new construction, and cost-conscious homeowners in northern climates who prioritize heating efficiency. The modulating furnace and ECM blower deliver measurable utility savings that recoup the investment in 5-7 years.

Consider Alternatives If

Buyers in extremely hot climates where cooling dominates usage should consider higher SEER units, and anyone needing variable-capacity cooling for tight temperature control.

Installation

Upflow or downflow configuration allows flexible positioning in basements, attics, or closets. The right-mounted plastic flue simplifies venting through exterior walls without masonry work. Dimensions of 30 inches tall by 24.5 inches wide fit standard equipment pads. Electrical requires 208 or 230V single-phase power with a 32.8 to 50-amp breaker. Standard refrigerant linesets of 3/8 inch liquid and 7/8 inch suction simplify field connections.

Ten-year parts coverage applies with online registration, a mild incentive that most HVAC buyers complete. Goodman evaporator coil carries separate warranty documentation. This duration is competitive but not exceptional, falling short of extended plans offered by higher-tier brands. Register immediately after installation to avoid coverage gaps.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does 97% AFUE actually save me in heating costs? +
A 97% AFUE furnace converts 97 cents of every fuel dollar into heat. Compared to 80% AFUE units, you'll save roughly 17-20% on annual heating bills in cold climates. For homes using $1,200 yearly in heating, expect $200-240 annual savings, paying for the efficiency premium in 5-7 years.
Can I install this as a replacement in my existing ductwork? +
Yes, if your current system is also 5 tons and uses similar linesets. The upflow configuration fits standard basement platforms. Verify your breaker panel has 32.8 amps minimum available. Ductwork should accommodate 2000 CFM maximum airflow without undersizing problems.
Is 14 SEER good enough for summer cooling? +
For moderate climates, 14 SEER is adequate and typical for mid-range units. Hot, humid regions benefit from 16+ SEER systems with variable capacity. Your actual cooling costs depend on insulation, window area, and thermostat settings more than SEER alone.
How does this compare to higher-priced Carrier or Lennox systems? +
Premium brands offer quieter operation, longer warranties, and higher SEER ratings. This Goodman delivers similar efficiency and reliability at 20-30% less cost. You trade brand prestige and noise levels for genuine dollar savings without performance sacrifice.
What's included with the 10-year warranty? +
Parts coverage for manufacturer defects after online registration. Excludes labor, refrigerant, and electrical work. Review the full Goodman terms before installation. Evaporator coil has separate warranty documentation included with the unit.
