Review

The Goodman 3.5 Ton system represents the value tier of residential HVAC, targeting homeowners and small contractors who need functional equipment without premium pricing. Manufactured by Online Supply with SKU LS381183850, this combination furnace and AC condenser delivers 80,000 BTU heating and 42,000 BTU cooling. The upflow/downflow A-coil configuration accommodates typical residential installations, while Energy Star certification signals baseline efficiency compliance. This is honest, adequate equipment for straightforward replacement scenarios.

The furnace achieves 80% AFUE efficiency with a two-stage burner and multi-speed blower motor, producing up to 2000 CFM airflow. Cooling operates at 16 SEER through a single-stage scroll compressor using R410A refrigerant. The system requires 22.4 to 35 amp electrical service at 120V single-phase, fitting standard residential panels. While these specs meet minimum efficiency standards, the single-stage compressor cannot modulate output, meaning the unit cycles on-off rather than running continuously at reduced capacity during mild weather.

Installation

Installation demands standard furnace and condenser placement with proper clearances. The included 50-foot suction line with half-inch insulation simplifies outdoor condenser connection. Valve connections use 3/8-inch and 7/8-inch sweat fittings requiring soldering skills. Metal exhaust flue venting must be installed to code. Most licensed HVAC contractors complete installations in one working day without complications.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides a 10-year parts warranty upon online registration, covering manufacturing defects in compressor, coil, and heat exchanger. This is standard coverage for mid-tier equipment, though some competitors offer 12-year terms. Registration is mandatory, so documentation and proof of purchase should be retained. Labor and refrigerant are not covered.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the actual cooling capacity in tons? +
The specs show conflicting information: labeled as 3.5 ton in the title but 5 ton in detailed specifications, with 42,000 BTU cooling output. The 42,000 BTU figure equals 3.5 tons, making the 5-ton spec likely a data entry error. Confirm actual capacity with your installer.
Can this system handle extremely cold winters? +
The 80,000 BTU heating output suits moderate climates but may require backup heating or oversizing in regions below 5 degrees Fahrenheit regularly. In severe climates, a higher-AFUE furnace or dual-fuel setup with auxiliary strips is more appropriate.
How does 16 SEER compare to modern units? +
16 SEER is respectable but not cutting-edge. Current SEER2 standards replace SEER, and high-efficiency systems reach 18 to 22 SEER2. This unit's single-stage compressor limits part-load efficiency, meaning real-world performance dips during mild weather operation.
Is this better than a cheaper builder-grade system? +
Yes, marginally. Goodman equipment typically outlasts disposable 13 SEER units by several years, and 80% versus 78% furnace efficiency adds modest savings. However, mid-tier Lennox or Carrier equipment at similar price points often includes two-stage cooling.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Parts only after online registration, excluding labor and refrigerant. Manufacturing defects in the compressor, coil, and heat exchanger are covered. Always register immediately upon installation to activate warranty protection.
