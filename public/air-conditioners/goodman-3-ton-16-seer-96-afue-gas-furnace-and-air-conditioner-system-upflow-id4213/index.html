Review

The Goodman 3-ton dual-fuel system represents a practical middle ground in the HVAC market. With 96% AFUE heating efficiency and 16 SEER cooling performance, it addresses homeowners seeking strong seasonal efficiency without premium pricing. Online Supply positions this A-coil configured unit as a flexible solution for upflow installations, and the dual-fuel capability adds practical value for regions where fuel switching makes economic sense.

The furnace delivers 120,000 BTU heating output across two burner stages, while the air conditioner produces 34,800 BTU cooling capacity. The variable-speed ECM blower handles up to 2,000 CFM, enabling precise airflow matching to demand. Electrical requirements are modest at 115V single-phase 60Hz with 18.7 to 30 amps maximum breaker capacity. The scroll compressor and R410A refrigerant provide established reliability and serviceable components available from most HVAC wholesalers.

Installation

Installation requires standard R410A handling and proper line sizing. The 3/8 inch liquid line and 7/8 inch suction line accommodate the system design, with 3/4 inch insulation thickness on the suction line mandatory for efficiency. Frequently Asked Questions

What does the 16 SEER rating actually mean for my cooling costs? +
SEER measures seasonal cooling efficiency. A 16 SEER system uses roughly 30% less electricity for cooling than a 12 SEER unit. Your actual savings depend on climate, usage patterns, and local electricity rates. In hot climates, expect 15-25% annual cooling cost reductions versus 10-year-old systems.
Can I install this system horizontally in an attic space? +
Yes. The A-coil configuration supports both upflow and horizontal mounting. Verify your attic framing supports the 30-pound weight and allows proper airflow clearance. Horizontal installation works well for attic ducts but requires careful drainage line slope to prevent condensate backup.
Should I choose this over a variable-capacity system? +
This single-stage compressor costs less and runs reliably but cycles on/off rather than modulating capacity. For consistent comfort in variable outdoor temperatures, variable-capacity systems offer superior comfort. This Goodman works well where temperatures swing less frequently or budget constraints matter most.
What's the difference between SEER and SEER2 ratings? +
SEER2 is the newer test standard using more realistic efficiency conditions. The 16 SEER rating shown here uses older SEER methodology. When comparing to new units, expect SEER2 ratings approximately 15-20% lower than SEER for equivalent performance levels.
Does the lifetime heat exchanger warranty cover installation or labor? +
The lifetime warranty covers the heat exchanger component only, not labor or installation costs. Parts coverage requires online registration within 30 days. You pay labor if the heat exchanger fails, typically running $400-800 for furnace access and replacement work.
