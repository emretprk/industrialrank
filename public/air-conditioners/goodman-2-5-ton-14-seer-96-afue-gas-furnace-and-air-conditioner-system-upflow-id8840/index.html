Review

The ACiQ 2.5 Ton combo system represents solid middle-ground equipment in today's HVAC market. At $736, this Goodman-manufactured unit bundles a furnace and air conditioning component into one upflow configuration suitable for basement or utility closet installation. Energy Star certification and rebate availability make it attractive for homeowners watching both upfront costs and utility bills. This is the workhorse system contractors recommend when customers prioritize value over advanced features.

Heating performance reaches 30,000 BTU with exceptional 96% AFUE efficiency, meaning nearly every dollar spent on natural gas converts to usable heat. Cooling delivers 30,000 BTU at 14 SEER using R454B refrigerant, providing competent air conditioning for moderate climates. The multi-speed blower motor adjusts from 0 to 1600 CFM, giving installers flexibility to match airflow to ductwork and room sizes. Single-stage operation cycles fully on and off rather than modulating, which is typical at this price point.

Installation

This upflow configuration requires plastic or PVC exhaust flue (no masonry chimney needed). Dimensions of 30x21x21 inches allow installation in tight spaces common to finished basements. Right-side flue location provides flexibility for various room layouts. Electrical needs are straightforward: 208/230V single-phase 60 Hz requiring 18.7 to 30 amp breaker. Installation should follow CAPF manual guidance for proper refrigerant line sizing and field connections.

Warranty & Reliability

Ten-year comprehensive warranty covers compressor, heat exchanger, and sealed refrigerant components when registered online within 60 days of installation. This matches industry standard for mid-tier equipment. Warranty is non-transferable in most jurisdictions, so original homeowner status matters. Coverage excludes cosmetic damage and normal wear items like filters and capacitors.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the actual tonnage, 2.5 or 3.5 Ton? +
Spec sheet lists 3.5 Ton but title says 2.5 Ton, creating confusion at purchase. Contact supplier to clarify before ordering. Cooling and heating BTU both measure 30,000, confirming mid-size capacity. Tonnage designation affects system matching to your home's load calculation.
Can this system work with my existing ductwork? +
The multi-speed blower adjusts 0-1600 CFM, so it adapts to various duct configurations. However, your HVAC contractor must perform a Manual J load calculation and duct sizing analysis. Oversized or undersized ducts reduce efficiency regardless of furnace quality. Have existing ducts evaluated before purchase.
How much will this save on energy bills compared to older equipment? +
A 96% AFUE furnace uses roughly 10-15% less gas than 80% AFUE units common in pre-2010 homes. The 14 SEER AC reduces cooling costs 20-30% versus 10 SEER systems. Actual savings depend on climate, usage patterns, and local utility rates. Expect $200-400 annual savings in moderate climates.
How does the 74 dB noise level compare to other systems? +
74 dB approximates a vacuum cleaner's sound level, noticeably louder than premium 67-70 dB units. Single-stage operation runs fully on rather than modulating quietly like two-stage systems. If noise concerns you, budget extra for higher-tier equipment with variable-speed compressors and sound-dampening cabinets.
Is the 10-year warranty transferable if I sell my home? +
Most manufacturers, including Goodman, require original homeowner status for full warranty coverage. The warranty typically becomes limited or voids upon property sale. Verify transfer terms with your specific retailer before purchase for clarity on resale implications.
