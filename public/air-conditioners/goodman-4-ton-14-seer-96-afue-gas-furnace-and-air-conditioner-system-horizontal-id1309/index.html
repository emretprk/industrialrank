Review

The ACiQ 4-ton combination system delivers heating and cooling in a proven Goodman-based package. This 5-ton condenser paired with 120,000 BTU furnace represents practical engineering for contractors and homeowners replacing standard equipment. The horizontal A-coil configuration fits common attic and basement installations. At $3,136, this system sits in the accessible mid-range where reliability and decent efficiency matter more than premium features.

Heating efficiency reaches 96% AFUE with a two-stage natural gas or propane burner that modulates output based on demand. Cooling delivers 48,000 BTU at 13 SEER1 using R454B refrigerant, enough for moderate climates and medium-sized homes. The variable-speed ECM blower motor adjusts airflow up to 2,000 CFM, reducing runtime waste. These specifications represent solid middle-ground performance, not leading-edge, but appropriate for replacement cycles and budgets under $3,500.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 96% AFUE heating efficiency reduces fuel costs compared to 80-90% units, especially in heating-heavy regions
  • 13 SEER1 rating with R454B refrigerant meets current efficiency standards and qualifies for Energy Star certification
  • Two-stage burner and variable-speed ECM blower motor optimize performance across different load conditions
  • 120,000 BTU heating output handles mid-size homes effectively, paired with 48,000 BTU cooling capacity
  • 10-year warranty with online registration provides solid coverage without excessive complexity
  • Reciprocating compressor design is field-serviceable and proven reliable for contractor maintenance

Cons

Installation

This horizontal unit requires 115V single-phase 60Hz power with breaker sizing between 26.3 and 45 amps, straightforward for standard residential panels. The sweat valve connections use 3/8-inch liquid and 1 1/8-inch suction lines, standard for field installation. Plastic PVC exhaust flue compatibility simplifies venting. Overall installation complexity is moderate, typical for HVAC contractors, with no unusual ductwork or electrical demands.

Warranty & Reliability

ACiQ backs this system with 10 years parts coverage upon online registration, a solid middle-tier warranty. Registration takes minutes and documents proof of purchase. Coverage excludes wear items and improper installation. This warranty length is typical for mid-range systems, offering reasonable protection without the extended terms seen on premium equipment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will this system replace my old Goodman unit directly? +
Yes, it replaces GSX130483, GSX130484, and GSX130485 models. The ACiQ condenser uses standard 3/8-inch and 1 1/8-inch line connections. However, always verify your existing furnace compatibility and ductwork capacity with a technician before ordering.
What electrical work is needed for installation? +
The unit requires 115V single-phase 60Hz standard household power with a breaker sized between 26.3 and 45 amps. Most homes already have this, so new electrical work is rarely necessary. Your electrician can confirm your panel capacity in minutes.
How much will my heating bills drop with 96% AFUE? +
Switching from an 80% unit saves approximately 16% on heating costs. A 90% unit saves roughly 6%. Actual savings depend on climate, gas prices, and usage patterns. Cold regions benefit most from higher AFUE efficiency.
Is 13 SEER1 good enough, or should I pay more for 16+ SEER? +
13 SEER1 is competent for moderate climates and moderate cooling loads. Higher SEER units cost significantly more and recover costs only in hot climates or high-use scenarios. This unit balances efficiency with affordability for typical replacement scenarios.
Does the 10-year warranty cover parts if something fails? +
Yes, parts coverage is included with online registration at no extra cost. Labor and wear items are excluded. Registration protects you; skip it and warranty validity becomes disputable.
