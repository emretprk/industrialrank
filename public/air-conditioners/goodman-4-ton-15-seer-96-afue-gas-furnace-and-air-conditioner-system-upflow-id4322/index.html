Review

Goodman's 4-ton upflow system arrives as a solid mid-tier option for contractors and homeowners seeking balanced heating and cooling without premium pricing. The 96% AFUE furnace positions this unit well against competitors still selling 90-92% models, while the 15 to 16 SEER cooling performance stays competitive for moderate climates. SKU LS381183850 carries AHRI certification 7368842 and Energy Star qualification, signaling compliance with current efficiency standards.

The furnace delivers 120,000 BTU of heating capacity with two-stage burner control, while the condenser side produces 45,500 BTU cooling via a single-stage scroll compressor running on R410A refrigerant. Variable-speed ECM blower handles up to 2000 CFM across upflow or downflow applications. At 208/230V single-phase 60 Hz and requiring 23.9 to 40 amp breaker sizing, most homes accept this without panel modifications.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 96% AFUE furnace efficiency significantly reduces natural gas consumption and heating costs year-over-year
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor optimizes airflow from 0 to 2000 CFM, improving comfort and lowering electricity use
  • Two-stage burner and single-stage compressor provide reliable, predictable performance without short cycling
  • PVC/plastic exhaust flue compatibility simplifies venting and reduces installation costs versus rigid metal ducts
  • 10-year warranty with online registration covers parts and compressor, protecting your investment

Cons

  • SEER rating listed as 16 in specs but product title claims 15 SEER, creating specification confusion at point of purchase
  • 73 dB noise level at full capacity may be noticeable during evening operation in quiet neighborhoods
  • Single-stage condenser limits variable cooling, resulting in less precise temperature control than two-stage systems

Who Should Buy

Best For

Homeowners in northern climates replacing aging 80-85% AFUE furnaces will see immediate utility savings. Contractors seeking straightforward residential installs with modern efficiency and rebate eligibility will appreciate the A-coil configuration and ECM motor.

Consider Alternatives If

Buyers in cooling-dominant climates or those seeking highest-efficiency cooling should consider two-stage condenser systems instead.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do the specs show both 15 SEER and 16 SEER? +
The product title lists 15 SEER, but the specification sheet shows SEER1 as 16. This discrepancy likely reflects either a transitional rating between standards or a data entry error. Request AHRI certificate 7368842 from your dealer to confirm exact cooling efficiency before purchase.
Can this system work in my home with a 100-amp service? +
Yes. The unit requires minimum 23.9 amps and maximum 40 amps breaker capacity, making it compatible with virtually all residential electrical services. A licensed electrician should verify existing circuit capacity and install appropriate breakers.
How much will heating costs drop compared to my old 90% AFUE furnace? +
At 96% AFUE versus 90%, you recover 6 percentage points of fuel as usable heat. On a 100,000 BTU per hour draw, that equals roughly 6% annual heating cost reduction, varying by local gas prices, insulation, and heating degree days.
Is this better than a Bryant or Carrier equivalent? +
Goodman offers identical efficiency and reliability at lower cost due to streamlined manufacturing. Bryant and Carrier command premiums for branding and extended warranties. Performance-wise, all three deliver comparable 96% AFUE and mid-tier SEER ratings.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Parts and compressor for manufacturing defects when registered online. Does not cover labor, refrigerant, installation errors, or lack of maintenance. Standard across Goodman residential units.
