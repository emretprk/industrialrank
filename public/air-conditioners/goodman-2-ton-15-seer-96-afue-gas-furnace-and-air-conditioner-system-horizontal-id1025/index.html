Review

The Goodman 2-ton horizontal furnace and air conditioner system (SKU LS38343850) occupies a practical middle ground in the residential HVAC market. Priced at $2666, it targets budget-conscious homeowners and contractors who refuse to compromise on efficiency standards. This unit replaces the older GSX16S241 model and brings modern refrigerant compatibility and variable-speed blower technology to systems that might otherwise rely on decade-old equipment. The horizontal A-coil configuration makes it ideal for attic or horizontal installation scenarios common in many homes.

Performance data shows legitimate efficiency credentials. The furnace delivers 80,000 BTU heating output at 96% AFUE, meaning 96 cents of every dollar spent on gas converts to usable heat. Cooling capacity sits at 23,600 BTU with a 16 SEER1 rating, exceeding ENERGY STAR minimums and qualifying for state and utility rebates in most regions. The variable-speed ECM blower maxes out at 1200 CFM and adjusts automatically to room demand. However, the single-stage compressor lacks modulation, so on mild days the system runs at full capacity rather than scaling back to save energy.

Installation

Installation requires standard 115V, 60Hz single-phase power with a 12.4 to 20-amp breaker. The unit accepts both R410A and R22 refrigerant, giving flexibility for retrofit scenarios. Suction line (3/4 inch) and liquid line (3/8 inch) connections follow industry standards. Horizontal mounting dimensions measure 34.5 inches tall by 17.5 wide by 28.75 deep. PVC exhaust flue compatibility simplifies ductwork compared to metal requirements on older units.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the actual tonnage, 2-ton or 3-ton? +
The specification sheet contains conflicting data listing both 2-ton and 3-ton, though the product title and cooling BTU of 23,600 align with a 2-ton unit. Verify with Online Supply before purchase to confirm capacity matches your home's cooling load calculations.
Can this replace my old R22 system without major changes? +
Yes. The unit accepts both R410A and R22 refrigerant, allowing retrofit into existing copper line sets. However, mixing refrigerants is prohibited, so you'll drain the old system completely. PVC exhaust compatibility also simplifies furnace replacement.
How much will this save me annually compared to my 1995 furnace? +
A typical 1995 furnace runs 78-80% AFUE. At 96% AFUE, you'll recover roughly 16-18% of heating costs. For a $1200 annual heating bill, expect $190-220 in yearly savings. Payback occurs in 12-15 years before accounting for rebates.
Is 16 SEER1 better or worse than older SEER2 ratings I see advertised? +
SEER1 and SEER2 use different rating standards. SEER2 numbers run 15-25% lower than SEER1 equivalents. A 16 SEER1 unit roughly equals an 11-12 SEER2 system, so verify comparison apples-to-apples with manufacturers.
Does online registration really matter for the 10-year warranty? +
Yes. Without registration, coverage defaults to 5 years. Registration is free and takes 5 minutes on Goodman's website. Keep your receipt and paperwork to prove installation date.
