Review

Goodman has built a reputation for delivering dependable HVAC equipment at accessible pricing, and this 3-ton downflow system reinforces that position. At $736, the SKU LS38783850 lands squarely in the budget-to-mid-range category without sacrificing core efficiency metrics. The combination of a 96% AFUE furnace paired with an 18 SEER1 condenser makes this a genuine option for homeowners tired of utility bills climbing year over year. Online Supply backs the system with a 10-year warranty on parts.

The numbers justify the efficiency claims. The furnace delivers 80,000 BTU of heating output with two-stage burner control, while the condenser provides 35,000 BTU of cooling capacity. The variable-speed ECM blower motor dynamically adjusts between zero and 1,600 CFM, preventing the constant full-speed operation that plagues single-speed systems. Operating at 71 dB, this two-stage scroll compressor setup runs noticeably quieter than comparable single-stage units, a real benefit during evening cooling cycles.

Installation

Installation requires standard residential electrical service (115V, 1-phase, 60 Hz) with breaker capacity between 21.3 and 35 amps. The downflow configuration suits basements and attics with adequate clearance. Refrigerant line sizing calls for 3/8 inch liquid and 7/8 inch suction lines with 3/4 inch insulation. PVC exhaust venting is required. Most contractors complete installation in 4-6 hours with standard tools.

Warranty & Reliability

Online Supply provides 10 years of parts coverage after online registration, a solid baseline for this price tier. The Goodman evaporator coil carries its own warranty document. Coverage excludes labor, refrigerant, and damage from improper installation or maintenance. Registration is required to activate coverage, so homeowners should complete this immediately after installation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the product title say 17 SEER but the specs list 18 SEER1? +
SEER1 is a newer, more stringent efficiency metric replacing the older SEER standard. The condenser achieves 18 SEER1, which is actually stronger than the 17 SEER mentioned in marketing copy. You're getting better-than-advertised cooling efficiency. Always verify SEER1 ratings in modern systems for apples-to-apples comparison.
Can this system work with my existing ductwork in a 1,200 square foot home? +
At 3.5 tons with 1,600 CFM maximum airflow, this system suits homes up to roughly 1,400 square feet. Verify your ductwork can handle 1,600 CFM without exceeding 0.1 inches of static pressure. Have an HVAC contractor perform a Manual D load calculation to confirm proper sizing and duct adequacy before purchase.
Does the 96% AFUE furnace really save that much compared to an 80% system? +
Yes. A 96% furnace wastes only 4 cents of every heating dollar, versus 20 cents on an 80% model. Heating costs 40-60% of annual utility bills in cold climates. Over 15 years, the 96% system saves $1,500 to $3,000 in fuel costs, easily offsetting the modest equipment premium.
How does this Goodman compare to higher-end brands like Lennox or Carrier? +
This Goodman delivers comparable efficiency metrics at 40-50% lower cost. Premium brands add extended warranties, quieter compressors, and advanced controls. For budget-conscious buyers, Goodman's two-stage design and variable-speed blower provide 85% of the performance at 55% of the price.
What happens if I don't register the warranty online? +
Coverage is forfeited without registration. Contact Online Supply with your serial number and date of installation within 30 days of startup. Many contractors register systems on behalf of customers, but verify this happened before leaving the job site.
