Review

The Goodman 5-ton gas furnace and air conditioner combo represents a mainstream solution for residential equipment replacement. Offered through Online Supply as SKU LS381183850, this upflow-configured system combines a 100,000 BTU furnace with a 54,000 BTU condenser in an A-coil format. At the advertised SEER rating of 14, this unit sits solidly in the middle-tier efficiency category, neither cutting-edge nor dated. The variable-speed blower motor and two-stage heating burner hint at thoughtful engineering beyond commodity pricing.

Real-world performance data shows the cooling side delivers 16 SEER2 efficiency, outpacing the published 14 SEER spec. The 100,000 BTU furnace at 80% AFUE converts four dollars of fuel into three dollars of heat output, a respectable but not exceptional conversion rate. Cooling capacity of 54,000 BTU matches the 5-ton tonnage correctly for R410A refrigerant systems. The scroll compressor operates at 73 decibels, meeting residential noise standards. Electrical requirements span 208 to 230V single-phase at 60 Hz, with breaker sizing between 29.6 and 50 amps depending on circuit configuration.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 16 SEER2 cooling efficiency exceeds the 14 SEER spec, delivering better-than-advertised performance
  • Variable-speed blower motor reduces electricity consumption during partial-load operation, cutting blower runtime by up to 30% versus single-speed units
  • Two-stage burner on the furnace allows low-stage heating on mild days, improving efficiency over single-stage competitors
  • Scroll compressor in the condenser is proven for durability and quiet operation at 73 dB, quieter than rotary alternatives
  • Upflow configuration works for basement or utility closet installation in most residential layouts

Cons

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does this system show 14 SEER in the title but 16 SEER1 in the specs? +
SEER2 and SEER1 use different test protocols. The actual cooling efficiency tested under SEER2 conditions is 16, which is more conservative than old SEER ratings. This unit outperforms the conservative 14 SEER label, offering genuine efficiency gains over baseline models.
Can I install this in an attic or crawlspace instead of upflow? +
The A-coil configuration is certified for upflow and horizontal applications only. Horizontal installation in attics is possible if you have adequate return-air ducting. Downflow or other angles void the warranty and compromise refrigerant flow, so stick to approved orientations.
How much will this system cost to operate annually compared to a 95% AFUE furnace? +
At 80% AFUE, you lose 20 cents per dollar in heating fuel. Upgrading to 95% AFUE saves roughly 15 cents per dollar, reducing annual heating cost by 18-22% depending on climate zone. In cold climates, that gap equals 300 to 600 dollars per winter.
Is this Goodman system as reliable as Carrier or Lennox equipment? +
Goodman and Daikin own competing factories. Goodman's scroll compressors and two-stage burners match competitor reliability, though some contractors report fewer service calls with premium brands. This unit offers similar durability at lower installed cost, favoring value-conscious buyers over brand prestige.
What happens if I don't register the warranty online? +
Without registration, Goodman may deny warranty claims for defects. Registration is free and takes five minutes online. Always retain documentation and register promptly to ensure coverage remains valid throughout the 10-year protection window.
