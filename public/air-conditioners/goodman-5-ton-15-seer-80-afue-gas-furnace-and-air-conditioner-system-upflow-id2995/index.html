Review

The Goodman DSXC160601 represents practical equipment design for regional contractors and cost-conscious homeowners. This 5-ton system pairs an air conditioner with an 80,000 BTU furnace in an upflow A-coil configuration that fits standard attic or closet installations. Goodman's reputation for no-frills reliability, combined with a $2053 price point, positions this squarely in the value segment where budget-conscious buyers make their purchasing decisions.

Performance metrics reveal a competent mid-tier system. The 15.5 SEER cooling rating and R410A refrigerant deliver 60,000 BTU cooling output, while the 80% AFUE furnace generates 80,000 BTU heating. Two-stage operation on both sides means the compressor and burner modulate between capacities rather than cycling on and off, reducing temperature swings and energy waste. The variable-speed blower reaches 2000 CFM maximum, pulling roughly 37.2 amps minimum at 208V/230V single phase, keeping electrical requirements manageable for most homes.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 15.5 SEER cooling efficiency earns Energy Star certification and reduces summer electric bills noticeably
  • Two-stage compressor and two-stage burner allow modulated operation for better part-load performance and comfort
  • Variable-speed blower motor maximizes CFM (2000 max) while minimizing runtime noise at 75 dBA
  • Two-stage burner design provides better temperature control and faster heating response than single-stage units
  • 10-year warranty with online registration covers compressor and parts, standard for this price tier

Cons

  • 80% AFUE heating efficiency is entry-level, costing more annually than 95% AFUE systems in cold climates
  • Metal exhaust flue requirement limits installation flexibility compared to PVC-capable units
  • Requires 37.2 to 60 amp breaker, necessitating electrical service upgrade in older homes

Who Should Buy

Best For

Homeowners in moderate climates replacing older systems and contractors building spec homes where mid-range performance meets budget constraints. This unit delivers solid efficiency gains over 10+ year old equipment without the premium price tag of high-SEER alternatives.

Consider Alternatives If

Anyone in very cold climates or with high heating demands should prioritize 90%+ AFUE units, and homes with severely limited electrical capacity may face expensive panel upgrades.

Installation

Installation requires metal exhaust flue piping and 3/8 inch liquid lines with 7/8 inch suction lines for refrigerant. The 301-pound unit measures 38.25 by 35.5 by 35.5 inches, fitting standard alcoves. Electrical service must handle 37.2 to 60 amp draw depending on load. Most contractors can complete installation in 4 to 6 hours on a straightforward retrofit.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman includes a 10-year warranty on compressor and parts when you register online within 60 days of purchase. This covers defects but excludes labor, maintenance, or damage from improper installation. Registration is straightforward and extends coverage, making this warranty competitive for budget equipment.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 15.5 SEER compare to newer 16+ SEER systems? +
This unit's 15.5 SEER is solid but slightly trailing current high-efficiency models rated 16 to 18 SEER. Real-world cooling cost difference runs 5 to 10 percent annually. For most homeowners, 15.5 SEER payback happens in 7 to 10 years versus older 10 SEER equipment.
Can this system retrofit my existing furnace location? +
Yes, the upflow/downflow configuration adapts to most standard installations. Verify your existing return air ductwork matches the 2000 CFM maximum airflow and that your electrical panel has adequate capacity. Metal flue requirement may necessitate changes if you currently run PVC.
What makes two-stage operation beneficial? +
Two-stage burners and compressors run at partial capacity during mild weather, reducing energy consumption and improving comfort through less frequent cycling. In spring and fall, you'll notice steadier temperatures and quieter operation compared to single-stage units that blast at full power.
How does this compare to higher-priced Goodman or competitor units? +
Premium Goodman models add 16+ SEER ratings and 95% AFUE, costing $400 to 800 more. Regional brands like Lennox or Carrier charge similar premiums for equivalent efficiency. This unit trades cutting-edge specs for realistic pricing and solid real-world performance.
Is online warranty registration required? +
Yes, you must register within 60 days to activate the 10-year coverage. Registration takes 5 minutes online through Goodman's portal. Without it, warranty defaults to 5 years, making registration worthwhile insurance.
