Review

The Goodman 3-ton combo system represents the practical middle ground in residential HVAC. At $2,552, it positions itself well against older equipment still running 60-65% AFUE furnaces and 10 SEER AC units. This unit combines a 60,000 BTU furnace with a 34,800 BTU cooling capacity into one upflow configuration, making it suitable for single-stage replacement work. Goodman's reputation for straightforward engineering and parts availability gives contractors confidence in service calls 5-10 years down the road.

Performance specs tell the real story here. The 80% AFUE furnace with two-stage burner and variable-speed blower modulates heat output more efficiently than single-stage alternatives, reducing on-off cycling by roughly 30 percent. Cooling delivers 16 SEER1 efficiency, which outperforms the stated 14 SEER baseline. The scroll compressor runs on R410A refrigerant with standard 3/8 inch liquid and 7/8 inch suction lines. Maximum airflow reaches 1,600 CFM through the A-coil, supporting proper circulation in properly ducted homes. Energy Star certification confirms these efficiency levels meet federal minimums.

Professional installation is strongly recommended to ensure proper refrigerant charging and ductwork balance.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does 16 SEER1 compare to the advertised 14 SEER rating? +
SEER1 is the newer testing standard that more accurately reflects real-world conditions than legacy SEER ratings. This unit's 16 SEER1 rating indicates it actually performs better than the 14 SEER specification suggests. You'll see measurable cooling cost reductions versus older 10-12 SEER units, roughly 25-35 percent savings depending on climate and usage patterns.
Can this system handle both natural gas and propane? +
Yes, the burner accommodates both natural gas and propane with proper conversion kits installed by a licensed technician. Confirm your local utility supply before purchase. Conversion typically involves adjusting orifice sizes and regulator settings, a service most HVAC contractors complete in 1-2 hours for minimal additional cost.
Will the two-stage burner significantly reduce my heating costs? +
Two-stage burners reduce runtime by 20-30 percent compared to single-stage units through modulating operation. Combined with the 80% AFUE efficiency, you'll see noticeable savings on winter gas bills, especially in homes with good insulation. Exact savings depend on climate, home size, and thermostat settings, but typically 10-15 percent annual improvement is realistic.
How does this compare to higher-end 3-ton systems from Lennox or Carrier? +
Premium brands offer 95%+ AFUE furnaces and variable-capacity compressors that cost $3,500-4,500 installed. This Goodman system sacrifices those features for lower upfront cost. If you plan to stay 15+ years, premium efficiency pays back. For 7-10 year occupancy, this Goodman unit delivers better overall value with proven reliability.
Does online registration for the warranty require special steps? +
Goodman's online registration is straightforward and free. You'll need your AHRI certificate number (204633301 for this model), serial number, and installation date. Complete registration within 60 days of installation to activate the full 10-year coverage on parts and labor.
