This Goodman 5-ton system excels for homeowners and contractors seeking high heating efficiency without premium pricing. The 97% AFUE furnace and modulating burner deliver excellent gas-side performance, making it ideal for cold climates where heating dominates annual costs.

Review

The Goodman 5-ton downflow system represents a practical middle ground for homeowners balancing performance and budget. At 100,000 BTU heating capacity and 97% AFUE, this furnace dominates the heating side of the equation, making it especially compelling in northern climates. The unit weighs 73 pounds, fits tight spaces with its 21x21-inch footprint, and accepts PVC exhaust for flexible installation. Goodman's reputation for reliability backs up the hardware here, and contractor familiarity keeps labor costs predictable.

Performance specs reveal solid engineering. The modulating burner prevents short-cycling losses, while the variable-speed ECM blower maintains efficient airflow across the 2,000 CFM maximum range. Cooling arrives via a 57,000 BTU, two-stage scroll compressor delivering 18 SEER2 efficiency with R410A refrigerant. The 3/8-inch liquid line and 7/8-inch suction line are properly sized for heat transfer. Electrically, the unit demands 208/230V single-phase at 36.7 to 60 amps, typical for residential installs. These numbers translate to lower heating bills without premium equipment cost.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much heating fuel will this furnace save versus a 95% AFUE model? +
The 2-point AFUE gain typically saves 2 to 3 percent annually in heating energy. For a $1,200 annual heating bill, expect savings of 24 to 36 dollars yearly, or roughly 280 to 430 dollars over 10 years. ROI depends on local gas prices and installation cost premium.
Can I install this unit upflow instead of downflow? +
Yes, the specs confirm upflow/downflow configuration capability. However, consult the CAPT installation manual for ductwork and clearance requirements specific to your home. Horizontal discharge orientation differs from vertical, affecting return air plenum design.
Is 18 SEER2 adequate for a 5-ton system in a warm climate? +
18 SEER2 is competent but not premium. Competitors offer 20 to 24 SEER2 in 5-ton units. If cooling runtime exceeds 6 months annually, higher-SEER models may deliver better payback despite higher upfront cost. Run a detailed energy audit for your location.
How does this Goodman compare to Lennox and Carrier 5-ton systems at similar price points? +
Goodman prioritizes heating efficiency and value, while Lennox and Carrier typically emphasize cooling SEER2 ratings and noise reduction. This unit's 97% AFUE is competitive; cooling at 18 SEER2 lags premium brands. Choose Goodman for heating dominance, others for balanced performance.
What happens if I don't register the warranty online? +
Warranty coverage defaults to a shorter term without registration. Goodman requires online registration to activate the full 10-year protection. Dealers typically handle this, but verify completion in writing to avoid future disputes.
