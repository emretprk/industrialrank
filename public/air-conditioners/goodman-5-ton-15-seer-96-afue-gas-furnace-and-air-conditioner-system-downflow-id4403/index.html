Review

Goodman has built a solid reputation for delivering reliable HVAC equipment at reasonable price points, and this 5-ton downflow system exemplifies that philosophy. The unit combines a 100,000 BTU furnace with a 54,000 BTU air conditioner in a compact 30-by-21-by-21-inch footprint, making it suitable for basements, attics, and utility closets. Online Supply backs the system with a 10-year warranty and Energy Star certification, positioning it as a practical choice for contractors and homeowners balancing performance with budget constraints.

The furnace reaches 96% AFUE, which means only 4% of fuel energy escapes as waste, translating to lower heating bills over the unit's lifespan. The two-stage burner fine-tunes output to match demand, reducing temperature swings. The variable-speed ECM blower adjusts CFM from near-zero to 2,000 CFM, moderating electricity consumption during part-load operation. The air conditioner delivers SEER 16 cooling and 54,000 BTU capacity, acceptable for moderate climates but not competitive with 18-20 SEER systems gaining market share.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the real difference between 96% AFUE and the 80% furnaces my neighbor has? +
At 96% AFUE, only 4% of your fuel escapes as waste versus 20% with an 80% unit. Over 20 years, that saves roughly 15,000 therms of natural gas or significant propane costs, depending on usage and local rates. The higher upfront cost pays back through lower heating bills.
Can I install this in my attic, or does downflow only work in basements? +
Downflow works in both locations. In attics, ductwork runs downward through walls and floors to supply rooms below. Ensure proper PVC exhaust venting through an exterior wall and verify your attic can support the 30-pound furnace plus ductwork weight without structural issues.
Is SEER 16 enough cooling for hot summers, or should I upgrade? +
SEER 16 handles mild to moderate cooling demands effectively. If summer temperatures exceed 95°F regularly or you live in high-humidity regions, consider SEER 18-20 units with two-stage compressors for superior dehumidification and part-load efficiency.
How does the variable-speed blower actually save money compared to single-speed models? +
Single-speed blowers run at full 2,000 CFM whenever heating or cooling activates, wasting electricity during mild weather. This Goodman's ECM motor ramps to 500-1,000 CFM on part-load days, cutting blower electricity use by 30-50% annually while improving comfort consistency.
What happens to my warranty if I don't register online within 30 days? +
Non-registered units default to a shorter warranty period, typically 5-7 years instead of 10. Registration is free and takes five minutes. Keep the AHRI certificate and installation paperwork as proof for any future claims.
