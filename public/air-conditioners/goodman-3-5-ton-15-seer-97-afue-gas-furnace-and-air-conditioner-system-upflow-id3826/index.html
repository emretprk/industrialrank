Review

Goodman's 3.5-ton combo system positions itself as a practical middle ground in the residential HVAC market. It pairs a modulating furnace delivering 120,000 BTU heating output with a 42,000 BTU cooling capacity AC unit. This upflow configuration suits most basement or utility closet installations. The system carries Goodman's reputation for dependable performance at accessible pricing, making it popular with contractors stocking standard residential replacement units.

The furnace achieves 97% AFUE efficiency, meaning 97 cents of every fuel dollar converts to usable heat, drastically outperforming older 80% models still operating in many homes. The modulating burner continuously adjusts flame intensity rather than cycling fully on and off, reducing temperature swings and short-cycling wear. The variable-speed ECM blower motor ramps down during mild weather, cutting parasitic electrical draw. SEER1 rating of 16 on the cooling side provides solid efficiency for mid-tier AC performance, though single-stage operation limits modulation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does 97% AFUE actually save me compared to my old 80% furnace? +
A 17-percentage-point efficiency gain translates to roughly 20% lower heating costs annually. For homes burning $1,000 yearly in heating fuel, expect savings around $200. Over the system's 15-20 year lifespan, cumulative savings exceed $3,000 to $4,000 before accounting for fuel price increases.
Does this upflow configuration work in my basement installation? +
Yes, the 30-inch height and upflow design suit basements perfectly. The right-side flue venting through plastic PVC accommodates basement walls. Standard 3/8-inch liquid and 1-1/8-inch suction lines connect easily to outdoor condenser units, and the 2000 maximum CFM blower handles typical residential ductwork.
Why is the cooling capacity only 42,000 BTU for a 3.5-ton unit? +
The advertised tonnage appears inconsistent with actual specifications, which list 5-ton capacity. One ton equals 12,000 BTU, so 3.5 tons should be 42,000 BTU. The specs confirm 42,000 BTU cooling output, making this effectively a properly sized 3.5-ton system despite the conflicting tonnage notation in some documentation.
How does modulating burner technology compare to standard on-off burners? +
Modulating burners match heating output to actual demand, reducing overshooting and temperature swings. Standard burners run at full capacity then shut off completely, cycling frequently and creating temperature fluctuations. Modulating systems provide steadier comfort, reduce wear on components, and improve efficiency by avoiding excess heating waste.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Coverage includes parts and compressor defects when you register online. Labor costs, refrigerant recharges, and routine maintenance aren't covered. The warranty protects against manufacturing defects but excludes installer or homeowner errors, standard industry practice.
