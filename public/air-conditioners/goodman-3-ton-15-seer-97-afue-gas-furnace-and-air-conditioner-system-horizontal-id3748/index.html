Review

The Goodman CHPF3743D6 fills a specific market need, targeting HVAC contractors and homeowners constrained by horizontal installation requirements. At the 3-ton capacity and 97% AFUE rating, this unit positions itself above commodity-grade furnaces while remaining price-conscious. The horizontal A-coil design reflects Goodman's practical engineering approach, solving real-world placement challenges without charging premium prices typical of specialty configurations.

Performance data shows a modulating burner paired with a variable-speed ECM blower motor, combining responsive heating with energy discipline. The unit delivers 120,000 BTU input heating capacity with 97% efficiency, while the 3.5-ton air conditioning side produces 36,000 BTU cooling at 16 SEER1 efficiency. A 2,000 CFM maximum airflow supports proper ductwork sizing, and the 208/230V single-phase requirement keeps electrical upgrades minimal on most residential panels.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 97% AFUE efficiency means 3 percentage points better than standard 94% models, translating to roughly $50-80 annual savings for average households
  • Modulating burner adjusts output in stages, reducing temperature swings and improving comfort compared to single-stage competitors
  • Variable-speed ECM blower motor runs only as fast as needed, cutting electricity consumption and lowering operating costs
  • Horizontal configuration solves installation challenges in tight attics or crawlspaces where vertical furnaces won't fit

Cons

Frequently Asked Questions

How much will I actually save on heating costs compared to a 94% AFUE furnace? +
The 97% AFUE delivers approximately 3 percentage points better efficiency. On a $1,200 annual heating bill, this represents roughly $36-50 annual savings. Payback occurs in 8-12 years depending on climate, fuel costs, and runtime. Modulating burners reduce cycling losses, amplifying savings in moderate climates.
Will this horizontal furnace fit in my attic if I have space constraints? +
At 24.5 inches tall and 26 inches deep, it works in tight attics where vertical furnaces won't clear rafters or joists. Measure your actual opening height and verify right-side flue clearance. The 63-pound weight requires adequate support framing. Professional installation assessment is recommended for non-standard spaces.
What's the real difference between 16 SEER1 and 15 SEER rating specifications? +
SEER1 is the newer testing standard reflecting real-world cooling performance more accurately than legacy SEER ratings. At 16 SEER1, this unit delivers reliable summer cooling, though it trails 17-18 SEER1 premium models. Cooling efficiency matters less than heating in moderate climates where furnace runtime dominates annual consumption.
Should I choose R410A or R22 refrigerant for a new installation? +
Select R410A. The EPA phases out R22 production in 2030, making service increasingly expensive and parts scarce. R410A is the current standard, ensuring technician availability and refrigerant supply throughout the equipment's 15-20 year lifespan. Any new installation today should specify R410A.
Does the 10-year warranty cover the air conditioning compressor and coil equally? +
Yes, the 10-year warranty covers the furnace and compressor under the same registration. Goodman's evaporator coil warranty documentation is provided separately, confirming comparable coverage. Register online immediately after installation to activate all warranty protections.
