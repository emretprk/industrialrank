Review

The Goodman GMVC961205DN combines a gas furnace and air conditioner into one horizontal unit, solving a real problem for contractors working in confined spaces. This 5-ton system delivers 120,000 BTU heating and 60,000 BTU cooling from a footprint measuring 34.5 inches tall by 24.5 inches wide by 28.75 inches deep. Goodman positions this as an efficient, cost-effective solution for residential retrofits where space constraints make traditional vertical mounting impractical. It carries Energy Star certification, which matters for homeowners pursuing rebates or energy credits.

Performance specs reveal this unit punches above its marketing numbers. The published 15 SEER rating understates actual efficiency, as the condenser achieves 16 SEER1 on the newer testing standard. The furnace reaches 96% AFUE, meaning only 4% of gas energy is wasted up the flue. Two-stage operation on both heating and cooling reduces short-cycling and extends runtime efficiency. The scroll compressor and variable-speed ECM blower modulate output to match real demand, not just cycle on and off like single-stage systems.

Installation

Installation demands a trained, licensed technician due to gas safety protocols. The unit requires 115V single-phase 60 Hz power with breaker sizing between 37.2 and 60 amps depending on local code. Sweat-type refrigerant connections use 3/8 inch liquid lines and 7/8 inch suction lines. Flue venting requires plastic or PVC exhaust pipe rated for condensing furnace discharge. Horizontal mounting flexibility makes it valuable for basements or attics, but ductwork adaptation may be necessary.

Warranty & Reliability

Coverage includes a lifetime warranty on the heat exchanger, protecting against the costliest failure point, plus 10 years of parts coverage when registered online. This dual approach incentivizes proper registration and provides meaningful protection during the system's productive life. Lifetime heat exchanger coverage is generous for a mid-tier unit, though parts warranties lag behind premium competitors offering 15-year coverage.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the 16 SEER1 rating differ from the advertised 15 SEER? +
SEER1 uses updated testing standards reflecting real-world conditions more accurately than legacy SEER ratings. This unit's 16 SEER1 represents approximately 6.7% better cooling efficiency than a true 15 SEER system, translating to meaningful electricity savings over 15-20 years of operation, especially in hot climates with extended cooling seasons.
Can I install this myself or do I absolutely need a professional? +
Professional installation is non-negotiable. Gas line connections, flue venting, refrigerant charge, and safety interlocks require licensed technician expertise. Improper installation risks carbon monoxide exposure, gas leaks, or equipment damage. Most jurisdictions legally require licensed installation. Attempting DIY work voids warranty and creates liability.
What temperature rise should I expect from the 120,000 BTU furnace? +
Temperature rise depends on airflow. At maximum CFM of 2000, expect approximately 35-40 degree Fahrenheit rise across the coil. The variable-speed ECM blower adjusts to maintain proper rise, protecting the heat exchanger from overheating while optimizing comfort and efficiency across all load conditions.
How does this horizontal unit compare to vertical configurations? +
Horizontal units sacrifice some installation flexibility but excel in space-constrained locations. This model's compact 34.5-inch height fits attics and crawlspaces where vertical coils cannot. Performance and efficiency are comparable, but horizontal units typically cost more due to specialized manufacturing and require careful ductwork routing.
Is the lifetime heat exchanger warranty actually valuable? +
Yes. Heat exchanger replacement typically costs 800 to 1500 dollars in labor and parts. Lifetime coverage eliminates this risk, though it only applies if the furnace was installed and maintained correctly. Register your system online immediately after installation to activate this benefit.
