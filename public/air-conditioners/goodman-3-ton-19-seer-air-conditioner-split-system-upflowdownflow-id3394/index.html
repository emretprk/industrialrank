This 5-ton Goodman condenser excels for contractors seeking affordable, efficient cooling on moderate budgets. It's ideal for residential retrofit jobs where upflow/downflow flexibility matters and energy bills need trimming without premium pricing.

Review

The Goodman CAPF4961D6 positions itself as the practical middle ground in residential air conditioning. This 5-ton split system delivers 35,000 BTU cooling capacity with 18 SEER1 efficiency, earning ENERGY STAR certification without premium pricing. Goodman's reputation for straightforward reliability makes this condenser appealing to contractors who value dependable performance over cutting-edge features. The two-stage scroll compressor and ECM variable-speed blower suggest thoughtful engineering, though the price point keeps this unit accessible to cost-conscious buyers.

Performance specs reveal a competent workhorse designed for typical residential loads. The 18 SEER1 rating translates to meaningfully lower electric bills compared to 14-16 SEER units, a practical advantage over system lifespans averaging 15 years. Operating at 71 dB, the unit runs acceptably quiet for most installations, though not silent. The variable-speed blower motor maxes at 2,000 CFM and adjusts automatically to maintain comfort, reducing the energy waste common in single-speed competitors. Two-stage operation means the compressor runs at reduced capacity most of the time, conserving compressor life.

Installation

Installation demands standard HVAC knowledge without unusual complexity. The unit accepts 208/230V single-phase 60 Hz power and requires proper breaker sizing, though the conflicting min/max amp specs warrant clarification with Goodman before rough-in work. Sweat valve connections use industry-standard 3/8 inch liquid line and 7/8 inch suction line sizing. The 30 inch height, 24.5 inch width, and 21 inch depth fit most outdoor pad locations. Upflow/downflow flexibility simplifies mounting decisions in tight spaces.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman backs this unit with 10 years of parts coverage, conditional on online registration. This is competitive for mid-tier equipment and provides genuine peace of mind for residential buyers. The included evaporator coil warranty protects the indoor side of the system. Registration must happen promptly after installation to activate full protection, so confirm this step with your installing contractor.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet list both R410A and R22 refrigerant compatibility? +
This appears to be documentation overlap from Goodman's product line. Modern systems should use R410A exclusively. R22 was phased out for new equipment manufacturing. Confirm with your supplier which refrigerant charge this specific unit shipped with before installation begins.
Can this condenser work with existing ductwork from my old system? +
Usually yes, if the old unit was also 5 tons. The 2,000 CFM maximum airflow is standard for this tonnage. However, duct sizing and condition matter greatly. Have a contractor verify CFM delivery rates and sealing to avoid efficiency losses.
How much energy savings should I expect compared to older 14 SEER systems? +
The 18 SEER1 rating typically delivers 20-25 percent lower cooling costs versus 14 SEER equipment, depending on climate and usage patterns. In hot climates where AC runs 6-8 months yearly, you could save $300-500 annually on electricity alone.
How does this Goodman unit compare to higher-SEER alternatives? +
Premium brands offer 20-24 SEER ratings and lower noise, but at double the price. The 18 SEER1 Goodman hits the practical efficiency sweet spot for budget-conscious buyers. The tradeoff is slightly louder operation and fewer advanced features, not reliability.
Do I need to register the warranty immediately after installation? +
Yes. The 10-year coverage only activates with online registration. Most contractors handle this, but confirm in writing. Without registration, coverage drops to parts-only for limited duration, costing you labor protection.
