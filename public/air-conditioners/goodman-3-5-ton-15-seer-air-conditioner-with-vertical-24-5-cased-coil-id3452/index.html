Review

This vertical cased A-coil from Online Supply represents Goodman's mainstream cooling approach, designed as an indoor component in split-system air conditioning installations. It's positioned for contractors and homeowners seeking Energy Star certified equipment without premium pricing. The unit handles both upflow and downflow configurations, making it adaptable to most residential furnace arrangements. However, a critical spec discrepancy between the product title claiming 3.5 tons and the specification sheet listing 5 tons requires clarification before purchase decisions.

Performance metrics show a 16 SEER1 efficiency rating paired with 42,000 BTU cooling capacity, representing a solid step up from baseline 13-14 SEER equipment. The scroll compressor operates as single-stage, meaning it runs full capacity or shuts off without intermediate speeds. This approach trades variable efficiency for mechanical simplicity and reliability. Operating at 73 dB puts it in the moderate noise range. Electrical requirements are modest at 208/230V single phase with a 23.5 to 40-amp breaker window, simplifying installation for most residential circuits.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this unit really deliver 3.5 tons or 5 tons of cooling capacity? +
The product title claims 3.5 tons, but the official specs list 5 ton tonnage with 42,000 BTU output. This discrepancy is concerning and requires direct manufacturer clarification before purchase. Contact Goodman or Online Supply to confirm actual capacity for your system design requirements.
Can I install this coil with any brand air conditioner condenser? +
No. This coil is designed for Goodman systems. Using it with competitor equipment voids warranties and risks refrigerant incompatibility. Verify the condenser unit matches this coil's tonnage rating, refrigerant type (R410A), and valve configurations before installation.
How does 16 SEER1 compare to newer efficiency standards? +
16 SEER1 is solid mid-tier efficiency, roughly equivalent to 15 SEER2 under newer testing standards. It beats baseline 13-14 SEER systems by 15-25 percent, delivering meaningful utility savings. Premium variable-speed units reach 18-21+ SEER2, but cost significantly more.
Should I choose this single-stage coil or a two-stage alternative? +
Single-stage suits moderate climates with consistent cooling demands. Two-stage costs more but runs at reduced capacity during mild weather, saving energy. Choose single-stage for simplicity and lower upfront cost; choose two-stage if your region has 40+ days of partial-load weather annually.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
The warranty covers manufacturing defects in components and sealed systems when registered online. It typically excludes labor, refrigerant, and damage from improper installation. Review Goodman's complete terms for specific exclusions and claim procedures.
