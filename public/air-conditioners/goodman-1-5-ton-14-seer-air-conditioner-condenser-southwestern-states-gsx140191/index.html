Review

The Goodman GSX140191 is a straightforward, no-frills 1.5 ton air conditioner condenser built for budget-conscious replacements. It delivers 18,000 BTU of cooling through a traditional scroll compressor in single-stage configuration. At $1,345, it undercuts two-stage competitors by $400-600, making it attractive for homeowners replacing failed units or contractors managing property portfolios where acquisition cost matters more than premium efficiency.

Performance-wise, the 14 SEER rating sits solidly in the mid-tier, meeting minimum efficiency standards across most regions while falling short of high-efficiency models. The scroll compressor handles cycling on a 208/230V single-phase circuit rated 12-30 amps without demanding electrical upgrades. The 71 dB noise signature is present but acceptable for non-residential applications, though noticeably louder than variable-speed alternatives. Single-stage operation means you lose modulation benefits during mild weather.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 14 SEER sufficient for hot climates? +
14 SEER works in zones 4-8 but struggles in extreme heat (zones 9-10). Units running 8+ hours daily in 100+ degree heat will incur higher electric bills. Consider 16+ SEER for southern climates despite higher upfront cost.
What electrical upgrades are needed? +
This unit requires 208/230V single-phase 60Hz service with 12-30 amp breaker capacity. Most residential installations need minimal work. Verify your panel has available breaker space and proper gauge wiring before purchase.
How does single-stage compare to two-stage units? +
Single-stage cycles fully on or off, less efficient during mild weather. Two-stage units modulate down to 70% capacity, improving efficiency by 2-4 SEER points. You'll save money long-term with two-stage despite higher initial cost.
Will this condenser work with my existing indoor coil? +
Compatibility depends on your indoor air handler age and refrigerant type. This unit uses R410A, so older R22 systems need complete replacement. Have a technician verify matched tonnage and refrigerant specifications before ordering.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
Parts only, not labor or refrigerant. Registration is required. Excludes damage from improper installation, lack of maintenance, or electrical issues. Read the full warranty document before relying on it.
