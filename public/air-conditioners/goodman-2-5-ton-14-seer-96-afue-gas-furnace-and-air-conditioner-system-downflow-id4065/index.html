Review

The Goodman 2.5 Ton gas furnace and air conditioner system targets the sweet spot between budget constraints and efficiency demands. At $3,109, it competes directly with generic contractor specials while offering measurable performance upgrades. The downflow configuration makes it suitable for installations requiring horizontal or basement placement. Goodman's reputation for straightforward reliability, combined with a 10-year parts warranty, positions this system as a practical choice for cost-conscious homeowners and volume contractors.

Performance specifications tell a strong efficiency story. The furnace delivers 60,000 BTU heating output at 96% AFUE, meaning 96 cents of every heating dollar converts to usable warmth instead of escaping through the chimney. The variable-speed ECM blower motor can deliver up to 1,200 CFM and adjusts speeds continuously rather than running full blast. Cooling arrives via R410A refrigerant in a scroll compressor. The two-stage burner reduces cycling by operating at partial capacity during shoulder seasons, improving both comfort and efficiency.

Installation

This unit requires standard 208-230V single-phase electrical service with a 17.1 to 25 amp breaker, making it compatible with most residential panels. PVC exhaust simplifies venting through exterior walls or attics without rigid ductwork. Downflow orientation suits basements and utility closets better than upflow. Standard line set requirements include 3/8 inch liquid and 7/8 inch suction lines with 3/4 inch insulation. Installation difficulty remains moderate for certified HVAC technicians.

Warranty & Reliability

Goodman provides 10 years of parts coverage with online registration, a solid baseline for mid-tier equipment. The warranty covers compressor, blower motor, and furnace components against manufacturing defects. Registration is mandatory to activate full coverage, so documentation matters. Extended warranties are available but not included. This warranty reflects industry standard for non-premium brands, adequate for expected 15-20 year equipment lifespan.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the spec sheet list both 14 SEER and 16 SEER ratings? +
The discrepancy appears to be a data entry error between title and specs. The 16 SEER1 listing is likely the correct cooling efficiency rating. Contact the supplier to confirm actual SEER performance before purchase, as this affects cooling cost estimates and rebate eligibility.
Is the 3 Ton cooling tonnage correct for a 2.5 Ton system? +
This mismatch suggests either a spec error or intentional oversizing for cooling performance. A 2.5 Ton heating capacity typically pairs with 2.5 Ton cooling. Verify actual cooling tonnage with the seller before ordering to ensure proper system matching and AHRI certification validity.
How much can I save annually with the 96% AFUE rating? +
A typical household heating 1,500 square feet in a cold climate using an 80% AFUE furnace spends roughly $1,200 annually on heating fuel. Upgrading to 96% AFUE reduces that to approximately $1,000, saving $200 per year. Payback depends on local fuel costs and heating degree-days.
Can I install this myself, or do I need a contractor? +
Professional installation is strongly recommended. Furnace installation requires EPA refrigerant certification, gas line compliance with local codes, and AHRI certification verification. Improper installation voids the 10-year warranty and creates safety hazards. Budget $500-1,500 for professional installation.
What makes the variable-speed blower worth the extra cost? +
The ECM motor runs only at necessary speeds, reducing energy consumption by 20-40% compared to single-speed blowers. It also provides quieter operation, better humidity control, and longer equipment lifespan through reduced wear cycles.
