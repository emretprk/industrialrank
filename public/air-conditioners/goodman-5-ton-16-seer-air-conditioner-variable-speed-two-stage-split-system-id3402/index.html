Review

The Goodman 5-ton two-stage split system occupies the practical middle ground in residential HVAC. At 18 SEER, it outperforms entry-level units while remaining accessible to budget-conscious homeowners. The variable-speed ECM blower and two-stage compressor reflect real engineering choices that reduce runtime and energy waste. Goodman targets contractors and DIY-minded homeowners who understand that efficiency translates directly to monthly utility savings.

Real-world performance speaks clearly through the specs. The unit delivers 57,000 BTU cooling capacity running on R410A refrigerant, with a maximum CFM of 2,040 through a 23-inch by 20-inch filter. The two-stage condenser adjusts output based on demand, while the scroll compressor handles 68,200 BTU heating capacity when paired with supplemental electric heat options ranging from 5 to 20 kilowatts. That flexibility matters for both retrofit and new-build applications.

Installation demands attention to electrical requirements. The unit requires 208, 230, or 240-volt single-phase service with two separate circuits, 57-60 amps for circuit 1 and 50 amps for circuit 2. Valve connections use standard 3/8-inch liquid and 7/8-inch suction sweat fittings. Frequently Asked Questions

What does 18 SEER actually mean for my energy bills? +
SEER measures cooling efficiency, and 18 SEER typically saves 20-25% annually versus 13 SEER baseline units. For a $100 monthly cooling bill, expect savings around $20-25 monthly during peak season. Real savings depend on usage patterns, climate, and how often two-stage operation engages.
Will my current electrical panel handle this unit? +
You need dual circuits: 57-60 amps for circuit 1 and 50 amps for circuit 2. Most homes built after 1980 accommodate this, but older panels may require a service upgrade costing $500-2,500. Have a licensed electrician verify compatibility before ordering.
How does the variable-speed blower improve comfort? +
The ECM blower ramps speed based on cooling demand rather than running at full speed constantly. Lower speeds mean quieter operation, better humidity removal, and reduced temperature swings. Full-speed operation reaches 2,040 CFM, but part-load efficiency is the real benefit.
How does this compare to Lennox or Carrier units at the same price point? +
Goodman trades brand prestige for value. At identical efficiency levels, Goodman delivers the same performance at 10-15% lower cost. Lennox and Carrier offer slightly better warranties and quieter operation, but the 18 SEER cooling capacity and two-stage design match their comparable models.
What happens if I don't register for the warranty? +
Without registration, coverage drops to 5 years. Online registration takes two minutes and extends it to 10 years. Always register immediately after installation to secure full protection.
