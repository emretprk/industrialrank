Review

The Goodman 4-ton upflow system positions itself as a practical middle-ground solution in today's HVAC market. With a 92% AFUE furnace and 14 SEER air conditioner, this unit targets homeowners who want solid efficiency without premium pricing. The 100,000 BTU heating capacity and 60,000 BTU cooling capacity suit homes between 2,000 and 2,500 square feet. Online Supply's offering here delivers Goodman's reputation for durability in a configuration that works with standard residential installations.

Performance specs tell a straightforward story. The furnace's 92% AFUE means 92 cents of every fuel dollar converts to heat, a solid 10-15 point improvement over systems from the 1990s. The 14 SEER cooling efficiency ranks as competent for non-premium tiers, delivering 60,000 BTU cooling output via a scroll compressor and R410A refrigerant. The multi-speed blower motor running up to 2,000 CFM enables better air distribution, while the single-stage design keeps things simple and reliable. These specifications perform particularly well in moderate climates where heating and cooling demands balance evenly.

Installation

Installation requires 208/230V single-phase 60Hz power with a minimum 32.8-amp and maximum 50-amp breaker. Frequently Asked Questions

How does 92% AFUE compare to my current furnace? +
If your furnace is 10+ years old, it likely runs 78-82% AFUE. Upgrading to 92% AFUE reduces heating energy consumption by 10-15%, translating to $200-400 annual savings in cold climates. The payback period typically spans 5-7 years through energy savings alone.
Will this system work in my upflow basement installation? +
Yes, the upflow A-coil configuration is designed specifically for basement or utility closet placement. The 30-inch height, 21-inch width, and 21-inch depth fit standard utility closets. Confirm your return air ductwork can feed upward and ensure adequate clearance for the service access.
Is 14 SEER good enough for hot summers? +
14 SEER provides decent cooling in moderate climates with occasional hot spells. In consistently hot, humid regions, upgrading to 16+ SEER delivers noticeably better dehumidification and consistent temperature control. Single-stage operation also means less precise cycling than variable-speed alternatives.
How does this compare to higher-end Goodman models? +
Premium Goodman systems add variable-speed motors, two-stage cooling, and SEER ratings reaching 18-20. This unit's single-stage design is simpler and more affordable but offers less granular temperature control and slightly higher operating costs over its lifespan.
What does the 10-year warranty actually cover? +
The registered warranty covers manufacturing defects and component failures in both furnace and air conditioning parts. Labor is included, removing repair costs. It does not cover maintenance, improper installation, or damage from neglect.
