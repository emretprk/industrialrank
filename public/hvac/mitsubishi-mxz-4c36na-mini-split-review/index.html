Extended Analysis

The MXZ-4C36NA serves the gap between single-zone mini splits and full VRF systems. Where VRF makes economic sense above 10-12 zones, and single-zone splits work for individual rooms, the 4-zone MXZ serves the most common commercial retrofit scenario: a small office suite, a multi-room medical clinic, or a restaurant requiring independent control of 2-4 zones without the cost and complexity of a VRF system.

Mitsubishi's Hyper-Heat technology is the defining feature for cold-climate applications. Standard mini splits lose significant capacity below 25°F — a 3-ton system may deliver only 1.5-2 tons of heating at 5°F. Hyper-Heat maintains near-rated capacity down to 5°F and delivers usable heating down to -13°F. For commercial buildings in the northern U.S. and Canada, this performance profile eliminates the need for a backup heating system in most applications, which changes the project economics substantially.

The MXZ platform uses a single outdoor unit serving up to four indoor units simultaneously. Each indoor unit can be independently controlled for temperature, mode (heating or cooling), and fan speed. Unlike heat recovery VRF, the MXZ-4C36NA does not support simultaneous heating and cooling in different zones — all zones operate in the same mode at any given time. For applications requiring true simultaneous heating and cooling, Mitsubishi's City Multi VRF systems are the appropriate specification.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Mitsubishi Hyper-Heat technology?

Hyper-Heat (H2i) is Mitsubishi's enhanced cold-climate heat pump technology that maintains near-rated heating capacity at temperatures as low as 5°F and delivers usable heating output down to -13°F. Standard mini splits typically lose 40-50% of rated heating capacity at 17°F. Hyper-Heat achieves this through a flash injection circuit in the compressor that pre-heats the refrigerant entering the compressor, maintaining efficient compression at low ambient temperatures.

Can the MXZ-4C36NA heat and cool different zones simultaneously?

No. The MXZ-4C36NA is a heat pump type (not heat recovery), meaning all connected indoor units operate in the same mode — either all cooling or all heating — at any given time. For simultaneous heating and cooling in different zones from a single outdoor unit, Mitsubishi's City Multi heat recovery VRF series is required.

How many BTUs does each zone get from the MXZ-4C36NA?

Total outdoor unit capacity is 36,000 BTU/hr. The system distributes capacity among connected indoor units based on demand. Each indoor unit can be sized from 6,000 to 18,000 BTU, with total connected indoor capacity ranging from 100% to 130% of outdoor unit capacity. The system automatically allocates capacity among active zones based on actual load.

What is the maximum piping distance for the Mitsubishi MXZ-4C36NA?

Maximum refrigerant piping run from the outdoor unit to the furthest indoor unit is 82 feet of actual length, with a maximum equivalent length of 164 feet. The elevation difference between indoor and outdoor units cannot exceed 49 feet. These parameters cover the majority of low-rise commercial building retrofit applications.

What warranty does Mitsubishi provide on the MXZ-4C36NA?

7 years on parts and 7 years on the compressor with product registration. This is the longest standard warranty in the residential and light commercial mini split category. Installation by a Mitsubishi Diamond Contractor may extend the warranty period — verify current warranty terms with your local distributor.
