Extended Analysis

The MRCOOL Oasis H2i+ is MRCOOL's premium cold-climate unit, launched in 2022 as a direct response to Mitsubishi's Hyper-Heat dominance. It uses R-32 refrigerant rather than R-410A, which enables higher efficiency and lower global warming potential. The switch to R-32 is aligned with EPA's HFC phase-down schedule under the AIM Act — Mitsubishi's transition to R-32 across the GL line is ongoing and the Oasis has a head start.

Cold-climate performance is the headline claim: the Oasis H2i+ is rated to -22°F minimum operation, compared to -13°F for the Mitsubishi GL12NA. NEEP has listed the Oasis in its Cold Climate Air Source Heat Pump Listing since 2023. Independent measurements confirm the Oasis delivers approximately 13,000 BTU at -4°F — marginally above the Mitsubishi GL12NA at the same ambient. At -13°F, the Oasis continues to operate (the GL12NA shuts down) though at reduced capacity around 10,000 BTU.

Reliability is the open question for the Oasis. The product line is 3 years old in 2026. MRCOOL's earlier DIY products have accumulated 8+ years of field data; the Oasis series has not. The compressor used in the Oasis (a variable-speed rotary from a major OEM supplier) is a proven platform, but MRCOOL's quality control and long-term service infrastructure are not yet proven to Mitsubishi's standard. Buyers in zones 5–7 considering the Oasis as a primary heat source should weigh this uncertainty against the price advantage.

The equipment price advantage over the Mitsubishi GL12NA is approximately $150–$250 at current street prices. When installed by a contractor, total system cost is similar to the Mitsubishi. The Oasis is a credible alternative for buyers who prioritize the lower GWP of R-32, the extended cold-climate minimum temperature, or the marginal efficiency advantage — but who accept a shorter warranty and less established reliability record.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the MRCOOL Oasis H2i+ compare to Mitsubishi Hyper-Heat?
The Oasis H2i+ edges out the Mitsubishi GL12NA on paper: 20 SEER2 vs 19 SEER2, HSPF2 11.0 vs 10.0, and minimum operating temperature -22°F vs -13°F. The Mitsubishi leads on warranty (12 yr parts vs 7 yr) and established reliability data (15 yr track record vs 3 yr). For climate zones 1–4, both are excellent. For zones 5–7, the Mitsubishi's longer warranty and verified reliability are meaningful advantages.
What is R-32 refrigerant and why does it matter?
R-32 is a hydrofluorocarbon refrigerant with approximately 70% lower global warming potential than R-410A. The EPA is phasing down R-410A production and sale under the AIM Act; new equipment will be required to use low-GWP refrigerants by 2025–2026. The Oasis H2i+ is already on the post-transition refrigerant, which reduces the risk of refrigerant availability issues over the unit's 12–15 year service life.
Does the MRCOOL Oasis H2i+ have a DIY installation option?
No. The Oasis H2i+ uses a standard refrigerant circuit that requires professional EPA 608-certified installation and equipment. The DIY pre-charged lineset system is available only on MRCOOL's standard DIY product line, not the Oasis premium series.
Is the MRCOOL Oasis covered by the IRA tax credit?
Yes. The MRCOOL Oasis H2i+ 12K is ENERGY STAR certified and qualifies for the IRA Section 25C residential energy efficiency credit — 30% of qualifying costs, up to $2,000 per household per year. Verify the AHRI-certified combination with your installer.
How reliable is the MRCOOL Oasis H2i+?
The Oasis H2i+ product line launched in 2022. Three years of field data is insufficient to make a definitive long-term reliability statement. User reviews skew positive and contractor-reported failure rates are low. Until the product has 7–10 years of verifiable field data, IndustrialRank rates Oasis Reliability at 8.0 — good, but below Mitsubishi's 9.4.
