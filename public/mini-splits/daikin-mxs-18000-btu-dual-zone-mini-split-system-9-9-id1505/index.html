Review

The Daikin MXS 18.9 SEER2 dual-zone mini split positions itself as a practical mid-market solution for homeowners and contractors seeking multi-zone climate control without flagship pricing. This 18,000 BTU system (9,000 per zone) combines 230V single-phase operation with wall-mounted indoor units, making it adaptable to various residential layouts. At $1,526.40, it undercuts premium brands while maintaining respectable efficiency credentials and Daikin's established service reputation.

Performance metrics justify this system's positioning. The 18.9 SEER2 rating delivers substantial energy savings compared to standard equipment, while the 12.5 HSPF heating efficiency supports moderate climates effectively between 5°F and 60°F outdoor temperatures. Operating noise spans 22-58 dBA depending on configuration, with the quietest setting suitable for noise-sensitive environments. The 12.7 EER cooling coefficient indicates solid part-load efficiency during mild weather operation.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • 18.9 SEER2 rating exceeds ENERGY STAR requirements, reducing operational costs significantly over the unit's lifespan
  • HSPF of 12.5 provides excellent heating performance, useful in moderate climates down to 5°F outdoor temperatures
  • Dual-zone wall-mounted design serves two independent spaces simultaneously from a single outdoor compressor unit
  • Quiet operation ranges from 22-58 dBA depending on fan speed, making it suitable for bedrooms and offices
  • 12-year warranty with online registration provides extended coverage against manufacturer defects

Cons

  • Precharged refrigerant line limited to 98 feet, restricting placement flexibility between indoor and outdoor units
  • Swing compressor type is less efficient than rotary designs in some high-load scenarios
  • Minimum 15.8 amp breaker requirement necessitates dedicated 230V electrical circuit, adding installation costs

Who Should Buy

Best For

Homeowners with two separate rooms or small commercial spaces needing simultaneous heating and cooling will appreciate the dual-zone capability and efficiency. Contractors seeking a reliable, mid-range system for multi-zone residential retrofits will find this Daikin unit delivers good performance at competitive pricing.

Consider Alternatives If

Properties with outdoor unit placement beyond 98 feet from indoor units or those requiring operation below 5°F should explore alternative systems with extended refrigerant precharged lengths or enhanced cold-climate performance.

Installation

Installation demands a dedicated 230V circuit with 15.8 to 20 amp breaker capacity. The precharged refrigerant line accommodates up to 98 feet of copper tubing between outdoor and indoor units. Wall-mounted interior units measure 31.5 inches wide by 11.625 inches tall, requiring level surface mounting. Professional installation is strongly recommended given electrical requirements and refrigerant handling protocols.

Warranty & Reliability

Daikin provides 12 years of manufacturer defect coverage when you register online. This extended warranty is competitive within the mid-tier segment and reflects confidence in component longevity. Coverage typically includes compressor and sealed system components, though homeowners should verify specific terms with their installer before purchase.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I install this system myself? +
No. This unit requires 208/230V electrical work, refrigerant charging expertise, and evacuation procedures. DIY installation voids warranty and violates EPA regulations. Professional HVAC technicians must handle installation to ensure proper operation and legal compliance.
How far can I run the refrigerant lines? +
The system comes precharged for 98 feet of copper tubing between outdoor and indoor units. Beyond this distance, additional refrigerant charge is required, increasing installation costs. Longer runs may also impact efficiency slightly due to line losses.
Will this heat my home below freezing? +
Yes, but with limitations. The system operates down to 5°F outdoor temperature for heating, making it viable in moderate climates. In severe cold regions, you'll need backup electric resistance heating. Check your local winter temperatures before purchase.
How does 18.9 SEER2 compare to other dual-zone systems? +
18.9 SEER2 is above average for dual-zone equipment in this price range. It exceeds ENERGY STAR minimum requirements and rivals many premium brands. Over 10 years, this efficiency advantage translates to $300-500 in lower energy bills for typical usage.
What does the 12-year warranty cover? +
Manufacturer defects in compressor and sealed system components are covered. You must register online within specified timeframe. Wear items like filters and thermal expansion valves may require separate coverage verification with your installer.
