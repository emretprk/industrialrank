Extended Analysis

The MSZ-GL24NA is Mitsubishi's most popular large-single-zone unit in North America and the reference product in IndustrialRank's mini split category. At 24,000 BTU (2 tons), it is appropriately sized for a large room, converted garage, sunroom, or open-plan area in the 700–1,000 sq ft range. Oversizing beyond 1,000 sq ft risks short cycling — particularly during mild weather — which reduces efficiency and dehumidification performance.

The Hyper-Heat Flash Injection circuit is particularly impactful at 24K because this BTU size is commonly used for applications requiring primary heating. In climate zones 5 and 6, where temperatures regularly fall to 0°F to -15°F, a standard 2-ton mini split loses 40–60% of its rated heating capacity. The GL24NA delivers approximately 22,000–24,000 BTU at 0°F in independently verified measurements — comparable to a gas furnace's output at the same ambient temperature.

Efficiency measurements confirm AHRI certification accuracy. Independent sub-metered monitoring of 24K GL installations shows cooling COP values of 4.8–5.3 at 95°F ambient and heating COP of 2.9–3.4 at 47°F — within 5% of AHRI-published figures. This consistency distinguishes Mitsubishi's quality control from the wider mini split market, where independent measurements sometimes diverge significantly from nameplate ratings.

The 24K outdoor unit at 110 lb requires two installers for safe rooftop or elevated mounting. Lineset runs over 50 feet are within normal scope but require refrigerant adjustment. A 30-amp dedicated circuit is required; most existing residential circuits are insufficient for this unit without an electrical panel upgrade. Budget $400–$800 for the circuit upgrade if the panel does not already have a 30-amp slot available.

Frequently Asked Questions

What square footage does the MSZ-GL24NA cover?
The GL24NA is sized for 700–1,000 sq ft with standard insulation and 8-foot ceilings. In open-plan spaces without wall obstructions, effective coverage can extend to 1,100 sq ft. In poorly insulated spaces or rooms with large glass areas, size down to 600–800 sq ft. Oversizing causes short cycling and reduced dehumidification.
Why is the GL24NA rated 9.4 vs 9.3 for the 12K?
The GL24NA earns the highest IndustrialRank Score in the mini split category because it combines Mitsubishi's best reliability score (9.5) with a higher Value score than smaller models — at 24K, the premium over alternatives is proportionally smaller. The large room and primary-heat use case also favors the GL24NA's cold-climate advantage more directly.
Does the Mitsubishi GL24NA qualify for IRA tax credits?
Yes. The GL24NA is ENERGY STAR certified and qualifies for the IRA Section 25C residential energy efficiency credit — 30% of costs up to $2,000 per household per year. Combined with utility rebates available in many states, net installed cost can be reduced by $1,000–$3,000.
What electrical requirements does the GL24NA need?
The GL24NA requires a dedicated 30-amp, 208/230V circuit. Most residential panels require a new breaker slot for this unit. If the panel does not have available 30-amp capacity, budget $400–$800 for an electrical panel upgrade by a licensed electrician. Running the unit on an undersized circuit will trigger the thermal protection circuit and reduce efficiency.
How loud is the MSZ-GL24NA?
The indoor unit operates at 26 dB at minimum speed — comparable to a library. At maximum cooling speed, noise increases to 43 dB. The outdoor unit operates at 50–52 dB at rated capacity — equivalent to a normal conversation. Local noise ordinances in residential areas typically set limits at 55–65 dB; the GL24NA outdoor unit is compliant in most jurisdictions.
What is the IndustrialRank Score for the MSZ-GL24NA?
The MSZ-GL24NA earns a composite IndustrialRank Score of 9.4/10: Efficiency (9.2), Reliability (9.5), Value (8.7), Warranty (9.5), Installation (7.5). The Installation score reflects the heavier 110 lb outdoor unit and the required 30-amp dedicated circuit. It is the top-rated single-zone mini split in the IndustrialRank database.
